19. Labelling the 5 marbles with 1 to 5, and putting it in a container: What is the Experimental Probability of acquiring an odd-labelled marble in the second draw? What is the Theoretical Probability of acquiring an odd-labelled marble in the second draw?​


There are two possible ways to approach this problem, depending on whether the first draw is replaced or not.

Assuming that the first marble is NOT replaced:

There are 5 marbles in the container, so the probability of drawing an odd-labelled marble on the first draw is 2/5 (marbles 1, 3, and 5 are odd).If an even-labelled marble is drawn on the first draw, there are 4 marbles left in the container, with 2 odd-labelled marbles (marbles 3 and 5). So the probability of drawing an odd-labelled marble on the second draw, given that an even-labelled marble was drawn on the first draw, is 2/4 or 1/2.If an odd-labelled marble is drawn on the first draw, there are 4 marbles left in the container, with 1 odd-labelled marble (marble 3). So the probability of drawing an odd-labelled marble on the second draw, given that an odd-labelled marble was drawn on the first draw, is 1/4.The experimental probability of acquiring an odd-labelled marble in the second draw depends on the results of the first draw. If we conduct the experiment multiple times and record the results, the experimental probability will depend on how many times an even-labelled or odd-labelled marble was drawn on the first draw. For example, if we conduct the experiment 10 times and get the following results:First draw: even, odd, even, even, odd, odd, even, even, odd, evenSecond draw (when first draw was even): odd, odd, odd, even, evenSecond draw (when first draw was odd): odd, even, odd, even, odd

The experimental probability of acquiring an odd-labelled marble in the second draw is 4/10 or 2/5 (because 4 out of 10 second draws resulted in an odd-labelled marble), which is the same as the experimental probability of acquiring an odd-labelled marble on the first draw.


Assuming that the first marble IS replaced:

The probability of drawing an odd-labelled marble on the first draw is still 2/5.Since the first marble is replaced, the probabilities of drawing an odd-labelled marble or an even-labelled marble on the second draw are the same, regardless of the result of the first draw. So the theoretical probability of acquiring an odd-labelled marble in the second draw is still 2/5. This is because there are still 2 odd-labelled marbles and 3 even-labelled marbles in the container, and replacing the first marble does not change the probability of drawing an odd-labelled or even-labelled marble on the second draw.

30. true experimental design to quasi-experimental


Answer:

Like a true experiment, a quasi-experimental design aims to establish a cause-and-effect relationship between an independent and dependent variable. However, unlike a true experiment, a quasi-experiment does not rely on random assignment. Instead, subjects are assigned to groups based on non-random criteria.
