15. An order, signed by a judge and directed to a peace officer, commanding him to search for certain personal property and bring it before the court.


The order, authorized by a judge, that allows a peace officer to legally search a certain property and bring it before the court is called a SEARCH WARRANT.

One of the constitutional rights of the Filipino people is the right to property and privacy. This means that everyone has the right to do as he pleases with his property and to do it privately without needing to search for permission to do so.

Even so, for the sake of the public good, there are exemptions to this right by citizens and the legal way where one can forsake this right is when a judge issues a search warrant.

There are no constitutionalized guidelines on the requirements needed for a judge to be able to issue a search warrant, it is all dependent on the discretion of the judge. We hope that a judge’s moral character would compel him to issue a search warrant when and only when there is a reasonable suspicion of a crime and the possibility of harm being wrought upon others.

20. An order, signed by a judge and directed to a peace officer, commanding him to search for certain personal property and bring it before the court.


Search Warrant

This is an order in written form, signed by a judge and directed to a peace officer such as a policeman, to give him command of searching for specific personal property and bringing it before the court proceedings.

Importance and relevance

Search warrants are essential because it helps those who seek justice find the evidence they are looking for. It is also relevant because this operation will help ease and make the proceedings faster. Without search warrants, you have the right to decline the officer's order to search for things inside your property. We can also say that a search warrant is an order so they won't get charged with illegal trespassing from our personal property.

Application

Here are the steps on how a search process should be done.

A search warrant shall be filed in any court within whose territorial jurisdiction a crime was committed.

A search warrant will be issued to the qualified officer, appointed by the court to proceed with the search and seizure operation.

The officer will serve the copy of the search warrant personally. They can also do the 'sneak and peek' process that allows officers not to mention the search warrant operations.

After gathering all the necessary things, the officers will present the confiscated belongings that are relevant to the investigation.
