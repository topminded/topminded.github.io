12. How is the cost of electrical energy consumption computed?


Answer:

Electricity is measured in kilowatt-hours on your bill, not watt-hours. One kilowatt is equal to 1,000 watts, so to calculate how many kWh a device uses, divide the watt-hours from the previous step by 1,000. Using our previous example, this means you would divide 375 watt-hours by 1,000, resulting in 0.375 kWh.

Answer:

The cost of electrical energy consumption is computed by using the following formula

Explanation:

To calculate the energy consumption and associated costs, you take the total number of hours you use a device, then multiply it with the wattage usage of that device per hour, and divide it by 1000, or  

(Operating Hours  x  Watt Usage per hour ) ÷ 1000 x  Electricity Costs per kWh = Electrical energy consumption cost  

                           

Dividing it by 1000 changes it from watt-hours to kilowatt-hours. The unit for energy consumption is kilowatt-hour or kWh.  

For example:  

We want to compute for the electrical energy consumption of a light bulb if the electricity cost is Php 10 per kWh.

Operating Hours  x  Watt Usage per hour) ÷ 1000 x Electricity Costs per hour= (100 watts x 730 hours) ÷1000 x  Php 10  

= Php73.0

17. HOW IS ENERGY POWER RATING RELATED TO ENERGY CONSUMPTION?


Answer:

The larger the power rating in the electrical appliance, the more energy is used every second. The longer the usage time, the more electrical energy is consumed. 1 kilowatt-hour represents the amount of energy consumed in 1 hour by an electrical appliance at the rate of 1 kilowatt.
