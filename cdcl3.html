4. For many years chloroform (CHCl3) was used as an inhalation anesthetic in spite of the fact that it is also a toxic substance that may cause severe liver, kidney, and heart. Calculate the percent composition by mass of this compound.​


SOLUTION:

Step 1: Calculate the molar mass of CHCl₃

Note that the molar masses of C, H, and Cl are 12.011 g, 1.008 g, and 35.45 g, respectively.

[tex]\begin{aligned} \text{molar mass of} \: \text{CHCl}_3 & = \text{12.011 g + 1.008 g + 3(35.45 g)} \\ & = \text{119.369 g} \end{aligned}[/tex]

Step 2: Solve for the percentage composition of CHCl₃.

• For C

[tex]\begin{aligned} \%\text{C} & = \frac{n \times \text{molar mass of C}}{\text{molar mass of} \: \text{CHCl}_3} \times 100\% \\ & = \frac{1 \times \text{12.011 g}}{\text{119.369 g}} \times 100\% \\ & = \boxed{10.0621\%} \end{aligned}[/tex]

• For H

[tex]\begin{aligned} \%\text{H} & = \frac{n \times \text{molar mass of H}}{\text{molar mass of} \: \text{CHCl}_3} \times 100\% \\ & = \frac{1 \times \text{1.008 g}}{\text{119.369 g}} \times 100\% \\ & = \boxed{0.8444\%} \end{aligned}[/tex]

• For Cl

[tex]\begin{aligned} \%\text{Cl} & = \frac{n \times \text{molar mass of Cl}}{\text{molar mass of} \: \text{CHCl}_3} \times 100\% \\ & = \frac{3 \times \text{35.45 g}}{\text{119.369 g}} \times 100\% \\ & = \boxed{89.0935\%} \end{aligned}[/tex]

Hence, chloroform is composed of 10.0621% C, 0.8444% H, and 89.0935% Cl by mass.


5. The numerical values of the coefficients a, b and c, respectively for the reaction. aP4 + bCl2 → cPCl3 are _____________. a. 2, 4, 6 b. 2, 6, 4 c. 2, 3, 4 d. 1, 6, 4 ASAP ANSWER


The coefficients in the balanced chemical equation aP4 + bCl2 → cPCl3 are c = 2, a = 3, b = 4.

When balancing a chemical equation, you need to make sure that the number of atoms of each element on both sides of the equation are equal. In this case, you can see that 4 Phosphorus atoms and 8 Chlorine atoms are on the reactants side, and 4 Phosphorus atoms and 12 Chlorine atoms are on the products side.

So, the correct option is c. 2, 3, 4

15. The hydrogen sulfide in 80.0 gram crude petroleum was removed by distillation and uncollected in a solution of CdCl2. The precipitated CdS was filtered, washed, and ignited to CdSO4. Calculate the percentage of H2S in the sample if 0.125 g of CdSO4 was recovered


Answer:

The percentage of H₂S in the sample is 0.0255%.

Explanation:

This problem is an example of gravimetry. Gravimetry is one of the basic analytical methods where you study the composition of raw materials or substances. It deals with the quantitative determination of an analyte based on the mass of the solid compositions in a sample.

In solving gravimetric problems, stoichiometry is vital. For us to use stoichiometry, we first need a balanced equation, then we proceed to dimensional analysis.

The balanced chemical equation for the problem is:

CdSO₄ + H₂S  → CdS + H₂SO₄

Getting the molar mass of the compounds needed, we have:

Molar mass (MM) of CdSO₄ = 208.472 g/mol

MM of H₂S = 34.081 g/mol

Solving for the mass of H₂S in the sample using stoichiometry:

0.125 g CdSO₄ × [tex](\frac{1 mol CdSO4}{208.472 g}) x (\frac{1 mol H2S}{1 mol CdSO4}) x (\frac{34.081 g H2S}{1 mol})[/tex]

= 0.020435 grams H₂S

From the dimensional analysis, we can see that the units will cancel until the unit grams H₂S will remain.

Solving for percentage of H₂S in the sample:

Mass of sample = 80 gMass of H₂S in the sample = 0.020435 g% H₂S = [tex]\frac{mass H2S}{mass sample}[/tex] × 100%% H₂S = [tex]\frac{0.020435 g H2S}{80 g sample}[/tex] × 100%% H₂S = 0.0255%

Therefore, the percentage of H₂S in the sample is 0.0255%.

Rate gas 1/Rate gas 2 = sqrt(Molecular Weight gas 1/Molecular Weight gas 2)

Molecular Weight of Argon = 39.95 g/mol

Molecular Weight of CHCI3 = 119.5 g/mol; It was calculated as (1*12) +(1*1)+(3*35.5)=119.50 g/mol, where Atomic mass of C = 12 u, H = 1u, CI=35.5u.

Rate gas 1 (Argon) = 25mL/6.3 mins. = 39.7mL/mins=.

Let x, Rate gas 2 (CHCI3) = x

3.97mL/min/x = sqrt(119.5 g/mol/39.95 g/mol) = 1.73

Then, x = 2.30 mL/min.

Therefore, It will take 2.30 mL/min x 25 minutes = 57.50 minutes

26. Which of the following chemical formula is polar?a. CCl3 c.CH4b. AgCl d.NH3​


Answer:

D.NH3

Explanation:

Polar Molecules

Polar molecules occur when two atoms do not share electrons equally in a covalent bond. A dipole forms, with part of the molecule carrying a slight positive charge and the other part carrying a slight negative charge. This happens when there is a difference between the electronegativity values of each atom. An extreme difference forms an ionic bond, while a lesser difference forms a polar covalent bond. Fortunately, you can look up electronegativity on a table to predict whether or not atoms are likely to form polar covalent bonds.If the electronegativity difference between the two atoms is between 0.5 and 2.0, the atoms form a polar covalent bond. If the electronegativity difference between the atoms is greater than 2.0, the bond is ionic. Ionic compounds are extremely polar molecules.

Examples of polar molecules include:

Water - H2OAmmonia - NH3Sulfur dioxide - SO2Hydrogen sulfide - H2SEthanol - C2H6OSana po nakatulong

27. Chloroform, CHCl3, is a colorless, sweet- smelling liquid that was once used as anesthetic in surgery. CHCl3 may react with chlorine gas, Cl2, to produce carbon tetrachloride, CCl4, and hydrochloric acid, HCl. Compute for the percent yield of the reaction if 36.8 g of CCl4 was recovered when 30.0g of CHCl3 is allowed to react with excess Cl2.


Answer:

percent yield = 95.1%

Explanation:

Write the balanced chemical equation.

CHCl3 + Cl2 → CCl4 + HCl

Compute the theoretical yield.

theoretical yield = 30.0 g CHCl3 × (1 mol CHCl3 / 119.38 g CHCl3) × (1 mol CCl4 / 1 mol CHCl3) × (153.82 g CCl4 / 1 mol CCl4)

theoretical yield = 38.7 g

Solve for the percent yield.

percent yield = (actual yield / theoretical yield) × 100

percent yield = (36.8 g / 38.7 g) × 100

percent yield = 95.1%
