2. A rectangular water tank is 3 meters high. It has a square base whose side is 0.8 meters. What is the volume of the tank?​


The volume of a rectangular water tank can be calculated as:

volume = base area x height

    In this case, the base of the water tank is a square with side 0.8 meters, so its base area is:

base area = side x side = 0.8 m x 0.8 m = 0.64 square meters

The height of the water tank is 3 meters.

Therefore, the volume of the tank is:

volume = base area x height = 0.64 sq m x 3 m = 1.92 cubic meters

So the volume of the rectangular water tank is 1.92 cubic meters.

3. The surface area of an open cylindrical tank is 68 square meter. if the diameter is 2/3 of its height, what is the height of the tank


[tex]\tt{\huge{\red{Solution:}}}[/tex]

Since the diameter is 2/3 of height

d = (2/3)(h)

Since d = 2r

2r = (2/3)(h)

r = h/3 (1)

The formula for the surface area of an open cylindrical tank is

S = πr² + 2πrh

Substituting (1) and value of S to the formula to solve for the height

68 = π(h/3)² + 2π(h/3)(h)

68 = π(h²/9) + 2π(h²/3)

68 = (π/3)[(h²/3) + 2h²]

68 = (π/3)[(h²/3) + (6h²/3)]

68 = (π/3)(7h²/3)

68 = (7π/9)(h²)

h² = [68 / (7π/9)]

h² = 612/(7π)

h² = 27.8294

h = √27.8294

[tex]\boxed{h = \text{5.28 m}}[/tex]

Therefore, the height of the tank is 5.28 m.

29. A rectangular tank with a square base contains 3042 cm³ of water. The height of thewater level is 18 cm. Find the length of one side of the square base.​


Answer:

13 cm

Step-by-step explanation:

In order to determine the length of a square base, we must manipulate the volume's formula.

Volume = lwh

V= s²h

V/h = s²

√(V/h) = s

Substitute the given into the formula above,

√(3042/18) = s

√169 = s

13 = s

Therefore, the length of one side of a square base is 13 cm.
