7. solving linear systems by substitutioneq (1) 7x-4y=5eq (2) 8x+y=-11use the eq (2) first.​


To solve linear systems by substitution, we first use one of the equations to solve for one of the variables in terms of the other. Then we substitute that expression into the other equation and solve for the remaining variable.

In this case, we will use equation (2) first. We will solve for y in terms of x.

8x + y = -11

y = -8x -11

Now we substitute this expression of y into equation (1)

7x - 4y = 5

7x - 4(-8x - 11) = 5

7x - (-32x - 44) = 5

39x = 49

x = 49/39

Now that we know x, we can substitute it back into the original expression of y to find y.

y = -8x - 11

y = -8(49/39) - 11

y = -56/39

The solution of the system is x = 49/39, y = -56/39.

Alternatively, you can solve for x in equation (1) instead of y in equation (2), and continue the process to get the same solution.
