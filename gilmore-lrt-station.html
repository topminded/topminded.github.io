5. LRT-2 train left santolan station at 8:0 a. m.and arrived at the recto station at 8:30 a.m if the distance between the two station is 13 kilometers what was the average speed of the train


To find the average speed of the train, we can use the formula:

average speed = total distance ÷ total time

In this case, the total distance is 13 kilometers (the distance between Santolan Station and Recto Station), and the total time is 30 minutes (the time it took for the train to travel from Santolan Station to Recto Station).

However, the formula for average speed requires that the distance and time are expressed in the same units. Since the distance is in kilometers and the time is in minutes, we need to convert the time to hours:

30 minutes = 30 ÷ 60 hours = 0.5 hours

Now we can substitute the values into the formula:

average speed = 13 km ÷ 0.5 hours = 26 km/h

Therefore, the average speed of the train was 26 kilometers per hour.

Note: It's important to keep in mind that this is the average speed over the entire journey from Santolan Station to Recto Station. The actual speed of the train may have varied at different points along the way depending on factors such as traffic and station stops.

b.) 28 students had ridden the LRT only.

c.) 9 students had ridden the jeep only.

d.) 11 students did not ride any of the three.

Step-by-step explanation

3-CIRCLE VENN DIAGRAM:

A three-circle Venn diagram, named after English logician Robert Venn, is a diagram that uses three overlapping circles to explain how the elements of three sets are related.

In most set situations, it's easier to choose a larger set that includes all of the items from all of the sets under consideration. The universal set is the larger set, and it is commonly denoted by the letter E. The universal set is usually represented by a huge rectangle in a Venn diagram, while smaller sets are represented by circles within this rectangle.

There are three (3) circles given: LRT circle, Bus circle, and Jeep Circle.

In LRT’s circle, we have the following numbers: 28, 4, 15, and 25. Adding all these will result to 72 students.

In Bus’s circle, we have the following numbers: 23, 5, 15, and 25. Adding all these will result to 68 students.

In Jeep’s circle, we have the following numbers: 9, 4, 15, and 5. Adding all these will result to 3 students.

When the three circles in a Venn diagram overlap, the overlapping areas contain elements shared by either two or three circles.

Some important observations:

Elements in LRT and BUS = elements in LRT and Bus only (25) plus elements in LRT, Bus, and Jeep (The middle portion, which is 15).

Elements in LRT and JEEP = elements in LRT and Jeep (4) only plus elements in LRT, Bus, and Jeep (The middle portion, which is 15).

Elements in BUS and JEEP = elements in Bus and Jeep (5) only plus elements LRT, Bus, and Jeep (The middle portion, which is 15).

Questions to answer:

a.) How many had ridden the bus only?

Answer: 23 students had ridden the bus only. (See attached image #1, the shaded portion indicates the word “only”)

b.) How many had ridden the LRT only?

Answer: 28 students had ridden the LRT only. (See attached image #2, the shaded portion indicates the word “only”)

c.) How many had ridden the jeep only?

Answer: 9 students had ridden the jeep only. (See attached image #3, the shaded portion indicates the word “only”)

d.) How many did not ride on any of the three modes of transportation?

Add all the numbers in the all the circles: 28 + 4 + 15 + 25 + 23 + 5 + 4 = 109

We subtract this to the total number of students:

Total number of students – 109 = ___

120 – 109 = 11

Therefore, 11 students did not ride any of the three.
