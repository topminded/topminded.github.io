Answer:

Communicable diseases comprise infectious diseases such as tuberculosis and measles, while non-communicable diseases (NCDs) are mostly chronic diseases such as cardiovascular diseases, cancers, and diabetes.

13. 1. Leading causes of common communicable diseases -2. Best preventions against common communicable diseases -​


Answer:

1) Bacteria, viruses, fungi and parasites.

2) Handle and prepare food safety, wash hands often, get vaccinated, avoid wild animals, don't share personal items and many more.

Answer:

1. Communicable diseases are illnesses caused by viruses or bacteria that people spread to one another through contact with contaminated surfaces, bodily fluids, blood products, insect bites, or through the air.

2. Wash your hands often. This is especially important before and after preparing food, before eating and after using the toilet.

Get vaccinated. Immunization can drastically reduce your chances of contracting many diseases. Keep your recommended vaccinations up-to-date.

Use antibiotics sensibly. Take antibiotics only when prescribed. Unless otherwise directed, or unless you are allergic to them, take all prescribed doses of your antibiotic, even if you begin to feel better before you have completed the medication.

Stay at home if you have signs and symptoms of an infection. Don't go to work or class if you're vomiting, have diarrhea or are running a fever.

17. DIRECTION: Provide the Aims/Goals of each initiative in the right column.AIMS/GOALSGLOBAL HEALTH INITIATIVESGlobal Fight against Communicable DiseasesGlobal Prevention and Control of Tobacco andAlcohol UseGlobal Strategy to Reduce the Harmful Use ofAlcoholGlobal Strategy for the Prevention and Controlof Non-Communicable DiseasesGlobal Initiative for Mental Health​


Answer:

1.Global Fight against Communicable Diseases

Aims/Goals:

-Ending communicable diseases like Tuberculosis, HIV/AIDS, Hepatitis, and eliminating Malaria, other vector borne diseases and Neglected Tropical Diseases;

-Eradicating and eliminating vaccine preventable diseases and reducing the number of new infections, e.g. by improving access to immunizations; and

-Treating people affected by, and managing infectious diseases, non - communicable diseases and mental health.

2.Global Prevention and Control of Tobacco and Alcohol Use

Aims/Goals:

-To promote individual, community and government responsibility to prevent and reduce tobacco use through multi-sectoral participation in tobacco control.

-To reduce mortality and morbidity due to the use of tobacco.

3.Global Strategy to Reduce the Harmful Use of Alcohol

Aims/Goals:

-to improve the health and social outcomes for individuals, families and communities.

-considerably reduced morbidity and mortality due to harmful use of alcohol and their ensuing social consequences.

4.Global Strategy for the Prevention and Control of Non-Communicable Diseases

Aims/Goals:

-to map the emerging epidemics of noncommunicable diseases and to analyse their social, economic, behavioural and political determinants

5.Global Initiative for Mental Health​

Aims/Goals:

-to improve services for people living with mental health problems and psychosocial disabilities worldwide, especially in low- and middle-income countries

What are the key insights that have emerged so far?

- Multiple studies have examined the effectiveness of coordination efforts at the national, province and district level. While some countries report successes in improving coordination, it still remains a serious obstacle to more effective HIV/AIDS programmes;

- Evidence from a small number of countries suggests that scale-up of HIV/AIDS services is happening; and that it has, to-date, had no impacts (either positive or negative) upon the quantity of other types of health services delivered;

- Expansion of services appears to have been achieved by health workers working harder and longer hours, rather than through the addition of more staff but it is unclear what the scope for further enhanced productivity might be;

- Two emerging concerns were expressed over sustainability and the balance between treatment, prevention and care and support programmes.
