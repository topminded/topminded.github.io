7. Examples of local literature​


Local literature refers to works of literature that are written by authors who are from a particular region or country and reflect the cultural, social, and historical values of that place. Here are a few examples of local literature from different regions of the world:

Latin America: "One Hundred Years of Solitude" by Gabriel Garcia Marquez, "The House of the Spirits" by Isabel Allende, and "The Motorcycle Diaries" by Ernesto Che Guevara.

Europe: "The Tin Drum" by Gunter Grass, "The Book of Disquiet" by Fernando Pessoa, and "The Master and Margarita" by Mikhail Bulgakov.

North America: "The Catcher in the Rye" by J.D. Salinger, "The Color Purple" by Alice Walker, and "The Bluest Eye" by Toni Morrison.

These are just a few examples, and there are many more works of local literature from various regions and countries around the world.

Answer:

Foreign literature in information technology includes scholarly books, articles, and journals on topics such as digital transformation, artificial intelligence, cybersecurity, machine learning, and data science.

Local literature in information technology includes books, articles, and journals by local authors focusing on a specific country or region, such as the Philippines, India, and China. These works often discuss the unique challenges and opportunities posed by the latest technological advancements in the region.

Foreign studies in information technology include research projects undertaken outside of a country or region's borders, such as large-scale, multi-national research projects. These studies are often more comprehensive in nature and include broader topics such as the global impact of digital transformation and online security.

Local studies in information technology include research projects conducted within a country or region, such as analyzing the effects of the internet and mobile technology on local economies. These studies are usually more focused and provide insights into topics such as rural access to technology or the use of technology in education.

Explanation:

One example of local literature is "Noli Me Tangere" by Filipino national hero, Dr. Jose Rizal. It is a novel published in 1887 that depicts the Philippine society during the Spanish colonial era. The story revolves around the life of Crisostomo Ibarra, a young man who returns to the Philippines after studying abroad, only to find the injustices and corruption prevailing in his homeland. He encounters various characters, including the patriotic Padre Damaso, the corrupt Captain Tiago, and the beautiful Maria Clara, who all represent different aspects of the society.

The novel exposes the cruelty and oppression of the Spanish colonial government, the friars, and the elite ruling class. It also portrays the struggles and sacrifices of the Filipino people who fought for their freedom and independence. The novel inspired the Philippine Revolution of 1896, which led to the country's independence from Spain in 1898.

"Noli Me Tangere" is a significant contribution to Philippine literature because of its impact on the country's history and culture. It is a classic work that continues to inspire generations of Filipinos to fight for social justice, national pride, and freedom.
