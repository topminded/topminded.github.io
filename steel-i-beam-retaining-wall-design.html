2. An iron beam 26 ft long leans against a vertical wall. The foot of the beam is being drawn away from the wall at the rate 4 ft/sec. How fast is the top of the beam sliding down the wall at the instant when the foot of the beam is 10 ft from the wall?


[tex]\mathbb{SOLUTION:}[/tex]

Refer to the figure

[tex]\\[/tex]

Using Pythagorean theorem:

[tex]\mathsf{x^2+y^2=(26)^2}[/tex]

[tex]\mathsf{x^2+y^2=676} \longleftarrow \textsf{equation 1}[/tex]

[tex]\\[/tex]

To determine how fast is the top of the beam sliding, differentiate equation 1 with respect to time (Use implicit differentiation) and isolate dy/dt.

[tex]\mathsf{\dfrac{d}{dt}(x^2+y^2=676)}[/tex]

[tex]\mathsf{2x\dfrac{dx}{dt}+2y\dfrac{dy}{dt}=0}[/tex]

[tex]\mathsf{2y\dfrac{dy}{dt}=-2x\dfrac{dx}{dt}}[/tex]

[tex]\mathsf{\dfrac{dy}{dt}=-\dfrac{2x}{2y}\dfrac{dx}{dt}}[/tex]

[tex]\mathsf{\dfrac{dy}{dt}=-\dfrac{x}{y}\dfrac{dx}{dt}}[/tex]

[tex]\\[/tex]

when x = 10, y = ?

We can get the value of y if x = 10 from equation 1

[tex]\mathsf{x^2+y^2=676}[/tex]

[tex]\mathsf{y^2=676-x^2}[/tex]

[tex]\mathsf{y=\sqrt{676-x^2}}[/tex]

[tex]\mathsf{y=\sqrt{676-(10)^2}}[/tex]

[tex]\mathsf{y=24 \ ft}[/tex]

[tex]\\[/tex]

When x = 10, y = 24, dx/dt = 4 fps, dy/dt = ?

(Note: fps means feet per second)

Substitute x = 10, y= 24 and dx/dt = 4,

[tex]\mathsf{\dfrac{dy}{dt}=-\dfrac{x}{y}\dfrac{dx}{dt}}[/tex]

[tex]\mathsf{\dfrac{dy}{dt}=-\dfrac{10}{24}(4)}[/tex]

[tex]\mathsf{\dfrac{dy}{dt}=-\dfrac{5}{3} \ fps}[/tex]

[tex]\\[/tex]

[tex]\mathbb{ANSWER:}[/tex]

The beam is sliding down at a rate of:

[tex]\boxed{\mathsf{\dfrac{dy}{dt}=-\dfrac{5}{3} \ or \ -1.67 \ fps}}[/tex]

side a = distance between the wall and the base of the beam

side b = the wall

side c = the beam

angle A = angle formed by the wall and the beam

angle B = angle formed the the beam and ground

angle C = angle formed by the wall and ground (90degrees) or the right angle.

We are to find angle B using the known variable which are sides b and c. With these we can use the formula:

sinB= opposite/hypotenuse

The side opposite angle B is the height of wll which is side b and the hypotenus is the height of the beam which is side c. Solution:

sinB=opposite/hypotenuse

sinB=b/c

sinB=13.9ft/20ft

sinB=0.695

14. An 8-foot steel beam leans against a 5-foot tree. What angle should the ground and the beam form for the beam to reach the top of the tree? Type an answer rounded off to one decimal place


Answer:

The angle between the ground and the beam should be approximately 32.7 degrees for the beam to reach the top of the tree.

Step-by-step explanation:

We can use trigonometry to find the angle between the ground and the beam. Let's call this angle θ.

We can use the tangent function, which is defined as the opposite side divided by the adjacent side in a right triangle, to find θ. In this case, the opposite side is the height of the tree, which is 5 feet, and the adjacent side is the distance from the tree to the base of the beam, which is 8 feet.

So, we have:

tan θ = opposite/adjacent = 5/8

Taking the inverse tangent (tan⁻¹) of both sides, we get:

θ = tan⁻¹(5/8)

Using a calculator, we find that:

θ ≈ 32.7 degrees

Therefore, the angle between the ground and the beam should be approximately 32.7 degrees for the beam to reach the top of the tree.

16. What power will be required to raise a 14,700N steel beam to a height of 14meters in 10minutes?​


[tex]\tt{\huge{\blue{Explanation:}}}[/tex]

The power of an object doing a work W in a given time t is given by

[tex]\boxed{P = \frac{W}{t}}[/tex]

where:

P = power

W = work

t = time

[tex]\tt{\huge{\red{Solution:}}}[/tex]

Solving for W

W = Fd

W = (14,700 N)(14 m)

W = 205,800 J

Solving for P

Note: 10 min = 600 s

[tex]P = \dfrac{W}{t}[/tex]

[tex]P = \dfrac{\text{205,800 J}}{\text{600 s}}[/tex]

[tex]\boxed{P = \text{343 W}}[/tex]

Therefore, the amount of power required is 343 W.
