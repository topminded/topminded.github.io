21. 5. Trade unions are important institutions because​


The objectives of trade unions are to defend and advance the interests of its members at work. The goal of trade unions is to establish close working ties with employers. This can occasionally take the shape of an agreement between the company and the union that outlines their shared goals and interests. Employers all around the world are aware of the numerous advantages collaborating with a recognized trade union may provide for their business. Trade unions, in particular, can assist employers to:

1. Develop trust among employees - In order to foster trust and dedication among the workforce and ensure that issues can be found and resolved swiftly and fairly, unions offer a channel for communication between employees and employers.

2. Ensure safety at work - By promoting safe working conditions and minimizing stress-related illnesses brought on by, for instance, long hours, bullying, or working in subpar conditions, union officials contribute to a reduction in the number of accidents that occur at work.

3. Auditing for labor code noncompliance - Unions are an excellent resource for information on workplace practices and are in a good position to cooperate with employers to detect and rectify subpar working conditions and labor standard violations. Enforcing labor regulations can be significantly helped by unions.

4. Give them access to education and training - Unions place a high premium on assisting members in accessing education and training. According to research, union certification regularly has a beneficial impact on how much and what kind of training is offered to employees.

5. Save money - Early detection of issues at work can result in significant cost savings, for instance by lowering the expenses employers incur due to employee sickness (including lost productivity, sick pay, temporary staff coverage, and accident-related compensation payments), as well as by lowering staff turnover.
