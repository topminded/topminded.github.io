1. What happens to the pressure and heat in the evaporator??


Answer:

In an evaporator, the pressure is reduced and the heat is being absorbed by the refrigerant.

Explanation:

Evaporator

Thermodynamics taught us that heat travels from a warmer area/ substance to a cooler one ("from hot to cold"). Subsequently, the evaporator should have a lower temperature than its surroundings, just like in air conditioning unit mechanism.

An evaporator is a type of device that absorbs the heat from its surrounding. Heat gained from heat absorption will cause the liquid refrigerant inside the device to boil to the point where refrigerant will leave the evaporator in a form of saturated or super heated gas.

An evaporator is a vital a part of an air conditioner. In an air conditioning unit, when the liquid refrigerant reaches the evaporator, the pressure will be reduced which cause the heat to scatter. This phenomenon will cause the heat from the warm air to be absorbed by the refrigerant, reaching its low boiling point at a fast rate.

In a simpler way of explanation, basically an evaporator absorbs heat while boiling the liquid refrigerant off.

Types of Evaporator:Falling Film Evaporator - handles viscous solution; used in the food, sugar, chemical and fermentation industriesMultiple-Effect Evaporator - combination of single evaporators; used in order to save heat and energy consumptionCirculation Evaporator - used for crystallizers, viscous fluids, waste streams and other difficult process fluids

8. this happens when gas loses heat ? a . deposition b . sublimation c . evaporation d . condensation​


Answer:

D. Condensation

Explanation:

Condensation is the change of the physical state of matter from the gas phase into the liquid phase, and is the reverse of vaporization. The word most often refers to the water cycle. It can also be defined as the change in the state of water vapor to liquid water when in contact with a liquid or solid surface or cloud condensation nuclei within the atmosphere. When the transition happens from the gaseous phase into the solid phase directly, the change is called deposition.
