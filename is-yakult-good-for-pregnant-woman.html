7. 7. At what instance will estrogen and progesterone levels drop on the 24th day? A. The woman is pregnant. B. The woman not pregnant. C. The woman is ovulating. D. The woman is bleeding. ​


Explanation:
Assuming a normal menstrual cycle of 28 days, estrogen and progesterone levels will drop on the 24th day if the woman is not pregnant and is not taking hormonal birth control.

In a typical menstrual cycle, estrogen and progesterone levels rise and fall in a pattern that helps to regulate ovulation and menstruation. If ovulation occurs around day 14, then the levels of these hormones will begin to decrease in the second half of the cycle, leading up to the start of menstruation.

So, if a woman has a normal menstrual cycle of 28 days, her estrogen and progesterone levels will drop on the 24th day if she is not pregnant and is not taking hormonal birth control. This drop in hormone levels is what triggers the shedding of the uterine lining and the start of menstruation. Therefore, the correct answer is

Answer:
B. The woman is not pregnant.
