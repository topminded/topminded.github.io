Water can move in a circular pattern due to a phenomenon called "vortex motion" or "vortices."

Vortices are created when a fluid (such as water) flows past an object or obstacle, causing the fluid to rotate around a central axis. The rotation of the fluid creates a low-pressure area in the center of the vortex, which pulls in more fluid and causes the vortex to grow in size and intensity.

In the case of a circular pattern of water movement, a vortex is usually formed by an object or disturbance in the water, such as a rock, a boat, or a sudden change in the water depth or current. As the water flows around the object, it begins to rotate and create a circular pattern of movement.

The circular motion of the water in a vortex can also be influenced by external factors such as wind, waves, or tidal currents. In these cases, the circular motion of the water can become more complex, with multiple vortices interacting with each other to create larger patterns of movement.

Overall, the circular pattern of water movement is a result of the physics of fluid dynamics, which governs how fluids (such as water) behave and interact with their environment.

12. Describe the difference between El Niño and La Niña. Fill in the table Below• Wind Direction• Water Movement • Area of the World these weather• Pattern Orginate• Effects of these weather pattern• In the economy ( Non sense answer will be reported ) ​


Answer:

El Niño refers to the above-average sea-surface temperatures that periodically develop across the east-central equatorial Pacific. It represents the warm phase of the ENSO cycle. La Niña refers to the periodic cooling of sea-surface temperatures across the east-central equatorial Pacific

WIND DIRECTION

During El Niño, the jet stream is oriented from west to east across the southern portion of the United States. Thus, this region becomes more susceptible to severe weather outbreaks during the winter. Conversely, during La Niña, the jet stream and severe weather are likely to be farther north

WATER MOVEMENT

El Niño events are associated with a warming of the central and eastern tropical Pacific, while La Niña events are the reverse, with a sustained cooling of these same areas

PATTERN ORGINATE

El Niño and La Niña affect not only ocean temperatures, but also how much it rains on land. Depending on which cycle occurs (and when), this can mean either droughts or flooding. Typically, El Niño and its warm waters are associated with drought, while La Niña is linked to increased flooding

EFFECTS OF THESE WEATHER PATTERN/IN THE ECONOMY

ENSO affects crop production because it disrupts normal weather patterns. More specifically, El Niño creates water shortages and La Niña creates water abundance, including flooding. Water shortages reduce crop planting areas, delay planting seasons, and generally lower crop yields

Water lily

Water lily, (family Nymphaeaceae), any of 58 species in 6 genera of freshwater plants native to the temperate and tropical parts of the world. Most species of water lilies have rounded, variously notched, waxy-coated leaves on long stalks that contain many air spaces and float in quiet freshwater habitats. The stalks arise from thick, fleshy, creeping underwater stems that are buried in the mud. The showy, fragrant, solitary flowers are borne at or above the water surface on long stalks that are attached to the underground stems. Each cuplike flower has a spiral arrangement of its numerous petals.
