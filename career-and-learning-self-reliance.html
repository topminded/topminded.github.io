1. give me explanation of career and learning self-reliance discovering c's?​


pa brainliest po

Career self-reliance refers to an individual's ability to manage their own career development and progression, including setting goals, identifying opportunities for growth and development, and taking action to achieve those goals. It involves taking personal responsibility for one's career path, rather than relying on others to guide or direct it.

Learning self-reliance is similar, but pertains to an individual's ability to take responsibility for their own learning and development. This includes identifying areas for improvement, seeking out resources and opportunities for learning, and taking action to acquire new knowledge and skills.

The "C's" in this context typically refer to certain key skills or attributes that are essential for success in both career and learning self-reliance. These may include:

Curiosity: A willingness to ask questions and seek out new knowledge and experiences.

Creativity: The ability to think outside the box and generate innovative ideas.

Critical thinking: The ability to analyze information and ideas to make informed decisions.

Communication: The ability to effectively convey ideas and information to others.

Collaboration: The ability to work effectively with others to achieve shared goals.

Courage: The willingness to take risks and face challenges in pursuit of goals.

By developing these skills and attributes, individuals can become more self-reliant in both their careers and their learning, empowering themselves to take control of their own paths and achieve greater success.

Explanation:

The 7 C's of lifelong skills refer to seven essential skills that are important for personal and professional development. These skills are critical thinking, communication, collaboration, creativity, cultural competence, character, and career and learning self-reliance. Of these skills, career and learning self-reliance is especially important when choosing a career.

Career and learning self-reliance refers to the ability to take ownership of one's career development and ongoing learning. It involves being proactive in identifying career goals and seeking out opportunities to develop new skills and knowledge. This skill is crucial because it enables individuals to adapt to changing job markets and to stay relevant and competitive in their careers.

When choosing a career, it is important to consider your interests, skills, values, and long-term goals. However, it is also important to recognize that your career path may not be linear, and that you may need to pivot or adapt to changing circumstances. Having strong career and learning self-reliance skills can help you navigate these changes and make informed decisions about your career.

In addition to helping with career decision-making, career and learning self-reliance also plays a role in ongoing professional development. By taking ownership of your learning, you can stay up-to-date with industry trends and advancements, build new skills, and position yourself for future career opportunities.

Overall, career and learning self-reliance is an important skill to have when choosing a career and navigating the professional world. By taking ownership of your career development and ongoing learning, you can position yourself for success and adapt to changing circumstances throughout your career.
