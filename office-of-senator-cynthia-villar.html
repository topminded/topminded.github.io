14. Senators term of office​


Answer:

The Constitution prescribes that the Senate be composed of two senators from each State (therefore, the Senate currently has 100 Members) and that a senator must be at least thirty years of age, have been a citizen of the United States for nine years, and, when elected, be a resident of the State from which he or she is chosen. A senator's term of office is six years and approximately one-third of the total membership of the Senate is elected every two years.

20. How to remove senators from office philippines?


THE REMOVAL OF SENATORS FROM OFFICECAN A SENATOR GET IMPEACHED?

- Impeachment is the act of charging a government official through legislative means.

- This means that the senate and the house of representatives are responsible for the trial regarding the specific individual's impeachment.

- In the Philippines, the following officials can be impeached:

the President, the Vice President, the Members of the Supreme Court, the Members of Constitutional Commissions (i.e. Civil Service Commission, Commission on Elections, and the Commission on Audit),and the Members of the Ombudsman can be impeached.

- In conclusion, a senator or any member of the legislative department for that matter, cannot be impeached.

SO HOW CAN WE REMOVE A SENATOR FROM OFFICE?

- Instead of impeachment, a senator can be removed by any other means of the law.

- Here are some crimes that can remove a senator from office:

Treason,Bribery,Graft,Corruption,Betrayal of Public Trust,and more...
