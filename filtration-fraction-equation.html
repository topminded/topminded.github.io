6. What is a filtrate? what substances form the filtrate?


What is Filtrate ❓

Filtration is a physical separation process that separates solid matter and fluid from a mixture using a filter medium that has a complex structure through which only the fluid can pass.

What substances form the filtrate❓

Filtrate refers to liquid which has passed through the process of filtration. In filtration, all fluids are strained. Larger particles are stuck in the straining media, while tinier particles as well as liquids pass through, becoming filtrate. It is, therefore, the end product of liquid filtration.

15. Why is gravity filtration more preferable than vacuum filtration during the hot filtration process?


Filtration is a laboratory technique that allows you to isolate one component of a solution or suspension from another. This can be a solid ("precipitate," collected in the filter paper) or a liquid ("filtrate," collected in the flask). There are two main methods: vacuum filtration, which makes use of a Hirsch or Buchner funnel, and gravity filtration, which solely depends on gravity. The appropriate filtration method depends on the objective of your experiment. Generally, if you want to perform fast filtration, you use the vacuum filtration method; otherwise, you use gravity filtration.

The temperature can also be an important parameter in filtration. The hot filtration process is used when you want to isolate the filtrate and avoid unwanted recrystallization. Sometimes, crystals may form in the filter paper during cold filtration, so the entire filtration apparatus is heated on a hot plate. The formation of crystals can lead to blockage in the funnel. Gravity filtration, as opposed to vacuum filtration, complements hot filtration because it ensures that only the filtrate is collected in the flask. Heating the solution lets some of the crystals remain in dissolved form so the filtration process needs to be slow. Gravity filtration is ideal for this.
