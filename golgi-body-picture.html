2. What is the difference between endoplasmic reticulum and golgi body?A. ER packages fats and golgi bodies transport these fats.B. ER transports fats that the golgi bodies package.C. ER transports materials around the cell and golgi bodies package and transport proteins only.D. ER packages and transports proteins only and golgi bodies transport materials around the cell.​


Answer:

C. ER transports materials around the cell and golgi bodies package and transport proteins only.

Complete Explanation:

The endoplasmic reticulum (ER) and Golgi body are both important organelles present in the eukaryotic cells. They work together to transport and process various substances within the cell.

The endoplasmic reticulum is involved in the processing and transportation of proteins, lipids, and other molecules throughout the cell. There are two types of endoplasmic reticulum: rough (RER) and smooth (SER). The RER has ribosomes attached to it, giving it a “rough” appearance. It is responsible for the packaging and transportation of proteins synthesized by the ribosomes. The SER lacks ribosomes and is involved in lipid synthesis, storage, and metabolism.

The Golgi body, on the other hand, is responsible for modifying, sorting, and packaging macromolecules, particularly proteins and lipids, for transportation to their final destinations. The Golgi body receives vesicles containing proteins and lipids, modifies them by adding or removing certain molecules, and then repackages them into vesicles for transportation to various organelles in the cell or for secretion outside the cell.

Therefore, option/letter C is the correct answer as the ER packages and transports various kinds of molecules including proteins and lipids, while the Golgi body processes, sorts, and packages these molecules, particularly proteins and lipids, for transportation to their final destinations.

Answer:

Answer:
