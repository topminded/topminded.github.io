7. compare and contrast1.focus vs. Epicenter2.active volcano vs. Dormant volcano3.compression vs. Tension vs. Shearing4.tectonic earthquake vs. Plutonic earthquake vs. Volcanic earthquake5.effusive eruption vs. Explosive eruption​


1. Focus vs. Epicenter

Difference:

The main difference between focus and epicentre is their location. The focus is that part of the earth where the rocks that are stressed under the surface of the earth is present. On the other hand, the part of the earth’s surface that is present right below the focus is known as the epicentre.

Similarities:

Both determinants of the origin of the ground movements.

2. Active volcano vs. Dormant volcano

Difference:

Active volcanoes have a recent history of eruptions; they are likely to erupt again. Dormant volcanoes have not erupted for a very long time but may erupt at a future time.

Similarities:

Dormant volcano is similar to an active volcano since it has the probability or a likelihood to erupt like an active volcano.

3. Compression vs. Tension vs. Shearing

Difference:

Tension or stress is the opposing force produced by a body in reaction of imposing a force on it e.g. rope chain, beams, or any body under mechanical stress.

Compression is a force applied to a body longitudinally inwards so that the length of the body reduces by dl and thus increase in other two dimension by infinitesimally smaller value.

Shear is an unaligned force exerted on a body where upper half is in one direction and other half is in opposite direction to the shear plane. The shear tension is produced only at the plane of shear formed.

4. Tectonic earthquake vs. Volcanic earthquake

Difference:

Tectonic earthquakes are caused by the movement of plates when energy accumulated within plate boundary zones is released.

Tectonic earthquakes are usually larger than volcanic earthquakes.

Similarities:

Both volcanoes and earthquakes occur due to movement of the Earth's tectonic plates.

5. Effusive eruption vs. Explosive eruption

Difference:

When gas and broken fragments of magma are shot up into the atmosphere, this is called an explosive eruption. The broken fragments are called pyroclasts, or if they are smaller than 2 mm we call it volcanic ash.

When lava flows out of a volcano, this is called an effusive eruption.

The resolution comes when Moses divides the Red Sea with God's help, allowing them to cross safely to the other side. The Egyptian army is drowned as the waters close in on them.
