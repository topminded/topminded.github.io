12. what is the meaning of echolocation ​


Echolocation is a biological sonar system used by some animals, such as bats, whales, and dolphins, to navigate and find prey in their environments. It works by emitting high-pitched sounds, usually too high for humans to hear, and then listening to the echoes that bounce back off objects in the environment. By interpreting the echoes, the animal can determine the distance, size, and location of objects, as well as their movement.

For example, a bat emits a series of high-frequency sounds and listens for the echoes that bounce back off nearby objects, such as insects or obstacles. By analyzing the echoes, the bat can determine the size, shape, and location of the object, and then use this information to navigate and catch prey.

Echolocation is an important adaptation for animals that live in environments where vision is limited or obscured, such as in caves, at night, or underwater.
