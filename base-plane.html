What is Geometry?

Geometry is a discipline of mathematics that studies the shape of individual objects, their spatial connections, and the qualities of surrounding space.

Example:

A triangle is a three-sided shape with 180 degrees of internal angles. A square, rectangle, or quadrilateral is a four-sided shape with 360 degrees of inner angles.

Point

A location is a point in geometry. It has no dimensions, i.e. no width, length, or depth. A dot represents a point. A line is defined as a set of points that stretches in two directions indefinitely.

Example:

the point of a needle, the sharpened end of a pencil, the tip of a compass Point A, point B, point C, and point D will be read as A, B, C, and D, respectively.

Line

In geometry, a line is a one-dimensional shape that is straight, has no thickness, and extends in both directions indefinitely. It's frequently referred to as the shortest distance between two points. P and Q are the two points on the line.

Example:

Segments can be anything from the length of a table to the length of a straight road. A line, on the other hand, has no clear beginning or finish. A line is a part of a segment, but a segment is not a part of a line. We may see many straight-line examples all around us, such as the edges of buildings and the highways we travel on.

Plane

A plane is a flat, two-dimensional surface that extends indefinitely in mathematics. The two-dimensional equivalent of a point (zero dimensions), a line (one dimension), and three-dimensional space is a plane.

Example:

A plane has a thickness of zero, a curvature of zero, a width of infinite, and a length of infinite. In real life, it's tough to envision a plane; all the flat surfaces of a cube or cuboid, as well as the flat surface of paper, are all true examples of a geometric plane.

Step-by-step explanation:

Cylinder is a solid bounded by a closed cylindrical surface and two parallel planes. The bounding cylindrical surface of a cylinder is called the lateral surface, and the two bounding parallel planes are called the bases.

Cylinder is a solid bounded by a closed cylindrical surface and two parallel planes.

Properties of a cylinder

1. The bounding cylindrical surface of a cylinder is called the lateral surface, and the two bounding parallel planes are called the bases. The area of the lateral surface is denoted by AL and the area of the base is denoted by Ab.

2. The bases of a cylinder are equal.

3. The altitude of the cylinder is the perpendicular distance between the bases. It is denoted by h.

4. Every section parallel to the base is equal to the base.

5. Any two parallel sections, neither of which cuts a base are congruent.

6. The right section is perpendicular to the axis of the cylinder. The area of the right section is denoted as AR.

7. Axis of the cylinder is the line that connects the centroids of bases. The length of the axis is equal to the length of the element, it is deoted as L.

8. For right cylinder, the area of the right section is equal to the area of the base and the length of the axis is equal to the altitude.
