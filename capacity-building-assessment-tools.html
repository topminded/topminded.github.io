1. what is building community capacity​


Answer:

Building community capacity refers to the process of strengthening the skills, knowledge, resources, and networks within a community to enable its members to work together effectively to address local challenges and achieve their goals. This process involves identifying the existing assets and potential resources within a community, building on them, and leveraging them to improve the quality of life of the community members. Building community capacity can include activities such as organizing community events, providing training and education, promoting civic engagement, and creating partnerships between community members and external organizations. The goal is to empower individuals and groups within the community to take collective action, promote social change, and create a more sustainable and resilient community.

7. Ano ang vulnerability at capacity assessment?​


Answer:

Vulnerability and Capacity Assessment (VCA) uses various participatory tools to gauge people's exposure to and capacity to resist natural hazards. It is an integral part of disaster preparedness and contributes to the creation of community-based disaster preparedness programmes at the rural and urban grass-roots level.

Answer:

Ang Vulnerability and Capacity Assessment (VCA) ay gumagamit ng iba't ibang participatory tool upang sukatin ang pagkakalantad ng mga tao at kapasidad na labanan ang mga natural na panganib. Ito ay isang mahalagang bahagi ng paghahanda sa sakuna at nag-aambag sa paglikha ng mga programa sa paghahanda sa kalamidad na nakabatay sa komunidad sa rural at urban grass-roots level.

Answer:

The UNDP Capacity Assessment Methodology User‘s Guide (hereafter ―the User‘s Guide‖) gives UNDP
