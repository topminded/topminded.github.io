30. solutions for proper ways to segregate the non-biodegradable and biodegradable​


Answer:

Proper segregation of non-biodegradable and biodegradable waste is important for protecting the environment and reducing pollution. Here are a few solutions for proper ways to segregate these types of waste:

Use different colored bins: One way to properly segregate non-biodegradable and biodegradable waste is by using different colored bins. For example, use a green bin for biodegradable waste and a blue bin for non-biodegradable waste.Label the bins: Clearly labeling the bins with the type of waste that should be placed in them can help people understand what should be put in each bin.Provide education and awareness: Educating people about the importance of proper waste segregation and providing information about what types of waste belong in each bin can help to increase participation.Implementing recycling programs: Implementing a recycling program that separates recyclable materials such as paper, plastic, and glass can help reduce the amount of non-biodegradable waste that ends up in landfills.Implement composting: Encourage people to compost biodegradable waste such as food scraps, yard waste, and paper, as this will reduce the amount of waste that goes to landfills, and can be used as a nutrient-rich fertilizer for plants.Enforcing penalties for non-compliance: Enforcing penalties for those who do not follow the proper waste segregation procedures can help ensure compliance and encourage people to take the issue seriously.
