4. Provides for maternal and child healthcare family planning


Healthcare Family Planning

Family planning refers to a woman's freedom to decide whether or not she conceives and whether to carry her pregnancy to term. There is still a significant disconnect between women's reproductive goals and their access to safe abortion and contraception in many regions of the world. In 2015, 12% of married or in-union women worldwide who were of reproductive age did not have access to family planning, according to data gathered by the United Nations Population Division. The percentage of unmet need varies significantly by region, from 5% in Eastern Asia to 26% in Central Africa. Unmet need is influenced by a variety of factors, such as a lack of access to information and services, worry about adverse effects, and disapproval from loved ones.

In cases where maternity care is a woman's first interaction with the health care system, integrating family planning services into maternal health services can be a successful tactic for lowering unmet demand. Prenatal care gives women the chance to learn about postpartum family planning, enabling them to adopt appropriate birth spacing practices. Women gain from this strategy by increasing their employment rates, which boost household income and enable women to make investments in their future selves and their families.
