13. The town of Brookfield sent out a survey to its residents to determine what new landmark they should build in the City Center. Of the residents who were surveyed 2/5, of the residents voted for a park, 1/4 of the residents voted for a garden, and the remaining residents voted for a zoo. If a total of 14,000 residents were surveyed, how many residents voted for a zoo?


The proportion of residents who voted for a park is 2/5, and the proportion who voted for a garden is 1/4. Therefore, the proportion of residents who voted for a zoo is:

1 - (2/5 + 1/4) = 1 - (8/20 + 5/20) = 1 - (13/20) = 7/20

If 7/20 of the residents voted for a zoo, and a total of 14,000 residents were surveyed, the number of residents who voted for a zoo is:

(7/20) x 14,000 = 4,900

Therefore, 4,900 residents voted for a zoo.

23. example of commercial art sketch draw​


People have created commercial art for centuries. As soon as someone developed a product, they probably thought about ways to get more people to buy it. Here's an example of an advertising image for a beverage called Selecto.

Advertisement for Selecto, 1889

Selecto ad

Done in 1889 for an Algerian beverage company, it's in French but still easy to understand. With its bright colors and placement of the bottle of Selecto in the middle of the table, the image clearly suggests Selecto as the drink of choice.

Here's another example, an American travel poster from around 1937.

Advertising poster for New York City municipal airports, ca. 1937

NYC Municipal airports 1937

Created under the auspices of the Works Progress Administration, a federal program that helped employ artists during the Great Depression, this poster advertises the ability to travel from two New York City municipal airports. The clear renderings of two types of airplanes, bold wording, and a simple color scheme create a compelling graphic that urged customers to consider NYC as the departure point for their travels.

26. Resident bacteria cause infictions when they move from their original resident to another. Example: when the bacteria in the mouth moved to the ear, these cause ear infiction.​


Resident bacteria and infections.

I'm sorry, but the statement you provided is not entirely accurate. Resident bacteria are a normal and necessary part of our body's microbiota, and they typically do not cause infections when they remain in their normal residence.

In fact, resident bacteria can actually help protect against infection by competing with harmful pathogens for resources and by modulating the immune system.

However, if the resident bacteria were to move to an area of the body where they are not normally found, they may cause an infection. This can occur through various means, such as injury, surgery, or medical devices, that allow the bacteria to bypass the body's natural defenses and colonize a new site.

Additionally, not all bacteria that cause infections come from the body's resident microbiota. Pathogenic bacteria can come from external sources, such as contaminated food or water, or from other individuals who are infected.

It's important to understand that infections are complex and can be caused by a variety of factors, including the type of bacteria involved, the site of infection, and the host's immune response. Proper hygiene, infection control practices, and prompt medical treatment are important in preventing and managing infections.
