13. Critical reading leads to critical thinking. Explain the statement


Critical reading is a technique for discovering information and ideas within a text. While critical thinking on the other hand is a technique for evaluating information and ideas, for deciding what to accept and believe.

In reality, critical reading should always lead you to critical thinking.

Because critical thinking allows us to monitor our understanding as we read.  If we sense that assertions are ridiculous or irresponsible (critical thinking), we examine the text more closely to test our understanding (critical reading).  

Conversely,  critical thinking depends on critical reading.  You can think critically about a text (critical thinking), after all, only if you have understood it (critical reading).  We may choose to accept or reject a presentation, but we must know why. We have a responsibility to ourselves, as well as to others, to isolate the real issues of agreement or disagreement. Only then can we understand and respect other people’s views.  To recognize and understand those views, we must read critically.
