12. where can we usually find aquifers​


Answer:

Groundwater can be found in a range of different types of rock, but the most productive aquifers are found in porous, permeable rock such as sandstone, or the open cavities and caves of limestone aquifers. Groundwater moves more readily through these materials, which allows for faster pumping and other methods of extracting the water. Aquifers can also be found in regions where the rock is made of denser material — such as granite or basalt — if that rock has cracks and fractures.

15. What are the two types of aquifers​


Answer:

There are two general types of aquifers: confined and unconfined. Confined aquifers have a layer of impenetrable rock or clay above them, while unconfined aquifers lie below a permeable layer of soil.


16. What is an aquifer?


Answer:

An aquifer is an underground layer of water-bearing permeable rock, rock fractures or unconsolidated materials. Groundwater can be extracted using a water well. The study of water flow in aquifers and the characterization of aquifers is called hydrogeology.

22. Aquifers are areas of permeable rock that hold water.​


Aquifers

Aquifers are vast expanses of porous rock where groundwater permeates every pore. High permeability materials like sands, gravels, or heavily fractured rock are examples of good aquifers. A body of material with extremely limited permeability is called an aquitard.

Further explanation

A body of permeable rock or sediment filled with groundwater is known as an aquifer. When it rains, groundwater seeps into an aquifer via the soil. Through springs and wells, it can surface after moving through the aquifer.

A body of rock and/or sediment known as an aquifer is used to store groundwater. The term "groundwater" refers to rainwater that has seeped into the soil below the surface and accumulated in voids underground.

Aquifers may be divided into two categories: confined and unconfined. Unconfined aquifers are found underneath a permeable layer of soil, whereas confined aquifers are covered by an impenetrable layer of rock or clay.

Aquifers may be formed by a wide variety of sediments and rocks, including gravel, sandstone, conglomerates, and fractured limestone. Aquifers may be classed based on the kind of rocks or sediments that make up their composition.

It's a popular misperception that aquifers are subterranean lakes or rivers. Because aquifers are permeable, groundwater can seep into or out of them, but it cannot move quickly enough to flow like a river. The permeability of the rock affects how quickly groundwater travels through an aquifer.
