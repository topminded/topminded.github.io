BALANCED CHEMICAL EQUATIONS

- In Chemistry, a balanced equation for a chemical reaction is an equation in which the number of atoms for each element or compound in the chemical reaction including the total charge is the same for both the reactants and the products. In other words, the mass and the charge are balanced on both sides of the reaction. The law of conservation of mass in balancing a chemical equation states that no atoms are lost or generated in any chemical reactions. Instead, the atoms join together in different reactions to produce products . This is why, in a balanced symbol equation, the total number of atoms of each element is the same or equal on both sides of the equation.

26. . Calculate how many grams of iron can be made from 16.5 grams of Fe2O3 by the following equation. Fe2O3 + 3 H2 -------------> 2 Fe + 3 H2O


StoichiometryDiscussion:

Stoichiometry is the quantitative study of reactants and products in a chemical reaction.

Whether the units given for the reactants (or products) are moles, grams, liters (for gases), or some other units, we use moles to calculate the amount of product formed in a reaction. This approach is called the mole method, which means simply that the stoichiometric coefficients in a chemical equation can be interpreted as the number of moles of each substance. For example, industrial ammonia is synthesized from hydrogen and nitrogen as follows:

[tex]\text{N}_{2}(g) + 3\text{H}_{2}(g) → 2\text{NH}_{3}(g)[/tex]

The stoichiometric coefficients show that one molecule of [tex]\text{N}_{2}[/tex] reacts with three molecules of [tex]\text{H}_{2}[/tex] to form two molecules of [tex]\text{NH}_{3}[/tex]. It follows that the relative numbers of moles are the same as the relative number of molecules:

[tex]\begin{array}{ccc} \text{N}_{2} & + & 3\text{H}_{2} & → & 2\text{NH}_{3} \\ \text{1 molecule} & & \text{3 molecules} & & \text{2 molecules} \\ 6.022 × 10^{23} \: \text{molecules} & & 3(6.022 × 10^{23}) \: \text{molecules} & & 2(6.022 × 10^{23}) \: \text{molecules} \\ \text{1 mol} & & \text{3 mol} & & \text{2 mol} \\ \end{array}[/tex]

Thus, this equation can also be read as "1 mole of [tex]\text{N}_{2}[/tex] gas combines with 3 moles of [tex]\text{H}_{2}[/tex] gas to form 2 moles of [tex]\text{NH}_{3}[/tex] gas." In stoichiometric calculations, we say that three moles of [tex]\text{H}_{2}[/tex] are equivalent to two moles of [tex]\text{NH}_{3}[/tex], that is,

[tex]\text{3 mol} \: \text{H}_{2} \bumpeq \text{2 mol} \: \text{NH}_{3}[/tex]

where the symbol [tex]\bumpeq[/tex] means "stoichiometrically equivalent to" or simply "equivalent to." This relationship enables us to write the conversion factors

[tex]\frac{\text{3 mol} \: \text{H}_{2}}{\text{2 mol} \: \text{NH}_{3}} \: \text{and} \: \frac{\text{2 mol} \: \text{NH}_{3}}{\text{3 mol} \: \text{H}_{2}}[/tex]

Similarly, we have [tex]\text{1 mol} \: \text{N}_{2} \bumpeq \text{2 mol} \: \text{NH}_{3} \: \text{and} \: \text{1 mol} \: \text{N}_{2} \bumpeq \text{3 mol} \: \text{H}_{2}[/tex].

Problem:

Calculate how many grams of iron can be made from 16.5 grams of Fe₂O₃ by the following equation. Fe₂O₃ + 3 H₂ → 2 Fe + 3 H₂O

Given:

[tex]\text{mass of} \: \text{Fe}_{2}\text{O}_{3} = \text{16.5 g}[/tex]

balanced chemical equation: Fe₂O₃ + 3 H₂ → 2 Fe + 3 H₂O

Required:

[tex]\text{mass of Fe}[/tex]

Solution:

Step 1: Calculate the molar mass of Fe₂O₃ and Fe.

For Fe₂O₃

molar mass of Fe₂O₃ = (55.85 g/mol × 2) + (16.00 g/mol × 3)

molar mass of Fe₂O₃ = 159.7 g/mol

For Fe

molar mass of Fe = 55.85 g/mol × 1

molar mass of Fe = 55.85 g/mol

Step 2: Calculate the mass of Fe formed by using dimensional analysis (factor-label method).

Since [tex]\text{1 mol} \: \text{Fe}_{2}\text{O}_{3} \bumpeq \text{2 mol Fe}[/tex], the mass of Fe formed is

[tex]\text{mass of Fe} = \text{16.5 g} \: \text{Fe}_{2}\text{O}_{3} × \frac{\text{1 mol} \: \text{Fe}_{2}\text{O}_{3}}{\text{159.7 g} \: \text{Fe}_{2}\text{O}_{3}} × \frac{\text{2 mol Fe}}{\text{1 mol} \: \text{Fe}_{2}\text{O}_{3}} × \frac{\text{55.85 g Fe}}{\text{1 mol Fe}}[/tex]

[tex]\boxed{\text{mass of Fe = 11.5 g}}[/tex]
