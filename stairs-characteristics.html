6. what is the characteristics of , stairs​


Answer:

Stairs are typically designed and built with several key characteristics, including:

Steps: Stairs consist of a series of horizontal platforms called steps, which are designed to provide a stable surface for people to walk on as they ascend or descend the stairs.

Risers: The vertical distance between each step is called the riser. Consistent riser heights are important for safety and comfort when using the stairs.

Treads: The horizontal surface of each step is called the tread. Treads should be wide enough to allow comfortable and stable footing, and should also be slip-resistant to reduce the risk of falls.

Handrails: Handrails are typically installed on one or both sides of the stairs to provide support and stability while using the stairs.

Nosing: The edge of the tread that extends beyond the riser is called the nosing. Nosing can be designed to be rounded or squared, but should be clearly visible to avoid tripping hazards.

Landings: Landings are flat areas that provide a place for people to rest while using the stairs. They can also be used to change the direction of the stairway or provide a transition to a different level.

Overall, the design and construction of stairs is intended to provide safe, comfortable, and efficient movement between different levels or areas within a building.

1. Doorknob:

- How it works: A doorknob is a simple machine that makes it easier to open and close doors. When you turn the knob, it rotates a spindle that operates a latch, which connects or disconnects the door from its frame.

- Characteristics: Doorknobs are usually made of metal or plastic, and are designed to be easy to grip and turn. They typically have a cylindrical shape with a rounded or flattened knob on one end and a threaded spindle on the other.

- Type of simple machine: Wheel and axle

2. Screw:

- How it works: A screw is a threaded cylinder with a pointed end and a spiral groove that allows it to be driven into a material like wood or metal. As the screw rotates, the threads create force that pulls the screw deeper into the material, creating a secure hold.

- Characteristics: Screws come in a variety of sizes and shapes, and are typically made of metal or other hard materials. They are often used to fasten parts together or to secure objects in place.

- Type of simple machine: Inclined plane wrapped around a cylinder (also known as a screw)

3. Stairs:

- How it works: Stairs are a series of flat platforms or steps that allow people to move from one level to another. They are designed with a specific rise (height between steps) and run (depth of each step), which allows people to easily climb or descend the stairs.

- Characteristics: Stairs are usually made of wood, concrete, or other durable materials. They can come in a variety of styles, ranging from simple and functional to ornate and decorative.

- Type of simple machine: Inclined plane

4. Safety pins:

- How it works: A safety pin is a simple machine that consists of a coiled spring and a clasp. The clasp is attached to one end of the spring, while the other end is formed into a sharp point. When the clasp is released, the spring pulls the sharp end towards it, allowing it to be used to pierce fabric or other materials.

- Characteristics: Safety pins are usually made of metal and come in a variety of sizes. They are often used for fastening clothing or as a temporary fix for a tear or hole in a garment.

- Type of simple machine: Lever (the clasp acts as a fulcrum for the spring)

Step-by-step explanation:

We can use the formula for the area of an isosceles trapezoid to solve for the width of the stairs halfway to the top.

Area = [(b1 + b2) / 2] * h

where b1 and b2 are the lengths of the longer and shorter bases, respectively, and h is the height or the width of the stairs.

We can see that the height of the trapezoid is equal to the width of the stairs. Let's denote the width of the stairs halfway to the top as x. We know that the height of the trapezoid is half of the distance between the two bases. Therefore:

h = (6.5 - 4.3) / 2

h = 1.1 m

Now we can plug in the values in the formula and solve for x:

Area = [(6.5 + 4.3) / 2] * 1.1

Area = 5.4 m^2

Area of the trapezoid is also equal to the product of the height and the average of the bases. Thus:

Area = h * [(b1 + b2) / 2]

5.4 = 1.1 * 5.4

Since we are only asked to solve for the vertical distance, then, we simply have to add the vertical space between consecutive stair. The first 10 stairs and the last 5 stairs make up the total of 15 stairs given in the problem.  

Vertical Distance = 113 + 134 = 247 ft

However, take note that this number only covers the distance in one direction. If you descend the stairs, you travelled 247 ft vertically. If you ascend the stairs, you cover another 247 ft.

Therefore, the total vertical distance traveled ascending and descending is:

Total Vertical Distance = 247*2 = 494 ft

Answer:

1. Doorknob:

The doorknob is a type of simple machine known as a wheel and axle. It consists of a cylindrical-shaped knob (wheel) that is attached to a metal rod (axle) that goes through the door. The user can rotate the knob to turn the axle, which in turn operates the latch mechanism that locks or unlocks the door.

Characteristic: The doorknob is designed to make it easier for a person to open and close a door by using rotational force, which is more convenient and efficient than trying to push or pull the door directly.

2. Screw:

The screw is a type of simple machine that is often used to hold objects together or to move them from one place to another. It consists of a threaded metal shaft (known as the screw thread) that is wound around a cylinder-shaped rod (known as the screw shank).

Characteristic: The screw works by converting rotational motion into linear motion, allowing the user to apply a great amount of force over a small distance. The screw is also designed to hold objects securely in place, as the threaded shaft creates a tight grip when turned.

3. Stairs:

Stairs are a type of simple machine known as an inclined plane. They consist of a series of flat steps that are connected by sloping sections, which allows the user to ascend or descend at a gradual angle.

Characteristic: Stairs are designed to make it easier for people to climb or descend from one level to another by reducing the amount of force required to lift themselves vertically. The sloping sections of the stairs allow the user to exert less force over a longer distance, which reduces the amount of work required to climb or descend.

4. Safety pins:

Safety pins are a type of simple machine known as a lever. They consist of a long, thin metal rod with a spring-loaded mechanism on one end and a sharp, pointed tip on the other.

Characteristic: The safety pin works by using the lever principle to apply force to an object. When the user presses down on the spring-loaded mechanism, the sharp tip of the pin is forced through the object, holding it in place. The lever mechanism also allows the user to apply a great amount of force over a small distance, making it an effective tool for holding objects securely.
