The effects of tobacco smoke on the respiratory system include: irritation of the trachea (windpipe) and larynx (voice box) reduced lung function and breathlessness due to swelling and narrowing of the lung airways and excess mucus in the lung passages.

Due to the question not being clear, I'd have to start with the prejudiced thought that your question is about smoking.

The effects of Deney's smoking will be very catastrophic. Given he never stopped since college, I'd have to make a guess that he was about 25-28 years old when he started smoking, which is already long enough to leave sufficient damage to the lungs.

Primary Effects

The chemicals in the smoke will start damaging and inflaming the alveoli, the small "air sacs" of your lungs. But that's only the beginning. His teeth will start becoming slightly discoloured, along with halitosis characterised by a cigar smoke-like breath. Next, is the behaviour. He'll start acting a bit irritable and anxious when around others.

Next Effects

The main culprit of heart diseases include smoking, which unfortunately can make your cholesterol higher, therefore the blood passages become clogged from the inside. This is accompanied by developed mood changes, loss of appetite (possibly due to addiction), unhealthy fingernails, teeth, and smelly hair.

Serious, Long-Term Effects

Deney will start developing worse diseases and possibly cancer. The diseases include COPD (Chronic Obstructive Pulmonary Disease), Lung or Heart Cancer (In worst cases both), cervical cancer, blood clotting, diabetes complications, increased risk of leukemia, etc. Basically, cigarettes are a stew of health problems.
