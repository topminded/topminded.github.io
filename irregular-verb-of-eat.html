8. what is the irregular verbs and singular verbs?


Irregular verbs are verbs that don't use -d, -ed, or -ied when stated in the past tense. Singular verbs are used when the subject is also singular.

9. Regular verbs and irregular verbs meaning


REGULAR VERBS

are verbs whose past forms are only added with ed or d.

Examples:

look - looked

purge - purged

rinse - rinsed

shade - shaded

raise - raised

care - cared

bake - baked

cook - cooked

wait - waited

want - wanted

warn - warned

faint - fainted

paint - painted

IRREGULAR VERBS

Are verbs that become different in its past form. Other verbs do not change, they stay in their form.

Examples:

Verbs that change

swim - swam

write - wrote

win - won

see - saw

come - came

take - took

run - ran

find - found

become - became

sit - sat

eat - ate

Verbs that stay the same

shut - shut

set - set

put - put

bet - bet

let - let

quit - quit

hurt - hurt

cut - cut
