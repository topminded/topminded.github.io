6. Physical inactivity: Things you should do less

The activity that occupies the top spot (read: smallest portion) in the physical fitness pyramid is… you guessed it, the physical inactivity level! While we don’t necessarily remove inactivity in your day to day (we still need rest and sleep periods!), we suggest cutting it down and not being too inactive.

For students like you, inactivity might look like hours spent binge-watching your favorite shows on Netflix or mindlessly scrolling on social media! You know the drill. There is such a thing as “too much” screen time. So go ahead and watch an episode or two, but make sure to balance it with other physical activities in the pyramid.

How often should I do these physical inactivities?

Sparingly

Examples of physical inactivity:

Watching TV

Surfing the internet

Sitting down… too much

If you were to be honest, where does most of your time go in the physical fitness pyramid? This tool is a guide you can use to start living an active and healthy lifestyle now so you can be up and running inside and outside the classroom. The physical, emotional, and academic benefits are limitless! What are you waiting for?


2. what connection can you draw between primitive time and the present time physical education?​


Answer & Explanation:

Physical education has evolved over time, from primitive times to the present day. In primitive times, physical activity was an integral part of daily life and was necessary for survival, such as hunting, gathering, and manual labor. In contrast, physical education in the present day has become more structured and focuses on the development of physical skills, fitness, and health.

However, despite the differences in approach and focus, the underlying goal of physical education remains the same, which is to improve physical health and wellness. Today's physical education programs incorporate modern technology and scientific understanding to provide a comprehensive approach to physical fitness and health. Additionally, physical education in the present day also aims to promote teamwork, sportsmanship, and physical literacy.

In conclusion, there is a clear connection between primitive time and present-day physical education, as the fundamental goal of improving physical health and wellness has remained unchanged throughout history.
