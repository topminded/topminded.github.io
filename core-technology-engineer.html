13. Why would human rights be at the core of scientific and technological advancement?


Answer:

Why should human rights be at the core of scientific and technological advancement?

No, it should not.

At the core of scientific and technological development should be only two things: the desire to know and the needs of humanity, in this order.

Human rights are just one of the many needs of humanity. It is an important one, but acquiring knowledge is a process that involves freedom of spirit, unencumbered of anything.

Human rights should be put in a perspective that is a social and economic one. Of course that involves certain research processes, but fundamental research has nothing to do with human rights. Once a discovery is made and a technology is developed (based on that discovery), only then must the implications of such upon society be estimated. Of course, many times the only drive to develop a technology is profit, and consequences are not always the happiest ones….

30. what is core inner core and outer core


The outer core of the Earth is a fluid layer about 2,300 km (1,400 mi)[1] thick and composed of mostly iron and nickel that lies above Earth's solid inner core and below its mantle. 
The Earth's inner core is the Earth's innermost part. It is primarily a solid ball with a radius of about 1,220 kilometres (760 miles), which is about 70% of the Moon's radius.
