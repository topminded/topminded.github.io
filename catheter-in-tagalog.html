28. A nurse assesses the insertion site of a peripheral iv catheter and notes that the site is red, warm to touch, and a hard, palpable cord along the vein track. the client reports discomfort when the site is touched. the nurse should interpret that these signs are most likely due to:


The following signs of redness, warm to touch, hard, palpable cord along the vein track, and the patient's report of discomfort in the site of peripheral IV catheter is a common manifestation of phlebitis.

Phlebitis, from the root word 'phleb' means veins and '-itis' inflammation is the inflammation of veins due to trauma affecting the endothelial linings of the veins. The trauma will cause vein irritation and swelling. From the scenario, the insertion of IV catheter leads to the formation of phlebitis.

Why IV catheterization can potentially cause phlebitis?

Inserting IV catheter incorrectly can cause irritation and damage to the veins.Incorrect size of the catheter may also cause inflammation and trauma to the veins. Nurses usually select the sizes of the IV cath depends on the sizes and depths of the veins. The medication administered through the IV is too strong and the patient developed adverse reaction to the medication.Phlebitis will also occur if the IV catheter remains for too long.

If phlebitis is due to the insertion of IV catheter, here are some of the nursing interventions:

Discontinue the IV catheter. Apply warm compress as prescribedClean the injection siteElevate the affected extremityIf infection is suspected and if there is a purulent discharge (nana) clean the site aseptically and send culture to the laboratory for further investigation. Notify the health care provider for the plausible orders of anti-inflammatory medication.
