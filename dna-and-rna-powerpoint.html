3. Which correctly lists the flow of genetic information during transcription and then translation? RNA, DNA, protein DNA, protein, RNA protein, DNA, RNA DNA, RNA, protein


The correct flow of genetic information during transcription and translation of a cell is DNA, RNA and Proteins.

DNA contains the blueprint of a cell including the information needed during the replication process.  Hence, DNA acts as a brain of a cell to continue living, repairing and replicating the cell.

The replication process takes place in RNA and Proteins.  These two are intertwined together: RNA is required to make proteins yet proteins are involved in the production of RNA.  They cannot be separated from each other because separation means death of a cell.  

The flow goes on and on.  The transcription and translation starts again from DNA to the RNA and Proteins.

12. 5. Which of the following represents the flow of genetic information in the cells? A. DNA to Protein to RNA B. DNA to RNA to Protein C. Protein to DNA to RNA D. RNA to Protein to DNA​


Answer:

B. DNA to RNA to Protein

Explanation:

The central dogma of molecular biology describes the flow of genetic information in cells from DNA to messenger RNA (mRNA) to protein. It states that genes specify the sequence of mRNA molecules, which in turn specify the sequence of proteins.DNA, or deoxyribonucleic acid, is the hereditary material in humans and almost all other organisms. Nearly every cell in a person's body has the same DNA.Ribonucleic acid (RNA) is a molecule similar to DNA. Unlike DNA, RNA is single-stranded. An RNA strand has a backbone made of alternating sugar (ribose) and phosphate groups. Attached to each sugar is one of four bases--adenine (A), uracil (U), cytosine (C), or guanine (G).
