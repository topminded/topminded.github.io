1. compare binary and multiple fission


BINARY FISSION: nucleus divides into two parts, occurs during normal conditions, cytoplasm divides after each nuclear division, gives rise to only two new individuals (E.g. amoeba)
MULTIPLE FISSION: nucleus divides into many parts, take place during unfavourable conditions (encysted stage), cytoplasm does not divide after every division, forms many individuals (E.g. Plasmodium)

8. What is nuclear fission


Division of nucleus into two parts.Nuclear fission is the process in which a large nucleus splits into two smaller nuclei with the release of energy. In other words, fission the process in which a nucleus is divided into two or more fragments, and neutrons and energy are released.

The mass changes and associated energy changes in nuclear reactions are significant. For example, the energy released from the nuclear reaction of 1 kg of uranium is equivalent to the energy released during the combustion of about four billion kilograms of coal.
