4. How many kw hours are consumed by 25-60 watt light bulbs burning 5 hours in a 120 volts circuit?


Answer:

To calculate the number of kilowatt-hours consumed by 25-60 watt light bulbs burning for 5 hours in a 120 volts circuit, you can use the following formula:

Kilowatt-hours = (watts * hours) / 1000

In this case, the watts can vary between 25 and 60, so you will need to calculate the kilowatt-hours consumed for both values and then add them together to get the total.

For 25 watts:

Kilowatt-hours = (25 * 5) / 1000

Kilowatt-hours = 0.125

For 60 watts:

Kilowatt-hours = (60 * 5) / 1000

Kilowatt-hours = 0.3

Therefore, the total number of kilowatt-hours consumed by the light bulbs is 0.125 + 0.3 = 0.425. This means that the light bulbs consumed 0.425 kilowatt-hours over the course of 5 hours.

23. The secondary voltage is 220 Volt and primary voltage is 110 volt, then the number of turns ratio is?​


Answer:

A transformer with a primary winding of 1000 turns and a secondary winding of 100 turns has a turns ratio of 1000:100 or 10:1. Therefore 100 volts applied to the primary will produce a secondary voltage of 10 volts.

25. What is a volt, watt, and a ampere


Answer:

volt

Explanation:

volt is whare u put money like the bank

Answer:

A volt is the measurement of the amount of electrical push or force in a circuit. The wiring in a typical house carries 115-120 volts of electrical force.

A watt is the measurement of power, or how fast work is done. Power is measured in kilowatts (1kW = 1000 watts).

An ampere is the unit used to measure how much current flows through a given part of a circuit in one second. In most houses, circuit breakers are designed to allow 15 or 20 amperes of current to pass through a circuit safely.

The three basic units of measurement-volts, watts, and amperes are related to each other. If you know two of these measurements, you can use this relationship to find the other measurement. Suppose a small appliance uses 240 watts at the normal household voltage of 120. To find the amperes needed, you divide the number of watts by the number of volts.

Formula: watts ÷ volts = amps
