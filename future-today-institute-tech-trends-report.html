8. The future of GDSS, including trends and developments.​


A global distribution system, sometimes known as GDS, is a computer distribution system used by tourist providers, including airlines, to present available services, process bookings, and issue tickets on a global level. Without significant investment in their own proprietary networks, GDSs provide travel industry suppliers with a distribution channel that they might not otherwise be able to afford on a global scale. The GDS serves as a conduit between the booking engine used by the travel provider and the purchaser of travel services.

GDSs, however, only manage bookings; they do not control the supply of suppliers; this responsibility rests with the supplier's own reservation system. Bypassing the GDS and giving customers direct access to the supplier's own reservation systems, the World Wide Web has introduced a widely accessible and extensive channel of distribution for airlines and other travel suppliers. Similar to this, new communication interface facilities, such as language translators that change a proprietary reservation format into one that can interface with a GDS or the WWW, have opened up alternative distribution channels for the supplier and may in the future cause concern among intermediaries in the travel distribution process.

10. How can the Trend Spotter identify the new trends in the future?​


Look for the trends that are disappearing

Companies often make the mistake of only focusing on rising trends. To spot the newest trends before they hit, you need to pay attention to the trends that are no longer working. If you can identify why a trend is ending, you'll be able to spot space for a new trend. Though there's a lot more to it than that. Trendspotting means identifying trends on a local, regional and/or global level, understanding those trends, identifying best practices surrounding those trends and identifying innovation opportunities regarding those trends
