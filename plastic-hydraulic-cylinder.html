6. In a hydraulic press, the small cylinder has the diameter of 8 cm while the larger piston has the diameter of 22 cm. If the force of 600 N is applied to the small piston, what is the force on the larger piston, neglecting friction?


Answer:

We can use Pascal's law, which states that the pressure applied to an enclosed fluid is transmitted equally in all directions. In a hydraulic system like this, the pressure on the smaller piston is transmitted to the larger piston, and the force on the larger piston is directly proportional to its area.

Let's start by calculating the area of each piston:

Area of small piston = π(8 cm/2)^2 = 50.24 cm^2

Area of large piston = π(22 cm/2)^2 = 380.13 cm^2

Now we can use the equation:

Force = Pressure × Area

We know that the force applied to the small piston is 600 N, and we can assume that the pressure is the same throughout the system (neglecting friction). So we can solve for the force on the larger piston:

Force on large piston = Pressure × Area of large piston

Pressure = Force on small piston / Area of small piston

Pressure = 600 N / 50.24 cm^2 = 11.94 N/cm^2

Force on large piston = Pressure × Area of large piston

Force on large piston = 11.94 N/cm^2 × 380.13 cm^2 = 4548.66 N

Therefore, the force on the larger piston is approximately 4548.66 N when a force of 600 N is applied to the smaller piston.
