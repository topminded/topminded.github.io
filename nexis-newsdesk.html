18. r 1 = nExy – ΣχΣy [n2x2 – (Σx)2][nEy2 – (Σy)2]​


Answer:

The formula r1 = nExy – ΣχΣy [n2x2 – (Σx)2][nEy2 – (Σy)2] represents the Pearson product-moment correlation coefficient, which measures the linear relationship between two variables, x and y.

The variables in the formula represent the following:

n: the number of observations in the sample

Ex and Ey: the expected values of x and y, respectively

Σx and Σy: the sums of the values of x and y, respectively

Σx2 and Σy2: the sums of the squares of the values of x and y, respectively

The formula calculates the correlation between x and y by dividing the covariance of x and y by the product of their standard deviations. The correlation coefficient ranges from -1 to 1, with a value of -1 indicating a perfect negative linear relationship, a value of 1 indicating a perfect positive linear relationship, and a value of 0 indicating no linear relationship between the variables.
