3. make a story about failure to success ​


Once upon a time, there was a young man named Jack who had big dreams of becoming a successful businessman. He was smart, ambitious, and hardworking, and he had a lot of great ideas.

Jack started his first business when he was just 21 years old. He poured all of his savings into it and worked tirelessly day and night to make it a success. But despite his best efforts, the business failed within a year, leaving Jack completely broke and disheartened.

Feeling like a failure, Jack started to doubt himself and his abilities. He wondered if he would ever be able to achieve his dreams or if he was just meant to fail.

But then, something inside of him shifted. He realized that failure was not the end, but rather a stepping stone on the path to success. He picked himself up and decided to try again, but this time he was determined to learn from his mistakes and do things differently.

With his newfound resilience and determination, Jack started a new business. He took the lessons he had learned from his previous failure and applied them to his new venture. He worked smarter, not harder, and focused on building a strong foundation for his business.

Slowly but surely, Jack's new business began to grow. He secured funding from investors and expanded his team. He was able to attract new customers and build a loyal following.

Over time, Jack's business became a massive success. He was able to achieve his dreams and even surpassed his own expectations. But more importantly, he had learned that failure was not the end, but rather a necessary part of the journey to success.

Through his failure, Jack had learned to be more resilient, to persevere through challenges, and to never give up on his dreams. And because of that, he was able to turn his failure into the greatest success of his life.

5. Make an Extemporaneous speech about Defining failure in Academic Achievements​


Answer:

Defining Failure in Academics

Make an Extemporaneous speech about Defining failure in Academic Achievements

Good afternoon everyone. Today, I would like to talk about the topic of defining failure in academic achievements.

Failure is a subjective term and it is often perceived differently by individuals. For some, it may mean getting a lower grade than they expected, while for others it may mean not getting accepted into their desired school or program.

However, in the context of academic achievements, failure can be defined as the inability to reach a desired goal or outcome. It doesn't mean a lack of intelligence or ability, but rather the experience of learning and growth that helps individuals improve and succeed in the future.

Furthermore, it is important to understand that failure is not permanent and is not a reflection of one's self-worth. Rather, it is a learning opportunity to reflect on what went wrong, what can be improved and how to move forward.

In conclusion, failure in academic achievements should not be viewed as the end, but rather as a stepping stone towards success. It is through our failures that we develop resilience, determination, and a growth mindset. So, the next time you face a setback in your academic pursuits, remember to embrace it as a valuable learning experience. Thank you.

24. make a story about failure to success ​


Answer:

Once there was a young man named John who had a dream of becoming a successful entrepreneur. He started a small business with a partner, but unfortunately, the business failed due to a lack of funding and poor management. John was devastated, but he refused to give up.

He took a break to reflect on what went wrong and identified the mistakes they made. He realized that he needed to improve his skills and knowledge to succeed. So, he enrolled in a business course and learned everything he could about marketing, finance, and operations.

there was a boy in the age of 12 year old that have so much failure in school and one day the boy is being successful in school and have his own dreams

Once there was a 12-year-old boy named John who had always struggled in school. He found it hard to focus in class, often forgot his homework, and did poorly on tests. To make matters worse, he had a group of classmates who teased him for his academic struggles.

Feeling discouraged and defeated, John was ready to give up on school altogether. But one day, a new teacher named Ms. Smith joined the class. Ms. Smith noticed that John was struggling and took the time to work with him one-on-one to identify his strengths and weaknesses. She encouraged him to set small goals for himself and to celebrate his successes, no matter how small.

John started to work harder in school, spending more time studying and asking for help when he needed it. He also started to believe in himself and his ability to learn. Slowly but surely, his grades began to improve.

One day, the class was given a difficult math test. John had always struggled in math and was nervous about how he would do. But as he looked at the test, he realized that he knew how to solve every problem. He had studied hard and had finally mastered the material.

When the test was graded, John was thrilled to see that he had received an A. He ran to Ms. Smith's desk to show her the grade, and she smiled and gave him a high-five. John felt a sense of pride and accomplishment that he had never felt before.

From that day forward, John continued to work hard in school and set goals for himself. He no longer felt discouraged by his failures but instead saw them as opportunities to learn and grow. With the help of a supportive teacher and his own determination, John had transformed from a struggling student to a successful one.
