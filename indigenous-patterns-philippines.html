3. _______ the common economic patterns of indigenous groups


Indigenous economies refer to traditional and local economic systems of indigenous peoples. These systems include a variety of land-based small-scale economic activities and practices as well as sustainable resource management. Indigenous economies are often characterized by a subsistence mode of production.

10. what are the Philippine indigenous arts?


Answer:

Philippine Indigenous Arts

These are the arts made by the indigenous peoples of the Philippines. It includes works in raw materials such as extract from trees, fruits, and vegetables.

Forms of the Philippines Indigenous Arts

Sculpture or Carving

Sculpture is a three-dimensional artwork created by shaping or combining hard materials typically stones, marbles, metal, glass and wood. There are also some plastic materials such as clay, textiles, polymers and softer metals.

Carving is one of the techniques used in sculpture.

The most popular woodcarvings are those of the anitos (nature gods), santos (saints), and statues of Christ and the Blessed Mother.

Weaving

Weaving is making cloth and other objects wherein threads or strands of material are passed under and over each other.

Pina cloth and abaca fiber are the most used backstrap loom to produce blankets and clothings.

Weaving has different forms, functions and materials. Most of the common forms of weaving are in the form of hats, mats, bags, baskets, textiles, clothes and blankets.

Pottery

Pottery are objects that are first shaped of wet clay, then hardened by baking. Pottery includes both decorative and practical items such as bowls, vases, dishes, and lamps.

Filipino potters make pots of different sizes, shapes, and designs. Palayok, banga and tapayan are some of the known pottery products.

Physical Ornaments

Physical ornamentation can be categorized to three areas: the use of traditional costumes, jewelries and tattoos. The designs vary depending on the location, users and function of the ornaments.

The following lessons were included in the training based on the needs assessment, in-depth interviews, and deliberations: Binukid alphabet, basic counting, self-introduction, functional conversation statements, expression of common feelings, common questions, and Binukid words related to body parts, food, directions, colors, animals and insects, fruits, relationships, basic resources, and Binukid words related to body parts, food, directions, colors, animals and insects, fruits, relationships, and basic resources, parts of their clothing and/or accessories, tastes, and seasons. 19. indigenous water transportation of the Philippines.


The indigenous water transportation of the Philippines is an important aspect of the country's maritime heritage. Before the arrival of the Spanish colonizers in the 16th century, the people of the Philippines already had an advanced system of water transportation.

One of the most common types of indigenous watercraft in the Philippines is the "banca," a narrow and long canoe-like boat made of wood. Bancas can vary in size, from small ones used for fishing and short trips, to larger ones used for transportation of goods and people between islands. Some bancas are equipped with outriggers or "katig," which help stabilize the boat and prevent it from tipping over.

Another type of indigenous watercraft is the "vinta," a colorful sailboat traditionally used by the Badjao or sea gypsies in the southern Philippines. Vintas have a distinctive triangular sail made of cloth and bamboo, and are often adorned with colorful flags and other decorations.

The "paraw" is another traditional boat commonly used in the Visayas region. It is a double-outrigger sailboat that is known for its speed and agility. The paraw is often used for fishing, transportation, and as a recreational vessel.

In addition to these types of boats, there are also other indigenous watercraft in the Philippines, such as the "lantsa" or larger wooden boats, and the "baroto" or dugout canoe.

Today, modern boats and ships have replaced many of the traditional watercraft in the Philippines, but some of these indigenous boats are still in use in rural areas and for traditional activities such as fishing and festivals.
