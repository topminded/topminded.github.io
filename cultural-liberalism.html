In this paragraph how will you reflect it to our culture ​


Answer:

Explanation:

The paragraph suggests that culture is not a fixed set of values and beliefs, but rather a dynamic system of infinite possibilities and choices. Within Philippine culture, there are various arguments and strategies that can be extracted to either degrade or ennoble our society, enslave or liberate its people, suppress or enhance its productive potential.

For example, Philippine culture is rich in traditions and values that prioritize family, community, and respect for elders. However, some cultural practices such as corruption, nepotism, and patronage politics can degrade and corrupt our society. On the other hand, Philippine culture also has a history of resistance and struggles for liberation, such as the revolution against Spanish colonialism and the People Power Revolution, which demonstrated the power of collective action and the potential for societal enhancement.

Thus, it is important for individuals within Philippine culture to critically examine the arguments and strategies that exist within their culture matrix, and to choose those that promote the enhancement and liberation of society, while challenging and rejecting those that degrade and suppress it.

•People's Reform Party(PRP)- The People's Reform Party is a center-left political party in the Philippines. Founded on April 12, 1991, as the political party of former Agrarian Reform Secretary Miriam Defensor Santiago for her bid as president in the 1992 Presidential Elections.

30. How does the liberal culture of South Asia free them from the past colonial influences?


Answer:

The Spanish colonial period in the Philippines was the period during which the Philippines were part of the Spanish Empire as the Captaincy General of the Philippines from 1565 to 1898. The islands were part of the larger Spanish East Indies. Forty-four years after Ferdinand Magellan landed in the Philippines and died in the Battle of Mactan in 1521, the Spanish explored and colonialized the islands, starting with the founding of Cebu by Miguel Lopez de Legazpi in 1565. Manila was made the capital of the Philippines in 1571. This was the time of the reign of King Philip II of Spain, whose name has remained attached to the country. The Spanish colonial period ended with the Philippine Revolution and Spanish-American War in 1898, which marked the beginning of the American colonialization of the Philippines.
