14. These are used for the purpose of measuring dimensions. A. Cutting tools B. Liming tools C. Measuring tools D. Testing tools​


✍Question:

These are used for the purpose of the measuring dimensions?

Answer:

C. Measuring Tools

✏Explanation:A Measuring Instrument is a device to measure a physical quantity. In the physical sciences, quality assurance, and engineering, measurement is the activity of obtaining and comparing physical quantities of real-world objects and events. Established standard objects and events are used as units, and the process of measurement gives a number relating the item under study and the referenced unit of measurement. Measuring instruments, and formal test methods which define the instrument's use, are the means by which these relations of numbers are obtained.

All measuring instruments are subject to varying degrees of instrument error and measurement uncertainty. These instruments may range from simple objects such as rulers and stopwatches to electron microscopes and particle accelerators. Virtual instrumentation is widely used in the development of modern measuring instruments.

#carry on learning

1.Tape Measure Besides their obvious functions for measuring length, width, height and in some cases depth, tape measures can also be used to determine outside diameters of tubing (circumference/Pi)

2.Vernier Caliper An accurate sliding rule measuring tool, similar to micrometers, that can be used to measure inside and outside diameters as well as depths. There are several different kinds of vernier caliper; the earliest units were merely slide rulers with opposing stepped jaws; however, other versions with the addition of a dial face make even more accurate measurements on a smaller scale. The newest vernier calipers now use a digital readout that measure to the ten-thousandth of an inch or even smaller.

3.Thread gauge Essential for determining threads per inch when measuring bolts, nuts and hydraulic fittings. Available in standard or metric, you should have both because a 3/8-24 and a 10mm X 1 bolt look almost identical until the third thread stops you from completing the job. Thread gauges are also helpful in determining which tap or die to use or what pitch thread file to use when rethreading spindles, nuts and bolts. Usually included in a good set of taps and dies.

4.Feeler gauge Feeler gauges are made up of several thin strips of metal, each machined to a specific thickness for use in setting the proper air gap on breaker points and for measuring valve clearances on cylinder heads. Brass feeler gauges are also available for measuring the gap on magnetic inductive pickups in electronic ignition. When used in conjunction with a straight edge, feeler gauges can also measure manifold and cylinder surfaces for flatness and indicate how much milling is necessary to bring a surface back to flush. Most feeler gauges are sold in a set with many others of varying thicknesses, usually in one thousandth of inch increments. Very short feelers are convenient for getting down inside a distributor, but long feeler gauges are available for measuring front end alignment or head-to-block clearance. A long bent feeler gauge is made for measuring valve-to-rocker-arm clearances.

5.Micrometer This accurate measuring tool comes in two different configurations, one for measuring inside dimensions and one for outside measurements. Micrometers usually come in pairs or in sets with several measured rods included to increase their range. Accurate to within several thousandths, micrometers are an essential tool for machinists and engineers. They are handy for measuring piston or crankshaft journal size, disc brake rotor runout, cylinder bore size, and also the depth of a drilled hole
