Wedding Rituals and SuperstitionsRituals are repeated behaviors based on a belief, in the absence of logic or reason, that it will affect the outcome of a given situation. Rituals are repeated behaviors based on defined patterns, whereas superstitions are repeated behaviors based on a belief, in the absence of logic or reason, that it will affect the outcome of a given situation.Superstitions are the belief that certain events or objects will bring either good or bad luck.

Here are some of the wedding rituals and superstitions:Something Old, New, Borrowed, and Blue: When someone gets married, we've all heard this rhyme, but what does it mean? Wearing "something old" represents the bride's past, while "something new" represents the happy future of the couple. The bride is supposed to borrow her "something borrowed" from a happily married person in the hope that some of that person's good fortune will rub off on her. "Something blue" represents loyalty and love.Carrying the Bride Over the Threshold: This superstition originated in medieval Europe, when many people believed that a bride was particularly vulnerable to evil spirits via the soles of her feet. The groom carried the bride into their new home to avoid bringing in any evil spirits.Dove Flying: However, if something bad happens to these doves, it will bring bad luck to the marriage as well.
