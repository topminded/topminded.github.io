5. Ano ang pagkakatulad ng inarching at grafting​


Answer:

Approach grafting and inarching are similar in that both involve the use of a scion which is still attached to its own root system at the time of grafting. In fact the two terms are sometimes used interchangeably. Both share this feature (attached scion) with natural grafting, and are likely to have been the first attempts at grafting by early agriculturists. Unlike "detached" scion grafting (i.e. all other methods), the scion is less likely to become water stressed, resulting in a high probability of success.

10. procedure of how to do inarching​


Answer:

Inarching is a technique of grafting in which a living branch of one tree is joined to the stem or branch of another tree. This technique is commonly used to propagate fruit trees or ornamental plants that are difficult to root from cuttings. Here are the general steps for inarching:

Choose the plants: Select two plants of the same species or two closely related species, one of which will serve as the rootstock and the other as the scion. The rootstock should be strong, healthy, and capable of supporting the scion. The scion should be a branch of the desired plant that you want to propagate.

Prepare the plants: Make a clean cut on the stem or branch of the rootstock plant, about the same size as the scion. Remove the bark from the cut area of the rootstock, leaving the cambium layer exposed. Trim the scion by cutting off any leaves or side shoots, leaving only the tip and a small amount of wood.

Join the plants: Place the scion into the cut on the rootstock, making sure that the cambium layers of both plants are in contact. Bind the graft with a grafting tape, starting at the base of the scion and wrapping upward to the top of the rootstock. Be careful not to bind the graft too tightly, as this may damage the plants.

Support the graft: After binding the graft, provide support to hold the scion in place. You can use a stake or a wire to support the graft.

Monitor the graft: Check the graft regularly to make sure it is healing properly. Remove any shoots that appear below the graft union, as they are likely to be from the rootstock and not the scion. As the graft heals and grows, remove the binding material and any supporting stake or wire.

Care for the plant: Once the graft has healed and the scion has started to grow, care for the plant as you would for any other plant of the same species. Water the plant regularly, fertilize as needed, and prune as necessary to encourage healthy growth.

Inarching can be a challenging technique, but with patience and practice, you can successfully propagate a wide variety of plants using this method.
