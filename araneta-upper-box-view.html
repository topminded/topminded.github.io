16. Written upper are description of the art of Indonosia, Malaysia, Thailand, Cambodia, Singapore. A WORD POOL is given on the box upper which contains the arth and crafts that match.​


Elements and Principles of Arts and Crafts in Southeast Asia

1. Ikat technique

• quite complex; it produces patterned fabric which is diverse and vary by region. To create patterns, weavers tie-dye portions of weft yam before weaving begins.

2. Cotton Textiles

• also became part of Cambodian culture. Rural women often weave homemade cotton fabric. Krama, the traditional check scarves worn almost universally by Cambodian's are made of cotton.

3. Sihn

• is the Lao women's ankle-long skirt that has undeniable form and unique patterns. Cotton sihns are comfortable in the Lao heat. The bottom of the sihn is called the foot, 'sihn din' in Lao.

4. Ha Dang (Ha Dong)

• Many of our Vietnamese fabrics are jacquards from Ha Dong, the center of weaving and sericulture (silk worm production) for centuries. Old jacquard looms are still used, weaving patterns containing centuries-old symbols and characters.

5. Batik

• The most common fabric in some Southeast Asian countries is the 'batik' which is thought to be derived from the word 'ambatik' that can be translated into 'a cloth with little dots'. The drop action refers to the process of dyeing the fabric by making use of a resist technique, covering areas of cloth with a dye-resistant substance to prevent them from absorbing colors.

6. Batik

• Brunei's traditional textile, also called batik, is distinctly different than that of Indonesia, Malaysia, and Singapore. It has its own unique design that shows their national flower simpur, sumboi-sumboi (pitcher plant), and Brunei's traditional design of air muleh.

7. Flying Lantern

• are traditionally made from oiled rice paper on a bamboo frame which contains a fuel cell or small candles. When the fuel is lit, the flame heats the air inside the lantern. The Thai name is khom loi.

8. Sa paper

• is used to create lampshades, writing paper, greeting cards, and bookmarks, as mentioned handicrafts has been revived recently in Luang Prabang, Northern Laos. It is also used for calligraphy and for making festive decorations, umbrellas, fan and kites.

9. Wayang Kulit

• Shadow puppetry is known in Indonesia. Wayang Kulit is a traditional form of puppet shadow play performed in the Indo-Malayan archipelago.

10. Songkok

• is a cap widely or commonly worn in Southeast Asia. It was associated with Islam in Malaysia, while it was with the nationalist secular movement in Indonesia. Mostly worn among Muslim males in formal gatherings such as weddings, funerals or festivals.

25. MathThe Box on the upper right has a volume of [tex] {80cm}^{3} [/tex]Find the height of the box. ​


Answer:

Answer: The height of the box is 2 cm

Step-by-step explanation:

Solution: We discovered that in solving the volume of a cube or rectangular prism, we will just multiply the Three (3)

dimensions. It is given that the length is 10 cm, the width is 4 cm and the height is unknown but the volume is given

which is 80 cm. To solve for the height, we will substitute all the given values to the formula. So we have,

Volume = length x width x height

80 cm3 = 10 cm x 4 cm x height

80 cm3 = 40 cm2 = height

2 cm = height
