12. properties of thinner or paint remover​?


Answer:

A fluid or paste formulation designed to remove dried paint or varnish. Commercial paint strippers generally use systems based on either solvents or alkalis. Most of the solvent strippers use Methylene chloride. It is an effective solvent that penetrates and swells oil, alkyd, and vinyl paints and most varnishes. However, it is not selective and will penetrate all paint and overpaint layers. Solvent strippers often contain co-solvents (Acetone, methanol), acid or alkali activators, surfactants, and thickeners.

24. What makes the Paint Thinner harmful?​


Answer:

Paint thinner poisoning occurs when a toxic substance, known as a hydrocarbon, is ingested by mouth or by breathing. Paint thinners, gasoline and cleaning sprays can contain these hydrocarbons. Symptoms include burning in the mouth, throat or stomach; vomiting; or diarrhea.

26. Predict whether or not these substances will mix with each other or form solutions. Justify your Answers. a) Paint thinner and oil – b) Paint thinner and water –c) Oil and salt – d) Oil and sugar – e) Salt a _​


Explanation:

a) Paint thinner and oil will not mix as they are both non-polar and oil is not soluble in non-polar solvents.

b) Paint thinner and water will mix as paint thinner is a polar solvent and water is a polar solvent.

c) Oil and salt will not mix as oil is non-polar and salt is an ionic compound, which is also not soluble in non-polar solvents.

d) Oil and sugar will not mix as oil is non-polar and sugar is an organic compound, which is not soluble in non-polar solvents.

e) Salt will dissolve in water as it is an ionic compound and water is a polar solvent, which can dissolve ionic compounds.
