10. In the nang shadow puppet show of thailand, what elements of art is least important


Answer

Value is the least important element of art in a Nang Yai performance.

Explanation

Value is an element of art that refers to the tone of colors from light to dark. The color value of Nang Yai is barely noticed because of the nature of the performance. It is a theatrical play that uses strong lights to create silhouettes or form of the puppets. That is why value is the least noticable element in a Nang Yai performance.

19. Describe the national costume of Thailand, Myanmar, Cambodia


Answer:

Traditional Thai clothing is called chut thai (Thai: ชุดไทย), which literally means 'Thai outfit'. It can be worn by men, women, and children. Northern and northeastern women may wear a pha sin instead of a pha nung and a pha chung hang with either a blouse or a suea pat.

The national costume of Myanmar is the longyi, Burmese pronunciation: [lòʊɴd͡ʑì]), an ankle-length wraparound skirt worn by both males and females.

Sampot is the national garment of Cambodia, dating back to the Funan era when important Chinese diplomats asked the Cambodian king to order the people to cover themselves. Still commonly worn today in rural areas, the sampot is worn by both men and women as a form of sarong.
