9. What example of an Over-The-Counter medicine neutralizes stomach acid which may come into liquid, chewable gummy, or tablet?​


Answer:

Antacids

Explanation:

How antacids work

Antacids are over-the-counter (OTC) medications that help neutralize stomach acid.

They work differently from other acid reducers such as H2 receptor blockers and proton pump inhibitors (PPIs). Those drugs work by reducing or preventing the secretion of stomach acid.

Antacids can be used to treat symptoms of excess stomach acid, such as:

acid reflux, which can include regurgitation, bitter taste, persistent dry cough, pain when lying down, and trouble swallowing

heartburn, which is a burning sensation in your chest or throat caused by acid reflux

indigestion, which is pain in your upper gut that can feel like gas or bloating

Types of antacids

Antacids usually come in the following drug forms:

liquid

chewable gummy or tablet

tablet that you dissolve in water to drink

Explanation:

An example of a neutral audience could be a group of individuals who are attending a public lecture on a new scientific discovery that has not been widely reported in the media. Since they have no prior knowledge or opinion about the topic, they can listen to the speaker without any preconceived notions or prejudices.
