Prophase is the first step of mitosis, during which chromosomes condense and the nuclear envelope dissolves. Metaphase follows prophase. During metaphase, the chromosomes align in the center of the cell at the equatorial plate and the spindle fibers attach to the centromeres of the chromosomes.

Stages of mitosis: prophase, metaphase, anaphase, telophase. Cytokinesis typically overlaps with anaphase and/or telophase. You can remember the order of the phases with the famous mnemonic: [Please] Pee on the MAT.

10.B. metaphase I

Metaphase I: Brief stage in which tetrads line up in the equatorial plane of the cell. Anaphase I: Homologous chromosomes separate and migrate to opposite ends of cell.

11.C. mitosis

Mitosis is the process in cell division where the nucleus divides into two nuclei, each with an identical set of chromosomes. Mitosis is divided into four phases: prophase, metaphase, anaphase, and telophase.

12.B. centromeres

During anaphase, sister chromatids are separated at the centromere and are pulled towards opposite poles of the cell by the mitotic spindle.
