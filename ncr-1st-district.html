The Philippines is a country located in the Pacific Ring of Fire, making it vulnerable to natural disasters such as earthquakes. With a long history of catastrophic earthquakes, it is essential to ensure the earthquake resistance of structures, especially in highly populated areas. This comparative analysis aims to assess the earthquake resistance of structures in three different regions in the Philippines: Region 1, the Cordillera Administrative Region (CAR), and the National Capital Region (NCR). The analysis aims to determine if there are significant differences in the earthquake resistance of buildings and other structures in these areas. By comparing the data from these regions, we can identify any disparities in building codes and construction practices that may contribute to differences in earthquake resistance. This analysis will provide valuable insights for policymakers, engineers, and builders to create better, more effective measures to prepare for and mitigate the effects of future earthquakes.
