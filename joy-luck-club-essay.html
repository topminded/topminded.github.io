6. There is a Math Summer Camp Program in your barangay that leads by GSCS Math Club. As a Math Club Secretary, you are asked to do an informative essay about how important it is for 21st Century learners to have logical thinking skills – inductive and deductive reasoning​


Answer:

In the 21st century, logical thinking skills are increasingly important for learners of all ages. The ability to reason logically, using both inductive and deductive methods, is a crucial skill that helps individuals solve problems, make decisions, and think critically. This is why the Math Summer Camp Program, led by the GSCS Math Club, focuses on developing these skills among students.

Inductive reasoning involves drawing general conclusions based on specific observations. It involves collecting data and making observations to arrive at a general principle or theory. For example, a student might observe that all of the dogs they have ever encountered have fur. Based on this observation, they can use inductive reasoning to conclude that all dogs have fur.

On the other hand, deductive reasoning involves starting with a general principle or theory and using it to draw specific conclusions. It involves using logical rules and reasoning to arrive at a conclusion based on a set of premises or assumptions. For example, a student might start with the premise that all mammals have fur. From this premise, they can use deductive reasoning to conclude that dogs, which are mammals, must have fur.

Both inductive and deductive reasoning are essential for 21st century learners because they allow individuals to think critically and make informed decisions based on data and evidence. These skills are particularly valuable in fields such as science, engineering, and mathematics, where problem-solving and critical thinking are key. They are also important in everyday life, helping individuals make sense of the world around them and make informed decisions.

In conclusion, the development of logical thinking skills, including inductive and deductive reasoning, is crucial for 21st century learners. The Math Summer Camp Program led by the GSCS Math Club is an excellent opportunity for students to develop these skills and apply them in real-world situations. By fostering these skills, we can equip learners with the tools they need to think critically, solve problems, and make informed decisions throughout their lives.

12. summary of joy luck love?


The Joy Luck Club by Amy Tan is about the intertwined stories of four Chinese mothers and their four American raised daughters, the difference of both generations evident. The stories constantly shift points of view per chapter, alternating from a Chinese mother to their Chinese-American daughter. At the start of the story, the Joy Luck Club divulged that Jing-mei must meet her twin sisters, something her late mother, Suyuan Woo, was not able to do before her death. The unfolding of the story starts with Suyuan Woo’s backstory. Suyuan Woo had narrowly escaped the Japanese in her hometown, Kweilin, but unfortunately, leaving behind both her daughters. As a mother, Suyuan Woo constantly pushed her third daughter, Jing-mei to give her best, but failing as it was not her daughter’s nature, putting a strain in their mother-daughter relationship. An-mei grew up with tough love by her two maternal figures: Popo, her grandmother, and own mother. Feeling alienated during her childhood, and had only one wish: to be found. An-mei’s daughter, Rose, had her fair share of struggle with the death of her sibling, Bing; believing that she had caused his untimely death. As an adult, her marriage with Ted leads to a divorce, stemming from problems of indecisiveness, subordination, and reliance. Lindo was subjugated in an arranged marriage, but cleverly escaped with her timing and wit. As an adult, she later brags about the success of her daughter, Waverly’s chess career, mostly to Suyuan. Waverly still projects uncertainty towards her mother regarding cultural differences regarding Rich and Shoshana . Ying-ying was raised to be subordinate as a Chinese female. She had over time became superstitious, thought to believing to marry a neighbor she disliked. Lena, Ying-ying’s daugher, had somewhat inherited her mother’s superstitiousness. Ying-ying helps out her daughter’s marital unhappiness with husband, Harold. Overall, the Joy Luck Club centers on each individual with their own story to tell. Some stories, overlapping, some stories, completely new. The commonality of all the mothers was the traditional Chinese culture. The values of being a subordinate to males and elders, to not ask many questions, and be very superstitious. The commonality of all the daughters was the Chinese upbringing from their mothers fusing with the culture in a place they grew up in, and the adversities they face as adults. At the start of the story, the fear that Jing-mei had experienced regarding meeting her sisters to tell about their mother had ignited that action among the remaining mothers; an action to reconcile, and possibly tie loose ends with their daughters, to avoid the same fate as the mother and daughter relationship between  Suyuan and Jing-mei.
