Answer:

Similarities of dry iron and steam iron

Now that you know the main differences between a dry iron vs a steam iron, it's decision time. In summary, a dry iron is great for most fabrics. ... You can always opt for a steam iron to get the best out of both irons, since it doubles as a dry iron. The steam and spray button helps with getting out wrinkles

Meaning of Dry Iron

A dry iron has a soleplate that heats up. You can press it onto clothing to get clean, smooth garments. Whereas a steam iron has steam holes and outputs that emit hot steam to get out stubborn wrinkles, faster and easier

Meaning of Steam Iron

" Steam is the key to ironing because it helps get rid of wrinkles quickly. Steam permeates the fibers of the fabric, and the heat from the iron makes them stay in place." In other words, without steam, don't count on those pants being pressed as well.

There are three types of geothermal electricity-generating technologies in operation: dry steam, flash steam, and binary cycle.

Dry steam- is steam that is at the temperature of saturation, but does not contain water particles in suspension. It has a very high dryness fraction, with almost no moisture.

Flash steam- take high-pressure hot water from deep inside the earth and convert it to steam to drive generator turbines.

Binary cycle- in thermodynamics is a power generation method that uses two circuits, high temperature one and lower temperature.

15. People used the power of steam, developed machine tools, established iron productions, and the manufacturing of various products through the printing press.


INDUSTRIAL AGE 1700S - 1930S

People have learned to use the power of steam, developed machine tools, established iron production, and the manufacturing of various products including books through the printing press.

Industrial Revolution

The industrial revolution was marked by the invention of the steam engine to support production engines, trains and sailing ships. Various work equipment that originally depended on human and animal power was later replaced by steam engine power. As a result, production can be multiplied and distributed to various regions more massively.

The Industrial Revolution began in England around 1870 showing that Britain was the originator of the modern economy. In addition, the Industrial revolution also encouraged Britain to explore the outside world and carry out colonialism.

The imperialization and colonization carried out by the British were so extensive that they reached almost all continents in the world, namely America, Asia, Africa, and Australia. So England has the nickname of the land where the sun never sets. English has also become the world's language of communication to this day. Having a colony that many of the characteristics of England can be said to be superior in the military and economic fields.

There are three stages of the industrial revolution

First, the Domestic System, the home industry stage for workers in their own homes. Industrial products are in the form of handicrafts that are being worked on by workers based on the work they get.

Second, Manufacturing, a factory with dozens of workers at the back of the employer's house. The house in the middle of the residence and the front as a shop to sell products. The employer-employee relationship and work are closer because the workplace is one.

Third, Factory System, an industry that uses machines. The development of the industrial revolution in England was marked by the invention of machines that were useful for the industrial world.

James Watt in 1763 invented the steam engine. In the 1780s the Boulton and James Watt companies produced steam engines both for British use and for an export trade. In 1768, scientists Richard Arkwright and John Kay invented the fast-producing loom (flying, shuttle and water frame).
