15. Similarities and difference of reproduction of monet's water lilies by bernardo lira and water lilies and japanese bridge by claude monet​.


Answer:

There are several similarities and differences between the reproduction of Monet's Water Lilies by Bernardo Lira and Water Lilies and Japanese Bridge by Claude Monet.

One of the main similarities is that both paintings depict the same subject matter, which is Monet's famous Water Lilies series. Both paintings feature a serene and peaceful landscape with lilies floating on the surface of a pond, surrounded by trees and a bridge.

However, there are also some differences between the two paintings. One of the main differences is the style of the paintings. Lira's reproduction of Monet's Water Lilies has a more realistic and detailed style, while Monet's original Water Lilies and Japanese Bridge has a more impressionistic and loose style. This can be seen in the brushstrokes and the way the colors are applied to the canvas.

Another difference between the two paintings is the composition. Lira's reproduction has a more traditional composition, with the lilies and the bridge placed in the center of the painting. Monet's original, on the other hand, has a more dynamic composition, with the lilies and the bridge placed off-center, creating a sense of movement and balance in the painting.

Overall, both paintings are beautiful and evocative, but they differ in style and composition, reflecting the individual styles and visions of the two artists.
