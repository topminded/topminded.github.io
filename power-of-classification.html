11. What are the three classifications of power tools / equipment?​


Answer:

Power tools usually run on one of three types of power: compressed air, electricity, or combustion. When compressed air is used, air is pushed through the device in order to move various mechanical parts. The tool's air compressor is usually powered by an electric motor or a combustion engine.

Answer:

1. Air compressor - An air compressor has compressed energy in the form of gasoline or electric energy. When you use this device, it continues to store the compressed energy in the tank until it reaches its limitation. Note that the capacity and pressure power of an air compressor varies, depending on its model.

Air compressors are used for a wide range of applications. For example, they are an essential component of an air conditioner, heater, and a ventilator. They are also used to power up pneumatic tools including jackhammers, nail guns, etc.

2. Side grinder - Also known as angle grinders or disc grinders, side grinders are a handheld power tool which cut, grind, and polish metal and non-metal objects. A compressed tool, petrol engine, or an electric motor is required to power these tools.They are available in both corded and cordless forms and can be used for cutting and smoothing out hard edges and materials like stone, metal, wood, etc.

3. Bandsaw - A bandsaw features a steel band with a sharp, toothed blade that is stretched between wheels to cut materials like wood and metal. This impactful tool was essentially used in metalworking, woodworking, and lumbering. The purpose of a bandsaw is to split a material in equal lengths with ease and precision.

While stationary bandsaws are more commonly found in workshops, there are a few portable ones out there as well.

15. *Define what is Hand Tools and its classifications. *Define what is Machine/Power Tools*Define what is Pneumatic Tools​


Answer:

Hand Tools are tools that are powered by hand rather than by electricity or other external sources of power. These tools are generally simple and easy to use and are used for a wide range of applications, including woodworking, metalworking, and automotive repair. Some common examples of hand tools include hammers, pliers, screwdrivers, wrenches, and hand saws.

Hand tools can be classified into several categories based on their intended use, such as cutting tools, gripping tools, fastening tools, measuring tools, and striking tools.

Machine/Power Tools are tools that are powered by electricity, gasoline, or compressed air, among other sources of power. These tools are generally more complex and sophisticated than hand tools, and they are used for a wide range of applications, including manufacturing, construction, and woodworking. Some common examples of power tools include drills, saws, grinders, and sanders.

Power tools can be classified into several categories based on their intended use, such as drilling machines, cutting machines, grinding machines, and sanding machines.

Pneumatic Tools are tools that are powered by compressed air, and they are commonly used in manufacturing and construction applications. Pneumatic tools are typically more powerful and efficient than hand tools, and they are able to perform tasks that would be difficult or impossible for human workers to accomplish manually. Some common examples of pneumatic tools include pneumatic hammers, pneumatic drills, and pneumatic screwdrivers. Pneumatic tools can be classified into several categories based on their intended use, such as impact wrenches, pneumatic sanders, and air ratchets.

1. Coercive power is the ability to force someone to do something through the use of punishment or threats.

2. Expert power is the ability to influence others through expertise or knowledge in a particular field.

3. The power of reward is the ability to offer incentives or rewards in exchange for desired behavior or actions.

4. Referent power is the ability to influence others through personal characteristics or charisma.

5. Legitimate power is the ability to influence others through the authority given to a person or position by society or the law.

6. Purchasing power is the ability to influence others through the use of money or financial resources.

7. The power of eminent domain is the ability of the government to seize private property for public use, with compensation.

8. The power of taxation is the ability of the government to collect taxes from individuals and organizations.

9. Police power is the ability of the government to regulate and control the actions and behavior of individuals and organizations for the protection of public health, safety, and welfare.
