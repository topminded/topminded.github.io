10. DEFINE THE FOLLOWING TERMS: A. LITERATURE REVIEW B. ABSTRACT C. APPENDIX D. RESULTS E. METHODOLOGY F. INTRODUCTION​


A. Literature Review - It is an evaluation and analysis of existing published research studies, articles, and other scholarly works, which are relevant to a particular research topic. It aims to identify gaps in existing knowledge, establish the need for further research, and provide a theoretical framework or context for the research.

B. Abstract - It is a brief summary of a research paper, article, or thesis that highlights the main purpose, methods, results, and conclusions of the study. It usually appears at the beginning of the paper, and it helps readers to quickly determine if the paper is relevant to their interests and worth reading in full.

C. Appendix - It is a supplementary material added at the end of a research paper or a thesis that provides additional information, data, analysis, or references to support or clarify the main text. It often includes tables, graphs, images, or surveys that are too lengthy or detailed to be included in the main text.

D. Results - It is the section of a research paper or a thesis that presents the findings, outcomes, or data collected from the research. It summarizes the main findings or answers to the research questions, often using statistical analysis or graphs to illustrate the results.

E. Methodology - It is the systematic and detailed description of the methods, procedures, and techniques used in a research study or experiment. It provides a clear and logical explanation of how the research was conducted, including the sample population, data collection methods, data analysis techniques, and ethical considerations.

F. Introduction - It is the opening section of a research paper, article, or thesis, which provides an overview of the research topic, its significance, and the research questions or hypotheses to be tested. It also establishes a clear and concise thesis statement, outlines the key points and arguments to be made in the paper, and provides the reader with a roadmap of the paper.
