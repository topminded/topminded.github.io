10. Definition of sweep scrub wax or polish floors using brooms mops or powered scrubbing and waxing machines ​


Explanation:

Sweeping, scrubbing, waxing, or polishing floors using brooms, mops, or powered scrubbing and waxing machines refers to the process of cleaning and maintaining the appearance of floor surfaces in a building.

Sweeping involves using a broom to remove dirt, dust, and debris from the floor surface. Scrubbing involves using a mop and cleaning solution to remove dirt and stains from the floor surface. This can also be done with powered scrubbing machines which makes the process more efficient and effective.

Waxing or polishing floors is the process of applying a protective layer of wax or polish on the floor surface, to protect it from wear and tear and improve its appearance. This can also be done with powered waxing or polishing machines which makes the process more efficient and effective.

This process of sweeping, scrubbing, waxing, or polishing is crucial for maintaining the cleanliness and aesthetic appeal of a building and it's typically carried out by custodial, janitorial or maintenance staff.

16. common problems of polishing machinesplease answer this question ​


Answer:

Polishing machines are widely used in various industries, and just like any other machine, they can face several problems that can affect their performance. Some common problems with polishing machines include:

Vibration: High levels of vibration can cause damage to the machine and affect the quality of the polish. This can be caused by an imbalance in the rotating components or by worn out bearings.Burn marks: Burn marks can occur when the polishing pad overheats due to excessive pressure or friction. This can be prevented by using the correct speed and pressure settings for the material being polished.Poor surface finish: A poor surface finish can be caused by using the wrong type of polishing pad or abrasive, or by not maintaining the machine properly.Overheating: Overheating can be caused by a clogged air filter, a lack of lubrication, or a faulty cooling system.Uneven wear: Uneven wear on the polishing pad can cause streaks or an inconsistent finish. This can be caused by using the wrong type of pad or abrasive, or by applying too much pressure in one area.Clogging: Clogging can occur when abrasive particles become trapped in the polishing pad, reducing its effectiveness. This can be prevented by regularly cleaning the pad and using the correct type of abrasive.Electrical issues: Electrical problems can be caused by a variety of factors, such as a faulty power source, loose connections, or worn out components.

By addressing these common problems, you can ensure that your polishing machine is operating at its best and producing high-quality results.
