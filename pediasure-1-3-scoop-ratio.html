8. what is a bahavior by melting 1 scoop of sugar​


Answer:

The chemical reaction we are most familiar with is that of melting: sugar decomposes at a temperature ranging between 184 and 186°C.

This is a very recent discovery we owe to a team of researchers in Illinois. Basically, when we heat sucrose gently, this produces a phenomenon known as “apparent melting”.

In other words, sugar crystals do not actually melt but produce a proper reaction called “inversion”.

What really happens is that the two molecular components of sugar – glucose and fructose – decompose.

In their turn, they give way to “caramelisation”, consisting of two phases.
