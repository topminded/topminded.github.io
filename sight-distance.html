12. An airplane pilot over the Pacific sights an atoll at an angle of depression of 5°. At this time, the horizontal distance from the airplane to the atoll is 4629 meters. What is the height of the plane?


Answer:

The height of the plane at 5° angle of elevation is 405 meters.

Step-by-step explanation:

Click attached image to view the illustration of the problem

Given:

Angle of elevation θ =  5°Horizontal distance from below airplane to the atoll (adjacent): 4,629 metersHeight (vertical) from the plane to the horizontal distance (opposite):  x

Trigo ratio:

tan θ = opposite/adjacent

tan 5° = x/(4,629 meters)

x = (tan 5°) (4,629 meters)

x = (0.0875) (4,629 meters)

x = 405.0375  or  405 meters

The height of the plane at 5° angle of elevation is 405 meters.

26. A lighthouse 20m high with the base at sea level is sighted by a ship .If the angle of elevation from the ship to the top of the lighthouse is 12degree, what is the distance from the ship to the lighthouse?


Answer:

94 meters

Step-by-step explanation:

What is asked?The distance from the ship to the lighthouse.What are given?20 meters - height of the lighthouse12 degrees - angle of elevationHow to solve?First we will assign variables to the important variables (points and lines) of the problem to make it easier to determine.

A = top of the lighthouse

B= location of the ship

C = base of the lighthouse

a = distance between the lighthouse and the ship

b= height of the lighthouse (20 m)

c = the diagonal sight from the ship to the top of the lighthouse

We also have 12 degrees as the measurement of angle ABC. That means the right angle (90 degrees) is angle ACB and angle BAC measures 78 degrees.

Observing from the data, we can form a right triangle. Because of that, we can use the trigonometric functions. With the known values (b and the angles), we will use the formula:

[tex]tan \theta = \frac{opposite}{adjacent}[/tex], we will use the angle BAC which measures 78 degrees.

Solution:

[tex]tan \theta = \frac{opposite}{adjacent}\\tan 78 = \frac{a}{b}\\tan 78 = \frac{a}{20m}\\a= tan 78 (20m)\\a= (4.7)(20m)\\a= 94 \ meters[/tex]

Answer:

Therefore, the distance from the ship to the lighthouse is 94 meters.
