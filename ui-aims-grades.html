11. Ruben's final grade in 6 subjects are 88,89,84,90,91 and 86? What is the least grade that he should aim for in that 7th subject if he has to have an average of 88?​


Answer:

The minimum grade that Ruben must get in his 7th subject is 88.

Step-by-step explanation:

Let the value of the unknown be "x". Follow the formula in getting  average.

[tex]\frac{88 + 89 + 84 + 90 + 91 + 86 + x}{7}[/tex] = 88

[tex]\frac{528 + x}{7}[/tex] = 88 [then,cross multiply]

528 + x = 88 (7)

528 + x = 616

[Then, tanspose 528 to the other side of the equation to get the value of x and change the value into negative]

x = 616 - 528

x = 88

Verification:

[tex]\frac{88 + 89 + 84 + 90 +91 +86 + 88}{7}[/tex] = 88

[tex]\frac{616}{7}[/tex] = 88

88 = 88

Using numerical data to investigate a phenomenon or issue, quantitative research is a methodical and empirical approach. Although quantitative research can be used to find trends and patterns, its primary goal is to test hypotheses, establish causal relationships, and draw generalizations about a population or phenomenon from data analysis. In order to ensure that the results are representative of the population being studied, quantitative research typically makes use of statistical measures and methods to analyze the data.

Numerous fields, including psychology, economics, the social sciences, and the natural sciences, can benefit from quantitative research. In most cases, structured instruments like surveys, questionnaires, and experiments are used to collect data. Statistics tables, graphs, and charts are frequently used to summarize and interpret the data in quantitative research results.

In conclusion, although quantitative research is capable of identifying trends and patterns, its primary objective is to test hypotheses and draw generalizations from numerical data analysis.

27. what is the difference between UI and GUI​


Answer:

Command Line Interface

Explanation:

GUI is "graphical user interface" and UI is just "user interface." GUI is a subset of UI. UI can include non-graphical interfaces such as screen readers or command line interfaces which aren't considered GUI. Also, the opposite of GUI is CLI - Command Line Interface.

Answer:

Being a grade 10 junior high school student is a significant milestone in a student's academic journey. In many countries, it marks the transition from the lower to the upper secondary level of education. This means that students are expected to handle more challenging subjects and higher academic expectations.

One of the biggest changes that grade 10 students experience is the increase in academic workload. They are introduced to more complex subjects such as advanced mathematics, science, and literature. The amount of homework and projects also increases significantly, which means that time management and organizational skills are critical for success. In addition, students have to start thinking about their future academic and career goals, as their grades in grade 10 will play a significant role in determining their opportunities for higher education.

Despite the challenges, being a grade 10 student can also be an exciting time. It is an opportunity to explore different areas of interest and develop new skills. Students can participate in extracurricular activities such as sports, music, art, and community service. They can also develop their leadership and teamwork skills by joining student organizations and clubs. By embracing these opportunities and balancing their academic and personal lives, grade 10 students can have a fulfilling and memorable year.
