Aristotle, a great philosopher initiative the earliest mass communication model called “Aristotle’s Model of Communication”. Aristotle Model of Communication is formed with 5 basic elements

(i) Speaker, (ii) Speech, (iii) Occasion, (iv) Audience and (v) Effect.

Aristotle advises speakers to build speech for different audience on different time (occasion) and for different effects.

Speaker plays an important role in Public speaking. The speaker must prepare his speech and analysis audience needs before he enters into the stage. His words should influence in audience mind and persuade their thoughts towards him.

Speaker – Alexander

Speech – about his invasion

Occasion – War field

Audience – Soldiers

Effect – To defeat Persia

6. Directions: Develop you own model of communication. Include a two- to three- paragraph description of your model and explain any special characteristic of it. WWW​


Answer:The communication model is a simple description of the communication process that relates one communication component to another.

The communication model is made to make it easier to understand the communication process and see the basic components that need to be in a communication. Communication is also a process. This can be seen from every symptom or event that does not escape the existence of a communication that exists between humans.

Conceptually, there are 3 communication models:

   linear model

   interactional model

   transactional model.

The communication model that I am trying to develop is a communication model in the education sector. So far, teaching and learning activities in the classroom usually use a linear model (passive students) or interactional models (active students). I will try to combine the two communication models into a new model where the role of the teacher (sender of information) is an active and dominant role while the role of students (recipient of information) is passive but has the flexibility to be active and interact with the sender of information.

This communication model is named an integrative communication model. Where in the learning process (communication) the sender will start by throwing the main questions that are difficult for students to answer. This will create confusion for students who are the recipients of the message. Students will try hard to understand the meaning of the teacher's question. In this situation, communication runs linearly where the recipient of the message cannot provide feedback and the student is only passive.

After starting with a main question, the teacher will convey the theory little by little while throwing some rhetorical questions related to the theory presented. In this situation, students will be provoked to continue to answer what is asked by the teacher because the questions are rhetorical, unlike difficult questions at the opening of the lesson. The theory presented by the teacher will slowly be structured into a concept of understanding where the question that was originally rhetorical turns out to be an inducement to the answer to the question at the beginning which silences the class. And at the end of the session where all the theory has been arranged and all the rhetorical questions make a connecting chain. The big questions that become the focus of learning will be answered and understood by students (recipients of information).

The characteristic of this communication model is that it makes the recipient of the information look like an active person, whereas in fact it is passive because it only provides feedback on an information/question that actually does not require an answer, but only a inducement to achieve the intended goal.

14. Develop your own model of communication. Include a two-to three-paragraph description of your model and explain any special characteristic to it. subject:(oral communication)


Let’s go over the basic communication model. The model is a visual representation of how a message goes from sender to receiver.

Communication Model

Sender

The sender is the person who wants to deliver a message. Pretty self explanatory, right?

Encode

For a sender to transfer a message, they must first translate the message into symbols for the receiver (the one who receives the message). This means taking thoughts, emotions and images and translating them into something the receiver can understand. These symbols often include:

wordspicturessoundssense information (e.g., touch or smell).

The process of translating these messages into symbols is called encoding.

Message Channel

After encoding the message, it’s time to communicate the message to a receiver. To do this, we must pick a channel for the message to go through. The channel is the type of medium used to transmit messages between senders and receivers. Examples of channels are:

Verbal

Face-to-faceOver the telephone

Written

LettersNewspapers

Visual Media

TelevisionPhotographs

Decode

Once the message is received by someone, it’s time for the decoding process. Just like a sender must encode messages to communicate, receivers must sense and interpret the symbols to fully understand the message. They then decode the symbols back into images, emotions and thoughts to make sense of them.

Receiver

The receiver is the person who receives the message.

Let’s connect it all.

Now that you know all of the steps of the communication process, it easily connects. When messages are decoded exactly as the sender has intended, the sender and receiver have matching perceptions of the message being communicated. When everyone is on the same page, effective communication occurs.
