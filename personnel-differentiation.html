6. What is personnel management


Answer:

Personnel management is the process of managing employees within an organization to ensure that they are productive, motivated, and committed to the goals of the organization. It involves a variety of activities such as recruitment and selection, training and development, performance management, compensation and benefits, employee relations, and workplace health and safety.

Explanation:

The main goal of personnel management is to create a work environment that is conducive to productivity, employee satisfaction, and retention. This requires careful planning, communication, and collaboration between management and employees. Effective personnel management can help organizations to attract and retain talented employees, reduce turnover, and increase overall productivity and profitability.
