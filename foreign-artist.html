13. How are our contemporary artists able to merge the local and the foreign


Answer:

Merging the “local” and the “foreign” precedes Post-Modernism- consider for examples Pablo Picasso’s painting “Les Demoiselles d’Avignon” where a Spaniard living in France used African and Iberian icons to produce a new style which came to be known in various manifestations as Cubism and Primitivism. Conservative purists may argue that Picasso “appropriated” iconic forms that belonged to a different culture. My view is that this is a simple example of an artist refuting the notion of “us” and “them”. There have been millions of examples since (and even before) then, but the point is that very little of what we experience and produce is absolutely “local” and very little is absolutely “foreign”.
