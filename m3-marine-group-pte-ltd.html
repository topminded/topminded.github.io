24. why do marine mammals produce fewer off spring than most other animal groups? ​


Answer:

It's because of the internal fertilization; offsprings are protected from the environment; external fertilization offsprings are many to increase chances of survival.

The tadpoles are prey with a low survival rate: so more eggs will increase fitness. They can afford more eggs because there is no aftercare burden.

Mammals invest heavily in aftercare of young, which increases survival rate, but is too costly and demanding to produce many.
