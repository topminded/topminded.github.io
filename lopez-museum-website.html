11. what differentiate traditional museum from digital museum?​


Traditional vs Digital Museums

Answer:

The main difference is that traditional museums can be seen personally and the artifacts are real. On the other hand, digital museums are alternatives that offer pictures, video, and interactive software of artifacts that cannot be visited or not present in the traditional museum.

Explanation:

The traditional museum presents artifacts and artworks to the public so that they may understand these objects within a setting that provides historical and cultural context to further visitors’ knowledge. Then online museums were designed to reach a larger number of people and motivate physical visits to museums, informing potential visitors who were planning their visits. They also intended to encourage the research and personal interests of visitors through collection information as well as assist school visits and research for students.

But within the traditional physical museums, artifacts are often regarded as passive since they are positioned so that people can see these objects but cannot interact with them. Older generations are accustomed to this practice; however, potential younger visitors now desire more stimulating experiences.

28. what is the role played by fiestas and museums in preserving of our cultural practices and symbolsFiestas-Museums-​


FIESTAS

By preserving cultural components including food, clothes, shelter, and language, culture can be preserved in a variety of ways. Putting physical cultural objects on exhibit at museums is one way to do this. This approach to preservation is typically regarded as "genuine" and "memorializing."

Festivals can also help preserve intangible cultural assets like living cultural knowledge, identity, meaning, and basic values ingrained in certain ancient customs or rituals, which museums are unable to fully show.

The festival format's adaptability allows it to preserve and impart a culture's important significance and basic values in more direct, communicative, and engaging ways.

Festivals, as opposed to museum exhibits, provide experiential learning. Due to the fact that they do not prohibit interaction between the audience and what is being displayed, the distinctive cultural tradition displays that are given at festivals offer an environment for an unconstrained and sensually unrestricted experience. A good example was the Jeju Haenyeo Festival this year.

Activities that are interactive and immersive can enhance learning. A pleasurable event also helps memories stick around longer. Such instances are Jeju dialect speaking competitions at the Tamna Cultural Festival and the Deoksuri Traditional Folk Festival.

The spread of a particular sort of tradition, pungmul, throughout the island has also been aided by host communities' participation in festival planning and execution. Each of the 24 administrative villages, or "dong," in Jeju City, has its own pungmul troupe. They learn and practice traditional performances outside of the festival period, and they also perform both locally and abroad.

This demonstrates how cultural fairs can help preserve traditional traditions by inspiring local artists to incorporate them into their daily lives.

MUSEUMS

The rich art, culture, and natural history of a country are all preserved at museums, which have historically served as a window into the past. The preservation of regional culture is greatly aided by museums. No matter what the future holds, a culture may be recorded and remembered with meticulous documentation and artifact preservation. People from various cultural backgrounds can also share and comprehend it. Here are a few justifications for why museums are so crucial to the maintenance of cultural heritage.

They impart cultural knowledge to others

People from the majority culture must be taught about minority cultures and their way of life if they are to respect them and survive globalization. The most effective approach to accomplish this is through a respected museum exhibit of local culture. Cultural erosion suddenly starts to decline in societies with educated people who value minority cultures.

They Link People from Diverse Backgrounds

People with that heritage and others from other backgrounds who are interested in learning about that heritage will both visit museums in search of information about other cultures. Heritage and culture-focused museums bring people together and build a network of support for various races and organizations. These kinds of support systems stop civilizations from vanishing and languages from dying.

In the fight against language and cultural extinction, museums and the people who visit them play a crucial role. When the minority culture aspires to make its offspring conform to that of the dominant culture because it feels inferior or unimportant, these losses take place. We are already preventing cultural losses by utilizing a museum to foster respect for and interest in these minority cultures.
