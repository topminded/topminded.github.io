Answer:

Did you mean square roots? If so, I'll explain to you what square roots are.

The square root of a number x is the number that is when multiplied by itself, results to x. For example, x = y × y therefore the square root of x is y, because when you multiplied y by itself (y times y), it results to x.

another example, 3 × 3 = 9. The square root of 9 is 3, because when you multiplied 3 by itself (3 × 3), it gives the product of 9.

The square root of 4 is 2. (2×2=4)

The square root of 64 is 8. (8×8=64)

The square root of 144 is 12. (12×12=144)

When you square a number, that particular number becomes the square root of its square.

Examples:

7² = 49, therefore 7 is a square root of 49.

10² = 100, therefore 10 is a square root of 100.

That's just the basics. A positive integer always has two square roots, a positive and a negative integer. Example,

81 = 9×9 or (-9)(-9)

121 = 11×11 or (-11)(-11)

The square roots of 81 are ±9, or positive and negative 9.

The square roots of 121 are ±11, or positive and negative 11.
