[tex] \begin{array}{l} \displaystyle \lim_{x \to \infty} \left(\sqrt{x^2 + ax} - \sqrt{x^2 - bx}\right) \\ \\ \textsf{As }x\to +\infty, \sqrt{x^2 + ax}\:\textsf{ approaches }+\infty \\ \textsf{while the radicand of }\sqrt{x^2 - bx}\textsf{ results to one} \\ \textsf{of the indeterminate forms }\infty - \infty. \textsf{ It is not} \\ \textsf{obvious how their difference behaves. However,} \\ \textsf{the limit equals} \\ \\ \footnotesize \displaystyle \lim_{x \to \infty} \dfrac{\sqrt{x^2 + ax} - \sqrt{x^2 - bx}}{1} \cdot \dfrac{\sqrt{x^2 + ax} + \sqrt{x^2 - bx}}{\sqrt{x^2 + ax} + \sqrt{x^2 - bx}} \\ \\ \displaystyle = \lim_{x \to \infty} \dfrac{x^2 + ax - (x^2 - bx)}{\sqrt{x^2 + ax} + \sqrt{x^2 - bx}} \\ \\ \displaystyle = \lim_{x \to \infty} \dfrac{ax + bx}{\sqrt{x^2 + ax} + \sqrt{x^2 - bx}} \\ \\ \textsf{Now we divide numerator and denominator by }x \\ (\textsf{noting that }x=\sqrt{x^2}): \\ \\ \displaystyle = \lim_{x \to \infty} \dfrac{\frac{(a + b)x}{x}}{\sqrt{\frac{x^2 + ax}{x^2}} + \sqrt{\frac{x^2 - bx}{x^2}}} \\ \\ \displaystyle = \lim_{x \to \infty} \dfrac{a + b}{\sqrt{1 + \frac{a}{x}} + \sqrt{1 - \frac{b}{x}}} \\ \\ \displaystyle = \dfrac{a + b}{\sqrt{1 + 0} + \sqrt{1 - 0}} \\ \\ = \boxed{\dfrac{a + b}{2}}\end{array} [/tex]

If n is odd, then the limits at infinity are the same as the limit as x approaches infinity of the term with the highest power and the sign of a. 28. Find the limit as x approaches infinity of (2x^2 - 5x + 1)/(4x^2 + 3x - 2).​


To find the limit as x approaches infinity, we can divide both the numerator and the denominator by the highest power of x in the expression, which is x^2. Doing this we get:

(2x^2 - 5x + 1) / (4x^2 + 3x - 2) = (2 - 5/x + 1/x^2) / (4 + 3/x - 2/x^2)

As x approaches infinity, both -5/x and 3/x will approach zero, and 1/x^2 and 2/x^2 will approach zero even faster. So we have:

lim x→∞ (2x^2 - 5x + 1) / (4x^2 + 3x - 2) = (2 - 0 + 0) / (4 + 0 - 0) = 1/2

Therefore, the limit as x approaches infinity of the given expression is 1/2.
