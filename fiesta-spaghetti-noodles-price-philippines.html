18. Fiesta - Philippines celebration of patrons.​


Answer:

Fiesta in the Philippines is a celebration of the patron saint of a particular town or city. It is a religious and cultural event that is deeply rooted in the country's history and traditions. The fiesta is usually held on the saint's feast day, which is typically celebrated with a novena, a nine-day prayer, and culminates with a grand feast.

During the fiesta, the streets are decorated with colorful banners and streamers, and various activities are held such as parades, processions, and street parties. People wear their best clothes and prepare special dishes to share with their family and friends. It is a time for reunions, bonding, and strengthening of community ties.

The fiesta is an important part of the Filipino culture, and it reflects the country's strong Catholic faith and the spirit of unity and camaraderie among its people. It is a time for Filipinos to express their gratitude to their patron saint and to celebrate their blessings and achievements as a community.
