10. Why is Manchester coding not a good choice for writing data to a magnetic disk?


Answer:

Manchester coding is a special case of binary phase-shift keying (BPSK), where the data controls the phase of a square wave carrier whose frequency is the data rate.

Manchester code ensures frequent line voltage transitions, directly proportional to the clock rate; this helps clock recovery.
