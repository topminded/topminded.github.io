9. 5. A 25 kg block is raised by a crane 4 meters above the ground. What is the potential energy of the block?​


Potential Energy is 980 J

Explanation:

Step 1: Enumerate the given values.

Given:

m = 25 kg

h = 4 m

g = 9.8 m/s²

Step 2: Identify what is being asked.

Required:

Potential Energy

Step 3: Solve for Potential Energy

Solution:

The formula for Potential Energy is given by:

PE = mgh

where: PE - Potential Energy

m - mass

g - gravitational acceleration constant

h - height of object from the reference level

Substituting the value of m, g and h in the formula,

PE = (25 kg)(9.8 m/s²)(4 m)

PE = 980 kgm²/s² or 980 Nm or 980 J

FRICTION

Friction can operate as a force in either the same direction as the motion or the opposite direction. In the opposite direction as the forces that are exerting themselves. In the same direction as the forces that are exerting themselves.

Friction is an example of a force that acts to oppose the motion. The force that acts between two surfaces in a direction that is perpendicular to the velocity of the object that is moving between them is referred to as friction.

Within the framework of friction, an illustration

To Go From Place to Place on Foot - When we walk on a surface that is otherwise smooth, friction ensures that our feet do not slide around. However, when we are walking on ice or other surfaces that are similarly slippery, our grip becomes less effective, and we end up losing our footing.

The coefficient of friction, which the symbol fr denotes, is the ratio of the resistive force of friction, which is denoted by the symbol Fr, to the normal or perpendicular force, which is denoted by the symbol N. It is the force that acts to draw the two objects together. Therefore, Fr = Fr/N is the equation that corresponds to this value. Consequently, it is impossible to get a value for the friction coefficient larger than 1.

23. A mass of 10Kg is taken from the ground for 10m uphill on the wedge. The wedge makes an angle of 30° with the ground. Find the potential energy of the block.​


Answer:

To find the potential energy of the block, you can use the formula:

PE = mass * gravity * height

where mass is the mass of the block in kilograms, gravity is the acceleration due to gravity (9.8 m/s²), and height is the height above the ground in meters.

In this case, the mass of the block is 10 kg, the acceleration due to gravity is 9.8 m/s², and the height is 10 m. Plugging these values into the formula, we get:

PE = 10 kg * 9.8 m/s² * 10 m

PE = 980 Joules

Therefore, the potential energy of the block is 980 Joules.
