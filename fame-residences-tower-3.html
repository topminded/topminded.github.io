8. A stone is dropped from a tower, if it reaches the ground in 3 seconds determine how high is the tower.​


Answer:

initial velocity (u) = 0time (t) = 3sHeight = - HAcceleration due to gravity = -g

Apply second kinematical equation of motion:

[tex] \implies \tt-H = ut + \dfrac{1}{2} (-g)t^2[/tex]

[tex] \implies \tt H = 0 \times 3 + \dfrac{g}{2} (3)^2[/tex]

[tex]\implies \tt H =\dfrac{10}{2} \times 9[/tex]

[tex]\implies \tt H =5 \times 9[/tex]

[tex]\implies \underline{ \underline{\tt H =45 \: m}}[/tex]

Note : for easy calculation I've taken the value of acceleration due gravity as 10 m/s².

20. A new transmission tower will be put up midway between two existing towers. On a map drawn on a coordinate plane, the coordinates of first existing tower are (-5, -3) and the coordinates of the second existing tower are (9, 13). What are the coordinates of the point where the new tower will be placed?​


[tex]\Large\mathbb{SOLUTION:}[/tex]

Statement: A new transmission tower will be put up midway between two existing towers. On a map drawn on a coordinate plane, the coordinates of first existing tower are (-5, -3) and the coordinates of the second existing tower are (9, 13).

Required: What are the coordinates of the point where the new tower will be placed?

Let,

[tex]\sf{(x_1, \: y_1) = (-5, \: -3)}[/tex][tex]\sf{(x_2, \: y_2) = (9, \: 13)}[/tex]

[tex]\bold{Formula : } \qquad \qquad \qquad \qquad \qquad \qquad \qquad \qquad \\ \boxed{ \rm \: Midpoint = \bigg( \dfrac{x_1 + x_2}{2}, \: \dfrac{y_1 + y_2}{2} \bigg) \: } [/tex]

Substitute the given values into the above formula and solve for Midpoint:

[tex]\sf:\implies{Midpoint = \bigg(\dfrac{x_1 + x_2}{2}, \: \dfrac{y_1 + y_2}{2} \bigg)}[/tex]

[tex]\sf:\implies{Midpoint = \bigg( \dfrac{ - 5 + 9}{2}, \: \dfrac{ - 3 + 13}{2}\bigg)}[/tex]

[tex]\sf:\implies{Midpoint = \bigg(\dfrac{4}{2}, \: \dfrac{10}{2} \bigg)}[/tex]

[tex]\sf:\implies{Midpoint = \bigg(2, \: 5 \bigg)} \\ [/tex]

Therefore, the coordinates of the point where the new tower will be placed is (2, 5).

29. A new transmission tower will be put up midway between two existing towers. On a map drawn on a coordinate plane, the coordinates of first existing tower are (-5,-3) and the coordinates of the second existing tower are (9,13). = [tex]( \frac{x1+x2}{2} , \frac{y1+y2}{2} )[/tex]
x1 = -5    x2 = 9
y1 = -3    y2 = 13

Midpoint = [tex]( \frac{-5+9}{2} , \frac{-3+13}{2} )[/tex]

Midpoint = [tex]( \frac{4}{2} , \frac{10}{2} )[/tex]

Midpoint = (2, 5)

The coordinates of the point where the new tower will be placed is at (2,5).
