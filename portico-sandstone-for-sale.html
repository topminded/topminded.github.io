17. Description of sandstone​


Answer:

SAND STONE is a sedimentary rock composed of sand-size grains of mineral, rocks or oragnic material. It also contains a cementing material that binds the sand grains together and may contain matrix of silt- or clay-size particles that occupy the spaces between the sand grains.

Sandstones are made of sand grains that have been cemented together. Like sandpaper, sandstones usually have a rough, granular texture, but to really identify a sandstone you have to peer closely at its surface and look for individual sand grains. They are sedimentary rock consisting of sand or quartz grains cemented together, typically red, yellow, or brown in color. Sandstone was a popular building material from ancient times. 29. importance of sandstone​


Answer:

Sandstones are siliciclastic sedimentary rocks that consist mainly of sand-size grains (clast diameters from 2 to 1/16 millimetre) either bonded together by interstitial chemical cement or lithified into a cohesive rock by the compaction of the sand-size framework component together with any interstitial primary (detrital) and secondary (authigenic) finer-grained matrix component.


30. Sandstones shape and hardness


Rock formations that are primarily composed of sandstone usually allow percolation of water and other fluids and are porous enough to store large quantities, making them valuable aquifers and petroleum reservoirs. Fine-grained aquifers, such as sandstones, are better able to filter out pollutants from the surface than are rocks with cracks and crevices, such as limestone or other rocks fractured by seismic activity.
