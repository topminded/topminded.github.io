1. number of atom of Diphosphorous pentasulfide (P²H⁵)​


Answer:

14

Explanation:

The chemical formula for diphosphorus pentasulfide is P4S10, not P²H⁵.

To calculate the number of atoms in diphosphorus pentasulfide (P4S10), we can count the number of atoms for each element in the molecule and multiply it by the subscript.

In this case, there are 4 atoms of phosphorus (P) and 10 atoms of sulfur (S) in one molecule of diphosphorus pentasulfide. Therefore, the total number of atoms in one molecule of diphosphorus pentasulfide is:

4 atoms of P + 10 atoms of S = 14 atoms in total

So, there are 14 atoms in one molecule of diphosphorus pentasulfide (P4S10).

Answer:

Sugar

Explanation:

During photosynthesis, plants take in carbon dioxide (CO2) and water (H2O) from the air and soil. Within the plant cell, the water is oxidized, meaning it loses electrons, while the carbon dioxide is reduced, meaning it gains electrons. This transforms the water into oxygen and the carbon dioxide into glucose or known as simple sugar.

Also, plants don't release carbon dioxide during this process. Glucose is produced together with oxygen as they are the by-product of photosynthesis.
