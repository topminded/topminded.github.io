10. Discuss the fractal animals and their body plan


Rangeomorphs or fractal animals were strange, fractal beings living in the oceans roughly 575 million years ago. A recent 3D reconstruction shows that when fast-moving Cambrian creatures appeared the stationary creatures went extinct.

The primitive life forms were ideally suited to the Ediacaran Period's nutrient-rich, placid seas which lasted from about 635 million years ago to 541 million years ago. But the stationary creatures were no match for the rapid swimmers that emerged during the following Cambrian period, and the changing chemistry of seawater did not provide the rangeomorphs with the nutrients they needed to survive.

A formal rangeomorphic morphology model shows a fractal body plan with a self-similar, axial, apical, alternative branching. The resulting morphological reconstruction for 11 taxa reveals an adaptive radiation based on space-filling strategies in 3D.

The rangeomorph fractal body plan is shown to optimize the surface region, consistent with the water column (osmotrophy) taking up of diffusive nutrients.
