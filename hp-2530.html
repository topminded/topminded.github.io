29. what is HP case study​


Answer:

The HP case study refers to an analysis of the business strategies and practices of Hewlett-Packard (HP), a multinational technology company based in the United States. The case study typically covers HP's history, organizational structure, market positioning, product development, and competitive advantage, as well as its successes and failures over the years.

The HP case study is often used in business schools and management courses as an example of how a company can adapt to changing market conditions and technology trends. It provides insight into how a large, established company can maintain its competitive edge by innovating and diversifying its product lines, and by making strategic investments and acquisitions.
