4. Water is flowing in a fire hose with a velocity of 1.0 m/s and a pressure of 200000 Pa. At the nozzle the pressure decreases to atmospheric pressure (101300 Pa), there is no change in height. Use the Bernoulli equation to calculate the velocity of the water exiting the nozzle


Answer:

[tex]V_{2} = 1.094 m/s[/tex]

Explanation:

using Bernoulli's equation:

[tex]\frac{P_{1} }{pg} + \frac{V_{1} ^{2} }{2g} + z_{1} = \frac{P_{2} }{pg} + \frac{V_{2} ^{2} }{2g} + z_{2}[/tex]

where:

P = pressure

ρ = density of water

g = gravity

z = height

v = velocity

-------

no change in height:

[tex]\frac{P_{1} }{pg} + \frac{V_{1} ^{2} }{2g} = \frac{P_{2} }{pg} + \frac{V_{2} ^{2} }{2g}[/tex]

to solve the exit velocity:

[tex]V_{2} = {\sqrt{(\frac{P_{1} - P_{2} }{pg} + \frac{V_{1} ^{2} }{2g})(2g) } }[/tex]

[tex]V_{2} = {\sqrt{(\frac{200 - 101.3}{(1000)(9.81)} + \frac{1^{2} }{2(9.81)} )(2)(9.81)} }[/tex]

[tex]V_{2} = 1.094m/s[/tex]

26. Water is flowing in a fire hose with a velocity of\( 1.2\,m/s \) and a pressure of \(2521,000\,Pa \). At the nozzle, the pressure decreases to atmospheric pressure \( 2171,000\,Pa \), there is no change in height. Calculate the velocity of the water exiting the nozzle.​


Answer:

We can use Bernoulli's equation to solve this problem, which states that the sum of the pressure, kinetic energy per unit volume, and potential energy per unit volume of a fluid flowing along a streamline is constant. In this case, we can assume that the potential energy per unit volume is constant because there is no change in height. Therefore, we can write:

\begin{aligned} P_1 + \frac{1}{2}\rho v_1^2 &= P_2 + \frac{1}{2}\rho v_2^2 \ \frac{1}{2}\rho v_1^2 &= (P_2 - P_1) + \frac{1}{2}\rho v_2^2 \ v_2 &= \sqrt{\frac{2(P_2 - P_1)}{\rho} + v_1^2} \end{aligned}

where P_1P

1

is the pressure at the hose, v_1v

1

is the velocity of the water at the hose, P_2P

2

is the pressure at the nozzle, \rhoρ is the density of water, and v_2v

2

is the velocity of the water at the nozzle.

Substituting the given values into the equation, we get:

\begin{aligned} v_2 &= \sqrt{\frac{2((2171,000,Pa) - (2521,000,Pa))}{\rho} + (1.2,m/s)^2} \ &= \sqrt{\frac{2(-350,000,Pa)}{1000,kg/m^3} + 1.44,m^2/s^2} \ &= \sqrt{-700,m^2/s^2 + 1.44,m^2/s^2} \ &= \sqrt{0.74,m^2/s^2} \ &= 0.86,m/s \end{aligned}

Therefore, the velocity of the water exiting the nozzle is 0.86\,m/s0.86m/s.
