In domino what does the term downing mean

Downing means: it is knock or bring to the ground. One player starts the game by downing (playing the first tile) one of their tiles. This tile begins the line of play, which is a sequence of tiles in which neighboring tiles have corresponding, i.e. equal, values.

Setting, leading, downing, or presenting the first bone are all terms used to describe playing the first bone of a hand. Smacking down the bone is a term used by Dominoes fans to describe this technique. Following each hand, the bones are shuffled, and each player draws the amount of bones needed, which is usually seven. The game is played clockwise. Players must each play a bone with an end that fits one of the templates' open ends. Dominoes (or dominos) is a game in which rectangular "domino" tiles are used. A domino set, also known as a deck or pack, is made up of domino game pieces. The standard Sino-European domino set includes 28 dominoes, also known as bones, cards, tiles, tickets, stones, or spinners. Each domino is a rectangular tile with two square ends that are separated by a line. Each end has a number of spots (also known as pips, nips, or dobs) or is completely blank. The backs of the dominoes in a series are all the same, either blank or with a traditional pattern. A domino box is a generic gaming device that, like playing cards or dice, can be used to play a variety of games.

How to Play  

Tiles and suits

Domino tiles, also known as bones, are twice as long as they are wide, with a line dividing them into two squares in the middle. The value is determined by the number of spots or pips on either hand.The values in the most popular version (Double Six) range from zero or 0 (no pips) to 6. The average of the two numbers, i.e. the cumulative number of pips, is known as a tile's rank or weight, and a tile with more pips is heavier than a lighter tile with less pips.

Rules

The most common category of game is layout games, which are divided into two categories: blocking games and scoring games.

• The majority of domino games are blocking games, in which the aim is to empty one's hand while blocking the opponent's. Finally, a ranking can be calculated by counting the pips in the losing players' hands. • In scoring games, scoring is different and occurs more during game play, making it the primary goal. • 'Hector's Rules,' a common variant played mostly in Singapore, provides for playing double tiles on opponents' hands and rewards a bonus play of an additional tile directly after playing a double tile.

12th-Century The first Chinese dominoes made in the 12th-century were hand carved from animal bone, typically ox bones, and are called "Gwat Pai" ( meaning "bone tiles") in the North of China or "Goo Pai" in the more widespread Mandarin dialect. The black spots were made by drilling shallow holes into the bone that were then inlaid with pieces of thin ebony. The first dominoes that appeared in the UK were made by French prisoners-of-war who would make them from sheep and cow bones left over from their rations, and then sell them to supplement their pitiful allowances. Later, other sets were made by sailors to pass the time while on long voyages. So-called "vegetable ivory", made from the Tagua Nut, known as the "ivory nut", has been used for over two-hundred years by craftsmen to make dice, dominoes, and chess pieces. The "ivory nut" is close-grained and very hard, and its structure, colour, and grain is similar to that of mammal ivory, though slightly softer.

19th-Century By the mid 19th-century, European dominoes were still commonly manufactured from slim narrow pieces of bone, but now with an ebony wood back which was glued and then fixed to the bone with a brass pin (known as a spinner) through the centre of the tile. This development was probably due to a lack of suitable thick pieces of animal bone, requiring the ebony layer to strengthen the tile, which made it possible to stand dominoes on their edges. The first plastic called Bois Durci was invented in 1855 by the Frenchman Charles Lepage. He made a particular point of recommending it for the manufacture of dominoes, chessmen, etcy. Bois Durci was made from ebony or rosewood sawdust mixed with albumen taken from eggs or even blood. The sawdust was mixed and soaked in a mixture of the albumen and water, then dried and subjected to intense heat and pressure in a hydraulic press. In 1856 came the invention of the next manmade plastic material, first called Parkesine and now known as Xylonite or Celluloid. This synthetic material was invented in 1917 by L H Bakeland who developed the process of condensing phenols and formaldehyde that produced a hard resiliant type of plastic that could be easily moulded into everyday products.
