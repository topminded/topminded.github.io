24. What is the first book of aristotle that talks about the first comprehensive and programmatic study of virtue ethics?


Nicomachean Ethics is Aristotle's first book which contains a comprehensive explanation on virtue ethics

As we all know, Aristotle is among the very first few ancient philosophers who talks about virtue ethics. Also, there are many books that he has written, and Nicomachean Ethics is one of them. The book explains about the definition, characteristics, as well as how one can achieve happiness by living in virtue.

Virtue ethics is the term used to explain what constitute an action that is considered good. There are several traits that entail this term: the ability to say the truth (honesty), courage, and being just. If, say, one person, has the quality of those traits, then we can safely say that that person is a good person. However, before demonstrating those traits to the world, we must learn first how to love ourselves.
