1. Complex sugar that makes fungal cell wall


The complex sugar that makes up the fungal cell wall is called chitin.

Chitin is a complex carbohydrate that is also found the in the exoskeleton of some arthropods such as shrimp, millipedes, centipedes, etc. Basically, chitin is also a string of carbohydrates, a characteristic that it shares with cellulose, the carbohydrate found in the cell walls of plants.

How does chitin hold up its structure? The secret is found in the bonds that hold it together, called glycosidic bonds. These glycosidic bonds enable rings of glucose to string up together to form strings of chitin. This is responsible for the solid structure and insolubility of chitin in water.
