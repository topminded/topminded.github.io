Mood disorders are those in which the person experiences severe disturbances in mood and emotion. They include depressive disorders and bipolar and related disorders. Depressive disorders include major depressive disorder, which is characterized by episodes of profound sadness and loss of interest or pleasure in usual activities and other associated features, and persistent depressive disorder, which marked by a chronic state of sadness. Bipolar disorder is characterized by mood states that vacillate between sadness and euphoria; a diagnosis of bipolar disorder requires experiencing at least one manic episode, which is defined as a period of extreme euphoria, irritability, and increased activity.

Mood disorders appear to have a genetic component, with genetic factors playing a more prominent role in bipolar disorder than in depression. Both biological and psychological factors are important in the development of depression. People who suffer from mental health problems, especially mood disorders, are at heightened risk for suicide.

16. term used for disorders that disrupt thinking,feelings,moods,and behaviors and impair daily functioning​


Answer:

Mental Disorder

Answer:

Mental Disorders

Explanation:

Disorders that disrupt thinking, feelings, moods and behaviors that may impair daily functioning​ are referred to as mental disorders.

Mental disorders can be divided to two: psychiatric/psychological and neurological. Psychological or even psychiatric disorders tend to be caused by changes in mental states or state of the mind, but not necessarily a biological origin, meaning the brain-which falls under neurological disorders.

Some examples of psychological conditions are the following:

depressionanxiety disordersschizophreniaeating disordersaddictive behaviors

Neurological disorders on the other hand result from damage to the brain or changes in the structure of the brain. They almost always are accompanied by psychological disorders as well. Some examples are:

Alzheimer's diseasedementiamultiple sclerosisbrain tumors (benign or malignant)

These diseases are handled by specialists:

Psychologist: for mental health issues

Psychiatrist: for mental health issues with brain changes

Neurologist: for diseases involving the brain

26. What happens if the food chain is disrupted? What factors disrupt the balance of this species?Give ways on how to prevent this disruption​


A food chain can be defined as a network of links with a hierarchic structure. It usually starts with small prey animals or producer organisms like wood or grass and ends with predator species like bears.
The food chain can also end one step further with decomposers like bacteria or worms which would process the predator species once it dies, depending on the definition.
The proper functioning of the food chain is crucial for healthy development of species on our planet. However, there are several reasons why a food chain may become disrupted.
