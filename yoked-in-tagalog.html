Kinnor, ancient Hebrew lyre, the musical instrument of King David. According to the Roman Jewish historian Josephus (1st century ad), it resembled the Greek kithara (i.e., having broad arms of a piece with the boxlike neck), and kinnor was translated as “kithara” in both the Greek Old Testament and the Latin Bible.

25. why does jeremiah counsel the people of Jerusalem to accept the yoke of babylon?​


Answer:

Jeremiah was a prophet who lived during the time when the Babylonian empire was expanding its control over the Middle East. He counseled the people of Jerusalem to accept the yoke of Babylon because he believed that it was God's will. Jeremiah saw the Babylonian conquest as a divine punishment for the sins of the people of Jerusalem, who had turned away from God and were practicing idolatry, social injustice, and moral corruption.

Jeremiah believed that the Babylonian conquest was a necessary step in the process of the Israelites' spiritual and moral regeneration. He urged the people to submit to Babylonian rule and to avoid any attempts to rebel or resist, as he believed that such actions would only lead to greater suffering and destruction. He also promised that God would eventually deliver them from Babylonian captivity and restore them to their homeland, but only after a period of repentance and renewal.

In essence, Jeremiah believed that the Babylonian captivity was a necessary evil that would ultimately lead to the spiritual and moral redemption of the Israelites. By accepting the yoke of Babylon and submitting to God's will, the people of Jerusalem could begin the process of atonement and spiritual renewal.
