The vertex of the parabola is at (0, 0), the x-intercepts are (-3, 0) and (3, 0), and the y-intercept is (0, 8). The width of the archway at the base is 6 inches.

b. The general equation for a parabolic function is y = ax^2 + bx + c. To find the equation for this specific archway, we can use the vertex form, which is y = a(x - h)^2 + k, where (h, k) is the vertex. We know that the vertex is at (0, 0), so h = 0 and k = 0. We also know that the width of the archway at the base is 6 inches, so the x-intercepts are at (-3, 0) and (3, 0). Plugging these values into the vertex form, we get:

y = a(x - 0)^2 + 0

y = ax^2

To find the value of a, we can use the y-intercept, which is (0, 8). Plugging this point into the equation, we get:

8 = a(0)^2

a = 8

So the equation for the parabolic bag handle is:

y = 8x^2

c. To approximate the area under the curve of the parabolic bag handle, we can use a definite integral. Since the archway is symmetric around the y-axis, we can find the area of half the archway and then double it. The definite integral for the area of half the archway is:

A = 2 ∫[0, 3] 8x^2 dx

A = 2 (8/3)x^3 |[0, 3]

A = 2 (8/3)(27)

A = 144 square inches

So the approximate area under the curve of the parabolic bag handle is 144 square inches.
