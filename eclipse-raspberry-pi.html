23. Raspberries did not grow well this year. The raspberries did not grow well because we did not get much rain. Raspberry plants are placed in moderately lit areas all receiving the same amount of light each day. Each plant will receive different amount of water. The size of the berries will be recorded daily once the plants begin to produce berries. The raspberry plants receiving 1 cup of water daily grew to ½ inches in diameter and those receiving two cups of water grew to 3/4 inches in diameter. The raspberry plants receiving the most water grew the biggest. Therefore, the hypothesis is supported that this year's raspberries did not get enough rain. Questions: 1. What is the problem?_ 2. State the hypothesis 3. Describe the experiment used in the problem. 4. What are the presented data? 5. Give the conclusion drawn from the text.​


The Scientific Investigation

1. The problem in the given situation is: "Can the amount of water affect the growth of raspberries?"

       The problem in a scientific method refers to the question that the researcher or investigator would like to find out. The problem of a scientific method is essential as it is the main reason why one will conduct an experiment or scientific investigation. The problem should be stated clearly and well. It will be good also if the independent and dependent variables are established already in the problem. The independent variable is the factor being tested in the investigation while the dependent variable is the one that is being affected by the independent variable. The dependent variable is also the data to be gathered in the experiment. In the situation given, the independent variable is the amount of water the plant receives while the dependent variable is the size of the raspberries.

2. The hypothesis is: The raspberries did not grow well because they did not get much rain.

       The hypothesis refers to the tentative answer to the problem or question. This is also described as an educated or a scientific guess. This can be rejected or accepted depending on the results of the experiment.

3. The experiment conducted included preparing two set-ups or two plants of raspberries. Both set-ups were placed in the same location where they receive the same amount of light. The two plants, however, will receive different amounts of water. The first plant receives one cup of water daily and the other plant receives two cups of water. To gather data, the researcher measured the diameter of the berries. The size of the berries was measured daily starting once the plants started producing berries.

The experiment is part of the scientific investigation where the researcher finds answers to the problem by testing the hypothesis. The designed experiment should measure the relationship between the variables.

4. The presented data are:

   a. The Raspberries receiving one cup of water daily grew to 1/2 inches in diameter.

  b. The raspberries receiving two cups of water daily grew to 3/4 inches in diameter.

The data are the results of the experiments. After gathering the data, they will be subjected to analysis in order to come up with a conclusion.

5. The conclusion made is "The raspberry plants receiving the most water grew the biggest."

The conclusion is the answer already to the question. Here, we can tell whether the hypothesis is accepted or rejected. In order to come up with an appropriate conclusion, it is important that we gather and analyze well the data.
