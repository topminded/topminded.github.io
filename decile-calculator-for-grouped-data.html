There are several formulae in vogue to calculate decile, and this method is one of the simplest one where each decile is calculated by adding one to the number of data in the population, then divide the sum by ten and then finally multiply the result by the rank of the decile, i.e., 1 for D1, 2 for D2… 9 for D9.

27. How will you illustrate following measures of grouped of data percentile, deciles and quartiles?


This is how it's steps

Rank the data from lowest to highest.

Multiply the sample size by k/100 to find the depth of the kth percentile.

If the depth is a whole number, add 0.5. If the depth is not a whole number, round up to the next higher whole number.

The kth percentile is the value in the depth position.
