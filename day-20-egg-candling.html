12. 4. On Christmas, a shopkeeper needs 820 candles. If each packet contains 20 candles, how many packets he should buy?


DIVISION

[tex]\normalsize{\blue{\overline{\quad\quad\quad\quad\quad\quad\quad\quad\quad\quad\quad\quad\quad\quad\quad\quad\quad\quad\quad\quad\quad\quad\quad\quad\quad\quad\quad\quad\quad}}}[/tex]

On Christmas, a shopkeeper needs 820 candles. If each packet contains 20 candles, how many packets he should buy? 41 PACKETS

[tex]\large\underline{\mathbb{SOLUTION}:}[/tex]

Divide 820 by 20:

[tex]\begin{gathered} \sf{ \: \: \: \: \: \: \: \: \: \: \green{41}}\\ \sf{20 | \overline{\: \: \:820 \: }} \\ \sf{ - 0} \\ \sf{ \: \: \: \: \: \: \overline{8}2} \\ \sf{ \: \ - \underline{80}} \\ \sf{ \: \: \: \: \: \: \: \: \: \: 20} \\ \sf{ \: \: \: \: \: \ - \underline{20}} \\ \sf{ \: \: \: \: \: \: \: \: \: \: \: \ \red{0}} \\ \end{gathered}[/tex]

Hence, the shopkeeper needs to buy 41 packets of candles.

To check if our answer answer is true, use the arithmetic sequence formula aₙ = a₁ + (n - 1)d, where our first term (a₁) is 20, common difference(d) = 20, and the nth term (aₙ) = 41

[tex]\boxed{\sf{a_{n} = a_{1} + (n - 1)d}}[/tex][tex]\sf{a_{41} = 20 + (41 - 1)20}[/tex][tex]\sf{a_{41} = 20 + (40)20}[/tex][tex]\sf{a_{41} = 20 + 800}[/tex][tex]\sf{a_{41} = \green{820}}[/tex]

Hence, proved. The shopkeeper needs to buy 41 packets of candles.

22. Material candle leaves burning paper egg in the pot clothes Change that material undergo​


Answer:

Solution

Physical change :

A change in which a substance undergoes a change in its physical properties i,e shape, size, color, state, or appearance of the substance change is called a physical change, which is generally reversible in nature.

In these physical changes, the chemical composition remains intact and no new substance gets formed.

Chemical change :

A change in which the chemical properties of a substance get altered is called a chemical change.

The chemical change is also called as a chemical reaction which is generally Irreversible in nature.

In a chemical change, one or more new substances always get formed during the reaction.

Burning of paper and wood:

The burning of paper and wood is known as an example of a chemical change.

Because here, on heating paper or wood, ash is obtained which has a different chemical composition than that of paper or wood.

Also, the process is irreversible as we can't obtain paper again from ash.

Hence, the Burning of paper and wood is a chemical change.
