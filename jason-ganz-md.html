25. Jason is planting rows of corn. He needs 4/9 square feet. There are 8 square feet in Jason's backyard, how many rows of corn does Jason have?


Multiplication and Division of Fraction

In mathematics, multiplication of fraction is simple as multiplying/dividing numerator by numerator, then denominator by denominator.  For division of fraction, reciprocate the second fraction then multiply. Fraction is important in real life, it was applied in finance, computation of amount of liquid and portion of the subjects.  

Fraction is defined as a numerical quantity that is not a whole number (e.g., 1/3, 2/5).

To multiply fractions, follow these three steps:   Multiply the top numbers (the numerators).   Multiply the bottom numbers (the denominators).   Simplify the fraction if necessary. To divide fractions, follow these three steps:   Reciprocate the second fraction Change the division sign into multiplication Multiply the top numbers (the numerators).   Multiply the bottom numbers (the denominators).   Simplify the fraction if necessary

The given problem is “Jason is planting rows of corn. He needs 4/9 square feet. There are 8 square feet in Jason's backyard, how many rows of corn does Jason have?”

The given problem is an application of division of fraction. When each row  requires 4/9 square feet, so divide it from the total area of the backyard.

Solution

8 ÷  4/9

It can transform into 8/1 x 9/4 = 72/4

72/4 = 18

Answer

18 rows

26. calculate for the range and md 4,8,3,7,5,4,9,4,8,8​


Answer:

The median of the data set is 6.

Explanation:

The median is the middle number in a sorted list of numbers. So, to find the median, we need to place the numbers in value order and find the middle number.

Ordering the data from least to greatest, we get:

3   4   4   4   5   7   8   8   8   9   

As you can see, we do not have just one middle number but we have a pair of middle numbers, so the median is the average of these two numbers:

Median= 5 + 7 / 2 =6

The range of data set is 6.

Explanation:

The range is the difference between the highest and lowest values in the data set.

Ordering the data from least to greatest, we get:

3   4   4   4   5   7   8   8   8   9   

The lowest value is 3.

The highest value is 9.

The range = 9 - 3 = 6.
