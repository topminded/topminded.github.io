1. Ano ang opac? Paano gamitin ang opac?


Ang OPAC o Online Public Access Catalog ay catalog ng mga libro at iba pang mga materyales na nasa isang library. Noong unang panahon, card catalog ang gamit para dito. Ngunit sa panahon ng computer at internet, naging makabago na din ang pag-oorganisa ng datos ng mga materyales sa isang library. Pwedeng ang OPAC ay ma-aaccess lamang sa loob ng library, o pwede din na ito ay ma-aaccess sa labas gaya ng isang regular na website. Sa pamamagitan ng OPAC, mas mapapadali ang paghahanap ng libro.  

Karamihan sa mga library ngayon ay gumagamit na ng OPAC, pero may mga aklatan pa din na gumagamit ng card catalog. Mahalagang alam natin gamitin ang alinman sa mga paraan na ito.  

Ang paggamit dito ay para lang din sa mga search engines gaya ng Google.

DISCUSSION

Pancoast tumour, also known as superior sulcus tumour, refers to a type of cancer that arises in the lung apex and invades the surrounding soft tissues [1]. Superior sulcus tumours are usually non-small cell lung cancers, and the most common histology encountered in the superior sulcus tumours are squamous cell bronchogenic carcinomas, as it resulted to be in this case [3].

Even though the tumour arises from the lung, symptoms are usually, if present, rarely pulmonary (such as cough, dyspnoea, or chest pain). Instead, they are a result of a growth and invasion of the local structures, which may cause the classical Pancoast syndrome, only seen in 25% patients, and which consists of shoulder-arm pain (if they grow and invade the bone), and Horner´s syndrome (ipsilateral miosis, ptosis and anhidrosis due to invasion of the cervical sympathetic plexus) [1].

Chest X-ray is usually the first imaging technique performed in patients with a pancoast tumour. Unfortunately, because of their location in the lung apices, this cancer is usually very difficult to detect, as the tumour may be seen as a subtle soft-tissue mass that may be confused with benign apical pleural thickening if it isn´t very big. The presence of associated bone destruction may be difficult to ascertain on the basis of radiographs, so in many patients, a thoracic CT is required to confirm the presence of a tumour [2].

The main objectives of CT in patients with a superior sulcus tumour are to confirm the presence of a pulmonary mass, to allow accurate staging of the disease, and to guide the biopsy. In the superior sulcus tumour, CT is the best modality for depicting bone abnormalities adjacent to the primary mass, such as rib and vertebral body erosion, information that may influence the surgical approach taken and the extent of the resection [2].

The presence of adenopathies is associated with poorer prognosis, and they may occur in up to 20%, as it happened in this case [2].

The key to be guided to the final diagnosis on this case was the detection of a new asymmetric apical opacity. The fact that the mass was accompanied by bone destruction led the differential diagnosis to neoplastic causes such as a bone tumour invading the lung or a lung cancer (pancoast tumour) causing bone destruction [4].

14. What does the Opacity effect do?


Answer and Explanation

In Adobe Photoshop, the Opacity effect allows the user to controls how transparent a layer is gonna be. The transparency is determined by the value of 0 -100%. The layer is visibly "solid" at 100% while the layer below is being "blocked" or "covered". As the value goes below 100%, the transparency of the layer becomes noticeable and the Layer below becomes more visible. When it reaches 0%, the layer becomes invisible and the layer below is completely seen. When opacity is set to 100%, it is called "opaque".

Function

The Opacity effect is commonly used to create a smoke or "ghosting" effect. It can also be used to reduce the "weight" of the visual composition.

30. Paggamit ng Card Catalog at OPAC​


PAGGAMIT NG CARD CATALOG AT OPAC

Ang card catalog ay isang sistemang nakatutulong upang mapadali ang paghahanap ng mga libro sa silid-aralan. Ito ay koleksiyon ng tatlong uri ng card na tulad sa isang mahabang listahan ng lahat ng nilalaman ng aklatan. Ang mga card na ito ay nakaayos nang paalpabeto at nakalagay sa isang kabinet na may maliliit na sulong o drawer. Ang bawat card ay may call number na nagtuturo kung saan sa silid-aralan ito matatagpuan.

Card ng may-akda o author card. Ito ang ginagamit kung alam mo ang pangalan ng may-akda ng aklat na iyong hinahanap. Ang pangalan ng may-akda ay nakasulat sa itaas ng card tulad ng nasa larawan. Ang numero sa kaliwang itaas nabahagi ang call number at ito ang nagtuturo kung saang bahagi ng silid-aklatan matatagpuan ang aklat na hinahanap.

Card ng pamagat o title card. ito ang ginagamit kung alam mo ang titulo o pamagat ng aklat na iyong hinahanap. Sa title card, ang pamagat ay ang nakasulat sa itaas ng card.

Card ng paksa o subject card. Ginagamit ito kung hindi mo pa tiyak ang sangguniang hahanapin o kung naghahanap ka pa lamang ng mga Sangguniang maaari mong gamitin sa iyong pananaliksik. Ang paksa ang unang nakasulat sa itaas na bahagi ng subject card.

Sa kasalukuyang panahon, ang mga card na tulad nito ay napapalitan na ng mga programa sa Computer tulad ng OPAC (Online Public Access Catalog) kung saan mahahanap ang kailangang aklat sa ilang click lang. Kung konektado ang aklatan sa internet o naka-online ito ay maaari na ring mahanap ang aklat kahit nasa ibang lugar ang naghahanap nito.
