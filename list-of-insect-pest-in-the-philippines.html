Methods of controlling insect pests and diseases:

- Cultural control: This involves practices such as crop rotation, sanitation, and maintaining proper plant nutrition to prevent insect pest and disease infestations.

- Biological control: This involves the use of natural predators, parasites, or pathogens to control insect pests and diseases.

- Chemical control: This involves the use of pesticides or fungicides to control insect pests and diseases.

2. In my locality, farmers use a combination of cultural and chemical controls to manage insect pests and diseases. For instance, they practice crop rotation, intercropping, and pruning to minimize the spread of diseases. They also use pesticides to control insect pests, but they are careful to follow the recommended application rates and timing to minimize the negative impacts on the environment and human health.

Farmers also use integrated pest management (IPM) strategies, which involve monitoring for pest populations, using thresholds to determine when action is necessary, and selecting the most appropriate control method based on the specific pest and crop. This approach minimizes the use of chemicals and encourages the use of biological control agents when possible.

The use of these practices is important because it promotes sustainable agriculture and minimizes the negative impacts of insect pests and diseases on crop production, the environment, and human health.

28. natural enemies of insect pest​


The enemy of insect pest is FROG i think

Natural enemies of insect pests fall into three categories: general predatory insects, parasitic insects, and insect pathogens (fungi, bacteria, viruses, or nematodes).
1. Predatory insects- are usually much larger than their prey. They are generally voracious feeders that kill and eat a wide variety of insects as they grow and reproduce. Ground beetles, minute pirate bugs, lacewings, and ladybeetles are examples of predatory insects.
2. Insect parasites- also called parasitoids, are some of the most effective natural enemies, however, they are tiny and easily overlooked. Many of these are non-stinging wasps.
3. Insect pathogens- are a third type of natural enemy. Insect pathogens include fungi, bacteria, viruses, and nematodes that infect or attack insect pests, resulting in their death.
