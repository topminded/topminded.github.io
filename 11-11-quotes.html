interest = principal * rate * time

In this case, the principal is 25,000, the rate is 11%, and the time is 2 years. Therefore, the interest amount the entrepreneur will pay on the loan on the maturity date is:

interest = 25,000 * 0.11 * 2

interest = 5,500

So the entrepreneur will pay a total of 5,500 in interest on the loan.

To find out how much the entrepreneur will have to pay the bank at the end of the two years, you can use the formula:

total payment = principal + interest

In this case, the principal is 25,000 and the interest is 5,500, so the total payment the entrepreneur will have to make at the end of the two years is:

total payment = 25,000 + 5,500

total payment = 30,500

Therefore, the entrepreneur will have to pay a total of 30,500 at the end of the two years
