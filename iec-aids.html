27. why does ieces of wood thrown upward fall back to the ground? a. because gravity caused the piece of wood to fall.b because air caused the piece of wood to fallon the ground.c. because the weght of a piece of.wood .made itmove fasterd. because acceleration caused the piece of wood to fall on the ground​


Answer:

The correct answer is A.

Explanation:

Pieces of wood thrown upward fall back to the ground because of gravity. Gravity is the force that pulls all objects towards each other, and it is the reason why objects fall to the ground when they are thrown upward. As the piece of wood is thrown upward, it is acted upon by gravity, which causes it to slow down and eventually stop moving upward. Then, gravity continues to act on the wood, pulling it back down to the ground. The other options listed (B, C, and D) do not accurately describe the reason why the wood falls back to the ground.
