4. Digestive System and It's Disorders Direction: Give the common diseases or disorders of every parts of our digestive system.​


Answer:

1. Gastroesophageal reflux disease (GERD)

According to Everyday Health, GERD happens when stomach acids are allowed to back up and flow into the esophagus. While many people have occasional heartburn, patients with this digestive system disease usually have a burning sensation and chest pain after meals or at night at least twice per week. Some patients with GERD also notice bad breath, trouble swallowing and breathing difficulties.

2. Diverticulitis

This disorder happens when the small pouches lining the large intestine become infected and inflamed. According to Livestrong, diverticulitis causes severe lower left side abdominal pain, constipation, fever, and nausea.

3. Inflammatory Bowel Disease (IBD)

This is a disease of chronic swelling and inflammation in various parts of the digestive tract. There are two types of IBD, including Crohn’s Disease, which affects the digestive tract, and ulcerative colitis, which affects the large intestine. Abdominal pain and diarrhea are often symptoms of IBD, says Healthline, but some patients also experience fatigue, rectal bleeding, night sweats, decreased appetite, and problems with bowel movements.

SPECIAL: Doctor: You Can Stop Digestion Woes, Heartburn, Gas, Constipation, More

4. Celiac Disease

As many as 83 percent of people with celiac disease are unaware, says the National Foundation for Celiac Awareness. Celiac disease is a severe intolerance to gluten in foods, and consuming gluten will cause an autoimmune response with symptoms like diarrhea, vomiting, abdominal bloating, weight loss, and constipation.

5. Irritable Bowel Syndrome (IBS)

The cause of this digestive system disease isn’t well understood, and the symptoms can be confusing and vary widely from patient to patients. For instance, some experience diarrhea while others are constipated. Frequent stomach discomfort and abdominal bloating are also symptoms of IBS, says Everyday Health.

7. Types of Diseases in Digestive System​


Types of Diseases in Digestive System​

Digestive diseases are disorders of the digestive tract, which is sometimes called the gastrointestinal (GI) tract.  In digestion, food and drink are broken down into small parts (called nutrients) that the body can absorb and use as energy and building blocks for cells.  The digestive tract is made up of the esophagus (food tube), stomach, large and small intestines, liver, pancreas, and the gallbladder.

Digestive diseases include:

Gallstones, cholecystitis, and cholangitis Rectal problems, such as anal fissure, hemorrhoids, proctitis, and rectal prolapse Esophagus problems, such as stricture (narrowing) and achalasia and esophagitis Stomach problems, including gastritis, gastric ulcers usually caused by Helicobacter pylori infection and cancer Liver problems, such as hepatitis B or hepatitis C, cirrhosis, liver failure, and autoimmune and alcoholic hepatitis Pancreatitis and pancreatic pseudocyst Intestinal problems, such as polyps and cancer, infections, celiac disease, Crohn disease, ulcerative colitis, diverticulitis, malabsorption, short bowel syndrome, and intestinal ischemia Gastroesophageal reflux disease (GERD), peptic ulcer disease, and hiatal hernia
