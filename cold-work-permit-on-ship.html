Answer:

Energy should be transferred to an object in order to move it. Transferring energy can be in the form of force. This amount of energy transferred by the force to move an object is called work or work done. Thus, the relation between Work and Energy is direct. That is, the difference in the Kinetic energy of an object is work done by an object.

What is the Relation between Work and Energy?

Work and energy are directly proportional to each other. Work done by an object can be mathematically expressed as:

W=1/2mv 2f−1/2 mv 2i

Where,

W is the work done by object measured using Joules.

m is the mass of the object measured using kilograms.

vi is the initial velocity of an object measured using m/s.

vf is the final velocity of an object measured using m/s.

Thus, the work-energy theorem states that:

The net work done by the force on an object is equal to change in its kinetic energy.

26. 3. A ship with a mass of 20,000 T accelerates from 14 knots to 17 knots over a period of 30 minutes. If its main engine does 3.00 X 10 J of work on the ship, how far did the ship reach?​


Answer:

The ship traveled a distance of 11.76 km during the 30-minute acceleration period.

Step-by-step explanation:

To solve the problem, we can use the formula for work done:

[tex]\sf\qquad\dashrightarrow W = Fd[/tex]

where:

W is the work doneF is the force appliedd is the distance traveled

We can use the formula for kinetic energy to find the initial and final kinetic energies of the ship:

[tex]\sf\qquad\dashrightarrow KE = \dfrac{1}{2} mv^2[/tex]

where:

KE is the kinetic energym is the mass of the shipv is the velocity

The change in kinetic energy of the ship is:

[tex]\sf\qquad\dashrightarrow\Delta KE = \dfrac{1}{2} mv_{2}^2 - \dfrac{1}{2} mv_{1}^2[/tex]

where:

v2 is the final velocity of the shipv1 is the initial velocity of the ship

We can use the formula for work-energy theorem to relate the work done by the engine to the change in kinetic energy of the ship:

[tex]\sf\qquad\dashrightarrow W = \Delta KE[/tex]

Substituting the values given in the problem, we get:

[tex]\sf 3.00 \times 10^{10}\: J = \dfrac{1}{2} (20000 \times 1000) [(17 \times \dfrac{1852}{3600})^2 - (14 \times \dfrac{1852}{3600})^2][/tex]

Simplifying and solving for d, we get:

[tex]\sf:\implies \boxed{\bold{\:\:d = 11.76\: km\:\:}}\:\:\:\green{\checkmark}[/tex]

Therefore, the ship traveled a distance of 11.76 km during the 30-minute acceleration period.


27. What are the common flags used to hoist in a normal working condition of the ship?


FLAGS HOIST IN COMMON SHIP

Flag etiquette combines maritime law and custom (expectations of behaviour within the sea-faring community). Being unaware of your commitments could lead to disrespect at home or abroad by sending a signal you didn't mean to send or a fine for breaking the law. Flag etiquette and rules are important to many sailors. Only with the right flag, correctly positioned, can you give the correct message and a clear indication.

There are numerous types of flags flown on common ships. They are:

1. Country Flag

Must fly the Sunrise flag from sunrise to sunset when the ship is within the country's borders. The crew of a ship must maintain an inventory of country flags based on the ship's route and port calls.

Example: fly the Egyptian flag while passing the Suez Canal.

2. National Flag

The National (N-sign) flag is the flag that represents the port or country in which the ship is registered. When a ship enters a port, it must raise its anchor. Typically, it will be lifted as part of the ship. Therefore, the country flag and N sign flag can sometimes be distinct.

Example: Harbour of registry - NASSAU, so the ship has to lift up the Bahamas flag.

3. Signaling Flag

According to the international maritime code of signals, different colors and coded flags are assigned to the letters A through Z, the numerals 0 through 9, and the pendants used to respond.

Each letter has its own significance, and they are used to signal the situation/condition to those in proximity.

Example: Letter "B" bravo flag has to hoist when the ship is carrying out fuel/bunker operation.

4. Company Flag

For ships operating under a legal corporation, such as cargo ships, the flag must be flown. However, not required, and some ships hoisted when necessary.

Example: Msc, Mol, and Nyk flags.
