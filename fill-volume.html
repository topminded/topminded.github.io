12. 2. How many volume of spheres are used to fill a cylinder?​


Answer:

The number of spheres needed to fill a cylinder depends on the size of the spheres and the size of the cylinder.

Assuming that the spheres are all the same size and the cylinder is also uniform, the number of spheres needed to fill the cylinder can be calculated by dividing the volume of the cylinder by the volume of each sphere.

The formula for the volume of a cylinder is:

Vcylinder = πr^2h

where r is the radius of the cylinder and h is the height of the cylinder.

The formula for the volume of a sphere is:

Vsphere = 4/3πr^3

where r is the radius of the sphere.

To find the number of spheres needed to fill the cylinder, we need to divide the volume of the cylinder by the volume of each sphere:

Number of spheres = Vcylinder / Vsphere

= (πr^2h) / (4/3πr^3)

= (3h) / (4r)

Therefore, the number of spheres needed to fill a cylinder is equal to three times the height of the cylinder divided by four times the radius of the sphere

The number of spheres required to fill a cylinder depends on the sizes of the spheres and the cylinder. Assuming that the spheres are all identical and their diameters are smaller than the height and diameter of the cylinder, we can calculate the number of spheres required using the following steps:

1. Calculate the volume of one sphere using the formula:

V_sphere = (4/3) * π * r^3

where r is the radius of the sphere.

2. Calculate the volume of the cylinder using the formula:

V_cylinder = π * r^2 * h

where r is the radius of the cylinder and h is its height.

3. Divide the volume of the cylinder by the volume of one sphere to get the number of spheres required to fill the cylinder:

N_spheres = V_cylinder / V_sphere

So, for example, if the cylinder has a radius of 10 cm and a height of 20 cm, and the spheres have a diameter of 2 cm, then the radius of each sphere is 1 cm, and we can calculate:

1. The volume of one sphere:

V_sphere = (4/3) * π * (1 cm)^3
= 4.19 cm^3

2. The volume of the cylinder:

V_cylinder = π * (10 cm)^2 * 20 cm
= 6,283.19 cm^3

3. The number of spheres required:

N_spheres = V_cylinder / V_sphere
= 6,283.19 cm^3 / 4.19 cm^3
= 1,501.8

Therefore, we would need approximately 1,502 spheres to fill the cylinder in this example.
