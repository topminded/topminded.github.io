2. An aqueous solution was prepared by dissolving 5.1 g of solid NaOH in enough water to make 1800 mL of solution. What is the molarity of NaOH in the solution?


Molarity (M)Discussion:

Molarity is one of the commonly used units in chemistry. Molarity is defined as the number of moles of solute per liter of solution --- that is,

[tex]\boxed{\text{molarity} = \frac{\text{moles of solute}}{\text{volume of solution (L)}}}[/tex]

For example, a 1.46 [tex]M[/tex] glucose solution contains 1.46 mol of the solute (glucose) in 1 L of the solution. Of course, we do not always work with solution volumes of 1 L. Thus, a 500-mL solution containing 0.730 mol of glucose also has a concentration of 1.46 [tex]M.[/tex]

[tex]\\[/tex]

Problem:

An aqueous solution was prepared by dissolving 5.1 g of solid NaOH in enough water to make 1800 mL of solution. What is the molarity of NaOH in the solution?

Given:

[tex]\text{mass of solute = 5.1 g}[/tex]

[tex]\text{volume of solution = 1800 mL = 1.800 L}[/tex]

[tex]\text{solute = NaOH}[/tex]

Required:

[tex]\text{molarity}[/tex]

Solution:

Step 1: Calculate the molar mass of solute.

molar mass of solute =(22.99 g/mol × 1) + (16.00 g/mol × 1) + (1.008 g/mol × 1)

molar mass of solute = 39.998 g/mol

Step 2: Calculate the number of moles of solute.

[tex]\text{moles of solute} = \frac{\text{mass of solute}}{\text{molar mass of solute}}[/tex]

[tex]\text{moles of solute} = \frac{\text{5.1 g}}{\text{39.998 g/mol}}[/tex]

[tex]\text{moles of solute = 0.127506 mol}[/tex]

Step 3: Calculate the molarity of solution.

[tex]\text{molarity} = \frac{\text{moles of solute}}{\text{volume of solution (L)}}[/tex]

[tex]\text{molarity} = \frac{\text{0.127506 mol}}{\text{1.800 L}}[/tex]

[tex]\boxed{\text{molarity} = 0.071 \: M}[/tex]

4. 1. What is the mass % (in m) of a solution prepared by dissolving 30.0 g of NaOH in120.0 g of water?mass of solution =mass % (mm)=mass of NaOH*100 =mass of solution​


Percent by massDiscussion:

Percent by mass (also called percent by weight or weight percent) is the ratio of the mass of a solute to the mass of the solution, multiplied by 100. The formulas used in solving percent by mass are as follows:

[tex]\boxed{\text{percent by mass} = \frac{\text{mass of solute}}{\text{mass of solution}} × 100}[/tex]

[tex]\boxed{\text{percent by mass} = \frac{\text{mass of solute}}{\text{mass of solute + mass of solvent}} × 100}[/tex]

The percent by mass is a unitless number because it is a ratio of two similar quantities.

[tex]\\[/tex]

Problem:

What is the mass % of a solution prepared by dissolving 30.0 g of NaOH in 120.0 g of water?

Given:

[tex]\text{mass of solute = 30.0 g}[/tex]

[tex]\text{mass of solvent = 120.0 g}[/tex]

[tex]\text{solute} = \text{NaOH}[/tex]

[tex]\text{solvent} = \text{H}_{2}\text{O}[/tex]

Required:

[tex]\text{mass percent}[/tex]

Solution:

[tex]\text{mass percent} = \frac{\text{mass of solute}}{\text{mass of solute + mass of solvent}} × 100[/tex]

[tex]\text{masd percent} = \frac{\text{30.0 g}}{\text{30.0 g + 120.0 g}} × 100[/tex]

[tex]\boxed{\text{mass percent} = 20.0\%}[/tex]

8. 6.A student prepared 250.0 g solution of NaOH by dissolving 15.0 g of NaOH in water. If the density of the solution is 1.04 g/ml, calculate the following; (atomic masses; Na = 23, H = 1 amu, O = 16 amu). a.Molarity of the solution. b.Normality of the solution c.Molality of the solution. d.Mole fraction of KCl.


Answer:

a. Molarity of the solution

Molarity is defined as the number of moles of solute per liter of solution.

First, let's find the number of moles of NaOH in 15.0 g:

moles of NaOH = mass / molar mass = 15.0 g / 40.00 g/mol = 0.375 mol

Then, we can calculate the volume of the solution in liters:

volume of solution = mass / density = 250.0 g / 1.04 g/mL = 240.4 mL = 0.2404 L

Finally, we can calculate the molarity of the solution:

molarity = moles of NaOH / volume of solution = 0.375 mol / 0.2404 L ≈ 1.56 M

b. Normality of the solution

Normality is defined as the number of equivalents of solute per liter of solution.

For NaOH, the equivalent weight is the molar mass divided by the number of hydrogen ions that the compound can donate in an acid-base reaction, which is 1 for NaOH.

equivalent weight of NaOH = molar mass / number of equivalents = 40.00 g/mol / 1 = 40.00 g/equiv

The number of equivalents of NaOH in 15.0 g can be calculated as:

equivalents of NaOH = mass / equivalent weight = 15.0 g / 40.00 g/equiv = 0.375 equiv

Finally, we can calculate the normality of the solution:

normality = equivalents of NaOH / volume of solution = 0.375 equiv / 0.2404 L ≈ 1.56 N

Note that the molarity and normality of the solution are the same for NaOH because it donates only one equivalent per mole.

c. Molality of the solution

Molality is defined as the number of moles of solute per kilogram of solvent.

First, we need to calculate the mass of water in the solution:

mass of water = total mass - mass of NaOH = 250.0 g - 15.0 g = 235.0 g

Then, we can calculate the molality of the solution:

molality = moles of NaOH / mass of water in kg = 0.375 mol / 0.2350 kg = 1.60 mol/kg

d. Mole fraction of NaOH

The mole fraction of NaOH in the solution is the number of moles of NaOH divided by the total number of moles of all components in the solution.

First, we need to calculate the total number of moles in the solution. The moles of water can be calculated as:

moles of water = mass of water / molar mass of water = 235.0 g / 18.02 g/mol = 13.05 mol

The total number of moles in the solution is therefore:

total moles = moles of NaOH + moles of water = 0.375 mol + 13.05 mol = 13.43 mol

The mole fraction of NaOH can now be calculated as:

mole fraction of NaOH = moles of NaOH / total moles = 0.375 mol / 13.43 mol ≈ 0.028

16. a sodium hydroxide solution is prepared by dissolving 6.0 g NaOH in 1.00 L of solution. Assuming that 100% dissociation occurs, what is the pOH and the pH of this solution


✏️pH and pOH

[tex]\sf{\large{\red{SOLUTION:}}}[/tex]

Step 1: Calculate the molar mass of solute (NaOH).

Note that the molar masses of Na, O, and H are 22.99 g/mol, 16.00 g/mol, and 1.008 g/mol, respectively.

[tex]\begin{aligned} \text{molar mass of solute} & = \text{22.99 g/mol + 16.00 g/mol + 1.008 g/mol} \\ & = \text{40.00 g/mol} \end{aligned}[/tex]

Step 2: Calculate the number of moles of solute.

[tex]\begin{aligned} \text{moles of solute} & = \frac{\text{mass of solute}}{\text{molar mass of solute}} \\ & = \frac{\text{6.0 g}}{\text{40.00 g/mol}} \\ & = \text{0.15 mol} \end{aligned}[/tex]

Step 3: Calculate the molarity of solution.

[tex]\begin{aligned} \text{molarity} & = \frac{\text{moles of solute}}{\text{volume of solution}} \\ & = \frac{\text{0.15 mol}}{\text{1 L}} \\ & = \text{0.15 mol/L} \\ & = 0.15 \: M \end{aligned}[/tex]

Final Step: Calculate the pOH and pH of the solution.

Note that NaOH is a strong base. Its OH⁻ concentration is equal to the molarity of solution (0.15 M) since it has only one OH⁻ present. We can calculate its pOH directly using the formula.

• For pOH

[tex]\begin{aligned} \text{pOH} & = -\text{log} \: [\text{OH}^-] \\ & = -\text{log} \: (0.15) \\ & = \boxed{0.82} \end{aligned}[/tex]

• For pH

[tex]\begin{aligned} \text{pH} & = 14 - \text{pOH} \\ & = 14 - 0.82 \\ & = \boxed{13.18} \end{aligned}[/tex]

Hence, the pOH and the pH of the solution are 0.82 and 13.18, respectively.

b. Normality of the solution = 62.4 N

c. Molality of the solution = 1.60 mol/kg

Explanation:

Solution calculations for NaOH

a. Molarity of the solution:

First, we need to calculate the number of moles of NaOH present in the solution:

Number of moles of NaOH = mass of NaOH / molar mass of NaOH

= 15.0 g / 40.0 g/mol (molar mass of NaOH)

= 0.375 mol

Next, we need to calculate the volume of the solution. We can do this by dividing the mass of the solution by its density:

Volume of solution = mass of solution / density of solution

= 250.0 g / 1.04 g/mL

= 240.4 mL

Now, we can calculate the molarity of the solution:

Molarity = number of moles of solute / volume of solution (in liters)

= 0.375 mol / (240.4 mL / 1000 mL/L)

= 1.56 M

Therefore, the molarity of the solution is 1.56 M.

b. Normality of the solution:

The normality of the solution is the number of equivalent weights of the solute present in one liter of the solution. For NaOH, the equivalent weight is equal to its molar mass divided by the number of hydrogen ions it can donate (which is 1):

Equivalent weight of NaOH = molar mass of NaOH / number of H+ ions it donates

= 40.0 g/mol / 1

= 40.0 g/equiv

The normality of the solution can now be calculated as:

Normality = molarity x equivalent weight

= 1.56 M x 40.0 g/equiv

= 62.4 N

Therefore, the normality of the solution is 62.4 N.

c. Molality of the solution:

Molality is defined as the number of moles of solute per kilogram of solvent. In this case, water is the solvent.

The mass of water present in the solution can be calculated by subtracting the mass of NaOH from the mass of the solution:

Mass of water = Mass of solution - Mass of NaOH

= 250.0 g - 15.0 g

= 235.0 g

We can now calculate the molality of the solution:

Molality = number of moles of solute / mass of solvent (in kg)

= 0.375 mol / (235.0 g / 1000 g/kg)

= 1.60 mol/kg

Therefore, the molality of the solution is 1.60 mol/kg.
