11. a hurricane in the gulf of mexico travel 360 km in 15 hour what is the average speed of this hurricane​


Answer:

The average speed of this hurricane is 24 kph.

Explanation:

Let us analyze the give problem.

Given:

distance - 360 km

time - 15 hours

To find the average speed of the hurricane, we are going to use the formula:

s = d / t

s is the speed.

d is the distance.

t is the time.

Average speed can be found using the total distance travelled divided by the total time required to travel that distance.

Solution:

Substitute the values of distance and time.

s = d / t

= 360 km ÷ 15 h

= 24 kph

Final Answer:

The average speed of this hurricane is 24 kph.

Speed, Distance and Time

Speed

Speed is a measure of how quickly an object moves from one place to another. It is equal to the distance traveled divided by the time.

Speed Formula:

s = d/t

Units of speed show length or distance over time. Some of the units used are miles per hour (mi/hr or mph), kilometers per hour (km/hr or kph) and meters per second (m/s).

Distance

Distance is the total movement of an object without any regard to direction.

Distance Formula:

d = st

To solve for distance use the formula, speed times time.

Time

Time is the duration in which all things happen.

Time Formula:

t = d/s

To solve for time use the formula above, which means distance divided by speed.

15. Every fall there have hurricanes n the tropics. therefore, there will be hurricanes in the tropics this coming fall. ​


The type of reasoning demonstrated by the above sentence is deductive reasoning.

DEDUCTIVE REASONING

From the statement, move to a broad perspective, namely that hurricanes occur in the tropics every fall.

We infer that the same event will reoccur using these basic patterns of occurrence. Since based the reasoning on a generic truth, it is deductive. The argument in the example above is, therefore, deductive.

Deductive reasoning is a logical procedure wherein a conclusion is derived from the unity of many premises that are usually accepted as true. Sometimes, deductive reasoning is referred to as top-down logic. Deductive reasoning depends on establishing logical premises and basing a conclusion on them.

If the premises are true, then the conclusion must also be true, according to this line of reasoning. Logic-Compliant Inferential Reasoning Examples: Golden retrievers are canines. Hence they possess ears. Because the Dodge Charger is a racing car, it is required to exceed 80 mph.

The primary distinction between inductive and deductive thinking is that inductive reasoning seeks to develop a hypothesis, whereas deductive reasoning seeks to test an existing theory. Inductive thinking proceeds from particular observations to broad generalizations, whereas deductive reasoning proceeds in the other direction.

30. what is the effect of El Nino on both pacific hurricanes (typhoon) and Atlantic hurricanes? ​


Answer:

Hurricane formation requires that winds be fairly uniform throughout the atmosphere. In other words, hurricanes cannot form if the vertical wind shear is too high. El Niño produces stronger westerly wind at upper levels of the atmosphere across the tropical Atlantic than in normal non-El Niño seasons.

Explanation:

hope it helps:D

Answer:

El Niño produces stronger westerly wind at upper levels of the atmosphere across the tropical Atlantic than in normal non-El Niño seasons. This increases the total vertical wind shear, basically shearing the tops from developing storms before a healthy circulation can form. El Niño events generally suppress Atlantic hurricane activity so fewer hurricanes than normal form in the Atlantic during August to October, the peak of Atlantic hurricane season.
