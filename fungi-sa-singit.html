29. 1. What do the fungi look like when magnified? 2. Can you see where the fungi are reaching out to form new fungi?​


Answer:

1, The appearance of fungi under magnification depends on the specific type of fungus being observed. However, many fungi have unique structures that can be observed under a microscope, such as spores, hyphae, and fruiting bodies. These structures can vary in size, shape, and color, and can provide important clues for identifying different types of fungi.

2. In some cases, it may be possible to observe fungi reaching out to form new growth under a microscope. For example, some types of fungi produce specialized structures called rhizomorphs, which are bundles of hyphae that can extend and branch out in search of new resources. Additionally, some fungi produce specialized reproductive structures, such as sporangia or basidia, which can give rise to new fungal growth. However, observing these processes may require specialized equipment and techniques, and may not be possible for all types of fungi.
