10. When a 480 Hz. tuning fork and a piano key are struck together, four beats are heard. When a 470 Hz. tuning fork and the same piano key are struck, six beats are heard. What is the frequency of the piano key?


Answer:

The frequencies of the piano are:

a. 484 Hz at 4 beats frequency

b. 476 Hz at 6 beats frequency

Explanation:

In our case, we are to solve for the frequency of the piano in two different beat frequencies.

Beat frequency is the difference of two frequencies. The unit measurement for frequency is Hertz (Hz) or cycles per second.

To solve for it, we will use the formula for beat frequency.

[tex]f_{beat} = f_{2}-f_{1}[/tex]

where

[tex]f_{beat}[/tex]     is the number of beats per second or beat frequency

[tex]f_{2}[/tex]         is the second given frequency

[tex]f_{1}[/tex]         is the first given frequency

For the given information

scenario 1:

f1 = 480 Hz

f2 = ?

fbeat = 4 beats

scenario 2:

f1 = 470 Hz

f2 = ?

fbeat = 6 beats

Solving the problem

1. Let us solve for the frequency of the piano at scenario 1:

[tex]f_{beat} = f_{2}-f_{1}[/tex]

4 Hz = f2 - 480 Hz

Transpose 480 Hz to the left side of the equation, we have:

f2 = 484 Hz

2. Now, let us solve for the frequency of the piano at scenario 2:

[tex]f_{beat} = f_{2}-f_{1}[/tex]

6 Hz = f2 - 470 Hz

Transpose 470 Hz to the left side of the equation, we have:

f2 = 476 Hz

Therefore, the frequency of the piano is 484 Hz at 4 beats frequency and 476 Hz at 6 beats frequency.
