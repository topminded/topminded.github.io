20. fourier series of 1-x2 on the interval of [-2,2] about po sa fourier series kay kuya po ito ty po


SOLUTION:

Fourier series of a function [tex]f(x)[/tex] in the interval of [tex]-L\le \:x\le \:L[/tex] is define as:

[tex]\boxed{\bold{\:f\left(x\right)=A_0+\sum _{n=1}^{\infty \:}A_n\cdot \cos \left(\frac{n\pi x}{L}\right)+\sum _{n=1}^{\infty \:}B_n\cdot \sin \left(\frac{n\pi x}{L}\right)}}[/tex]

Whereas,

[tex]\rm{\:A_0=\frac{1}{2L}\cdot \int _{-L}^Lf\left(x\right)dx}[/tex] [tex]\rm{\:A_n=\frac{1}{L}\cdot \int _{-L}^Lf\left(x\right)\cos \left(\frac{n\pi x}{L}\right)dx,\:\quad \:n > 0}[/tex] [tex]\rm{\:B_n=\frac{1}{L}\cdot \int _{-L}^Lf\left(x\right)\sin \left(\frac{n\pi x}{L}\right)dx,\:\quad \:n > 0}[/tex]

By applying the Fourier Series, given function [tex]f(x) = 1-x^2[/tex] and [tex]L = 2[/tex]. I.e.,

[tex]\rm{A_0 = \frac{1}{2\cdot 2} \cdot \int^{2}_{-2} (1-x^2)dx}[/tex] [tex]\rm{A_n = \frac{1}{2} \cdot \int^{2}_{-2} (1-x^2)\cos \left(\frac{n \pi x}{2} \right) dx}[/tex] [tex]\rm{B_n = \frac{1}{2} \cdot \int^{2}_{-2} (1-x^2)\sin \left(\frac{n \pi x}{2} \right) dx}[/tex]

Substituting to the Fourier Series

[tex]=\frac{1}{2\cdot \:2}\cdot \int _{-2}^2\left(1-x^2\right)dx+\sum _{n=1}^{\infty \:}\frac{1}{2}\cdot \int _{-2}^2\left(1-x^2\right)\cos \left(\frac{n\pi x}{2}\right)dx\cdot \cos \left(\frac{n\pi x}{2}\right)+\sum _{n=1}^{\infty \:}\frac{1}{2}\cdot \int _{-2}^2\left(1-x^2\right)\sin \left(\frac{n\pi x}{2}\right)dx\cdot \sin \left(\frac{n\pi x}{2}\right)[/tex]

Now. we simplify the definite integrals inside of the Fourier Series.

First Definite Integral:

[tex]\rm{= \int^{2}_{-2} 1-x^2 dx} \\\\ \rm{= \int^2_{-2} 1 dx - \int^2_{-2} x^2 dx } \\\\ \rm{= 4 - \frac{16}{3}} \\\\ \rm{= - \frac{4}{3}}[/tex]

Second Definite Integral:

[tex]\rm{= \int^2_{-2}(1-x^2) \cos \left(\frac{n \pi x}{2}\right) dx} \\\\ \rm{= \int^2_{-2} \cos \left(\frac{n \pi x}{2}\right) - x^2 \cos \left(\frac{n \pi x}{2}\right) dx} \\\\\ \rm{= \int^2_{-2} \cos \left(\frac{n \pi x}{2}\right) dx - \int^2_{-2} x^2 \cos \left(\frac{n \pi x}{2}\right) dx} \\\\ \rm{= 0 - \frac{32(-1)^n}{\pi^2 n^2}} \\\\ \rm{= \frac{32(-1)^n}{\pi^2 n^2}}[/tex]

Third Definite Integral:

[tex]\rm{= \int^{2}_{-2} (1-x^2)\sin \left(\frac{n \pi x}{2} \right) dx} \\\\ = 0[/tex]

Now let's substitute this back to our Fourier Series.

[tex]\rm{=\frac{1}{2\cdot \:2}\left(-\frac{4}{3}\right)+\sum _{n=1}^{\infty \:}\frac{1}{2}\left(-\frac{32\left(-1\right)^n}{\pi ^2n^2}\right)\cos \left(\frac{n\pi x}{2}\right)+\sum _{n=1}^{\infty \:}\frac{1}{2}\cdot \:0\cdot \sin \left(\frac{n\pi x}{2}\right)} \\\\ \rm{ =-\frac{1}{3}+\sum _{n=1}^{\infty \:}-\frac{16\left(-1\right)^n\cos \left(\frac{\pi nx}{2}\right)}{\pi ^2n^2}}[/tex]
