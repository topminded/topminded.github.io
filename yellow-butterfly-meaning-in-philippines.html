1. Read the passage about butterflies. Butterflies with purple wings used to be dominant in an environment. Over a period of time, it was observed that yellow butterflies were becoming the dominant color. What is the most likely reason for the change in the butterfly population? A Birds ate most of the sick yellow butterflies, allowing the purple to reproduce more. B There were fewer yellow butterflies, so they were able to reproduce at a faster rate. C The yellow butterflies were not eaten by birds and were able to reproduce more. D The purple butterflies stopped reproducing and only the yellow butterflies reproduced.


Answer:

C The yellow butterflies were not eaten by birds and were able to reproduce more.

Explanation:

A is incorrect because if birds ate the yellow butterflies there would not be as many

B is incorrect because fast rates of reproduction occur when there are high number, not low

D is incorrect as it is unlikely the purple butterflies naturally stopped reproducing. There would have to be a huge environmental catastrophe to happen and if it did all butterflies would be affected, not just a specific colour

28. Among a species of butterflies, the color blue (B) is a dominant trait. Yellow color (b) is a recessive trait. If a pure blue butterfly (BB) mates with a pure yellow (bb), what will the offspring look like?


Answer:

All blue

Explanation:

In order to determine what the offspring will look or like or its phenotype, we must first get the possible genotypes by means of a Punnett square.

        b            b

B     Bb         Bb

B     Bb         Bb

Since the genotype of all offspring is Bb, having a dominant trait B indicates that the phenotype is blue. Therefore, all the offspring will be blue.
