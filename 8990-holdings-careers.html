6. Which career professionals are required to hold at least a bachelor's degree? Check all that apply. Sound Technicians Music Directors Broadcast Analysts Actors Etchers Reporters


Career professionals that requires a Bachelor's degree.

The term Bachelor's Degree or Baccalaureate is a title given to a student who has finished a full course of study which traditionally lasts in a span of four years but it could take as long as seven years depending on the field of study. This type of degree often serves as the basic prerequisite for anyone entering the employment world. One could find many jobs that require a bachelor's degree in anything. These days, many students checks the list of highest paid bachelor degree jobs regularly when determining their choice of field to study.

Sound engineering technicians are people working in the technical aspect of music such as process of recording a song. They are often required to have a sound engineering degree. One may obtain this degree in a regular University that offers this course, or in specialized audio engineering schools. Employers often choose people who holds a degree as this job involves advanced technical knowledge that may affect an entire music production. Thus, a certification is very much necessary to to prove the basic competency of a person seeking to have this kind of job.


Music directors are people that lead musical groups, concerts, orchestras and many more. They are also promoters that attends several events to market the musicians. In a performing arts that is of a bigger scale such as conducting an orchestra, one would need a Master's degree, some would go further as to having a Ph.D, both titles higher than a Bachelor's degree. However, if one is seeking employment on an average scale, such as being a school music director or a church music director, employers would accept people who holds at least a Bachelor's degree in this field.


Broadcast Analysts, reporters and correspondents are people working to deliver news to the public in a form of a live TV broadcast, or in magazines, radios and websites. This career is required to have a Bachelor's degree in Journalism or in any related field if one is seeking employment in big media markets such as CNN, or BBC as a news anchor because this does not just involve putting many information in one cohesive story, but also organizing panel discussions and interviews. However, in smaller media outlets, it is possible to be accepted even without obtaining a Bachelor's degree given that one has a very good significant internship or experience in journalism. This job requires one to be extensively proficient in language and writing.


Actors are people performing acts on TV, also called a celebrity, theatrical and plays or  in the streets. One does not need to have a Bachelor's degree in drama or performing arts if the aim is to be a TV actor because this craft involves a natural flair in acting. One may be also be mentored or trained by professionals who have been in the field for a long time. However, it is also good to note that many actors were discovered in their school while still studying this specific craft. Holding a Bachelor's degree would not guarantee a person success in an audition for a role, but studying this could give a student potential connections to people working inside the industry which in the end, may help for a career launch.


Finally, Etchers or engravers are people who practices etching, a traditional process of using strong acid and chemicals to cut and engrave design in metals, glass and plastic to name a few. There are several universities that offers degree for this craft. However, many people these days who are in this career are often self taught or they were taught by a mentor with a good amount of experience in this field. Although this involves a technical aspect, but having a good portfolio with eye-catching designs are very much preferred by employers and having a good sense of style and creativity is best when it is from a natural talent than someone who has this as a skill.  

To wrap it up, Sound technicians, music directors, broadcast analysts and reports are career professions where a Bachelor's degree or a higher title is needed to obtain a successful job slot.

18. PERSONAL HEALTH CAREER PLANCAREER EXPLORATIONHealth Careers-engineer-police-medtech-nursingDescription Of The Health Career----​


Certainly! Here are brief descriptions of the health careers for engineer, police, medtech, and nursing:

1. Health Careers for Engineer: Biomedical Engineer

Biomedical engineering combines biology, medicine, and engineering to create new technologies and develop innovative applications that improve human health, such as prosthetics, biomedical imaging, and medical devices.

2. Health Careers for Police: Forensic Pathologist

Forensic pathologists are medical doctors who specialize in investigating the cause of death in cases of sudden, unexpected, or violent deaths. They work closely with law enforcement agencies to help solve crimes by conducting autopsies and analyzing evidence.

3. Health Careers for Medtech: Medical Technologist

Medical technologists are responsible for running complex medical tests and analyzing the results. They work in hospitals, clinics, and laboratories, using specialized equipment to study cells, tissues, and bodily fluids to diagnose diseases and monitor treatment.

4. Health Careers for Nursing: Nurse Practitioner

A nurse practitioner is a registered nurse who holds an advanced degree in nursing and is licensed to diagnose and treat patients. They work in a variety of healthcare settings, from primary care clinics to hospitals to nursing homes, and provide a range of services, including physical exams, medication management, and patient education.
