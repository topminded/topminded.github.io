8. Provide three (3) scenarios that show how the Ultrasonic Sensor can be useful. Explain why the Ultrasonic Sensor applies to these scenarios


Answer:

Here are three scenarios where ultrasonic sensors can be useful:

Autonomous robots and vehicles: Ultrasonic sensors can be used in autonomous robots and vehicles to help them navigate their surroundings and avoid obstacles. These sensors emit high-frequency sound waves and then measure the time it takes for the sound waves to bounce back. By using this data, the robot or vehicle can create a map of its surroundings and determine where obstacles are located.Parking sensors: Ultrasonic sensors are commonly used in parking sensors to help drivers park their vehicles safely. These sensors are typically mounted on the rear bumper of a vehicle and emit sound waves to detect the distance between the vehicle and nearby obstacles. When the vehicle gets too close to an obstacle, the sensor sends a signal to an alarm, which alerts the driver to stop.Level measurement: Ultrasonic sensors can be used to measure the level of liquids in tanks and containers. The sensor is mounted on the top of the tank and emits a sound wave that travels through the liquid and reflects off the surface of the liquid. The sensor then calculates the distance between the sensor and the liquid and uses this data to determine the level of the liquid in the tank.

In all of these scenarios, ultrasonic sensors are useful because they are non-invasive and can accurately detect the distance between an object and the sensor. Additionally, ultrasonic sensors are often relatively inexpensive and easy to install, making them a popular choice for a wide range of applications.

Answer:

Infrasonic waves

Elephant herds can communicate and synchronise their migrations even though they may be out of sight and separated by up to 20km. They do this by "speaking" and hearing with infrasonic waves which we cannot hear but can bend around obstacles rather than being absorbed like normal sound.

They do this by "speaking" and hearing with infrasonic waves which we cannot hear but can bend around obstacles rather than being absorbed like normal sound.Infrasonic wave pulses are also produced by a number of geophysical processes such as avalanches, earthquakes and various explosions, geomagnetic variations, meteors, ocean waves, severe weather and volcanos. Early detection of infrasonic waves that signify dangerous situations may provide warnings to nearby populations.

Ultrasonic Transducers

Ultrasonic transducers are used to produce and detect ultrasonic waves ( >20kHz). An ultrasonic transducer is a device that changes its physical size when an electric potential is applied to it. As it shrinks and swells in size it sends out compression waves into the medium around it and they have the same frequency as the applied electric potential. To be effective, the transducer (often a piece of ceramic material) needs to vibrate strongly (i.e. resonate). To do this it has to be at least a half a wavelength thick.
