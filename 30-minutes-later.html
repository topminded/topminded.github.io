16. You noticed that a plant in your garden began to wilt because you forgot to water it. you water the plant. about 30 minutes later you discover that the plant is standing erect again how is this observation related to osmosis?


Good Day...

Plants transpires water to the atmosphere and lessen the concentration of water in the system. Plants will die if no action is taken like watering the plants. Watering the plants helps the plant recover from wilting.

Transpiration lessen the concentration of water in plants causing the high concentration of water in the soil (by watering the plant) passes the semipermeable membrane of the plant roots since water concentration in plant is less. The movement of water from higher concentration to lower concentration is the process called osmosis.

27. You noticed that a plant in your garden began to wilt because you forgot to water it. you water the plant. about 30 minutes later you discover that the plant is standing erect again. This could be explained by what phenomenon? Why do you think so?


Answer:

This could be explained in a process known as transpiration. Many nonwoody plants rely almost exclusively on water pressure, or turgor, within their cells to keep them erect. However, plants are constantly losing water through small openings in their leaves (called stomata) in a process known as transpiration. While transpiration is vital for photosynthesis and helps transport nutrients from the roots to the rest of the plant, the vast majority of the water absorbed by the roots is lost through this process.
