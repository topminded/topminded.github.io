11. 11. A type of geothermal heat pump wherein underground pipes circulate liquid that is heated or cooled by the earth? a. flash steam power plant b. binary cycle power plant c. open loop system d. closed loop system​


The type of geothermal heat pump wherein underground pipes circulate liquid that is heated or cooled by the earth is d. closed loop system.

In a closed loop geothermal heat pump system, a continuous loop of pipes is buried underground and filled with a heat transfer fluid, such as water mixed with antifreeze. The fluid circulates through the loop and exchanges heat with the earth, which remains at a relatively constant temperature year-round. The heated or cooled fluid is then pumped back to the heat pump unit inside the building, where it is used to heat or cool the indoor space. This closed loop system is different from an open loop system, which uses groundwater directly as the heat exchange fluid, and from binary and flash steam power plants, which generate electricity using high temperature geothermal steam.

30. Create an short essay about closed loop- open loop?


Answer:

Closed-loop and open-loop are terms commonly used in control systems engineering, which refers to the methods used to control and regulate systems. The main difference between these two methods lies in the feedback mechanism used to adjust the system.

An open-loop system operates without any feedback mechanism. The system's input is predetermined, and the output is produced based on a fixed set of rules or equations. The system's output is not compared to the desired output, so there is no corrective action taken. This type of system is commonly used in simple applications, such as turning on a light switch. Once the switch is turned on, the light will remain on until it is manually turned off, regardless of the room's lighting conditions.

On the other hand, a closed-loop system employs a feedback mechanism to adjust the system's output based on the desired output. The feedback mechanism measures the system's output and compares it to the desired output. If there is a discrepancy, the system adjusts its output to bring it closer to the desired output. For example, a thermostat is a closed-loop system that monitors the room temperature and adjusts the heating or cooling system to maintain the desired temperature.

Closed-loop systems are generally more complex than open-loop systems and require more sensors and controllers to operate effectively. However, they are also more accurate and reliable as they can adjust to changes in the system's environment. They are commonly used in applications where accuracy and precision are critical, such as in aerospace and medical devices.

In summary, open-loop systems are simpler and do not employ feedback mechanisms to adjust the system output, while closed-loop systems use feedback mechanisms to adjust the system output based on the desired output. While open-loop systems are suitable for simple applications, closed-loop systems are generally more accurate and reliable and are used in more complex applications where precision and accuracy are critical.

Answer:

Closed loop and open loop systems are two different types of control systems. In a closed loop system, feedback is used to adjust the output of the system in real-time. This means that the system is constantly monitoring itself and making adjustments as needed to maintain stability and accuracy.

On the other hand, an open loop system does not use feedback to adjust the output. Instead, it relies on predefined inputs to produce a fixed output. This makes these systems less flexible and more prone to errors, and they are generally used in simpler applications.

Both closed loop and open loop systems have their advantages and disadvantages. Closed loop systems are more complex and expensive to implement, but they offer greater accuracy and flexibility. Open loop systems are simpler and less expensive, but they are less accurate and less adaptable to changing conditions.

In conclusion, the choice between closed loop and open loop systems depends on the specific requirements of the application. While closed loop systems offer greater flexibility and accuracy, they may not be necessary for simpler applications that can be adequately handled by open loop systems.
