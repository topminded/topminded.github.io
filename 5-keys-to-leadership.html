15. what are the most important keys to effective leadership (lamb, Mckee,2004 and explain each ​


Answer:

According to Lamb and McKee (2004), there are several key factors that contribute to effective leadership. These include:

Vision: A clear and inspiring vision for the organization or team is essential for effective leadership. A leader must be able to articulate their vision in a way that motivates and inspires their team to work towards a common goal.

Communication: Effective communication is critical for successful leadership. A leader must be able to listen actively to their team, understand their needs, and communicate effectively to provide direction and feedback.

Emotional Intelligence: Emotional intelligence refers to the ability to understand and manage one's own emotions, as well as the emotions of others. Effective leaders possess high levels of emotional intelligence, which allows them to build strong relationships with their team, motivate and inspire them, and manage conflicts effectively.

Strategic Thinking: A leader must be able to think strategically and make decisions that align with the organization's goals and objectives. They must be able to analyze complex situations, identify opportunities and challenges, and make informed decisions that benefit the team and the organization as a whole.

Accountability: Effective leaders hold themselves and their team accountable for their actions and decisions. They take responsibility for their mistakes, learn from them, and hold others accountable for their performance as well.

Adaptability: Leaders must be able to adapt to changing situations and be flexible in their approach. They must be able to adjust their plans and strategies based on new information or changing circumstances.

Overall, effective leadership requires a combination of these key factors, along with a willingness to learn and grow as a leader. By mastering these skills, leaders can inspire and motivate their team to achieve great things and drive success for the organization.

30. differentiate leadership of yesterday to the leadership of today in the context of political leadership structure. answer this please​


Answer:

Think your job has changed significantly over the past decade or two? Compound that with the complexity of leading an entire organization!

Previous generations of leaders could at least count on a reasonably stable world, where change unfolded at a much slower pace. These days, the past is increasingly less predictive, the future is almost unimaginable, and the present exists for about a nano-second.

From an employee perspective, today’s leaders have to manage multiple generations of workers with values, interests, and needs that often conflict. From a customer perspective, today’s leaders face great expectations and less certainty than ever. “Good enough” doesn’t even come close anymore. We have an enormous number of choices, less tolerance, more self-interest, and a dramatically different definition of customer satisfaction and loyalty.

Put these all together and you get a huge chasm between the old leadership style of administrating and directing versus the new model of guiding and inspiring.

Not too long ago, leaders basically had to figure out what to do and then tell people what, when, where, and how to do it. Today’s managers and leaders face a whole new set of expectations in the way they motivate the people who work with or follow them.
