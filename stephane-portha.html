Integrals

In mathematics, the term "integral" can refer to a variety of ideas. Summing infinitesimal parts to discover the content of a continuous region is the most prevalent meaning of the fundamental object of calculus. Values that always accept integer values (e.g., integral embedding, integral graph), mathematical objects for which integers provide basic examples (e.g., integral domain), and specific values of an equation are all examples of other applications of "integral" (e.g., integral curve),

Integrals are mathematical objects that can be viewed as areas or generalizations of areas in calculus. The fundamental objects of calculus are integrals and derivatives. Antiderivative and primal are two more terms for integral. Integration is the process of computing an integral.
