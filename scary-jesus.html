5. why ia apo compared to the"scary"jesus in the hallway?what do they same to have in common?


The Apo on the wall and the scary Jesus in the hallway both seem to always know what the kid is doing.


It says in the last 5 lines of the poem that, "That photo of Apo on the wall was already looking at me around, his eyes following me like he was that scary Jesus in the hallway, saying I know what you're doing."


If it was not clear, the father of the kid is a military man, and the picture of the wall is of then president and dictator, Ferdinand Marcos.


The poem is about Martial Law, and the comparison comes from how everybody used to be watched during that time.


The last line, "I know what you're doing," means more than being watched. It is more of a threat. Everyone had to be very cautious because the military, and other informants were everywhere. This is compared to how God sees all, and we should be mindful of our actions.


Aside from that, God is all-powerful. He can do anything to us. In the same way, during Martial Law, anything could be done to the citizens for any random reason.


It's often said that God is omnipotent (all-powerful), omniscient (all-knowing), and omnipresent (all-present). I think the poem makes the point that during Martial Law, Marcos was also all of those.


For the kid, though, I think they both gave him a feeling of unease. And this was the probably the feeling of the citizens at the time, uneasy and scared.


Hope this helps!
