8. A singular subject takes singular verb ; plural subject takes plural verb​


Explanation:

Yes, that is correct. In English grammar, subject-verb agreement is an important rule that states that a singular subject should be paired with a singular verb, while a plural subject should be paired with a plural verb. This ensures grammatical correctness and clarity in written and spoken language. Here are some examples:

Singular subject with singular verb: The dog runs in the park.

Plural subject with plural verb: The dogs run in the park.

Singular subject with plural verb (incorrect): The dog run in the park.

Plural subject with singular verb (incorrect): The dogs runs in the park.

By following the subject-verb agreement rule, we can avoid these types of errors and make our writing and speech more accurate and effective.

14. Singular subjects require singular verbs. Plural subjects require plural verbs.


Answer:

In any sentence, it is important that the subject and verb agree. A singular subject must take a singular verb so that they agree. If a subject is in the third person, the present singular verb is most always followed by –s or –es. Plural subjects must take plural verbs in order to agree.
