7. The town of Brookfield sent out a survey to its residents to determine what new landmark they should build in the City Center. Of the residents who were surveyed 2/5, of the residents voted for a park, 1/4 of the residents voted for a garden, and the remaining residents voted for a zoo. If a total of 14,000 residents were surveyed, how many residents voted for a zoo?


The proportion of residents who voted for a park is 2/5, and the proportion who voted for a garden is 1/4. Therefore, the proportion of residents who voted for a zoo is:

1 - (2/5 + 1/4) = 1 - (8/20 + 5/20) = 1 - (13/20) = 7/20

If 7/20 of the residents voted for a zoo, and a total of 14,000 residents were surveyed, the number of residents who voted for a zoo is:

(7/20) x 14,000 = 4,900

Therefore, 4,900 residents voted for a zoo.

12. From a point, 80 m from the base of a tower, the angle of elevation to the top of the tower is 28°. How tall is the tower?


Let the height of the tower be x.

We can solve this problem using one of the trigonometric ratios (SohCahToa). Since the missing value is opposite to the angle, and the adjacent length is given, we can use tangent.

tan θ = opposite/adjacent

tan 28˚ = x/80m

(tan 28˚)(80m) = x

x = 42.53675453...m

x ≈ 42.54 meters

21. The angle of elevation from a car to a tower is 60°. The tower is 150 ft tall. How far is the car from the tower?​


Answer:

50√3 m

Step-by-step explanation:

The point from the car to the tower represents the leg adjacent to the angle of elevation. The height of the tower represents the leg opposite to the angle of elevation.

We are finding the point from the car to the tower and the opposite and adjacent sides are given, so we will use the tangent function.

tan θ = o / a

tan 60 = 150 / a

a tan 60 = 150

a tan 60 / tan 60 = 150 / tan 60

a ≈ 50√3 m
