4. These are floating plastic lanes marker used to separate the lanes turbulence and serves as a guide for the swimmers to swim in a straight line​


The floating plastic lanes marker you are referring to are commonly known as lane ropes. They are an essential part of swimming competitions and training as they serve as a guide for swimmers to swim in a straight line and separate the lanes to minimize turbulence. Lane ropes are typically made of a series of plastic floats connected by a flexible cable, which allows them to be easily adjusted for different pool sizes and configurations. The floats are usually colored in alternating patterns to help swimmers identify their lane and maintain their course during a race or training session. Overall, lane ropes play an important role in ensuring fair and safe swimming conditions for athletes.

7. The water fountain in Circuit City, Makati are designed to spray a jet of water with a laminar flow at an angle of sixty degrees from a distance of 1.2 meters. How high are the jets expected to reach? Give your final answer in meters? ​


To find the height that the water jets are expected to reach, we need to use the formula for the range of a projectile, which is:

range = (initial velocity * time of flight) * sin(2 * launch angle)

where the time of flight is the time it takes for the water to reach the highest point in its trajectory, and the initial velocity is the speed at which the water is being propelled from the fountain.

To solve for the time of flight, we can use the equation:

time of flight = (2 * initial velocity * sin(launch angle)) / acceleration due to gravity

where the acceleration due to gravity is approximately 9.8 m/s^2.

Plugging in the given values, we get:

time of flight = (2 * (1.2 m/s) * sin(60 degrees)) / 9.8 m/s^2

time of flight = 0.244 seconds

Now that we have the time of flight, we can plug it back into the range formula to solve for the range, which is the height that the water jets are expected to reach:

range = (1.2 m/s * 0.244 s) * sin(2 * 60 degrees)

range = 0.29 meters

So the water jets are expected to reach a height of approximately 0.29 meters.
