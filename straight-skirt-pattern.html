11. A lao women’s ankle-long skirt and pattern are unique to each skirt


The ankle-long skirt that Lao women (and Thai women as well) is called Sihn.

Laos, just like many countries in Southeast Asia, is composed of many ethnic groups. The Sihn, which is a tube skirt, is part of the country’s national garment, as such, it is one of the symbols of Laos’ national and cultural identity. The patterns often use religious imagery and other traditional symbolic motifs like Giant, Bird, Naga, Lions, and many more.

For example in the Tai Daeng ethnic group, small lion motifs and snake skin can be found in their Sihns. In the Tai Dam ethnic group, bird motifs are abundant. Square of flowers is frequent motif from the Tai Lue ethnic group.

Sihns are acceptable formal garments that women can wear in professional settings and formal functions. Signs can be made from different textiles, depending on what is available.
