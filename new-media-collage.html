24. what is collage, decollage, graffiti, land art, digital arts,mixed media and printmaking​


Explanation:

collage: a piece of art made by sticking various different materials such as photographs and pieces of paper or fabric on to a backing.

decollage:Décollage is the technique of removing, cutting, or tearing away sections of an original image. The French word means to take off, become unstuck or become unglued.

graffiti:writing or drawings scribbled, scratched, or sprayed illicitly on a wall or other surface in a public place.

land art:Land art, variously known as Earth art, environmental art, and Earthworks, is an art movement that emerged in the 1960s and 1970s, largely associated with Great Britain and the United States but also includes examples from many countries.

digital art: Digital art is an artistic work or practice that uses digital technology as part of the creative or presentation process. Since the 1960s, various names have been used to describe the process, including computer art and multimedia art

mixed media: In visual art, mixed media is an artwork in which more than one medium or material has been employed.

printmaking: Printmaking is an artistic process based on the principle of transferring images from a matrix onto another surface, most often paper or fabric. ... The design is created on the matrix by working its flat surface with either tools or chemicals.

Title: The Rising Trend of Digital Detox and Its Importance in Modern Society

In this fast-paced digital era, people are constantly bombarded with an overwhelming amount of information from various sources, including social media, news websites, and email inboxes. As a result, many individuals are experiencing burnout and stress, which can lead to mental health issues and decreased productivity. To combat this, a new trend called digital detox has emerged, and it is becoming increasingly popular in modern society.

Digital detox is the process of disconnecting from technology and the digital world for a set period. This can involve turning off all electronic devices, abstaining from social media, and avoiding any technology-related activities. By doing so, individuals can clear their minds and reset their mental health, leading to increased productivity, reduced stress levels, and a better work-life balance.

The importance of digital detox cannot be overstated, especially in today's society, where technology has become an integral part of everyday life. People are constantly checking their phones, emails, and social media accounts, even during their free time, which can lead to a feeling of being "always on." This can result in burnout, which can negatively impact their personal and professional lives.

Digital detox can also help individuals to establish a healthier relationship with technology. By taking a break from technology, individuals can better understand their dependence on it and identify areas where they need to limit their usage. They can then establish healthy habits around technology use, leading to better mental and emotional well-being.
