11. What is the enzymatic relationship of gluconeogenesis to glycolysis?


Answer:

The main difference between glycolysis and gluconeogenesis is in their basic function: one depletes existing glucose, while other replenishes it from both organic (carbon-containing) and inorganic (carbon-free) molecules. This makes glycolysis a catabolic process of metabolism, while gluconeogenesis is anabolic.

16. What is the clinical significance of performing BUN and creatinine tests


The clinical significance of performing BUN and creatinine test is to find out whether the kidneys are functioning as they should. BUN stands for blood urea nitrogen and it measures the amount of urea in the blood which comes from the liver. Creatinine is a waste product of broken down creatine from the muscles. High levels of these two indicate a problem with the kidney or an indication that the dialysis is not effective. A doctor will be able to interpret the lab results and correlate clinical significance.

Symptoms of kidney problems:reduced urine outputswelling of extremitieslower back paindrowsinessconfusionnausea

When experiencing the symptoms above, it is best to consult a doctor so that an accurate diagnosis and treatment can be made.
