16. sino si general aung san?


Aung San, (born Feb. 13, 1915, Natmauk, Burma [now Myanmar]—died July 19, 1947, Rangoon [now Yangon]), Burmese nationalist leader and assassinated hero who was instrumental in securing Burma’s independence from Great Britain. Before World War II Aung San was actively anti-British; he then allied with the Japanese during World War II, but switched to the Allies before leading the Burmese drive for autonomy.

20. What is conflict of Aung san suu kyi


Aung San Suu Kyi is a Burmese politician and Nobel Peace Prize laureate who has been a central figure in the politics of Myanmar (formerly known as Burma) for many years. She was a prominent leader of the pro-democracy movement in Myanmar and spent many years under house arrest for her political activities.

However, in recent years, Aung San Suu Kyi has been embroiled in controversy over her response to the persecution of the Rohingya people, a Muslim minority group in Myanmar. The Myanmar military has been accused of committing human rights abuses against the Rohingya, including mass killings, rape, and forced displacement. Aung San Suu Kyi, who was serving as Myanmar's de facto leader as State Counsellor, was criticized for not doing enough to stop the violence and for defending the military's actions.

In 2019, Aung San Suu Kyi defended Myanmar against genocide charges at the International Court of Justice in The Hague. She argued that the military's actions were part of a legitimate counterterrorism operation, and denied that the Rohingya were subject to genocide.

Aung San Suu Kyi's handling of the Rohingya crisis has led to a significant loss of support and prestige both domestically and internationally, and has raised questions about her commitment to human rights and democracy. In addition, her government has been accused of limiting freedom of speech and cracking down on political dissent, further complicating her legacy.
