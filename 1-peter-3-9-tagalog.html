4. 3. Albert and Peter are skating. Albert pushes the 45-kg Peter with a force of 7 N. What is Peter's resulting acceleration?​


LAW OF ACCELERATIONAnswer with Explanation:

The word problem involves Newton's second law of motion. This law is also known as the Law of Acceleration which states that acceleration is directly proportional to the exerted net force on a body or object and as the same direction of the force, but is inversely proportional to its mass. This means that acceleration is higher when the net force exerted to the body is also greater and its mass is lesser.

This statement can be expressed by the formula F = ma, where F refers to the net force, m refers to the mass of the body and a refers to the acceleration.

In the given problem, we have the mass equivalent to 45 kilograms and the force equivalent to 7 Newtons. The unit Newtons is equal to kilograms•meters per square seconds (kg•m/s²).

Using the formula:

F = ma

7 = 45a

a = 7/45

a = 0.156 m/s²

Therefore, the acceleration of the moving 45-kg body pushed by a 7-N force is 0.156 m/s².

10. Peter’s age 13 years ago was 1/3 of his age 7 years hence. How old is Peter?


Let Peter's current age be x.  

13 years ago: x - 13

7 years from now: x + 7

His age 13 years ago was 1/3 of his age 7 years hence.

x - 13 = (x + 7)/3

Multiply both sides by 3.

3x - 39 = x + 7

2x = 46

x = 23

Peter is currently 23 years old.

To check:

13 years ago: 10 y/o

7 years from now: 30 y/o
