21. 100 words argumentative speech 8 paragraph. please paki answer an po ng Mabuti​


Answer:

Argumentative Speech Outline

.

Introduction:

Attention-grabber: Start with a strong statement or a question to grab your audience's attention and make them want to continue listening.

Thesis statement: Clearly state your position on the topic you will be discussing.

Brief overview: Provide a brief overview of the points you will be making in your speech to give your audience a sense of what to expect.

Body:

First point: Introduce your first point and provide evidence to support it.

Second point: Introduce your second point and provide evidence to support it.

Third point: Introduce your third point and provide evidence to support it.

Conclusion:

Restate your thesis: Summarize your main points and restate your thesis.

Call to action: End with a call to action, encouraging your audience to take some kind of action based on the information you have presented.

Example:

Introduction:

Attention-grabber: "Have you ever stopped to think about where your food comes from? Most of us don't, but the reality is that our food choices have a huge impact on the environment and the animals that call it home."

Thesis statement: "It is important for individuals to consider the ethical and environmental implications of their food choices and to choose plant-based options whenever possible."

Brief overview: "In this speech, I will discuss the ways in which animal agriculture is harmful to the environment, the negative impacts of factory farming on animals, and the health benefits of a plant-based diet."

Body:

First point: "Animal agriculture is one of the leading causes of greenhouse gas emissions, contributing significantly to climate change. Livestock production also requires a large amount of land and water resources, leading to deforestation and water pollution."

Second point: "Factory farming is inhumane and causes unnecessary suffering to animals. These facilities are designed for maximum profit and efficiency, rather than the well-being of the animals. Many animals are subjected to cramped and crowded conditions, and they often live in filth and suffer from a lack of proper medical care."

Third point: "A plant-based diet has numerous health benefits, including a reduced risk of heart disease, diabetes, and certain types of cancer. Plant-based options are also more environmentally sustainable, requiring fewer resources to produce and causing fewer negative impacts on the planet."

Conclusion:

Restate your thesis: "In conclusion, it is important for individuals to consider the ethical and environmental implications of their food choices and to choose plant-based options whenever possible."

Call to action: "I encourage you to think about the impact of your food choices and to consider incorporating more plant-based options into your diet. Together, we can make a difference for both the planet and the animals that call it home."
