7. The diameter of the bicycle’s wheel is 69.9 centimeters. How far will it move in one turn of a wheel?


Answer:

219.37 centimeters

Step-by-step explanation:

The circumference of the wheel is given by:

C = πd

where d is the diameter of the wheel and π is the constant pi, approximately equal to 3.14.

Substituting the given value for d:

C = π(69.9 cm) ≈ 219.37 cm

Therefore, in one turn of the wheel, the bicycle will move a distance equal to its circumference:

distance = circumference = 219.37 cm

So, the bicycle will move approximately 219.37 centimeters in one turn of the wheel.

14. The Diameter of a bicycle wheel is 65 cm. How does the bicycle travel when the wheel turns one revolution ​


Answer:

When the bicycle wheel turns one full revolution, it covers a distance equal to the circumference of the wheel.

The circumference of a circle is given by the formula:

Circumference = 2 * pi * radius

where pi (π) is a mathematical constant approximately equal to 3.14, and the radius is half the diameter of the circle.

So, in this case, the circumference of the bicycle wheel can be calculated as:

Circumference = 2 * pi * (diameter/2)

= pi * diameter

= 3.14 * 65 cm

= 204.1 cm (rounded to one decimal place)

Therefore, when the bicycle wheel turns one full revolution, it covers a distance of 204.1 cm or approximately 2.04 meters (rounded to two decimal places).

C = πd

where C is the circumference and d is the diameter.

In this case, the diameter of the bicycle wheel is 25 in.

So, the circumference of the wheel is:

C = πd = π(25) = 78.5 in.

This means that for every turn of the wheel, you will move a distance of 78.5 inches.

Step-by-step explanation:

To find the distance you will move in one turn of your wheel, you need to find the circumference of the wheel, which is the distance around the outside edge.

Step 1: Recall the formula for the circumference of a circle

The formula for the circumference of a circle is C = πd, where C is the circumference, π is a constant approximately equal to 3.14, and d is the diameter of the circle.

Step 2: Substitute the given diameter into the formula

In this problem, the diameter of the bicycle wheel is given as 25 inches. Substituting this into the formula, we get:

C = πd

C = π(25)

Step 3: Simplify using the value of π

Using the value of π as 3.14, we can simplify the expression:

C = π(25)

C = 3.14(25)

C = 78.5

Step 4: Interpret the result

The circumference of the wheel is 78.5 inches. This means that for every turn of the wheel, you will move forward 78.5 inches, or approximately 6.54 feet (since there are 12 inches in a foot).
