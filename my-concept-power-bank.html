12. what is Machiavellian Concept of Power ​


Answer:

The Machiavellian concept of power is based on the writings of Niccolò Machiavelli, an Italian Renaissance writer and philosopher who is best known for his book "The Prince." According to Machiavelli, the acquisition and maintenance of power require a leader to be willing to use any means necessary, including deception, manipulation, and even violence.

Machiavelli believed that the ideal leader is one who is feared rather than loved, as fear is a more reliable motivator for obedience and loyalty. He also argued that a leader should prioritize the maintenance of power above all else, even if this requires breaking promises or acting immorally.

Another key aspect of Machiavelli's concept of power is the importance of appearing virtuous and compassionate to the public, even if the leader's actions are in fact cruel or ruthless. Machiavelli believed that a leader who is perceived as virtuous and compassionate will be more likely to win the loyalty of the people, making it easier to maintain power.

Overall, the Machiavellian concept of power emphasizes the importance of strategic thinking, ruthless decision-making, and the ability to manipulate others in order to acquire and maintain power. While Machiavelli's ideas have been criticized for promoting immoral behavior, they have also been influential in shaping political theory and practice throughout history.  >-<

14. What are the present powers of the commercial bank?​


Answer:

Commercial banks have several powers in the present day, including:

Accepting deposits: Commercial banks are authorized to accept various types of deposits, such as savings deposits, current deposits, and fixed deposits, from individuals, businesses, and other organizations.

Lending money: Commercial banks are authorized to lend money to individuals, businesses, and other organizations in the form of loans, overdrafts, and credit facilities. This is a key function of commercial banks and generates income for the bank in the form of interest on the loans.

Issuing credit and debit cards: Commercial banks issue credit and debit cards to their customers, which can be used for various transactions, both online and offline.

Providing investment and insurance products: Many commercial banks offer investment and insurance products, such as mutual funds, stocks, bonds, and life insurance policies, to their customers.

Offering foreign exchange services: Commercial banks provide foreign exchange services, such as currency exchange and remittance services, to their customers.

Providing online and mobile banking services: Commercial banks offer online and mobile banking services that allow customers to access their accounts, transfer money, pay bills, and perform other transactions using their computer or mobile device.

Serving as custodian and trustee: Commercial banks serve as custodians and trustees for various assets, such as securities, mutual funds, and pension funds, on behalf of their customers.

Overall, commercial banks play a vital role in the economy by mobilizing savings and providing credit to individuals and businesses, while also offering a range of other financial services.

17. what is the comparison of lower power and high power​


Explanation:

The most obvious difference between a low power and a high power microscope is that a high power microscope can resolve smaller features, which is usually achieved with objective lenses of greater magnification.

However, there are several non-obvious differences that contribute to the higher resolution of a high power microscope. For example:

Immersion: It is the numerical aperture (NA) that determines optical resolution and the NA is limited by the refractive index of the medium between the lens and the sample. The higher the index of refraction, the higher the resolution. Air is 1.00, pure water is 1.33, and immersion oil is 1.52. High power microscopes have objectives and condensers specifically designed to be used with immersion oil between the objective and the sample.

￼

Light source: The shorter the wavelength, the greater the refractive angle and therefore the greater the resolution. In order to resolve the smallest possible features, the light source illuminating the sample must emit light with a wavelength that is shorter than visible light. This commonly takes the form of UV-emitting light sources such as a mercury-vapor lamp.

Sample stage: This is a minor difference, but high power microscopes have more precise stages. This is because navigating between features occurs at a much smaller scale and with much smaller increments than low power microscopes.
