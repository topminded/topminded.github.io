11. philippine deposit insurance corporation


Answer:

Mandates and Functions

PDIC is a government instrumentality created in 1963 by virtue of Republic Act 3591 to insure the deposits of all banks. PDIC exists to protect depositors by providing deposit insurance coverage for the depositing public and help promote financial stability.


12. Is “Philippine Deposit Insurance Corporation” a bank? ​


Explanation:

PDIC is a government instrumentality created in 1963 by virtue of Republic Act 3591 to provide depositor protection and promote financial stability. It is an attached agency of the Department of Finance. The PDIC's core mandates are deposit insurance and liquidation of closed banks.

28. why is that corporation in the Philippines should owned by a Filipino citizen with at least 60% if the outstanding capital stock? why could a foreign not enjoy the same privelige​


The Philippine constitution states that corporations in the Philippines should be owned by Filipino citizens with at least 60% of the outstanding capital stock in order to promote national economic development and protect the country's interests. This requirement ensures that the majority of the profits and decision-making power of the corporation remain within the country. Foreign ownership of corporations is allowed, but the ownership must not exceed 40% in order to prioritize the interests of Filipino citizens.

29. the difference of capital budgeting of foreign corporation and domestic corporation


Answer:

Domestic capital budgeting involves planning and budgeting for long term investments in the domestic economy or in the country that company is incorporated in. International capital budgeting involves planning and budgeting for long term investments in economies other than the domestic economy of the company.
