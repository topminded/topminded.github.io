Answer:

Both Albert Einstein and William James Sidis were incredibly intelligent individuals in their own right, but it's difficult to definitively say who was "smarter" as they had different areas of expertise and interests.

Albert Einstein was a physicist and mathematician who developed the theory of relativity and made groundbreaking contributions to the understanding of space, time, and the nature of the universe. He was awarded the Nobel Prize in Physics in 1921 and is widely regarded as one of the most influential scientists of the 20th century.

William James Sidis, on the other hand, was a child prodigy who demonstrated exceptional mathematical and linguistic abilities from a very young age. However, he did not achieve the same level of recognition and impact in his field as Einstein did in his.

In summary, both Einstein and Sidis were incredibly intelligent individuals who made significant contributions to their respective fields, but it's difficult to compare their intelligence as they excelled in different areas.

23. explain the William James Philosophy – “I & Me”​


Answer:

Almost 130 years ago, James (1890) introduced the distinction between “Me” and “I” to the debate about the self. The former term refers to understanding of the self as an object of experience, while the latter to the self as a subject of experience. Itprovides a useful tool for clarifying what one means when one speaks about the self. However, its exact meaning varies in cognitive science, especially in regard to what one understands as the self as subject, or “I.”
