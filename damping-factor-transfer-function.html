2. A 20 kg block on a frictionless surface is attached to a spring (k = 500 N/m). Find the damping constant, c, that will make the system: Solve critically damped constant first. a. Undamped b. Critically damped c. Under damped, 0.42 d. Over damped, 2


Answer:

b. Critically damped

Step-by-step explanation:

To find the damping constant that will make the system critically damped, we can use the following formula:

c = 2 * sqrt(m * k)

Where m is the mass of the block and k is the spring constant.

Plugging in the values given in the problem, we get:

c = 2 * sqrt(20 kg * 500 N/m) = 20 sqrt(5) N*s/m

Therefore, the damping constant that will make the system critically damped is 20 sqrt(5) N*s/m.
