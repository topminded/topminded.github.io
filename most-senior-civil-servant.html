24. when ethical standard collides with moral standard which one shall prevail? explain your answer in a view point of a civil servant​


Answer:

When ethical and moral standards collide, it can be a difficult situation for individuals, especially for civil servants who are expected to uphold both standards in their work. Ethical standards refer to a set of principles and values that govern professional behavior, while moral standards refer to personal beliefs and values about what is right and wrong.

In general, ethical standards should prevail over moral standards in the workplace, as civil servants are bound by their professional responsibilities to uphold the law, protect public interest, and serve the people. Ethical standards are often established through laws, regulations, and codes of conduct, and they provide a common framework for civil servants to follow in carrying out their duties.

For example, a civil servant may encounter a situation where a personal moral standard, such as the belief in honesty, conflicts with an ethical standard, such as the requirement to maintain confidentiality. In this case, the civil servant should prioritize the ethical standard and maintain confidentiality, as it is a professional responsibility that is required by law and serves the public interest.

However, it is important to note that moral standards can also inform ethical standards and contribute to the development of a civil servant's personal and professional values. Civil servants can use their personal moral standards to guide their behavior in situations where ethical standards are not clear or do not provide a definitive solution. In these cases, civil servants can seek guidance from their superiors or consult professional codes of conduct to make ethical decisions.

In conclusion, ethical standards should prevail over personal moral standards in the workplace, particularly for civil servants who have a professional responsibility to serve the public interest. However, moral standards can also play an important role in informing ethical standards and guiding civil servants in their decision-making processes.

29. What civilization contributed the most to Northern America?


The Native American civilizations, including the Cherokee, Iroquois, and Hopi, contributed significantly to the development and culture of Northern America. These civilizations had complex systems of government, agriculture, trade, and cultural practices that influenced the region for centuries. The European colonizers, including the British, French, and Spanish, also had a significant impact on Northern America, but their contributions were largely centered on exploitation and colonization rather than cultural exchange and development.
