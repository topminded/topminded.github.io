18. Which shows the correct order of the following atomic orbitals?A. f orbital, d orbital, p orbital, s orbitalB. p orbital, f orbital, s orbital, d orbitalC. s orbital, d orbital, f orbital, p orbitalD. s orbital, p orbital, d orbital f orbital​


The correct order of the following atomic orbitals is s, p, d and f.

The picture above is the order of energy levels of shells and subshells of an atom. There are 4 subshells namely s, p, d, and f. The number before the subshell indicates the skin. The 1s subshell has the lowest energy level, then goes up to the 2s, 2p, 3s, 3p subshell, until finally the highest is 8s. Of course, the number of electrons that can fill a certain subshell is also limited. Each subshell has a different maximum capacity to accommodate electrons.

1. The s subshell holds a maximum of 2 electrons.

2. The p subshell holds a maximum of 6 electrons.

3. The d subshell holds a maximum of 10 electrons.

4. The f subshell holds a maximum of 14 electrons.

Electron Configuration

Electron configuration is the arrangement of electrons in energy levels around the atomic nucleus. According to atomic theory, electrons occupy several levels from the first shell closest to the atomic nucleus, namely the K shell, to the seventh shell Q, which is farthest from the atomic nucleus. For this reason, the electron configuration is refined by a quantum mechanical model that uses four types of subshells to describe the electron orbitals in an atom.
