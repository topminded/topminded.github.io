1. Jack wants to measure the distance between his scout camp and another camp upstream. He can paddle his kayak at a rate of 5 mi/hr in still water, and the rate of the river is 3 mi/hr. If it takes Jack 10 hr to paddle to the camp and back, how far is the camp? a.) 10 mi b.) 16 mi c.) 18 mi d.) 80 mi e.) none of the above


Let's call the distance between the two camps "x" miles.

Since Jack is paddling upstream for half the trip and downstream for the other half, the time he spends paddling upstream is equal to the time he spends paddling downstream.

Let's use the formula distance = rate x time to set up two equations:

Distance upstream = (rate in still water - rate of the river) x time upstream

Distance downstream = (rate in still water + rate of the river) x time downstream

We know that the time upstream is equal to the time downstream, so we can set these two equations equal to each other:

(rate in still water - rate of the river) x time = (rate in still water + rate of the river) x time

Simplifying, we get:

(rate in still water - rate of the river) = (rate in still water + rate of the river) / 2

Substituting the given values, we get:

5 - 3 = (5 + 3) / 2

2 = 4/2

This equation is true, so we know that our assumption that the time upstream is equal to the time downstream is correct.

Now, we can use the formula distance = rate x time to find the distance between the two camps:

Distance upstream = (5 - 3) x time upstream = 2 x (10/2) = 10 miles

Distance downstream = (5 + 3) x time downstream = 8 x (10/2) = 40 miles

The total distance between the two camps is the sum of the distances upstream and downstream:

Total distance = 10 + 40 = 50 miles

Therefore, the answer is not one of the choices given. The distance between the two camps is 50 miles.

23. Differentiate the warm boot and the cold boot. Give and describe the importance of each.​


Answer:

A cold booting is when you turn ON the computer from an OFF position. On the other hand, the warm booting is when you reset a computer that is already in its ON state.

Cold booting is the process of starting up a computer that is switched off. Usually, it is performed by pressing the power button on the computer. Cold booting performs the “Power on Self-Test” (POST). It is a series of system checks at the beginning of the boot sequence. In other words, it checks whether all the requirements are met, and the hardware is working correctly before starting the operating system.

Warm booting is the process of restarting a computer. It can be initialized using the operating system. In Windows, the user can perform warm booting by selecting the restart option from the start menu. In Mac, the user can perform warm booting by selecting restart from the Apple menu.
