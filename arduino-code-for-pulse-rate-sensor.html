3. how can arduino uno water level sensor help to engineering students​


Arduino is an open-source electronics platform that can be used by engineering students to build a wide range of projects, including projects that involve water level sensors. A water level sensor is a device that measures the level of water in a tank or other container. It can be used to monitor the water level in a variety of applications, such as water treatment plants, irrigation systems, and aquaculture.

Engineering students can use an Arduino Uno, which is a microcontroller board, and a water level sensor to build a project that monitors and controls the water level in a tank. For example, they could use the sensor to measure the water level in a tank, and then use the Arduino to control a pump that automatically adds water to the tank when the level gets too low. This type of project can help engineering students learn about sensors, microcontrollers, and control systems, as well as apply their knowledge to solve real-world problems.

The heart rate and respiratory rate of eight preterm (average gestational age, 33.3 +/- 2.2 weeks) and 13 full-term infants were determined during quiet sleep at home during the first year of life. Both heart rate and respiratory rate were greater in preterm infants throughout the first six months. The difference was maximum at age 10 weeks (21.2 beats per minute and 13.7 breaths per minute), with the difference being statistically significant, at least at the P less than .01 level at ages 10, 12 and 14 weeks, while P greater than or equal to .01 at all other ages. The 10- to 14-week period is precisely the same period during which the incidence of sudden infant death syndrome (SIDS) has been reported to be maximum. 29. what is pulse rate? how can you get your pulse rate?​


To check your pulse at your wrist, place two fingers between the bone and the tendon over your radial artery — which is located on the thumb side of your wrist. When you feel your pulse, count the number of beats in 15 seconds. Multiply this number by four to calculate your beats per minute.
