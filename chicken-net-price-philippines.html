If Mr.Gamrod has 20 chickens left and he sells them for the initial price of P160 each, how much will the last chicken cost if all of the other 19 was bought? \begin{lgathered}A _{20} = 160


Answer:

Php. 540.00

Step-by-step explanation:

Formula:

An = A1 + (n-1)d

A{n} = the term that you want to find

A{1} = first term

n = the term position

d = common difference

Mr.Gamrod, knowing he is the only one selling chicken in their barangay decided to add P20 pesos to the price of his chickens everytime a chicken will be bought. If Mr.Gamrod has 20 chickens left and he sells them for the initial price of P160 each, how much will the last chicken cost if all of the other 19 was bought?

A{20} = 160 + (20−1)20

A{20} = 160 + (19)20

A{20} = 160 + 380

A{20} = 540

Mr. Gamrod will sell the last chicken for Php. 540.00.
