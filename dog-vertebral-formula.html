Identifying the vertebrates and invertebrates

Vertebrate is having vertebrae; having a spine or spinal section.

and also vertebrate  having a place or relating to the Vertebrata (or Craniata), a subphylum of chordate creatures, containing those having a cerebrum encased in a skull or head and a portioned spinal section; a significant scientific classification that incorporates warm blooded animals, birds, reptiles, creatures of land and water, and fishes.  

While invertebrate, any creature that comes up short on a vertebral segment, or spine, rather than the cartilaginous or hard vertebrates. In excess of 90% of all living creature species are spineless creatures.

28. Animal Body Covering How they are Born Dog Frog Crocodile Chicken Kind of Vertebrate vertebrate verebrate reterbrate vertebra Shark vertebrate.pa help po...​


Answer:

Dog:

Most puppies are born head first but some come tail first. Puppies are born inside a thin sac, which the mother will remove, enabling them to breathe. After each puppy, your dog should pass an afterbirth (placenta), which they often eat.

Frog:

Frogs typically lay their eggs in water. The eggs hatch into aquatic larvae called tadpoles that have tails and internal gills. They have highly specialized rasping mouth parts suitable for herbivorous, omnivorous or planktivorous diets. The life cycle is completed when they metamorphose into adults.

Crocodile:

Crocodiles bury their eggs in riverside nests. For up to three months, the mother waits nearby, protecting her eggs from predators or any other danger. As soon as the baby crocodiles are ready to hatch, they start to chirp. The mother digs down to the nest and carries her babies to the water in her mouth.

Chicken:

To lay a clutch of fertilized eggs, a hen must have first mated with a rooster. ... The hen will then lay this new life form in a comfortable and quiet spot. A hen will generally lay a clutch of approximately 12 eggs, with each egg being laid a day apart.

Shark:

Viviparity is the most advanced method of reproduction. The baby shark develops inside their mother's body, receiving nutrients and oxygen through an umbilical cord. This is the same method used by mammals. But, unlike mammals, when the pups are born they're immediately independent and have to fend for themselves.
