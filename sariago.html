11. fort santiago significance​


Answer:

It is located at the mouth of the Pasig River and served as the premier defense fortress of the Spanish Government during their rule of the country. It became a main fort for the spice trade to the Americas and Europe for 333 years. The Manila Galleon trade to Acapulco, Mexico began from the Fuerte de Santiago.

Directions: Complete the table below by indicating the contribution/works of the following prominent figures in Philippines Opera.Prominent Figure:Ladislao BonusLeandro LocsinOrchestra MolinaFirst Lady Imelda Romualdez MarcosNelia ManaloMercedes Matias SantiagoJovita FuentesCarlos "Botong" FranciscoPatricio MarianoLucrecia KasilagContribution/Works​


Prominent Figures in Philippines Opera

a. Ladislao Bonus - He served as the "soul and life" of the Raymundo Fermin-led Women's Orchestra that was established in Pandacan at some point in 1890. He led the Pasig Band, the Quiapo Arevalo Band, and the Marikina Orchestra.

b. Leandro Locsin - His most well-known creation, the Theater of Performing Arts (now the Tanghalang Pambansa) of the Cultural Center of the Philippines, had been completed in 1969. The Folk Arts Theater, one of the Philippines' biggest single-span structures with a span of 60 meters, was also created by Locsin in 1974.

c. Orchestra Molina - He wrote a sarswela called "Ana Maria" while he was still in school, among his many other compositions. His two most significant orchestral compositions are Ang Batingaw (1972) and Misa Antoniana Grand Festival Mass (1964). Prelude and Romanz for cello and piano (1928) and Bontok Rhapsody are two of his chamber works. Malikmata (1939) and We Were Moonlight (1941) are two of his piano compositions.

d. First Lady Imelda Romualdez Marcos  - In 1969, she founded the Cultural Center of the Philippines, opening up opportunities for additional Filipino operas to be performed at a respectable location of high excellence. The Cultural Center of the Philippines, which opened in 1969, has been the most recognizable representation of the "Marcos romance", the tale of Ferdinand and Imelda Marcos' marriage as a married couple and political team, ever since it was first constructed. Melodrama in the guise of a tale of suffering and salvation, Imelda Marcos, the victimized heroine of this tale, is actively sought after by the public.

e. Nelia Manalo – She is well-known as an opera diva in the Philippines. She portrayed Violetta, the title character in Giuseppe Verdi's La Traviata. Many people have acknowledged her for being an excellent performer since her debut.

f. Mercedes Matias Santiago  - After she played the part of Anina in Bellini's opera La Sonnambula, Mercedes Matias reached a pinnacle in her career when Philippine Commonwealth President Manuel L. Quezon sent her a seven-foot (two-meter) bouquet with the inscription Ruiseor de Filipinas (Nightingale of the Philippines). The Manila Symphony Orchestra (MSO) supported her throughout that performance (Tariman 2009). Matias performed vocal solos with the MSO in total in 1933, 1936, 1947, and 1952.

g. Jovita Fuentes - She made her stage debut at the Teatro Municipale di Piacenza in Puccini's Madame Butterfly as Cio-Cio-San. She also played Mimi in La bohème, Iris in a play by Pietro Mascagni, and Salome by Richard Strauss. She later returned and started teaching. She gained recognition as "The First Lady of Philippine Music" due to her accomplishments and achievements in her area, and in 1976 she attained the distinction of being the first female national artist in music.

h. Carlos "Botong" Francisco - In 1965, he was involved in the now-famous Angono Petroglyphs' discovery. Additionally, he worked on costume design for Philippine movies. His masterpieces depict the Blood Compact, the First Mass at Limasawa, The Martyrdom of Rizal, the Bayanihan, the Magpupukot, the Fiesta, the Bayanihan sa Bukid, the Sandugo, the Portrait of Purita, the Invasion of Limahong, the Serenade, and the Muslim Betrothal.

i. Patricio Mariano - Mariano developed a career as a literary translator into Tagalog. In addition to writing and translating zarzuelas, Mariano also produced operettas, short stories, poetry, and short stories. In theaters like the Zorrilla Theater, the Manila Grand Opera House, and the Rizal Theater in Tondo, Manila, Mariano's plays have been performed. Sampaguita ("Jazmine"), a play by Mariano, was presented in 1901 at the Zorilla Theater. His one-act play Ang Silanganan ("The East" or "The Levant") and his one-act play Ang Pakakak ("The Tuba") were both premiered in 1913 at the Manila Grand Opera House and in 1904 at the Rizal Theater, respectively.

j. Lucrecia Kasilag - Kasilag played a significant role in the growth of Philippine music and culture. She was extensively connected with the Bayanihan Philippine Dance Company and built the Bayanihan Folks Arts Center for study and theatrical performances. She was also one of the founders of the Bayanihan Dance Company and served as the previous president of the Cultural Center of the Philippines, the Asian Composers League, and the Philippine Society for Music Education. More than 350 musical compositions from folk songs to operas to orchestral works are credited to her.

21. francisco santiago contribution​


Answer:

The composer Francisco Santiago (1889-1947) is sometimes called the “Father of Kundiman Art Song.” While his masterpiece is considered to be his Concerto in B flat minor for pianoforte and orchestra, one of his most significant piece is his song “Kundiman, (Anak-Dalita)”, the first Kundiman art song.

24. Santiago Alvarez Background​


Answer:
