Compare and contrast between parameter and statistic. Use the given situa- tion above as your example.​


In statistics, a parameter is a numerical value that describes a characteristic of a population. In contrast, a statistic is a numerical value that describes a characteristic of a sample.

In the given situation, the population of the Philippines in 2015 is a parameter because it represents a characteristic of the entire population. On the other hand, if a sample of the population is taken to estimate the average height of the population, the resulting calculated value is a statistic because it represents a characteristic of the sample.

To further illustrate, suppose a researcher takes a random sample of 1,000 individuals from the population of the Philippines in 2015 and measures their height. The resulting calculated average height of the sample, 5 feet 5 inches, is a statistic. This statistic can be used to make inferences about the population parameter, which is the true average height of all residents in the Philippines in 2015.

In summary, a parameter is a characteristic of a population, while a statistic is a characteristic of a sample. The distinction between the two is important in statistics because sample statistics are used to estimate population parameters.
