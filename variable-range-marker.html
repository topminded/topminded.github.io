Use the observed frequencies to create a probability model for the color of the next randomly selected marker from the gigantic bucket of markers. Input your answers as fractions or as decimals rounded to the nearest hundredth. Type of marker Estimated probability Yellow Blue Red​


Answer:

Step-by-step explanation:

To create a probability model for the color of the next randomly selected marker from the bucket, we need to calculate the probability of selecting each color based on the observed frequencies.

The total number of markers that Jack counted is:

12 + 37 + 1 = 50

The estimated probability of selecting each color is:

Yellow: 12/50 = 0.24 or 24/100

Blue: 37/50 = 0.74 or 74/100

Red: 1/50 = 0.02 or 2/100

Therefore, the probability model for the color of the next randomly selected marker from the gigantic bucket of markers is:

Yellow: 0.24 or 24/100

Blue: 0.74 or 74/100

Red: 0.02 or 2/100

25. In solving problems involving systems of linear equations in two variables by graphing. Jose paid 216 pesos for the four markers and 3 pencils that he bought. Suppose that the total cost of the marker and the pencil is 60 pesos. What is the cost of the marker and the pencil?


Answer:

The cost of pencil is Php 24 while the cost of marker is Php 36

Step-by-step explanation:

Let m be the price of a marker

Let p be the price of a pencil

[tex]216=4m+3p\\\\60=m+p\\\\[/tex]

Now, let's arrange the second equation to make m the subject,

[tex]m=60-p[/tex]

Then, substitute it to the very first equation

[tex]216 = 4(60-p)+3p\\\\216=240-4p+3p\\\\216-240=-4p+3p\\\\-24=-p\\\\p=24[/tex]

Now that we know p, let's use the second equation to get m,

[tex]60=m+24\\\\60-24=m\\\\36=m\\\\m=36[/tex]

Therefore, the cost of pencil is Php 24 while the cost of marker is Php 36
