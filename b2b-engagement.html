28. b) umerate on the main objectives 2. Virginia Tool Co. is considering an investment in a B2B system for purchasing office supplies and non-operating inputs. The project would require an initial investment of $400,000 and have an expected life of 6 years. The income is expected to be $95,000 in each of the first 4 years and $80,000 in each of the next 2 years. The company's discount rate is 8 percent. Required: a. Calculate the payback period. b. Calculate the NPV on the project. c. Discuss whether this is acceptable.


Answer:

a. Payback period:

The payback period is the length of time required to recover the initial investment. To calculate the payback period, we need to determine the cash inflows for each year and add them up until they equal the initial investment of $400,000.

Year 1: $95,000

Year 2: $95,000

Year 3: $95,000

Year 4: $95,000

Year 5: $80,000

Year 6: $80,000

Payback period = 4 + ($120,000 / $80,000) = 5.5 years

Therefore, the payback period for the project is 5.5 years.

b. Net present value (NPV):

The NPV of a project is the difference between the present value of the cash inflows and the present value of the cash outflows. To calculate the NPV of the project, we need to discount each cash flow to its present value and then subtract the initial investment of $400,000.

Year 1: $95,000 / (1 + 0.08)^1 = $87,963

Year 2: $95,000 / (1 + 0.08)^2 = $81,301

Year 3: $95,000 / (1 + 0.08)^3 = $75,058

Year 4: $95,000 / (1 + 0.08)^4 = $69,205

Year 5: $80,000 / (1 + 0.08)^5 = $57,332

Year 6: $80,000 / (1 + 0.08)^6 = $53,017

NPV = ($87,963 + $81,301 + $75,058 + $69,205 + $57,332 + $53,017) - $400,000 = $116,876

Therefore, the NPV of the project is $116,876.

c. Acceptability:

The acceptability of the project depends on the company's criteria for investment decisions. A positive NPV means that the project generates a return greater than the required rate of return, which is 8% in this case. Therefore, the project is acceptable from a financial perspective.

However, the payback period is 5.5 years, which may be considered too long for some companies. Additionally, other factors such as the strategic fit with the company's overall goals and the potential risks associated with the project should also be considered before making a final decision.
