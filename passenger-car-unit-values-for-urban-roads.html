The car comes to a hard turn in the road and attempts to complete the turn without slowing down. Explain, using Newton's 1st Law, what will happen to the passenger in the front seat.​


Answer:

Upon sensing a collision the seat belts lock in place. When the car crashes, there is no unbalanced force acting on the person, so they continue forward (Newton's First Law). The person moves against the seat belt, exerting a force on it. The seat belt then exerts a force back on the person (Newton's Third Law).

4. a car moving at 10m/s crashes into a tree and stops in 0.26. Calculate the force the seatbelt exerts on a passenger in the car to bring him to a halt. the mass of the passenger is 70kg.​


FORCE

[tex]__________________________[/tex]

Given:(Vi) Intial Velocity = 10 m/s(Vf) Final Velocity = 0 m/sTime = 0.26 sMass = 70 kgAnswer & Solution:

First, find the acceleration

[tex]\sf A = \frac{(vf \: - \: vi)}{time} [/tex]

[tex]\sf A = \frac{(0 \: m/s \: - \: 10 \: m/s)}{0.26 \: s} [/tex]

[tex]\sf A = - 38.46 \: m/s^{2} [/tex]

Next, calculate the force

[tex]\sf F = ma[/tex]

[tex]\sf F = (70 \: kg)( - 38.46 \: m/s^{2} )[/tex]

[tex]\sf F = - 2692.2 \: N[/tex]

↬ Hence, the force that the seatbelt exerted is -2692.2 Newtons
