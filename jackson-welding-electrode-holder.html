13. what are the different types of electrode in welding​


Welding electrodes come in a variety of shapes and sizes, with different capabilities and uses. Electrodes can be solid, gas-filled, or liquid.

Solid Electrodes: These electrodes are made of a solid material, such as metal or carbon. They are the most common type of welding electrode, and are used to weld metals together.

Gas-Filled Electrodes: Gas-filled electrodes are filled with a gas, such as argon, helium, or CO2. They are used to weld thin metal sheets together.

Liquid Electrodes: Liquid electrodes are made of a liquid, such as water, oil, or glycerin. They are used to weld multiple pieces of metal together.

16. explain why is it necessary to choose correct welding electrode(welding rod) in a specific welding job​


Answer:

The first step in choosing an electrode is to determine your base metal composition. Your goal is to match (or closely match) the electrode composition to the base metal type, which will help ensure a strong weld.

Explanation:

For thin materials, it is important to select a stick electrode that produces a soft arc. An AWS E6013 is a good choice to prevent burn-through or distortion. Smaller diameter stick electrodes are also a good option for welding thinner materials, as they provide shallow penetration to help prevent similar issues.
