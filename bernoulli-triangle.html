2. what is bernoullis principle


Bernoulli's principle, physical principle formulated by Daniel Bernoulli that states that as the speed of a moving fluid (liquid or gas) increases, the pressure within the fluid decreases. Since the speed is greater in the narrower pipe, the kinetic energy of that volume is greater.

4. How do you solve (2x^(3)+y^(2))dy-x^(2)ydx=0 using Bernoulli's Equation?


Answer:

To solve the differential equation (2x^(3)+y^(2))dy-x^(2)ydx=0 using Bernoulli's equation, we follow these steps:

Step 1: Rewrite the equation in the form of the Bernoulli equation

The Bernoulli equation is of the form:

dy/dx + P(x)y = Q(x)y^n

where n is a constant.

Divide both sides of the given differential equation by y^2 to get:

(2x^(3)/y^2 + 1)dy/dx - (x^2/y) = 0

Now we have P(x) = -x^2/y and Q(x) = 2x^(3)/y^2 + 1. To make the equation into the Bernoulli form, we need to multiply both sides by y^2:

(2x^(3) + y^(2))dy - x^(2)ydy/dx = 0

Step 2: Substitute y^1-n = z

Let z = y^1-n. In this case, n = 2, so z = y^-1. We can find dz/dx and dy/dx in terms of z using the chain rule:

dz/dx = (1-n)y^-n dy/dx

dy/dx = -y^2 dz/dx

Substituting these expressions into the Bernoulli equation, we get:

(2x^(3) + y^(2))(-y^2 dz/dx) - x^(2)y(y^-1 dz/dx) = 0

Simplifying, we get:

-2x^(3)y^2 dz/dx - y^4 dz/dx + x^(2)dz/dx = 0

(-2x^(3)y^2 + x^2)dz/dx = y^4 dz/dx

(2x^3 - x^2y^2)dz = y^4 dx

Step 3: Integrate both sides

Integrate both sides with respect to x:

∫(2x^3 - x^2y^2)dz = ∫y^4 dx

The left-hand side is a bit tricky to integrate, but we can use integration by substitution to simplify it. Let u = x^2, du/dx = 2x, dz/dx = -y^2z^2:

∫(2x^3 - x^2y^2)dz = ∫(u(2x) - y^2)dz

= u(-y^2z) - ∫(-y^2z)du

= -x^2y^2z + (y^2/3)u^3 + C

= -x^2y^2z + (y^2/3)(x^2)^3/2 + C

The right-hand side is easy to integrate:

∫y^4 dx = (1/5)y^5 + C

So the general solution to the differential equation is:

-x^2y^2z + (y^2/3)(x^2)^3/2 = (1/5)y^5 + C

Step 4: Substitute back for z

Substituting z = y^-1, we get the final solution to the differential equation:

-x^2 + (y^2/3)x^(3/2) = (1/5)y + Cy^2

This is the general solution to the given differential equation using Bernoulli's equation

8. Explain how the Torricelli Law is an application of the Bernoulli's Principle.


Answer:

Torricelli's law applies to an inviscid, incompressible fluid (“ideal” fluid). You can ascertain results from applying the Bernoulli equation between the top of the reservoir and the exit hole. The relationship arises from an exchange of potential energy at the top of the reservoir to kinetic energy at the exit.

10. Explain in simple, Bernoulli's principle, the effect of the pressure and velocity.


Answer:

In fluid dynamics, Bernoulli's principle states that an increase in the speed of a fluid occurs simultaneously with a decrease in static pressure or a decrease in the fluid's potential energy. The principle is named after Daniel Bernoulli who published it in his book Hydrodynamica in 1738.
