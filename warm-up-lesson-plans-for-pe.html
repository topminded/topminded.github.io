15. GEAR UP! In any given physical activity, doing a warm-up is important. With the help of your PE teacher, practice the different warm-up exercises. Follow the proper mechanics of the exercise to avoid possible injury. The very reason for doing the warm-up is to prepare your body to more strenuous activity and prevent the body from possible harm. The warm-up activity can be performed with music. Warm-up Movements (10-15 minutes) Walk and jog Lunges Head-rolls, bends, and isolations Shoulder rolls- forward, back, up, and down Arm circles Cat stretch Alternate toe touch Hip raiser-crab position​


Answer:

Warming up helps prepare your body for aerobic activity. A warmup gradually revs up your cardiovascular system by raising your body temperature and increasing blood flow to your muscles. Warming up may also help reduce muscle soreness and lessen your risk of injury.
