2. what is a residual soil and how does it form​


Answer:

Residual soils are formed from the weathering of rocks and practically remain at the location of origin with little or no movement of individual soil particles. Transported soils are those that have formed at one location (like residual soils) but are transported and deposited at another location.

3. What is a loam soil? How is it formed? What are the different types of soil? Enumerate and differentiate them. Why does humus make the soil fertile? Why is soil an important part of the environment


SOIL

Loam soil

Is a type of soil, specifically a soil composed of a pulverized mixture of varying proportions of silt, sand and moistened clay.

This is often said to be the best type of soil for cultivating plants because it has the capability of holding enough water for the nourishment of the plant and due to the existence of humus, a brown or black complex variable material resulting from complete decomposition of plant or animal matter due to small and microscopic organisms that eats and then defecates it, to form the organic portion of soil than is usually found in loam.

HUMUS

The humus commonly consists of about 60% carbon, 6% nitrogen, and smaller amounts of phosphorus and sulfur that is good for the plants's growth. Because, it helps determine soil fertility level and humus also provides further essential benefits to the soil.

Sand soil

A type of soil containing 10 percent of clay and roughly 85 percent lax granular material that results from the disintegration of rock matters, composed of particles smaller than gravel but larger than silt.

Clay soil

A soil composed of an earthy matter that resembles mud and becomes plastic when moist but hard when fired, that consists mainly of purified particles of hydrous aluminum silicates and other minerals, and that is particularly used for making brick, tile, and pottery because it has the ability to be molded.

Why is soil an important part of the environment?

Because the soil makes up the majority of the Earth's surface, it is essential for vegetations to prosper and grow, and animals and humans depends on plants, trees and other foods that grows here.

Answer:

1.Soil is a material composed of five ingredients — minerals, soil organic matter, living organisms, gas, and water. Soil minerals are divided into three size classes — clay, silt, and sand (Figure 1); the percentages of particles in these size classes is called soil texture.

2.

2.Soil minerals form the basis of soil. They are produced from rocks (parent material) through the processes of weathering and natural erosion. Water, wind, temperature change, gravity, chemical interaction, living organisms and pressure differences all help break down parent material

3.Water, wind, temperature change, gravity, chemical interaction, living organisms and pressure differences all help break down parent material. The types of parent materials and the conditions under which they break down will influence the properties of the soil formed.

4.Weathering causes the disintegration of rock near the surface of the earth. Plant and animal life, atmosphere and water are the major causes of weathering. Weathering breaks down and loosens the surface minerals of rock so they can be transported away by agents of erosion such as water, wind and ice.

Soil Composition
