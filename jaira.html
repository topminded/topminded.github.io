Answer:

Yes, she can group her 10 small and 20 large shells equally in 2, 5, and 10.

Explanation:

It is all about grouping or divisibility rules. 20 (the large sea shells) is divisible by 2, 5, and 10 and the same goes by for the 10 (the small sea shells).

Divisibility Rules:

A number should end with an even number to be divisible by 2. 20 and 10 both ends with 0 which is an even number.

A number should end with 5 or 0 to be divisible by 5. The 20 and 10 ends with 0.

A number should end with 0 to be divisible by 10. The 20 and 10 ends with 0.

26. of the 10 kg of fruits jaira had sold, 5 kg were mangoes, 2 kg were lanzones, and the rest were papayas. How many kg were papayas? A. 1 B. 2 C. 3 D. 4​


Answer:

C. 3 kgs

Step-by-step explanation:

So 10 kg= 5 kg + 2 kg + N kg

Cancel units (kg), we get:

10=5+2+n

10=7+n

10-7=7-7+n

3=n

So the weight of the papayas was 3 kg.
