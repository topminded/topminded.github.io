1. A $40 makeup kit is discounted 70% after 2 months the sale price is discounted 30% what is the sale price


Answer: $8.4


Step-by-step explanation:

$40 x 70% or .70 = $28

70% of $40 is $28.

$40-$28= $12

$12 x 30% or .30 = $3.6

30 % of $12 is $3.6

$12 - $3.6 = 8.4

Step-by-step explanation:

Note that 70% = 0.7 and 30% = 0.3.

Multiply percent of discount to the original price, then subtract the product from the original price to get discounted price.

[tex]40(0.7)=28\\40-28=12[/tex]

[tex]12(0.3)=3.6\\12-3.6=8.4[/tex]

30. A carpenter is increasing his price for projects, claiming that the cost of materials is going up and accounts for 70% of his budget. In a random sample of 49 of his projects, it is found that in 30 of the projects, the cost if material is higher. What is the correct Hypothesis?answer: ca.Ho: p = 70% ; Ha: p ≠ 70%b.Ho: p > 70% ; Ha: p ≠ 70%c.Ho: p = 70% ; Ha: p > 70%d.Ho: p > 70% ; Ha: p = 70%


Answer:

c

Step-by-step explanation:

Null Hypothesis (H0): The cost of materials for the carpenter's projects has not increased, and the proportion of projects with higher material costs is equal to or less than 50%.

Alternative Hypothesis (Ha): The cost of materials for the carpenter's projects has increased, and the proportion of projects with higher material costs is more than 50%.

The significance level, denoted by alpha (α), is not given in the question. Assuming a significance level of 5%, we can use the following criteria to make a decision:

If the p-value is less than alpha, reject the null hypothesis and accept the alternative hypothesis.

If the p-value is greater than or equal to alpha, fail to reject the null hypothesis.

To test the hypothesis, we can use a one-tailed z-test for the proportion. The test statistic can be calculated as:

z = (p - P) / sqrt(P*(1-P)/n)

where p is the sample proportion of projects with higher material costs, P is the hypothesized proportion under the null hypothesis (P=0.5), and n is the sample size.

Substituting the given values, we get:

p = 30/49

P = 0.5

n = 49

z = (0.61 - 0.5) / sqrt(0.5*0.5/49) = 2.16

Using a standard normal distribution table or a calculator, we can find the p-value associated with this z-value to be approximately 0.015. Since this p-value is less than the assumed significance level of 0.05, we can reject the null hypothesis and conclude that the cost of materials for the carpenter's projects has increased, and the proportion of projects with higher material costs is more than 50%.
