19. Explain why privacy and confidentiality matter in sharing information to different social media platforms.


Explanation:

Privacy and confidentiality are important in sharing information to different social media platforms because they protect the personal information and sensitive data of individuals from being disclosed, misused, or exploited. Social media platforms often collect and store large amounts of personal data, such as names, addresses, email addresses, phone numbers, and even financial information. If this information is not properly protected, it can fall into the wrong hands and be used for identity theft, fraud, or other malicious activities. Additionally, the sharing of confidential information, such as medical or legal records, can have serious consequences for the individuals involved. By maintaining privacy and confidentiality in the sharing of information on social media, individuals can protect their personal information and ensure that it is only used for appropriate purposes.

23. concepts of social media privacy​


Explanation:

Social media study is a multidisciplinary field of research that investigates how individuals and groups use social media platforms to communicate, connect, and share information. It encompasses a wide range of topics, including psychology, sociology, anthropology, communication studies, computer science, and marketing. Some of the key concepts in social media study include:

Social network analysis: The study of the patterns of relationships and connections between individuals and groups on social media platforms.

Self-presentation: The way individuals present themselves on social media, including the images, text, and other content they share.

Social influence: The ways in which individuals and groups influence each other's attitudes, beliefs, and behaviors on social media.

Digital identity: The online identity that individuals construct through their interactions on social media.

Privacy: The management of personal information on social media platforms and the implications of its sharing or disclosure.

Viral content: The spread of content on social media, often through social networks or word-of-mouth.

User engagement: The ways in which users interact with social media platforms, including likes, comments, and shares.

Social media marketing: The use of social media platforms for advertising and promotion.

Cyberbullying: The use of social media to harass, intimidate, or bully others.

Algorithmic bias: The potential for algorithms used by social media platforms to perpetuate biases or discriminatory practices.
