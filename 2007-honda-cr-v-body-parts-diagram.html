1. A scene at the Northern Luzon Express Way: A mitsubishi Pajero moving it 100 km/hr is 0.5 km behind a honda CR-V moving in the same direction at 60 km/hr. a. How far does the pajero travel before it catches on upon the CR-V? b. How long will the pajero take to catch on the CR-V


Answer:

a. The distance the Pajero traveled to catch on the CR-V is 0.5 km

b. The time the Pajero needs to catch on the CR-V is 18 seconds

Explanation:

In this problem, our aim is to let the Pajero car catch the CR-V car which is behind 0.5 km.

Solving the problem

To catch the CR-V car, this means that the two cars have traveled the same distance.

This means that:

d(pajero) = d(cr-v)

And since the Pajero car is behind 0.5 km away from the CR-V car, therefore, the Pajero needs to travel 0.5 km to catch the CR-V car.

Next, since the Pajero car is faster than the CR-V, it is able to catch it.

Therefore, let us solve the time using the formula

v = d/t

t = d/v

Then, substitute the given data

t = 0.5 km/ 100 km/hr

t = 0.005 hr or

t = 18 seconds

Therefore, the Pajero car needs 18 seconds to catch up with the CR-V car.

2. determine the weight of a 2007 honda accord with a mass of 1,450,000 grams


#BrainlyFast

Problem:

Determine the weight of a 2007 honda accord with a mass of 1,450,000 grams.

Given Data:

m (mass) = 1,450,000 g

g (gravity) = 9.8 m/s²

w (weight) = ? unknown

Solution:

We will convert first the mass in grams (g) to kilogram (kg).

Conversion factor (1 kg = 1000 g)

1,450,000 g × 1 kg/1000 g = 1,450 kg

To solve for weight, we can use the formula w = mg since weight = force:

w = mg

   = 1,450 kg × 9.8 m/s²

   =  14, 210 kg.m/s²

   = 14, 210 N

Answer:

weight is equal to 14, 210 kg.m/s²

Weight is the same as force and the unit for both weight and force is Newton (N). Weight on earth may not be different on other planet since weight on Earth depends on the force of gravity on Earth (9.8 m/s²) which is different from other planet.
