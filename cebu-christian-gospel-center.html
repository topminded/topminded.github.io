8. in what ways does the gospel portrayal of the disciples reflect christian experience?​


The Gospel portrayal of the disciples reflects Christian experience in several ways. First, the disciples in the Gospels are portrayed as imperfect and flawed individuals who struggle with doubt, fear, and temptation. This mirrors the experiences of many Christians who also struggle with their faith and face various challenges in their spiritual lives.

Second, the disciples are shown as being transformed by their encounter with Jesus and his teachings. This reflects the Christian belief in the transformative power of Christ and the Holy Spirit in the lives of believers.

Third, the disciples are depicted as being sent out to share the message of Jesus with others. This mirrors the Christian belief in the Great Commission, which calls believers to share the Gospel with the world.

Overall, the portrayal of the disciples in the Gospels serves as a model for Christian experience and discipleship, highlighting the struggles, transformations, and mission of believers in the world.

10. John wrote his gospel for Christians who were already familiar with the synoptics. true or false


Answer:

False

Explanation:

Generally speaking, the synoptics tell us what Jesus said and did while John tells us who Jesus is. The synoptics focus on the signs and sayings of Christ; John emphasizes the identity of Christ. Early church father Clement of Alexandria called John “The spiritual Gospel” because of its deep insight into Jesus divinity.

On March 31, 1521, the first Mass was celebrated in Cebu where some 800 were baptized to form the first Catholic community, including Rajah Humabon. The Sto. Niño de Cebu became the oldest Christian artifact in the Philippines as a gift from Magellan to Rajah Humabon on account of their baptism. That is why Cebu City is known as the birthplace of Philippine Christianity.

On that day alone, according to one account, Magellan's priests baptized up to eight hundred Cebuanos.
