21. Hydroxyapatite, Ca10(PO4)6 (OH)2, is the enamel that forms the outer covering of our teeth. What is its formula mass?


Explanation:

To calculate the formula mass (also known as the molecular weight) of hydroxyapatite, we need to add up the atomic masses of all the atoms in its formula.

The formula of hydroxyapatite is: Ca10(PO4)6(OH)2

The atomic masses of each element are:

Ca: 40.08 g/mol

P: 30.97 g/mol

O: 15.99 g/mol

H: 1.01 g/mol

Using the formula mass = Σ (atomic mass x number of atoms), we can calculate the formula mass of hydroxyapatite:

Formula mass = (10 x 40.08) + (6 x 30.97) + (24 x 15.99) + (2 x 1.01)

Formula mass = 400.8 + 185.82 + 383.76 + 2.02

Formula mass = 972.4 g/mol

Therefore, the formula mass of hydroxyapatite is approximately 972.4 g/mol.
