13. strategic or long range planning 10 examples and explain​


Answer:

Strategic planning is a structured process, usually carried out by the executive, which determines long-term organizational goals. During this process, executives analyze the organization's current business and determine though various processes a strategic view of what they believe the organization should become.

The final strategic plan will usually consist of a number of statements and goals of what the organization should focus on, how they believe it should look, what markets they should be in and anticipated financial performance.

None of those goals are directly actionable, and this is where the long-range plan comes in, as it contains the steps and actions needed to achieve strategic plan goals.

Static long-range strategies of the time could not cope with these upheavals, and many but not all businesses abandoned long-term planning for some time.

17. ESSAY: Compare and contrast Strategic Planning with Strategic Management​


Answer:

Strategic planning and strategic management are two related but distinct concepts that are used to guide organizations towards achieving their goals and objectives.

Strategic planning is the process of developing a plan for an organization's long-term success. This process typically involves identifying the organization's goals and objectives, assessing the external and internal environment, and developing a plan of action to achieve those goals. The focus of strategic planning is on the future and on identifying opportunities for growth and development. It's a systematic and comprehensive approach which is based on a thorough analysis of the organization's internal and external environment, its strengths, weaknesses, opportunities and threats. It's also a formalized process that is usually carried out by top management and is reviewed and updated periodically.

Strategic management, on the other hand, is the ongoing process of implementing and monitoring the strategic plan. It involves the day-to-day management of the organization and the constant monitoring of progress towards achieving the goals and objectives set out in the strategic plan. The focus of strategic management is on the present and on taking action to achieve the organization's goals. It's a continuous process that involves monitoring and adjusting the strategic plan as needed to respond to changes in the internal and external environment. It's also a proactive process that involves taking advantage of opportunities and making changes to improve performance.

In summary, strategic planning is the process of developing a plan for the future, while strategic management is the ongoing process of implementing and monitoring that plan. Both are important for organizational success, but they serve different purposes and have different focuses. Strategic planning sets the direction for the organization, while strategic management ensures that the organization stays on course and takes action to achieve its goals.

18. How can Strategic planning be beneficial in your life? Give an example where a situation in your life could have been improved by Strategic Planning.​


Answer:

How can Strategic planning be beneficial in your life? Give an example where a situation in your life could have been improved by Strategic Planning.

Strategic planning can be beneficial in many ways. It can help you clarify your goals and objectives, identify potential challenges and opportunities, and develop a plan of action to achieve your goals. For example, let's say you want to improve your fitness level. Using strategic planning, you could set a specific goal (such as running a 5K race within the next six months), identify potential challenges (such as lack of time for training or difficulty sticking to a fitness routine), and develop a plan to overcome those challenges (such as setting aside specific times each week for training and finding a workout buddy to help keep you motivated). In this way, strategic planning can help you achieve your goals more effectively and efficiently.

Answer:

Strategic planning can be beneficial in your life by helping you to clarify your goals and objectives, and to develop a plan to achieve them. By taking the time to carefully think through your plans and consider potential obstacles and challenges, you can increase the likelihood of success and achieve your desired outcomes more efficiently and effectively.

For example, imagine that you have been offered a promotion at work, but you are unsure about whether or not to accept it. By engaging in strategic planning, you could clarify your career goals and objectives, assess the pros and cons of the promotion, and develop a plan to make the most of the opportunity if you decide to accept it. This could involve setting specific goals for your performance in the new role, identifying potential challenges and developing strategies to overcome them, and seeking out opportunities for professional development and networking. By taking a strategic approach to this decision, you can increase your chances of success in the new role and advance your career more effectively.

26. With your assigned group, create a strategic plan on how to promote your strand in Senior High School to incoming grade 11 students from other public schools in the division of Mandaluyong City. (25 points). All strategic marketing process should be identified. ​


Sure, here is a strategic plan for promoting a Senior High School strand to incoming grade 11 students from other public schools in the Division of Mandaluyong City:

1. Define the target market: Identify the specific group of students who are most likely to enroll in the strand. This could include students who are interested in the subject matter, those who have done well in related courses, or those who have expressed a desire to pursue a career in that field.

2. Conduct market research: Gather information on the needs and preferences of the target market, as well as their attitudes towards the strand. This can be done through surveys, focus groups, or interviews with students, parents, and teachers.

3. Develop a value proposition: Determine what sets the Senior High School strand apart from other options available to students. This could include unique course offerings, experienced teachers, or state-of-the-art facilities.

4. Develop a marketing message: Craft a compelling message that resonates with the target market and showcases the value proposition of the Senior High School strand. This message should be communicated consistently across all marketing channels.

5. Choose marketing channels: Identify the most effective channels for reaching the target market. This could include social media, school fairs, open houses, and other events where students and parents can learn more about the Senior High School strand.

6. Develop marketing materials: Create marketing materials that effectively communicate the value proposition of the Senior High School strand. This could include brochures, flyers, posters, and digital ads.

7. Implement marketing activities: Execute marketing activities using the chosen channels and marketing materials. This could include targeted social media ads, attending school fairs, and hosting open houses.

8. Monitor and evaluate results: Track the effectiveness of marketing activities and adjust the plan as needed. This can be done through surveys, attendance data, and enrollment numbers.

By following these steps, the Senior High School can effectively promote its strand to incoming grade 11 students from other public schools in the Division of Mandaluyong City.
