Step-by-step explanation:

The basketball coach of Caloocan High School can select the starting 5 players from the team's roster of 12 members in C(12, 5) = 7920 ways, where C(n, r) represents the number of combinations of r objects from a set of n objects.

Since there are 4 guards, 5 forwards, and 3 centers on the roster, there are 2 choices for the point guard position and 2 choices for the shooting guard position, giving 4 possible combinations for the guards. Similarly, there are 2 choices for the small forward position and 2 choices for the power forward position, giving 4 possible combinations for the forwards. There are 3 choices for the center position. Therefore, the total number of ways the starting five players can be created with 1 player for each position is 4 x 4 x 3 = 48.

There are 10! ways to arrange 10 different mathematics books in a bookshelf.

The champion can be selected from 10 contestants, the first runner-up can be selected from the remaining 9 contestants, and the second runner-up can be selected from the remaining 8 contestants. Therefore, the number of ways the champion, first runner-up, and second runner-up can be awarded to 10 contestants is 10 x 9 x 8 = 720.

The number of ways Mr. Pineda can choose 5 questions for a math quiz bee from a test bank with 20 questions is C(20, 5) = 15,504.

Any 2 of the 9 points on a circle determine a unique inscribed angle. Therefore, the number of distinct inscribed angles that can be drawn using the points as vertices is C(9, 2) = 36.

There are 7! ways for 7 students to stand in a row for a group photograph.

There are C(4, 2) = 6 ways to select 2 schools out of 4 schools.

The total number of four-digit numbers is 9 x 10 x 10 x 10 = 9000 (excluding numbers starting with 0). The number of four-digit numbers that do not contain digit 3 is 8 x 9 x 9 x 9 = 5832. Therefore, the number of four-digit numbers that contain at least one digit 3 is 9000 - 5832 = 3168.

There are two cases: (1) four females and two males on the committee, and (2) five females and one male on the committee.

Case 1: There are C(8, 4) ways to choose 4 females out of 8 females, and C(8, 2) ways to choose 2 males out of 8 males. Therefore, there are C(8, 4) x C(8, 2) = 28 x 28 = 784 ways to choose four females and two males.

Case 2: There are C(8, 5) ways to choose 5 females out of 8 females, and C(8, 1) ways to choose 1 male out of 8 males. Therefore, there are C(8, 5) x C(8, 1) = 56 x 8 = 448 ways to choose five females and one male.

The total number of ways to choose the committee is 784 + 448 = 1232.


9. symbol of caloocan logo​


Answer:

The symbol refers to the famous monument of Andres Bonifacio and the revolutionaries who bravely fought for the freedom and rights of Filipinos during the Spanish colonization.

Explanation:

One of the more popular places in Caloocan is called Monumento. The name itself is an ode to the famous monument that is located right in the middle of the roundabout of the highway where vehicles coming from the northern provinces, from Valenzuela and Bulacan, and from the southern part of the Metro meet and intersect.

Behind the monument, we can also see the familiar colors and symbols of the Philippine flag. Blue for peace and justice, red for bravery and patriotism, and the division between the two colors as the equality of each tribe, region, and native of the country.

Also seen is the sun with its eight rays that stand for the eight provinces that stood against the Spaniards during the revolution. Instead of three yellow stars, the seal included 16 white stars.
