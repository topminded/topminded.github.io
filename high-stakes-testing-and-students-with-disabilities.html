Answer:

Models of disability affect beliefs, values, attitudes and behaviours toward people living with disability. School staff (leaders, educators, teacher aides, office staff, and other site staff) require an understanding of the models of disability in order to provide quality teaching and learning experiences for students living with disability. This tool focuses on the social model of disability, which is about removing barriers to inclusion, and its application in schools.

IDEA

Understanding the differences between the medical and social models of disability and their history is essential to fully grasp the concepts of exclusion and inclusion. It is helpful for educators and teacher aides to determine which model represents their understanding of disability as this will shape decisions about the students they work with.

9. how do k-12 principals beliefs about disabilities affect their leadership of students with disabilities?​


Answer:

K-12 principals' beliefs about disabilities can significantly influence their leadership and the educational experiences of students with disabilities.

1. Attitudes and Expectations: Principals who hold negative attitudes towards students with disabilities may have lower expectations of their abilities and potential. This can lead to lower levels of support, opportunities, and resources provided to these students.

2. Inclusion: Principals who believe in the importance of inclusion and the value of diversity are more likely to create inclusive environments that provide equal opportunities for students with disabilities. This can include promoting the use of assistive technology, modifying curricula, and providing additional support services.

3. Collaboration: Principals who believe in collaboration and teamwork are more likely to work with teachers, parents, and other stakeholders to develop strategies that support students with disabilities. This can include creating Individualized Education Plans (IEPs), conducting regular meetings with parents and teachers to monitor student progress, and providing professional development opportunities for teachers to improve their skills in working with students with disabilities.

4. Resource Allocation: Principals who prioritize the allocation of resources to support students with disabilities are more likely to provide the necessary accommodations and support services needed to ensure their success. This can include providing additional staff, funding, and equipment to meet the diverse needs of students with disabilities.

Overall, principals who hold positive beliefs about disabilities and the potential of students with disabilities are more likely to create inclusive, supportive, and equitable learning environments that promote the success of all students. Conversely, principals who hold negative beliefs may unintentionally create barriers that limit the opportunities and experiences of students with disabilities.

17. Do you favor Inclusive for students with disabilities?​


Answer:

Yes

Explanation:

Because it can benefit children especially to those who have disabilities. Inclusive education will not only benefit the students with disabilities but also create an environment in which every student, including those who do not have disabilities, has the opportunity to flourish. One of the most important principles of inclusive education is that no two learners are alike, and so inclusive schools place great importance on creating opportunities for students to learn and be assessed in a variety of ways. Certainly this enhances the way in which educators provide supports and accommodations for students with disabilities, but it also diversifies the educational experience of all students.

Another important factor in effective inclusive education is the implementation of consistent behavioral supports throughout the learning environment.  This consistency is essential for the success of students with emotional or behavioral disabilities in the general education environment, but school-wide behavioral supports also help to establish high expectations throughout the school community as a whole.

Inclusive education for students with disabilities can only be successful when those students feel that they are truly a part of the school community.  This requires open and honest discussion about difference, and an institutional respect for people of all backgrounds and abilities

As such, effective models of inclusive education not only benefit students with disabilities, but also create an environment in which every student, including those who do not have disabilities, has the opportunity to flourish.

26. A monkey is chained to a stake in the ground. The stake is 3.00 m from a vertical pole, and the chain is 3.40 m long. How high can the monkey climb up the pole?


Stake = 3.00 m
Chain = 3.40 m

Chain - Stake = m
Chain - m = the height of the pole that the monkey can climb.

3.40 m - 3.00 m = 0.40 m
3.40 m - 0.40 m = 3.00 m

Therefore, the answer is 3.00 m.
