1. This device includes symbols for electronic and electrical design that can be traced using a drawing pencil. ​


Answer:

The device you are referring to is likely a drafting or drawing template specifically designed for electronic and electrical design. These templates typically include a variety of symbols and shapes commonly used in circuit diagrams and other types of electrical schematics.

The templates are usually made of durable plastic or other materials and are designed to be used with a pencil or other drawing tool. By placing the template over a sheet of paper and tracing the desired symbols, designers and engineers can quickly create accurate and consistent drawings without having to redraw common elements from scratch each time.

Examples of symbols that may be included on an electronic and electrical design template could include resistors, capacitors, diodes, transistors, and various types of connectors and switches. The specific symbols included will depend on the intended use of the template and the requirements of the particular design project.
