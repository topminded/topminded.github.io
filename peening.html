10. 4. peening can be done to the metal to counteract tge shrinkagesana po masagutan nyo ng tama​


Answer:

Peening is one way to counteract the shrinkage forces of a weld bead as it cools. Essentially, peening the bead stretches it and makes it thinner, thus relieving (by plastic deformation) the stresses induced by contraction as the metal cools. ... Before peening is used on a job, engineering approval should be obtained.

18. Self Check 2.1I. List two (2) don'ts in the use of:A.Hacksaw______B.Bench Vise______C.Ball peen Hammer______D.Chisel______E.Wrench______​


Answer:

1) Hacksaw

- Do not put too much pressure on the blade and don't saw quickly because it might break the blade

- Do not stroke faster because it will create too much heat

2) Bench Vise

- Stay away from the handle extension and extra clamping pressure to ensure you don't damage your work surface

- Don't proceed to tighten the vise more than is required.

3) Ball Peen Hammer

- Do not glance the blows that may cause the edge because it might result to injury

- Don't hit something with the hammer that it's not designed to hit

4) Chisel

- Do not hold the work with one hand while chiseling with the other hand.

- Do not use an all-steel chisel with a mushroomed face or a chipped edge

5) Wrench

-Do not use push on a wrench - losing your balance is more likely if the wrench slips.

-Do not use a wrench that is bent handle or damaged.
