5. it is an act of recognizing pregnant sow/gilt is from Their signs and symptoms​


Answer:

Non-pregnant, non-lactating females decrease the reproductive efficiency of swine operations. They generate production costs and occupy space in breeding and gestation facilities, yet they do not participate actively in the production of piglets. As a result, producers invest time and money with essentially no opportunity for return each day that these females remain in the herd. The most common reason for keeping non-pregnant, non-lactating females in swine operations is the failure to identify sows and gilts that do not conceive soon after breeding. Consequently, the development and implementation of effective pregnancy diagnostic procedures is an important component of an ef- ficient reproductive management program. This publication provides objective information concerning several methods used for pregnancy diagnosis in swine.

12. 100 essay about what is the important of care management of a pregnant sow​


Explanation:

Care management is crucial during the pregnancy of a sow to ensure the health and well-being of both the mother and her offspring. Here are some key reasons why proper care management of a pregnant sow is important:

1. Nutrition: Proper nutrition is essential during pregnancy to support the growth and development of the fetuses. Pregnant sows require a balanced diet that is high in protein, vitamins, and minerals to support fetal development and milk production.

2. Exercise: Exercise is important for pregnant sows to maintain muscle tone and prevent obesity, which can lead to complications during pregnancy and delivery.

3. Monitoring: Regular monitoring of the sow's weight, body condition, and behavior can help identify any potential health issues and ensure that she is receiving proper care.

4. Vaccinations: Vaccinations can protect the sow and her fetuses from infectious diseases, which can be especially harmful during pregnancy.

5. Environment: The sow's environment should be clean, comfortable, and free from stressors that can cause her to become agitated or anxious.

6. Delivery: Proper care management during delivery can help ensure a safe and successful birth, including the use of proper equipment and monitoring of the sow and her offspring.

Overall, proper care management plays a vital role in ensuring the health and well-being of pregnant sows and their offspring. By providing proper nutrition, exercise, monitoring, vaccinations, and a comfortable environment, farmers can help ensure successful pregnancies and healthy piglets.
