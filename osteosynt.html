2. what is the level of organization of osteocytes?​


Answer:

Osteocyte, a cell that lies within the substance of fully formed bone. It occupies a small chamber called a lacuna, which is contained in the calcified matrix of bone. Osteocytes derive from osteoblasts, or bone-forming cells, and are essentially osteoblasts surrounded by the products they secreted.

3. how are osteoprogenitor cells, osteoblast and osteocytes related​


Answer:

Osteoprogenitor cells are the 'stem' cells of bone, and are the source of new osteoblasts. Osteoblasts, lining the surface of bone, secrete collagen and the organic matrix of bone (osteoid), which becomes calcified soon after it has been deposited. As they become trapped in the organic matrix, they become osteocytes.Explanation:

Osteogenic cells are undifferentiated and develop into osteoblasts. When osteoblasts get trapped within the calcified matrix, their structure and function changes; they become osteocytes.

Ang organikong pataba na may magandang na kalidad ay walang mabahong amoy. 12. Four healthy people in their twenties got involved in injuries resulting in damage and death of a few cells. Which of the following cells are least likely to be replaced by new cells? a. Osteocytes b. Skin cells c. Neurons d. Liver cells


Correct option is:

B

"Neurons"

Neurons lack centrioles and hence they cannot undergo mitosis and divide,  they also do not regenerate.  

The liver is the only organ in the human body that is able to regenerate. They replace the damaged tissue and cells with new ones.  

Malpighian layer is the innermost layer of the epidermis. It is a site where active mitosis occurs and hence they are able to replace damaged cells by new cells.

Osteocytes play an important role in the repair and maintenance of the bone matrix. They initiate the process of regeneration by osteocyte apoptosis.

Thus, the correct answer is 'Neurons.'


13. Describe the structure of compact bone. how do nutrients reach the osteocytes in compact bone


Answer:

Osteocytes receive nutrients and eliminate wastes through blood vessels in the compact bone. Blood vessels in the periosteum and endosteum supply blood to blood vessels in the central canals. Nutrients leave the blood vessels of the central canals and diffuse to the osteocytes through the canaliculi.
