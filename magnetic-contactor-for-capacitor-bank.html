5. What is input coupling capacitor and output coupling capacitor?


To overcome the challenge of creating necessary DC bias voltage for an amplifier’s input signal without resorting to the insertion of a battery in series with the AC signal source, we used a voltage divider connected across the DC power source. To make this work in conjunction with an AC input signal, we “coupled” the signal source to the divider through a capacitor, which acted as a high-pass filter. With that filtering in place, the low impedance of the AC signal source couldn’t “short out” the DC voltage dropped across the bottom resistor of the voltage divider. A simple solution, but not without any disadvantages

12. The capacitance of a capacitor is 20 F. What is the charge on each plate of the capacitor when the capacitor is charged to a potential difference of 6.0?


Solving for the Charge

To understand the problem, let's breakdown the given quantities.

Capacitance (C) = 20 Farads

Potential Difference / Voltage (V) = 6 Volts

Charge (Q) = ?

Remember to use the formula for capacitance, then derive the equation needed to solve for the missing value–the charge.

C = Q/V

Q = CV

Substitute the values and solve according to the corresponding operation involved.

Q = CV

Q = (20 F) (6 V)

Q = 120 Coulombs

Q = 120 C

20. the capacitance of a capacitor is 20uF. What is the charge on each plate of the capacitor when the capacitor is charged to a potential difference of 6.0? ​


Answer:

The charge is 0.12 mC

Explanation:

Here, we are to solve the charge of a 6 V potential difference across a capacitor . To do this, we will be using the formula:

q = CV

where

q     is the charge, unit is in Coulombs, C

C     is the capacitance, unit is in Farad, F

V      is the potential difference, unit is in volts, V

Solving the problem

Let us solve for the charge using the given formula

q = CV

Then substitute he given data

q = (20 uF)(6 V)

q = (20 x 10⁻⁶ F)(6 V)

q = 1.2 x 10⁻⁴ C or

q = 0.12 mC

Therefore, the charge of the capacitor is 0.12 mC.

22. if you wish to store a large amount of energy in a capacitor bank, would you connect capacitors in a series or parallel? explain (ESSAY) Brainliest Answer will be worth it


Answer:

Parallel is your only reliable option.

You can increase the total energy either by increasing voltage or current. Increasing voltage would mean connecting many capacitors in series while increasing the current would mean connecting many capacitors in parallel.

The connection is parallel is very simple to understand. They all share the same voltage and each capacitor you add increase the energy in direct proportion of the capacitance value in farad. If you want 10 times the energy compared to a single capacitor, you connect 10 capacitors in parallel.

Connecting capacitors in series in the hope to increase the voltage is risky. Even if they are identical, one will charge or discharge faster and you end up destroying the weaker by exceeding the voltage or reversing it’s polarity.

Because doubling the voltage increase the total energy by 4, you may think it worth the risk to connect a few capacitors in series. But the capacitance of capacitors in series decrease by the same proportion so the math revert to the same ; each capacitor in series adds to the total power in a linear not square ratio.
