20. Introduction of The Global Food and Security


Food insecurity, defined as a lack of consistent access to enough nutritious food to maintain an active and healthy life, is a global challenge that affects over 820 million people worldwide. The issue of food insecurity is complex and multifaceted, involving factors such as poverty, conflict, climate change, and unequal distribution of resources. To address this challenge, there has been a growing focus on global food and security initiatives that aim to ensure that everyone has access to safe, nutritious, and affordable food. These initiatives involve a wide range of stakeholders, including governments, international organizations, NGOs, and the private sector.
