5. 1. What are the three methods of planting?2. Give one example for each method.3. What planting is applicable for rice and mungbean?4. What is dibbler?5. Give the difference between lowland rice and up farming.​


Answer:

There are three methods of planting crops by direct seeding: broadcast, hill, and drill. Actual planting is done either manually or with a mechanical planter. Another technique, called dibbling, is a form of hill planting.

Lowland rice is grown in fields that can be flooded and they are either rain-fed or irrigated. Upland rice is grown without flooding usually on hillsides in more mountainous regions. Different countries and regions have their own preferences for varieties of rice.

A dibber or dibble or dibbler is a pointed wooden stick for making holes in the ground so that seeds, seedlings or small bulbs can be planted. Dibbers come in a variety of designs including the straight dibber, T-handled dibber, trowel dibber, and L-shaped dibber.

27. 3. What planting is applicable for rice and mungbean?​


Answer:

Fields need to be well-tilled to kill weeds before planting a mung bean crop. Mung beans are planted in late May to early June, using the same type of planting equipment used for soybeans. Mung beans will flower 50 to 60 days after planting and will continue flowering for about a month. The seeds should be planted about 1.5 inches apart in rows set 15 inches apart, and about an inch deep. In areas where weeds are a major problem, plant seeds 3 inches apart in rows set 30 inches apart to allow room for row-crop cultivation and weed control.
