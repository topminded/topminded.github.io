1. A peptic ulcer is an inflammation of the?​


Answer:

Peptic ulcers are sores that develop in the lining of the stomach, lower esophagus, or small intestine. They're usually formed as a result of inflammation caused by the bacteria H. pylori, as well as from erosion from stomach acids.

3. What is the cause of peptic ulcer?​


Answer:

The most common causes of peptic ulcers are infection with the bacterium Helicobacter pylori (H. pylori) and long-term use of nonsteroidal anti-inflammatory drugs (NSAIDs) such as ibuprofen (Advil, Motrin IB, others) and naproxen sodium (Aleve). Stress and spicy foods do not cause peptic ulcers.

Function of bicarbonate in pancreatic juice:

Function of bicarbonate in pancreatic juice: Epithelial cells in pancreatic ducts are the source of the bicarbonate and water secreted by the pancreas. Bicarbonate is a base and critical to neutralizing the acid coming into the small intestine from the stomach.

How may peptic ulcers in the duodenum be produced?

Answer:

Peptic Ulcer is usually caused by an infection in the bacterium and long-term use of some anti-inflammatory drugs. The symptoms of this medical condition includes vomiting, pain when eating or drinking, weight loss, bloating, heartburn and anemia. Upon recognizing and feeling the symptoms, it is necessary to consult a nurse at the school clinic so you can be referred to a doctor who specialized in this medical condition.
