18. Is Politics Good or Bad? Explain Your Answer


Politics, in its essence, is the practice of influencing decisions that affect a society or a country. The purpose of politics is to establish a system of governance that serves the common good and provides for the well-being of citizens. However, like any human activity, politics can have both positive and negative aspects.

On the one hand, politics can be a force for good, promoting democracy, human rights, equality, and social justice. Through politics, citizens can express their opinions, participate in decision-making processes, and hold their leaders accountable. Politics can also lead to positive changes in society, such as policies that promote economic growth, protect the environment, or provide better access to education, healthcare, and other essential services.

On the other hand, politics can also have negative consequences, such as corruption, polarization, and conflicts. Politicians may prioritize their interests over those of their constituents, or use their power to oppress or discriminate against certain groups. Political parties and factions may engage in bitter rivalries that prevent effective governance and compromise the common good. Additionally, politics can lead to societal division, particularly when individuals and groups hold different beliefs, values, and priorities.

In conclusion, politics can have both positive and negative aspects, and its impact depends on the quality of governance, the integrity of politicians, and the engagement of citizens. Therefore, it is essential to encourage responsible and transparent politics that promotes the welfare of all members of society.

Why political dynasty is prohibited?

THE STATE SHALL PROHIBIT POLITICAL DYNASTIES IN ORDER TO PREVENT THE CONCENTRATION, CONSOLIDATION, OR PERPETUATION OF POLITICAL POWER IN PERSONS RELATED TO ONE ANOTHER.

What is political dynasty?

A political family (also referred to as political dynasty) is a family in which several members are involved in politics — particularly electoral politics. Members may be related by blood or marriage; often several generations or multiple siblings may be involved.

What is the advantage of political dynasty?

Political dynasties have the advantage of continuity. The more control the family has over the government unit, the more members of the family can occupy positions of power.
