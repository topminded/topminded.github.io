2. determine the molecular mass and percentage composition of C7H5N3O6​


To determine the molecular mass and percentage composition of C7H5N3O6, we need to know the atomic masses of carbon (C), hydrogen (H), nitrogen (N), and oxygen (O).

Atomic mass of C = 12.01 g/mol

Atomic mass of H = 1.01 g/mol

Atomic mass of N = 14.01 g/mol

Atomic mass of O = 16.00 g/mol

To calculate the molecular mass of C7H5N3O6, we need to add the atomic masses of all the atoms in one molecule:

Molecular mass = (7 x atomic mass of C) + (5 x atomic mass of H) + (3 x atomic mass of N) + (6 x atomic mass of O)

Molecular mass = (7 x 12.01) + (5 x 1.01) + (3 x 14.01) + (6 x 16.00)

Molecular mass = 161.14 g/mol

To determine the percentage composition of C7H5N3O6, we need to find the mass of each element in one molecule and divide it by the molecular mass, then multiply by 100 to get a percentage.

Mass of carbon = (7 x atomic mass of C) / molecular mass x 100%

Mass of carbon = (7 x 12.01) / 161.14 x 100%

Mass of carbon = 52.47%

Mass of hydrogen = (5 x atomic mass of H) / molecular mass x 100%

Mass of hydrogen = (5 x 1.01) / 161.14 x 100%

Mass of hydrogen = 0.31%

Mass of nitrogen = (3 x atomic mass of N) / molecular mass x 100%

Mass of nitrogen = (3 x 14.01) / 161.14 x 100%

Mass of nitrogen = 13.85%

Mass of oxygen = (6 x atomic mass of O) / molecular mass x 100%

Mass of oxygen = (6 x 16.00) / 161.14 x 100%

Mass of oxygen = 33.38%

Therefore, the molecular mass of C7H5N3O6 is 161.14 g/mol, and its percentage composition by mass is: 52.47% carbon, 0.31% hydrogen, 13.85% nitrogen, and 33.38% oxygen.

7. Find the empirical and molecular formula given the percent compositions or masses. Show your complete solution 26.4% Carbon, 3.3% Hydrogen, 70.3% Oxygen, Molecular Mass: 91.0g/mol.


Answer:

1. Empirical formula => CH2O2

2. Molecular formula => C2H4O4

Explanation:

From the question given above, the following data were obtained:

Carbon (C) = 26.4 %

Hydrogen (H) = 3.3 %

Oxygen (O) = 70.3 %

Molar mass of compound = 91.0 g/mol

Empirical formula =..?

Molecular formula =..?

1. Determination of the empirical formula of the compound.

C = 26.4 %

H = 3.3 %

O = 70.3 %

Divide by their molar mass

C = 26.4 /12 = 2.2

H = 3.3 /1 = 3.3

O = 70.3 /16 = 4.39

Divide by the smallest

C = 2.2 /2.2 = 1

H = 3.3 /2.2 = 2

O = 4.39 /2.2 = 2

Empirical formula => CH2O2

2. Determination of the molecular formula of the compound.

Molar mass of compound = 91.0 g/mol

Empirical formula => CH2O2

Molecular formula => [CH2O2]n

We shall determine the value of n as follow:

[CH2O2]n = 91

[12 + (2×1) + (2×16)]n = 91

[12 + 2 + 32]n = 91

46n = 91

Divide both side by 46

n = 91/46

n = 2

Molecular formula => [CH2O2]n

Molecular formula => C2H4O4

9. How does Recombinant DNA affect the structural and molecular composition of an organism?​


Answer:

Recombinant technology broadly refers to the molecular cloning of foreign DNA into bacterial extrachromosomal DNA elements (i.e., plasmids) that can be propagated in a bacterial host such as Escherichia coli. Plasmids are found in many bacterial species and can be transferred from one organism to another within a species by conjugation, resulting in transformation of the recipient cell. In nature, plasmids harbor an operon with several genes, the protein products of which function in a specific biochemical pathway. Plasmid-borne genes have been shown to be involved in antibiotic resistance in addition to cellular defense (bacteriocins), virulence, and metabolism. Thus the presence of the plasmid can give the host cell a survival advantage in its environment. On the other hand, recombinant DNAs are man-made rather than natural entities.

15. What is the empirical and molecular formula of Allicin, the active component of garlic. An analysis of the compound gives the following percent composition by mass: C: 44.4%; H: 6.21%; S: 39.5%; O: 9.86% (Molecular mass = 162.0 g/mol)?​


Solution (Empirical Formula)

Step 1: Assume that the mass of a compound is 100 g.

[tex]\text{mass C = 44.4 g}[/tex]

[tex]\text{mass H = 6.21 g}[/tex]

[tex]\text{mass S = 39.5 g}[/tex]

[tex]\text{mass O = 9.86 g}[/tex]

Step 2: Calculate the number of moles of each element.

[tex]n \: \text{C = 44.4 g} × \frac{\text{1 mol}}{\text{12.01 g}} = \text{3.70 mol}[/tex]

[tex]n \: \text{H = 6.21 g} × \frac{\text{1 mol}}{\text{1.008 g}} = \text{6.16 mol}[/tex]

[tex]n \: \text{S = 39.5 g} × \frac{\text{1 mol}}{\text{32.07 g}} = \text{1.232 mol}[/tex]

[tex]n \: \text{O = 9.86 g} × \frac{\text{1 mol}}{\text{16.00 g}} = \text{0.616 mol}[/tex]

Step 3: Represent an empirical formula.

[tex]\text{empirical formula} = \text{C}_{w}\text{H}_{x}\text{S}_{y}\text{O}_{z}[/tex]

Step 4: Divide the number of moles of each element by the least number of moles.

[tex]w = \frac{\text{3.70 mol}}{\text{0.616 mol}} = 6[/tex]

[tex]x = \frac{\text{6.16 mol}}{\text{0.616 mol}} = 10[/tex]

[tex]y = \frac{\text{1.232 mol}}{\text{0.616 mol}} = 2[/tex]

[tex]z = \frac{\text{0.616 mol}}{\text{0.616 mol}} = 1[/tex]

Step 5: Write the empirical formula.

[tex]\boxed{\text{empirical formula} = \text{C}_{6}\text{H}_{10}\text{S}_{2}\text{O}}[/tex]

Solution (Molecular Formula)

Step 1: Represent a molecular formula.

[tex]\text{molecular formula} = (\text{C}_{6}\text{H}_{10}\text{S}_{2}\text{O})_{n}[/tex]

Step 2: Calculate the empirical mass.

empirical mass = (12.01 g/mol × 6) + (1.008 g/mol × 10) + (32.07 g/mol × 2) + (16.00 g/mol × 1)

empirical mass = 162.28 g/mol

Step 3: Divide the molecular mass by the empirical mass.

[tex]n = \frac{\text{molecular mass}}{\text{empirical mass}}[/tex]

[tex]n = \frac{\text{162.28 g/mol}}{\text{162.0 g/mol}}[/tex]

[tex]n = 1[/tex]

Step 4: Multiply the subscripts by the value of n to obtain the molecular formula.

[tex]\text{molecular formula} = (\text{C}_{6}\text{H}_{10}\text{S}_{2}\text{O})_{1}[/tex]

[tex]\boxed{\text{molecular formula} =\text{C}_{6}\text{H}_{10}\text{S}_{2}\text{O}}[/tex]

21. A compound with the following composition has a molar mass of 60.10 g/mol:39.97% C, 13.31% H, and 46.62% N. Find the empirical and molecular formula​


SOLUTION: (Empirical Formula)

Step 1: Assume that the mass of a compound is 100 g.

[tex]\begin{aligned} & mass_{\text{C}} = \text{39.97 g} \\ & mass_{\text{H}} = \text{13.31 g} \\ & mass_{\text{N}} = \text{46.62 g} \end{aligned}[/tex]

Step 2: Calculate the number of moles of each element.

[tex]\begin{aligned} & n_{\text{C}} = \text{39.97 g} \times \frac{\text{1 mol}}{\text{12.01 g}} = \text{3.328 mol} \\ & n_{\text{H}} = \text{13.31 g} \times \frac{\text{1 mol}}{\text{1.008 g}} = \text{13.204 mol} \\ & n_{\text{N}} = \text{46.62 g} \times \frac{\text{1 mol}}{\text{14.01 g}} = \text{3.3276 mol} \end{aligned}[/tex]

Step 3: Represent an empirical formula.

[tex]\text{empirical formula} = \text{C}_x\text{H}_y\text{N}_z[/tex]

Step 4: Divide the moles of each element by the least number of moles.

In this case, the least number of moles is 3.3276 mol.

[tex]\begin{aligned} & x = \frac{\text{3.328 mol}}{\text{3.3276 mol}} \approx 1 \\ & y = \frac{\text{13.204 mol}}{\text{3.3276 mol}} = 4 \\ & z = \frac{\text{3.3276 mol}}{\text{3.3276 mol}} = 1 \end{aligned}[/tex]

Step 5: Write the empirical formula.

[tex]\boxed{\text{empirical formula} = \text{CH}_4\text{N}}[/tex]

Hence, the empirical formula of a compound is CH₄N.

-------------------------------------------------

SOLUTION: (Molecular Formula)

Step 1: Represent a molecular formula.

[tex]\text{molecular formula} = (\text{CH}_4\text{N})_n[/tex]

Step 2: Calculate the empirical mass.

[tex]\begin{aligned} \text{empirical mass} & = \text{12.01 g/mol + 4(1.008 g/mol) + 14.01 g/mol} \\ & = \text{30.05 g/mol} \end{aligned}[/tex]

Step 3: Divide the molar mass by the empirical mass to find the value of n.

[tex]\begin{aligned} n & = \frac{\text{molar mass}}{\text{empirical mass}} \\ & = \frac{\text{60.10 g/mol}}{\text{30.05 g/mol}} \\ & = 2 \end{aligned}[/tex]

Step 4: Multiply the subscripts by the value of n to obtain the molecular formula.

[tex]\begin{aligned} \text{molecular formula} & = (\text{CH}_4\text{N})_2 \\ & = \boxed{\text{C}_2\text{H}_8\text{N}_2} \end{aligned}[/tex]

Hence, the molecular formula of a compound is C₂H₈N₂.
