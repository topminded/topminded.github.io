In the given problem, apply your knowledge in algebraic expression and basic mathematics especially in solving word problem about perimeter.

Problem

The perimeter of triangular lot is 50m. The surveyor's tape bisects an angle. Find lengths x and y. Given a photo of a triangle.

Given

Perimeter = 50 m

Base1 = 12 m

Base2 = 8 m

Solution

Use the formula of a perimeter, P = A + B + C. To solve two unknown variables, it requires two equations.

P = A + B + C → 50 = 12 + 8 + x + y → Eq. 1

12/x = 8/y → 8x = 12y

Solve for x and y

8x = 12y → x = 12y/8 → x = 1.5y

50 = 12 + 8 + x + y, use x = 1.5y

50 = 20 + x + y

50 = 20 + 1.5y + y

50 - 20 = 2.5y

30 = 2.5y

y = 30/2.5

y = 12

x = 1.5y

x = 1.5(12)

x = 18

Thus, x = 18, and y = 12

Check

P = a + b + c

P = (12+8) + 18 + 12

P = 20 + 18 + 12

P = 50 m ok!

Answer

x = 18

y = 12

12. In europe, surveyors often measure angles in grads. there are 100 grads in one-quarter of a circle. how many grads are there in 2.63 radians?


Angle measure of a circle is often measured in degree (°) unit. However, in some occasions, it can be measured in radian (rad ^c) or gradian (grad or ^g) units.

Below is the conversion of these angle measures in respect to a full rotation in a circle:

degree ↔ radian
360° = 6.28 rad
degree ↔ gradian
360° = 400 grad
radian ↔ gradian
6.28 rad = 400 grad

You need to convert gradian to radian, so use the appropriate conversion.

Given:
Radian = 2.63

Conversion:
[tex]2.63 rad * \frac{400 grad}{6.28 rad}[/tex]
[tex]2.63 * \frac{400 grad}{6.28}[/tex]
[tex]\frac{1052 grad}{6.28}[/tex]
≈167.52 grad

When converted, 2.63 rad is approximately 167.52 grad. That will be the answer!

Your answers and solutions shall be graded base on the rubic provided below. 1. A surveyor is tasked to survey 10 households from a stretch of 25 houses. How many possib the surveyor chooses the sample? (Hint: no repetition) ​


This problem involves finding the number of combinations of 10 households that can be chosen from a total of 25 houses without repetition.

To solve this problem, we can use the formula for combinations:

C(n, r) = n! / (r! * (n-r)!)

where n is the total number of items, r is the number of items to be chosen, and ! denotes the factorial function (i.e., the product of all positive integers up to and including the given integer).

In this case, n = 25 (the total number of houses) and r = 10 (the number of households to be surveyed). So we can calculate the number of possible combinations as follows:

C(25, 10) = 25! / (10! * (25-10)!)

= 3,268,760

Therefore, there are 3,268,760 possible combinations of 10 households that can be chosen from a total of 25 houses without repetition.
