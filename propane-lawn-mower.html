1. A woman pushes a 50-N lawn mower with a force of 25 N. If the handle of the lawn mower is 45 degrees above the horizontal, how much downward force is the lawn mower exerting on the ground?


The downward force : 67.678 N

Further explanation

There are several forces that can work on objects, such as force due to Earth's gravity, normal force, or frictional force.

A woman pushes a 50-N lawnmower with a force of 25 N, and the handle of the lawnmower is 45 degrees above the horizontal

So in this system, there is a normal force, the friction force of a lawnmower with the ground, and the force gravity of a lawnmower

The forces acting on a lawnmower can be described on the x-axis (horizontal force) and the y-axis (vertical force)

On the x-axis the force :

1. Ff , friction  force

Ff = µ FN

2. The force applied by women

Fx = F cos 45

 

On the y-axis the force :

1. Normal force, Fn

2. gravity,

W = m.g

3. The force applied by women

Fy = F sin 45

Because what is being asked is the downward force of the lawnmower exerting on the ground, then we use forces that work in the y-axis direction (vertical force).

Of the three forces on the y-axis above, the downward force is the weight and the force applied by the woman

So that the total downward force:

[tex]\rm =W+F`sin~45\\\\=mg+F~sin~45\\\\=50~N+25~N~sin~45\\\\=50+17,678\\\\=67,678~N[/tex]

2. A lawn mower is pushed by a gardener with a force of 24.8 N to the right at an angle below the horizontal. The lawn mower moves a distance of 6.7m. How much work is done by the force exerted by the gardener in moving the lawn mower? THIS IS SUPER ASAP!


[tex]\tt{\huge{\blue{Explanation:}}}[/tex]

The work done by an object exerting a force F at a distance d is given by

[tex]\boxed{W = Fd}[/tex]

where:

W = work

F = force

d = distance

[tex]\tt{\huge{\red{Solution:}}}[/tex]

Solving for W

W = Fd

W = (24.8 N)(6.7 m)

[tex]\boxed{W = \text{166.16 J}}[/tex]

Therefore, the work done is 166.16 J.

30. 4. After Clarissa finishes mowing both lawns, she pushes the lawn mowerdown the sidewalk to her own house. If she pushes the mower over adistance of 30 meters and does 2700 joules of work, how much forcedoes she use? Substitute the known values into the equation for force.​


CALCULATE FORCE WITH DISTANCE AND WORK

After Clarissa finishes mowing both lawns, she pushes the lawn mower down the sidewalk to her own house. If she pushes the mower over a distance of 30 meters and does 2700 joules of work, how much force does she use?

ANSWERClarissa used a total force of 90N.

GIVEN

d = 30m

w = 2700j

SOLUTION

As we know, work is the product of the exerted energy (force) and its distance. Hence,

w = f × d

With this, we can make the force as the subject of the equation and find its value.

[tex]w = f \times d \\ \frac{w}{d} = f \\ f = \frac{2700}{30} \\ f = 90[/tex]

Therefore, Clarissa used a total force of 90N.
