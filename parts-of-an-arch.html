5. A bridge is supported on an elliptical arch of height of 12 meters and width at the base of 60 meters. A horizontal roadway is 4 meters above the center of the arch. How far is it above the arch at 9 meters from the center?


Answer:

To solve this problem, we can use the equation of an ellipse. An ellipse can be represented by the equation:

(x^2)/(a^2) + (y^2)/(b^2) = 1

where a is the semi-major axis (half of the width of the ellipse at the base), and b is the semi-minor axis (half of the height of the ellipse).

In this case, a = 30 meters and b = 6 meters, and we want to find the height of the roadway above the arch when x = 9 meters.

Substituting the values into the equation, we have:

(9^2)/(30^2) + (y^2)/(6^2) = 1

Expanding and simplifying the equation, we get:

y^2 = 36 - (81/100)x^2

Now we can substitute x = 9 meters:

y^2 = 36 - (81/100)(9^2) = 36 - 648/100 = 36 - 6.48

Taking the square root of both sides:

y = √(36 - 6.48) = √29.52 = 5.44 meters

So the roadway is 5.44 meters above the arch when x = 9 meters.

11. An arch over an entrance is semicircular with an opening that is 5 feet wide. Write an equation that models that arch.


Answer:

If the opening is 5 feet wide, I assume you mean from the center point of the straight edge to the top of the arch. Thus the radius of the arch is 5 feet. Since it is on top, I assume the arch points upward and so the equation is y = √(25-x2)

This equation can be found from the equation of a circle (x-h)2+(y-k)2=r2. The point (h,k) is the center of the circle (here we defined the center to be (0,0)) and r=5 so r2=25. Thus x2+y2=25. Then you solve for y but only use the positive answer since we're talking about the top semicircle.
