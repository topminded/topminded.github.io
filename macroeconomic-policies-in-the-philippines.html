What Is the Law of Supply and Demand?

The law of supply and demand is a theory that explains the interaction between the sellers of a resource and the buyers for that resource. The theory defines the relationship between the price of a given good or product and the willingness of people to either buy or sell it. Generally, as price increases, people are willing to supply more and demand less and vice versa when the price falls.

It is important for both supply and demand to understand that time is always a dimension on these charts. The quantity demanded or supplied, found along the horizontal axis, is always measured in units of the good over a given time interval. Longer or shorter time intervals can influence the shapes of both the supply and demand curves.

Our lives are constantly being influenced by economic policy. But for many, the policy is just lots of words, with no real meaning. This should help you understand what is behind the policy. Policy makers undertake three main types of economic policy:

Fiscal policy: Changes in government spending or taxation.

Monetary policy: Changes in the money supply to alter the interest rate (usually to influence the rate of inflation).

Supply-side policy: Attempts to increase the productive capacity of the economy.

23. Give 5 microeconomics and macroeconomics events events in the Philippines, with analysis. Analysis Reason behind, Possible solutionPossible outcome ​


Answer:

Microeconomics:

1. Increase in the price of gasoline - The increase in the price of gasoline can be attributed to factors such as the rise in crude oil prices in the global market and the depreciation of the Philippine peso against the US dollar. This increase affects consumers who use vehicles, and can lead to a decrease in their purchasing power as they need to allocate more funds for fuel.

2. Implementation of a minimum wage increase - This event affects the labor market, as it sets a higher minimum wage for workers. While this is good for workers who are able to earn more, it can lead to an increase in the cost of production for businesses, which can affect their profit margins. This can potentially lead to some businesses downsizing or even closing down.

Macroeconomics:

1. COVID-19 pandemic - The pandemic has had a significant impact on the economy of the Philippines. It has led to a decline in GDP and an increase in unemployment. The government has responded with measures such as lockdowns, stimulus packages, and vaccination programs, to try and mitigate the impact of the pandemic on the economy.

2. Tax Reform for Acceleration and Inclusion (TRAIN) Law - The TRAIN law is a tax reform package implemented by the government in 2018. It lowered personal income tax rates while increasing taxes on certain products such as petroleum, sugar-sweetened beverages, and cars. The law aims to increase government revenue while also providing relief to taxpayers. However, some have criticized the law for its potential negative impact on the poor, who may bear a disproportionate burden of the increased taxes.

3. Infrastructure projects under the Build, Build, Build program - The Build, Build, Build program is a government initiative aimed at improving infrastructure in the Philippines. It involves the implementation of various projects such as road networks, bridges, airports, and seaports. These projects aim to improve the country's transportation and logistics capabilities, and potentially attract more investment to the country. However, the program also requires significant funding, which may have an impact on the government's fiscal position.

4. Exchange rate fluctuations - The Philippine peso is subject to fluctuations in its exchange rate against other currencies, such as the US dollar. This can have an impact on the economy, as it affects the competitiveness of Philippine exports and the cost of imported goods. A stronger peso can make Philippine exports more expensive, while a weaker peso can increase the cost of imported goods, potentially leading to higher inflation.
