   HTML is a markup language that is used to make websites. HTML tags, which define the content and metadata of each page, are used by web designers to create them. CSS, or cascading style sheets, is a technique for defining the layout and look of items on a web page. As a result, the majority of websites use a combination of HTML and CSS to control how each page appears in a browser.

   Hand-coding pages (typing HTML and CSS from scratch) is preferred by some web designers, while others prefer to use a "WYSIWYG" editor like Adobe Dreamweaver. here are the general steps to create a portal using Jimdo or Wordpress:

Creating a portal with Jimdo:

1.) Sign up for a Jimdo account and choose a template that suits your needs.

Customize the design and layout of your portal using Jimdo's drag-and-drop editor.

Add pages, blog posts, and other content to your portal, and organize them into menus and submenus.

2.) Add features such as contact forms, search bars, and social media links to your portal.

Publish your portal to the web and promote it through social media and other channels.

Creating a portal with WordPress:

1.) Sign up for a WordPress account and choose a hosting plan.

2.) Install WordPress on your hosting server and choose a theme for your portal.

Customize the design and layout of your portal using WordPress's editor or a third-party page builder plugin.

3.)Add pages, blog posts, and other content to your portal, and organize them into menus and submenus.

Install and configure plugins to add features such as contact forms, search bars, and social media links to your portal.

4.)Optimize your portal for search engines and promote it through social media and other channels.

Keep in mind that creating a portal can be a complex process that requires technical skills and knowledge. If you're not familiar with web development, you may want to consider hiring a professional or using a website builder that offers more guidance and support.

29. what is the difference between WordPress.com and WordPress org pls explain​


Answer:

The key difference between WordPress.com and WordPress.org is who's actually hosting your website. With WordPress.org, you host your own site (we recommend this). With WordPress.com, on the other hand, it's WordPress.com that takes care of all of this for you (easier to start, less freedom).
