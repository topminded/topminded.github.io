8. what is the Sewing needle bar​


Answer:

The needle bar is the long cylinder that holds your needle. Provided the needle is seated in it properly (the needle is not backwards, not slightly rotated, and/or not fully inserted) this is the first check and adjustment made before anything else.

Answer:

1. Bar tacking sewing machine is used to secure pocket corners, belt loops, the open end of a button hole, zipper flies. Bar tacking machine is also used for small decorative tacks and shapes.

2. A buttonholer is an attachment for a sewing machine which automates the side-to-side and forwards-and-backwards motions involved in sewing a buttonhole.

3. A twin needle or double needle is used to produce two rows of parallel stitches on top with zig-zag looking interlocked stitches underneath.

4. A hi-speed lockstitch sewing machine is the best common mechanical system stitch made by the sewing machine. The term  of single needle stitching often found on dress shirt labels, referering to lockstitch machine. 

5. Overlock sewing machines usually run at high speeds, from 1000 to 9000 rpm, and most are used in industry for edging, hemming and seaming a variety of fabrics and products. 

6. Button stitching machine is a special type of machine which is used in garments industries to attach button so it is called button attaching machine

23. sewing machine needles uses​


Answer:

All woven materials are made with needles. This needle will pierce the fabric threads of woven fabrics. Needles are used for sewing on knits; the rounded tip allows the needle to pass between the fabric threads by separating them. Its function is to deliver thread from the top and through the project while the machine's hook grabs hold of the thread to entwine it with the bobbin thread. Your sewing machine will operate without a hitch if you use the proper needle.
