Step-by-step explanation:

To determine what can be said about Ana's score given her standard score of 1 to -1, we need to calculate her z-score using the formula:

z-score = (score - mean) / standard deviation

If Ana's z-score is 1, then we can write:

1 = (score - 50) / 5

Rearranging this equation, we get:

score = (1 * 5) + 50 = 55

If Ana's z-score is -1, then we can write:

-1 = (score - 50) / 5

Rearranging this equation, we get:

score = (-1 * 5) + 50 = 45

Therefore, Ana's score can either be 55 or 45, depending on whether her z-score is 1 or -1, respectively.

Since 55 is greater than the mean score of 50, we can say that Ana's score is above the mean score if her z-score is 1. On the other hand, since 45 is less than the mean score of 50, we can say that Ana's score is below the mean score if her z-score is -1.

Therefore, the correct answer is:

b. Ana's score is below the mean score if her z-score is -1, and Ana's score is above the mean score if her z-score is 1.

15. Sam and Alex play in the same soccer team. Last Saturday Alex scored 3 more goals than Sam, but together they scorec less than 9 goals. What are the possible number of goals Alex scored?pa help poneed ko po ngayon​


Step-by-step explanation:

Assign Letters:

the number of goals Alex scored: A

the number of goals Sam scored: S

We know that Alex scored 3 more goals than Sam did, so: A = S + 3

And we know that together they scored less than 9 goals: S + A < 9

We are being asked for how many goals Alex might have scored: A

Solve:

Start with: S + A < 9

A = S + 3, so: S + (S + 3) < 9

Simplify: 2S + 3 < 9

Subtract 3 from both sides: 2S < 9 − 3

Simplify: 2S < 6

Divide both sides by 2: S < 3

Sam scored less than 3 goals, which means that Sam could have scored 0, 1 or 2 goals.

Alex scored 3 more goals than Sam did, so Alex could have scored 3, 4, or 5 goals.

Check:

When S = 0, then A = 3 and S + A = 3, and 3 < 9 is correct

When S = 1, then A = 4 and S + A = 5, and 5 < 9 is correct

When S = 2, then A = 5 and S + A = 7, and 7 < 9 is correct

(But when S = 3, then A = 6 and S + A = 9, and 9 < 9 is incorrect)
