L + W = 20,000

L = W + 4, 000

Use the substitution method

L + W = 20,000

(W + 4000) + W = 20,000

2W + 4000 = 20,000

2W = 20,000 - 4000

2W = 16,000

W = 8,000 William's score

L = W + 4000

L = 8,000 + 4000

L = 12,000 Luca's score

9. Eric is twice as old as his brother Lucas. If 4 is subtracted from Eric's age and 4 is added to Lucas' age, their ages will be equal. What are the boys' ages now?


Lets represent:
x=Eric's age
y=Lucas's Age

The first equation:
x=2y

The second equation:
x-4=y+4
Substitute
2y-4=y+4
Subtract y to both sides
y-4=4
Add 4 to both sides
y=8

Therefore: Lucas's age is 8 while Eric's age is 16.

Hope this helps =)

We let Lucas' age be x, this would make Eric's age 2x. 
From the second sentence we would have;
[tex]2x-4=x+4 \\ 2x-4-x=x+4-x \\ x-4=4 \\ x-4+4=4+4 \\ x=8[/tex]

Therefore Lucas' age is 8 and Eric's is 2*8 or 16.

12. Reflection about Lucas 1: 26-38


“My reflexes about Lucas 1:26-38”

The story of the angel Gabriel visiting Mary in Luke 1:26-38 is a powerful reminder to all of us that God hears our prayers and answers them in surprising and unexpected ways. Mary humbly accepted the strange news, recognizing that she had been blessed by God with an incredible opportunity to be part of His divine plan. Her response reflects her commitment to obedience, submission, and trust. With faith in the unseen, Mary faced her challenges without hesitation or fear. By example, we learn how to be faithful stewards of God’s gifts through both delightful surprises and difficult challenges. The messages from this ancient passage remain relevant today: be open to hearing from God amid chaos, embrace big opportunities with courage, and offer prayers filled with full faith and devotion.
