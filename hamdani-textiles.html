Identify the similarities and differences of the textile of Ilocos and the textile of Cordillera Province, also the textile of Cagayan Valley from the textile of Mountain Province

The types of textiles produced from different regions depend on their place of origin and the availability of natural materials in the area. In addition, there are weaving traditions that are passed down from generation to generation in each tribe.

Explanation

Products that are finely crafted by hand such as textile products, each piece of fabric has a function, symbolism, beliefs, and culture of those who make it. This adds to the aesthetics of the product and elevates it to the level of a work of art. There are many textile manufacturers spread across the Philippines. Ilocos and Cordillera in the northern part of Luzon are the centers of weaving villages. In addition, the islands of Mindoro and Bicol also have weavers.

Textiles of Ilocos and Cordillera provinces

In terms of materials and functions, Ilocos and Cordillera textiles are both made using natural materials and are used for traditional clothing. Ilocos textiles are made from cotton and Cordillera textiles are made from abaca fiber. The designs made by Cordillera weavers are different from the designs made by Ilocos weavers. Ilocos textile designs have intricate geometric designs.

Cagayan Valley and Mountain Province textiles

Cagayan valley textiles use slatted textiles, while mountain province textiles use zigzags. The colors of the Cagayan valley textiles are dark that degrade with light and the designs are unusual, especially the stripes. The colors used in Mountain Province textiles use contrasting colors with dark colors such as black. The weaving style used in the Mountain Province uses traditional backstrap weaving and loom weaving. Not only traditional clothing, backstrap weavers also make traditional cloth and clothing for ceremonies. Loom weavers make hats, purses and other souvenirs.

Thus, the variety of textiles from different places is based on the patterns, designs, colors, and materials used by them.
