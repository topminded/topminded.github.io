4. what is perspective pictorial drawing?​


Perspective pictorial drawing is a technique used in art and design to create the illusion of depth and three-dimensionality on a flat surface, such as a piece of paper or canvas. It is a method of drawing that makes use of principles of perspective, which involve creating the illusion of distance and space by using lines and vanishing points to create a sense of depth.

In perspective pictorial drawing, objects are drawn in such a way that they appear to recede into the distance or extend out towards the viewer. This is achieved by using techniques such as linear perspective, atmospheric perspective, and vanishing points. Linear perspective involves using converging lines to create the illusion of distance and depth, while atmospheric perspective involves using color and shading to create the illusion of distance and atmosphere. Vanishing points are points on the horizon line where parallel lines appear to converge, further adding to the illusion of depth and space.

Perspective pictorial drawing is commonly used in architecture, landscape design, and product design, where accurate and convincing depictions of space and depth are important. It is also used in fine art, where it is used to create realistic and convincing representations of three-dimensional space on a two-dimensional surface.

LINEAR PERSPECTIVE. It deals with the organization of shapes in space.AERIAL PERSPECTIVE. It deals with atmospheric perspective and deals with atmospheric effects on tones and colors.
