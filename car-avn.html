Anton van Leeuwenhoek

Antoni van Leeuwenhoek is considered a father of microbiology as he observed and experimented with microscopic organisms in the 1670s, using simple microscopes of his own design. Scientific microbiology developed in the 19th century through the work of Louis Pasteur and in medical microbiology Robert Koch.

Answer:

Antoni van Leeuwenhoek

Explanation:

Antonie van Leeuwenhoek is widely considered to be father of microscope. While microscopes were known earlier Antonie van Leeuwenhoek made significant improvements to theirdesign. Using microscopes he made lot of discoveries in microbiology making him "father of microbiology" as well. Microscopes he created were able to magnify 275x times (however there is some speculation that he might had more powerful ones).
