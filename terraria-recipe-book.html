Ingredients:

1 (900 g) pack COOK EXPRESS CORNED BEEF BRISKET

6 tbsp MAGNOLIA BUTTER UNSALTED, softened

12 slices rye bread

6 tbsp Russian or thousand island dressing

6 tbsp sauerkraut, drained

1/2 pack MAGNOLIA QUICKMELT CHEESE, grated

Procedure:

Thaw corned beef overnight in the refrigerator. To reheat using the microwave, transfer contents to a bowl. Microwave 4-5 minutes over medium to high setting. To reheat using the stove top, transfer contents into a pot and boil for 5 to 10 minutes. Drain.

Shred corned beef or slice into 1/4 inch pieces. Set aside.

Brush about 1/2 tablespoon butter on one side of bread.  Place bread buttered side facing down, and then spread a tablespoon of dressing on the top side.

Place generous amounts of corned beef, sauerkraut, and grated cheese. Sandwich with a slice of bread with the buttered side up. Repeat procedure to make remaining sandwiches.

Preheat grill or non-stick pan over medium heat. Place sandwich in the pan and cook for about 1 minute on each side until bread is golden brown and cheese is melted.

27. A cook book containing recipes for making cakes, cookies, and other desserts.


QUESTION:

A cook book containing recipes for making cakes, cookies, and other desserts.

EXPLANATION:

LESSON: Author's Purpose

An author's purpose is the main reason the author is writing. There are many purposes an author can give. Some basics are:

To instructTo entertainTo informTo persuadeTo describe

The names live up to their name:

INSTRUCT - is to guide reader, it contains steps in a process and can also contain directions. ENTERTAIN - is basically to giving fun to the reader, it uses humor, narrations, and can mostly tell a story. INFORM - is to inform a reader with mainly factual informationPERSUADE - this purpose is trying to make the reader believe, think or feel about something (basically persuading the readers) DESCRIBE - uses description and details for the readers to consume

ANSWER:

A cook book containing recipes for making cakes, cookies, and other desserts.

INFORM

WHY? A cook book contains recipe for making cakes, cookies and deserts. It is also called a cookery book. This type of books gives the readers the things they NEED. Listed in the question that this book contains the recipe needed in making deserts which leads us to assuming the AUTHOR INFORMING the reader on the recipe that is FACTUALLY NEEDED in order of making a desserts.
