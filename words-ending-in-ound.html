Proper disposal of garbage is one of the most important steps we can take to control pollution in our surroundings. This means throwing our waste in designated trash bins or landfills instead of littering on the streets or bodies of water.

Conserving energy by turning off appliances and unplugging devices when not in use can also help reduce pollution. This lessens the demand for energy, which in turn lessens the need for power plants that emit harmful pollutants.

Regularly maintaining our vehicles and ensuring that they pass emissions tests can also help control pollution in our surroundings. This reduces the amount of harmful gases released into the air.

Planting trees and other vegetation can also help control pollution by absorbing carbon dioxide and other pollutants from the air. This not only helps clean the air but also enhances the aesthetic value of our surroundings.

Finally, being aware of our own actions and how they affect the environment is key to controlling pollution in our surroundings. Simple habits such as using reusable bags, bottles, and containers can go a long way in reducing waste and preventing pollution.
