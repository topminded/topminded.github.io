1. 2. A four-person committee is to be elected from an organizations' membership of 11people. How many different committees are possible?​.


Formula used: nCr = (n!) / (r!(n-r)!)

Given: n = 11 and r = 4

Solution:

11C4 = (11!) / (4!(11-4)!)11C4 = (11!) / (4!7!)11C4 = (11*10*9*8*7!) / (4!7!)11C4 = (11*10*9*8) / (4*3*2*1) = 7920 / 24 = 33011C4 = 330

Answer: Therefore, 11C4 = 330, which means that there are 330 different committee.

22. 9. How many committees can be formed by choosing 4 men from an organization of a membership of15 men?​


Answer

There are 15 ways to choose the first member, 14 ways to choose the second member, 13 for the third member, and 12 for the fourth member.

However, choosing A, then B, then C, then D is the same as choosing D, C, A, B, which is the same as choosing B, C, A, D, etc.

Therefore, we have counted each possible committee 24 times, since there are 4! = 24 ways of rearranging any four committee members.

To account for this, we will divide by 24 so we only count each possible committee once.

15⋅14⋅13⋅1224  

=1365  possible committees

Reflection on Leadership and membership.  

Leadership is a harmonious relationship in which one person influences others to work together to achieve a desired goal. If there is no member, leadership will not exist. Leadership is the process by which a manager or supervisor imaginatively directs, guides, and influences others to work hard for the purpose of attaining specified goals.

Leadership involves the following:

Establishing a clear vision Sharing mission, vision and goals with others so that they will follow willingly. Providing the information. Providing knowledge and methods to realize that vision.

Membership belongs, either individually or collectively, to a specific group or organization. The responsibility of a member is to follow all the rules and guidelines set by the leaders in order to achieve the goal. Every member should act and participate in every activity provided by the leaders.
