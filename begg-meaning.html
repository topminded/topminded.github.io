Main Idea, Key Sentence and Supporting Details A paragraph is made up of

sentences that explain or present the details of a topic.

1. The beginning sentence, which usually states the main idea.

2. The middle sentence and the ending sentence, which support the main idea

are the supporting details.

Main idea is the most important point or thought about the topic. So, the main

idea is what the writing is all about. Topic or Key Sentence is the sentence

where you can find the main idea. It states the main idea. It is commonly found

at the beginning, in the middle, or at the end.

Supporting details provide information that explains why, define, or prove the

main idea. Every sentence should be a supporting detail for your main idea.

Note: Topic refers to the person, place, thing or idea being written about.
