Answer:

A literary essay is a type of writing that analyzes a piece of literature. It can be a novel, a short story, a poem, or any other work of fiction or non-fiction. A literary essay typically examines the elements of a book, such as its characters, plot, setting, themes, and symbols, and evaluates how these elements contribute to the overall meaning of the work.

On the other hand, a journalistic essay is a type of writing that is based on factual information and events. It is typically written in a more objective and impersonal style than a literary essay, and it may be published in a newspaper, magazine, or online news outlet. A journalistic essay typically focuses on current events or issues, and it may be written in response to a specific event or issue.

In general, the main difference between a literary essay and a journalistic essay is the focus of each type of writing. A literary essay focuses on analyzing literature, while a journalistic essay focuses on reporting and explaining news and events.
