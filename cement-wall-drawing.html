7. 8. To build a sound wall along the highway, the amount of time t needed varies directly with the number of cement blocks c needed and inversely with the number of workers w. A sound wall made of 2400 blocks, using six workers takes 18 hours to complete. How long would it take to build a wall of 4500 blocks with 10 workers?​


Let's use the following variables:

t: the time needed to build the sound wall (in hours)

c: the number of cement blocks needed

w: the number of workers

From the problem statement, we know that:

t varies directly with c: t = k1 * c, where k1 is a constant of proportionality

t varies inversely with w: t = k2 / w, where k2 is a constant of proportionality

We can combine these two equations to get:

t = k1 * c * w / k2

To find the values of k1 and k2, we can use the information given in the problem:

When c = 2400 and w = 6, t = 18:

18 = k1 * 2400 * 6 / k2

When c = 4500 and w = 10, we want to find t:

t = k1 * 4500 * 10 / k2

We can solve for k1 and k2 by isolating them in the first equation:

k1 = 18 * k2 / (2400 * 6)

k2 = 18 * k1 * 2400 * 6 / (6 * 2400)

k2 = 18 * k1

Substituting these values into the second equation, we get:

t = (18 * k1 * 4500 * 10) / (18 * k1)

t = 4500 * 10 / 6

t = 7500

Therefore, it would take 7500 hours to build a wall of 4500 blocks with 10 workers.

24. C. Explain why walls made with hollow blocks needs to be plastered with cement and coated with paint​


Walls made with hollow blocks need to be plastered with cement and coated with paint for several reasons. The plaster provides a smooth, even surface for the paint to adhere to. It also helps to protect the blocks from water damage and mold growth. The paint helps to protect the wall from further damage, such as UV rays and extreme weather. Additionally, the paint helps to make the wall look more attractive and can help to hide any imperfections or blemishes on the wall.

27. If a 1000kg car is sent toward cemented wall with a speed of 20m/s and the impact stops the car in 0.5 with that average force is it brought to a stop. (with solution plz)


Hello!

If a 1000kg car is sent toward cemented wall with a speed of 20m/s and the impact stops the car in 0.5 with that average force is it brought to a stop.

We have the following data:

F (average force) = ? (in Newton or kg.m/s²)

m (mass) = 1000 kg

Vi (initial velocity) = 0 m/s

Vf (final velocity) = 14 m/s

t (time) = 0.5 s

We apply the data to the average force formula, see:

[tex]F = \dfrac{m*(V_f-V_i)}{t}[/tex]

[tex]F = \dfrac{1000\:kg*(20\:m/s-0\:m/s)}{0.5\:s}[/tex]

[tex]F = \dfrac{1000\:kg*20\:m/s}{0.5\:s}[/tex]

[tex]F = \dfrac{20000\:kg*m/s}{0.5\:s}[/tex]

[tex]F = 40000\:kg*m/s^2\to \boxed{\boxed{F = 40000\:N}}\Longleftarrow(average\:force)\end{array}}\qquad\checkmark[/tex]

Answer:

The average force is 40000 N
