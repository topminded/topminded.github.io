1. similarities of primary cells and secondary cells​


Answer:

The major difference between a primary cell and the secondary cell is that primary cells are the ones that cannot be charged but secondary cells are the ones that are rechargeable.

Explanation:

Battery or cells are referred to as the parallel combination of electrochemical cells.

What are Primary Cells?

-A primary cell or battery is one that cannot easily be recharged after one use, and are discarded following discharge. Most primary cells utilize electrolytes that are contained within absorbent material or a separator  and are thus termed dry cells.

What about Secondary Cells?

-A rechargeable battery, storage battery, or secondary cell, is a type of electrical battery which can be charged, discharged into a load, and recharged many times, as opposed to a disposable or primary battery, which is supplied fully charged and discarded after use.

Answer:

2.How is electrochemical energy produced?

- In electrochemistry, electricity can be generated by movements of electrons from one element to another in a reaction known as redox or oxidation-reduction reaction. Electrochemistry is the study of chemical processes that cause electrons to move.

3. What is primary cell?

-A primary cell is a battery that is designed to be used once and discarded, and not recharged with electricity and reused like a secondary cell. In general, the electrochemical reaction occurring in the cell is not reversible, rendering the cell unrechargeable

4. What is secondary cell?

-In contrast to a disposable or primary battery, which is supplied fully charged and discarded after use, a rechargeable battery, storage battery, or secondary cell can be charged, discharged into a load, and recharged many times.

5. What are the different examples of a primary and a secondary cell?

- Primary batteries are “single use” and cannot be recharged. Dry cells and (most) alkaline batteries are examples of primary batteries. The second type is rechargeable and is called a secondary battery. Examples of secondary batteries include nickel-cadmium (NiCd), lead acid, and lithium ion batteries.

14. Select Primary or Secondary for each statement describing a source. please help! an article written to explain how a bill becomes a law (Primary or Secondary) an autobiography by a nurse who served in the military (Primary or Secondary) an interview with an author about her work (Primary or Secondary) an educational video about the effects of viruses (Primary or Secondary)


1. Primary - an article written to explain how a bill becomes a law would be considered a primary source because it provides firsthand information on the legislative process from the perspective of the author.

2. Primary - an autobiography by a nurse who served in the military would be considered a primary source because it provides firsthand information on the nurse's experiences and perspectives.

3. Secondary - an interview with an author about her work would be considered a secondary source because it provides information about the author's work and opinions, but not firsthand information.

4. Secondary - an educational video about the effects of viruses would be considered a secondary source because it provides information about viruses that has been researched and compiled by others, rather than firsthand information.
