2. What K calls the constituent assembly of legislator


Answer:

The Constituent Assembly, is a term describing one of the three methods by which amendments to the 1987 Constitution of the Philippines may be proposed. The other two modes are via People's Initiative and Constitutional Convention. All three require a majority vote in a national referendum.

Constituent Assembly is composed of all members of the bicameral Congress of the Philippines (Senate and the House of Representatives). It is convened by Congress to propose amendments to the 1987 constitution. Under Article XVII of the Constitution of the Philippines, amendments pass upon a vote of three fourths of all members of Congress, wherein the Congress votes as separate houses for the amendment.[1] The convention of Congress into the term "Constituent Assembly" is not explicitly provided for in the Constitution, since the term is not used in the Constitution with it being only referred to as "Congress upon a vote of three-fourths of all its members".


3. ano ang bagong a sembleya legislative assembly ​


Answer:

The National Assembly of the Philippines (Tagalog: Kapulungáng Pambansâ ng Pilipinas, Spanish: Asamblea Nacional de Filipinas) refers to the legislature of the Commonwealth of the Philippines from 1935 to 1941, and of the Second Philippine Republic during the Japanese occupation. The National Assembly of the Commonwealth was created under the 1935 Constitution, which served as the Philippines' fundamental law to prepare it for its independence from the United States of America.

The National Assembly during the Japanese occupation of the Philippines during the Second World War in the Pacific was created by the 1943 Constitution. With the invasion of the Philippines, the Commonwealth government had gone into exile to the United States. It left behind a skeletal bureaucracy whose officials formed a government under the Japanese Imperial Army. In an attempt to win the loyalty of Filipinos, the Japanese established a nominally independent Republic of the Philippines, with a National Assembly as its legislative body. The Second Philippine Republic was only recognized by the Axis powers.[1]

4. Discuss the policy making of the Philippine legislators?​


Answer:

First, a senator drafts a bill. The idea of the bill comes from the congressman/congresswoman themselves. Second, the senator will introduce the said bill to the senate wherein it undergoes three readings. The first reading will serve as the introduction; the senator discusses the content and purpose of the bill. The second reading holds the votes of the senators if they want to accept the bill or if they want it to proceed to the third reading. The third reading gets the final say if the bill will continue to get to the president or if it will be archived. If the bill successfully passed the reading, the president will read the bill and decide if he wants to sign it or veto it. The bill can only turn into law for three reasons: (1) it is approved by the president, (2) 2/3 of the house override the president's veto, or the president failed to respond to the bill within 30 days. It will then take 15 days for the law to be effective.
