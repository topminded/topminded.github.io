13. why it is important to use the proper tools and equipment in sewing and tailoring?​


Answer:

What has been said already is completely correct. It does take a number of things to complete a project. Not just that, an understanding of the “material” before you try to sew anything. That can be leather, denim, canvas, silk, light weight cotton, polar fleece, etc. Take your pick. Each of them will require the right tools. You might have rotary cutters and for a very light material a rotary cutter can be small but for leather, that can vary depending on the weight of the leather. You can use scissors but tiny, thin ones won’t do well on leather but maybe okay for silks or light cotton type fabrics. Even then you might want longer scissors do a lot of cutting while the small ones might be good for cutting buttonholes or ripping apart sewn mistakes.

Realizing the nature of the fabric will make a difference on the size and type of needle used on the machine. When it comes to leather, once the needle goes through the leather, that hole stays. With other woven fabrics, a needle is pushing aside the strands in the fabric and in both the machine is carrying the thread and locking in place as the needle goes through. Leather needles can be completely different with a knife-like on about half of it to pierce the leather better. Sometimes, a heavy enough needle might work depending on the weight of the leather and even for things like canvas or denim.

With the consideration of the weight of the materials used, the type and weight of thread is another thing to consider. Will it hold up during use of the creation? You won’t use the same thread to sew leather to sew up a silken creation.

While considering the materials and the tools to cut and sew, not every sewing machine would have the strength to sew just anything and get the job done. The things I’ve mentioned are likely something to consider when putting together a tool box for any sewer.

16. how important are the sewing tools in dressmaking or tailoring?​


Explanation:

It Depends.

Some people have lots of tools for marking - like where to stop swing, where the dart goes, or the pleat. In the olden days, they used chalk and slivers of soap.

Some have an assortment of tools for snipping threads, ripping seams, and opening button-holes— but all of that can be done with a razor blade.

Some have lots of tools for measuring. I have at least 20. But you could get by with one simple ruler or measuring tape.

My point is - there’s an entire multi-million dollar industry dedicated to make sewing easier and quicker for those who love to sew. But really you could probably spend about ten dollars to get the items you absolutely have to have and do just fine.

Outside of the actual sewing machine, all you really need is some scissors, thread, pins, a measuring device of any kind, and something to mark with.

Answer:

1.The L-square is a two-piece ruler arranged in an L shape. The L-square is used to create scaled patterns from original sewing patterns.

2.Cutting Tools For Sewing Paper

Papercutting tools are an important tool in your sewing kit. You could use a regular pair of scissors, a special shorter blade scissor for patterns, or even a rotary cutter whose sole use is for cutting paper.

3.Thread clippers are small scissors used for nipping excess thread and parts of the fabric that have frayed or unraveled. ... These scissors also have notches on their blades, which allow you to make the buttonholes without cutting the edge of the fabric.

4.Marking tools are used to transfer the pattern symbols onto the fabric to accurately make the garment. ... The smooth edge makes a solid line marking and is used for delicate fabrics such as silk and chiffon.

5.Definition of tailor's chalk

: a thin flat piece of hard chalk or soapstone used by tailors and seamstresses for making temporary marks on cloth.

6.The importance of defining the tools and equipment in sewing is two-fold. First, it will give you a way to calculate startup costs and, secondly, in learning the functions of sewing tools and equipment, you will be able to define exactly what sort of duties a person in this industry performs.

7.Sewing pins are used to hold fabric together prior to sewing. They temporarily hold the fabric in place when attaching and cutting patterns, or while sewing. ... Sewing pins come in many lengths and thicknesses, and have different pinheads, materials, and tips, each with a specific purpose.

8.Pressing is an up and down movement when you need to open curves and flatten seams. Ironing is a side to side movement used purely to iron out creases.

9.A sleeve board helps you iron a shirt without making a crease in the sleeve. Sleeve boards are a great sewing tool and are useful for much more than just pressing sleeves. More examples. You can put a sleeve board on your ironing board to make the sleeves easier to iron.
