14. It is a type of cell referencing that uses the dollar sign ($) before the cell addresses in the formula that you do not want to change. A. Absolute Referencing B. Mixed Referencing C. Relative Referencing D. Total Referencing


Answer:

A.Absolute references

Explanation:

You can use an absolute reference to keep a row and/or column constant. An absolute reference is designated in a formula by the addition of a dollar sign ($) before the column and row. If it precedes the column or row (but not both), it's known as a mixed reference.

21. Explain the difference between RELATIVE CELL REFERENCING and ABSOLUTE CELLREFERENCING​


Answer:

There are two types of cell references: relative and absolute. Relative and absolute references behave differently when copied and filled to other cells. Relative references change when a formula is copied to another cell. Absolute references, on the other hand, remain constant no matter where they are copied.
