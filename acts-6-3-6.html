10. A force of 6 N acts on a 3 kg object for 10 seconds. 1. What is the object's impulse? 2. What is its change in velocity?​


Answer:

1. 60 Ns

2. 20 m/s

Explanation:

We can use the following equations to solve the problem:

Impulse = Force x Time

Change in velocity = Impulse / Mass

Given:

Force = 6 N

Time = 10 s

Mass = 3 kg

Impulse = Force x Time

Impulse = 6 N x 10 s

Impulse = 60 Ns

Therefore, the object's impulse is 60 Ns.

Change in velocity = Impulse / Mass

Change in velocity = 60 Ns / 3 kg

Change in velocity = 20 m/s

Therefore, the object's change in velocity is 20 m/s.
