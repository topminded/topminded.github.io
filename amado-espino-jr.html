1. 2.paano ipatutupad sa probinsiya ang nilagdaang Executive Order No. 0015-202 ni Governor Amado Espino III sa panahon ng EECQ​


Pangasinan Governor Amado Espino III reminds Pangasinenses that the season is about sacrificing. Espino issued on March 29 travel restrictions to and from the National Capital Region and other area's

LINGAYEN, Pangasinan – Non-essential travel of Pangasinenses to the National Capital Region (NCR), provinces of Bulacan, Cavite, Laguna, and Rizal, as well as the entry of persons from these areas to Pangasinan, are prohibited.

In Executive Order (EO) No. 0027-A-2021 issued on Monday, Governor Amado Espino III said this is in line with the directive of the Inter-Agency Task Force for the management of Emerging Infectious Diseases (IATF-EID).

“Persons from above said places shall be denied entry into Pangasinan for as long as the regulations under IATF-EID Resolution No. 104, series of 2021,” he said.

Espino added the persons from Pangasinan shall not be allowed to travel these places during the effectivity of the regulations under IATF-EID Resolution No. 104.

However, essential travels including but not limited to those by frontline services, government personnel, emergency, humanitarian causes, and movement of cargo, shall remain unhampered as stated in the EO.

“The travel must present competent proof of identity, a document stating workplace address, or any proof of essential travel in border checkpoints for the purpose of assessment,” it added.

Meanwhile, Espino reminded Pangasinenses in his Lenten season message on Monday that this season is about sacrificing.

-Muli po akong nanawagan sa inyong lahat na unahin ang kaligtasan ng inyong sarili at ng inyong pamilya sa paggunita nitong Semana Santa. Isabuhay po natin ito sa pamamagitan ng ibayong pag-iingat, pagtitiis, at pagsasakripisyo para sa kaligtasan ng ating sarili, ng ating pamilya, at ng ating komunidad (I am once again appealing to all of you to put first your own safety and your family in commemorating the Holy Week. Let us live out the season by being cautious, enduring, and sacrificing for our safety, our family, and the community), he said. (PNA)

10. what is division of amado V. Hernandez


Answer:

NAAW-Amado V Hernandez

AMADO V. HERNANDEZ

National Artist for Literature

(September 13, 1903 – May 24, 1970)

Amado V. Hernandez, poet, playwright, and novelist, is among the Filipino writers who practiced “committed art.” In his view, the function of the writer is to act as the conscience of society and to affirm the greatness of the human spirit in the face of inequity and oppression. Hernandez’s contribution to the development of Tagalog prose is considerable — he stripped Tagalog of its ornate character and wrote in prose closer to the colloquial than the “official” style permitted. His novel Mga Ibong Mandaragit, first written by Hernandez while in prison, is the first Filipino socio-political novel that exposes the ills of the society as evident in the agrarian problems of the 50s.

Hernandez’s other works include Bayang Malaya, Isang Dipang Langit, Luha ng Buwaya, Amado V. Hernandez: Tudla at Tudling: Katipunan ng mga Nalathalang Tula 1921-1970, Langaw sa Isang Basong Gatas at Iba Pang Kuwento ni Amado V. Hernandez, Magkabilang Mukha ng Isang Bagol at Iba Pang Akda ni Amado V. Hernandez.

28. Contribution of amado v hernandez in philippine literature


Amado V. Hernandez played an important figure in the Philippine literature. He was more than just a writer, he was a leader and a freedom fighter. He made use of the literary art as a medium to fight for what he believes like social consciousness and liberation. His works are shown in different socio-political beliefs which were believed to be brought about by his experience as an intelligence officer when the Japanese ruled in the Philippines.
