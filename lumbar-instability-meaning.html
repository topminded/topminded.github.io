3. Ano ang meaning ng MACROECONOMIC VOLATILITY AND POLITICAL INSTABILITY


Macroeconomic Volatility and Political Instability:

Ang macroeconomic volatility ay tumutukoy sa pabagu – bagong galaw ng kabuuang ekonomiya o ang mga malawakang pangyayaring pang – ekonomiya na nagdudulot ng pagbabago sa trabaho, pambansang kita, gross domestic product , implasyon, at presyo. Ang mga salik na nabanggit ay ang tuon ng makroekonomiks:

kabuuang antas ng presyo kabuuang produksyon o bilang ng kalakal at paglilingkod kabuuang empleyo ibang bahagi ng mundo at ang ugnayan nito sa panloob na ekonomiya

Ang pagtaas ng kabuuang presyo ay pangunahing pinagtutuunan ng pansin ng mga gumagawa ng batas o patakaran na nakaaapekto sa mga mamamayan sa kabuuan.

Ang kabuuang produksyon o bilang ng kalakal at paglilingkod ang nagiging batayan sa pagsukat sa kakayahan ng isang ekonomiya kung papaano matutugunan ang pangangailangan ng lipunan at ng buong bansa sa kabuuan.

Mahalaga na mapagtuunan ng pansin ang kabuuang empleyo para sa mga nagpaplano ng ekonomiya at bumubuo ng mga patakarang pangkabuhayan upang matiyak na may mapagkukunan ng ikabubuhay ang bawat pamilya sa lipunan.

Kailangan din tingnan ang ibang bahagi ng mundo at ang ugnayan nito sa panloob na ekonomiya sapagkat hindi maihihiwalay ang mga pangyayaring pandaigdigan sa kalagayan ng ekonomiya sa loob ng bansa. Katunayan, may malaking epekto ang kalagayang pang – ekonomiya ng ibang bansa sa ekonomiya ng iab’t – ibang bansa sa buong daigdig.

Ang political instability ay tumutukoy sa kawalan ng matatag na sistemang politikal ng ekonomiya.  Ito ang bahaging kinakailangan maisaayos ng pamahalaan sapagkat ito ay may kinalaman sa pangkalahatang presyo sa ekonomiya. Ito ay bilang pagsisiguro na ang mamamayan ay matutulungan na maitawid ang kanilang mga pangangailangan upang mabuhay ng sapat.

Risk factors

Anyone can develop back pain, even children and teens. These factors might put you at greater risk of developing back pain:

Age.

Back pain is more common as you get older, starting around age 30 or 40.

Lack of exercise.

Weak, unused muscles in your back and abdomen might lead to back pain.

Excess weight.

Excess body weight puts extra stress on your back.

Diseases.

Some types of arthritis and cancer can contribute to back pain.

Improper lifting.

Using your back instead of your legs can lead to back pain.

Psychological conditions.

People prone to depression and anxiety appear to have a greater risk of back pain.

Smoking.

Smokers have increased rates of back pain. This may occur because smoking prompts more coughing, which can lead to herniated disks. Smoking can also decrease blood flow to the spine and increase the risk of osteoporosis.

Prevention

You might avoid back pain or prevent its recurrence by improving your physical condition and learning and practicing proper body mechanics.

To keep your back healthy and strong:

Exercise.

Regular low-impact aerobic activities — those that don't strain or jolt your back — can increase strength and endurance in your back and allow your muscles to function better. Walking and swimming are good choices. Talk with your doctor about which activities you might try.

Build muscle strength and flexibility. Abdominal and back muscle exercises, which strengthen your core, help condition these muscles so that they work together like a natural corset for your back.

Maintain a healthy weight.

Being overweight strains back muscles. If you're overweight, trimming down can prevent back pain.

Quit smoking.

Smoking increases your risk of low back pain. The risk increases with the number of cigarettes smoked per day, so quitting should help reduce this risk.

16. Standard radiograph of the lumbar region


The remaining vertebrae between the top of the sacrum and the last thoracic bone are known as the lumbar vertebrae. The coccyx serves as the terminal vertebrae or rudimentary tail, and the sacral vertebrae are caged within the pelvic bones. The lower back's five vertebrae are represented by these.

Five vertebrae (L1 through L5) make up the lumbar area of the spine, also referred to as the lower back. The lumbar portion of the spine is located between the sacrum and the thoracic, or chest, part of the spine. The lumbar spine often exhibits a lordosis, or modest inward bend.

A pulled muscle is a common ailment that leads to back pain (strain). Back discomfort can occasionally be brought on by medical disorders including a slipped disc, sciatica (a pinched nerve), or ankylosing spondylitis. Rarely, back discomfort may indicate a serious condition like cancer, a fractured bone, or an infection.

20. Why is lumbar puncture contraindicated in obstructive hydrocephalus?


Answer:

Increased intracranial pressure (pressure in the skull) is a contraindication, due to risk of brain matter being compressed and pushed toward the spine. Sometimes, lumbar puncture cannot be performed safely (for example due to a severe bleeding tendency).

23. This is the abnormal curvature of the lumbar spine​


Answer:

Kyphosis is a spinal disorder in which an excessive outward curve of the spine results in an abnormal rounding of the upper back. The condition is sometimes known as "roundback" or—in the case of a severe curve—as "hunchback." Kyphosis can occur at any age, but is common during adolescence.
