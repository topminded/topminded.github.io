12. explain the difference between summer monsoon and winter monsoons


Answer:

Summer monsoons have a dominant westerly component and a strong tendency to converge, rise, and produce rain. Winter monsoons have a dominant easterly component and a strong tendency to diverge, subside, and cause drought.

22. 23. Which of the following statements explains wind convergence? a. When NE monsoons meet SW monsoons in opposite direction b. When NE monsoons have the same direction with the SW monsoons c. When NE monsoons take place d. When SW monsoons take place​


Answer:

Explanation:

bevause it explains wind coverage

Option (a) "When NE monsoons meet SW monsoons in opposite direction" explains wind convergence.

Wind convergence refers to the meeting of two air masses with different directions of movement. In the context of monsoons, wind convergence occurs when the northeast monsoon winds, which blow from the northeast, meet the southwest monsoon winds, which blow from the southwest, in opposite directions over the Indian subcontinent. This convergence of winds leads to the formation of a low-pressure zone, which in turn results in the monsoon rainfall pattern over the region.

30. 8. Which of the following types of monsoon brings heavy rain in the PhilippinesA. East West monsoonB. Northeast morisoonC. Northwest monsoonD. Southwest monsoon​


Answer:

D. Southwest monsoon

Explanation:

The southwest monsoon blows in from the equatorial Pacific, bringing excessive rainfall and gusty winds to the Philippines from June to November. Low season in the Philippines occurs during the habagat season because of frequent rainfall and high humidity. During this season, deadly typhoons (the Eastern Hemisphere's equivalent of hurricanes) can make landfall. A series of flash floods and landslides are regular incidents during this season.
