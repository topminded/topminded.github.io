7. A certain hydrocarbon (a compound con- sisting of carbon and hydrogen) is 82.66% carbon by mass and has a molar mass of 58.12 g. determine the molecular formula of this hydrocarbon.


[tex]\tt{\huge{\green{Solution:}}}[/tex]

Step 1: Assume that the mass of the compound is 100 g.

mass C = 82.66 g

mass H = 100 g - 82.66 g = 17.34 g

Step 2: Calculate the number of moles of each element.

[tex]n \: \text{C = 82.66 g} × \frac{\text{1 mol}}{\text{12.01 g}} = \text{6.883 mol}[/tex]

[tex]n \: \text{H = 17.34 g} × \frac{\text{1 mol}}{\text{1.008 g}} = \text{17.20 mol}[/tex]

Step 3: Represent an empirical formula.

[tex]\text{empirical formula} = \text{C}_{x}\text{H}_{y}[/tex]

Step 4: Divide the number of moles of each element by the least number of moles.

[tex]x = \frac{\text{6.883 mol}}{\text{6.883 mol}} = 1[/tex]

[tex]y = \frac{\text{17.20 mol}}{\text{6.883 mol}} = 2.5[/tex]

Note: Since the subscript of H is not a whole number, which is 2.5, all subscripts must be multiplied by a certain number to become a counting number. In this case, 2.5 must be multiplied by 2 to become 5.

Note: Subscripts are always counting numbers.

Multiplying the subscripts by 2

x = 1 × 2 = 2

y = 2.5 × 2 = 5

Step 5: Write the empirical formula.

[tex]\text{empirical formula} = \text{C}_{2}\text{H}_{5}[/tex]

Step 6: Represent a molecular formula.

[tex]\text{molecular formula} = (\text{C}_{2}\text{H}_{5})_{n}[/tex]

Step 7: Calculate the empirical mass.

empirical mass = (12.01 g × 2) + (1.008 g × 5)

empirical mass = 29.06 g

Step 8: Divide the molar mass by the empirical mass to find the value of n.

[tex]n = \frac{\text{molar mass}}{\text{empirical mass}}[/tex]

[tex]n = \frac{\text{58.12 g}}{\text{29.06 g}}[/tex]

[tex]n = 2[/tex]

Step 9: Multiply the subscripts by the value of n to obtain the molecular formula.

[tex]\text{molecular formula} = (\text{C}_{2}\text{H}_{5})_{2}[/tex]

[tex]\boxed{\text{molecular formula} = \text{C}_{4}\text{H}_{10}}[/tex]

Therefore, the molecular formula of a certain hydrocarbon is C₄H₁₀
