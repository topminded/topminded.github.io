3. Differentiate CASE STUDY and HISTORICAL ANALYSIS?​


Answer:

The main difference between a case study and a historical analysis is that a case study typically focuses on a specific, unique example or instance, while a historical analysis examines broader patterns and trends over time. Case studies are often used to explore complex, context-specific issues in depth, while historical analysis is used to understand the broader historical context and significance of events and phenomena.
