12. 1. It is the back and forth movement of sound.2. It is the transmission of sound waves to locate objects.3. It is a reflection of sound, arriving at thelistener sometime after the direct sound.4. It is any undesirable sound which disturbsthe human or animal life.5. It is the softness or loudness of sound.​


Answer:

1. Sound Waves

Sound waves are called longitudinal waves because the particles move back and forth in the direction of the wave movement.

2. Echolocation

Echolocation - the transmission of sound waves to locate objects.

3. Echo

Echo is distinct, reflected sound wave from a surface.It is a reflection of sound that arrives at the listener with a delay after the direct sound. A true echo is a single reflection of the sound source.

4. Noise Pollution

Noise pollution refers to a sound that is annoying, a nuisance or undesired for the ears and that which can impact the activity or behavior of the animal and human life. ... Noise is regarded as a pollutant majorly because it disrupts the natural flow of hearing or the normal hearing sense.

5. Dynamics

Dynamics - The volume of sound; the loudness or softness of a musical passage.
