17. A demographer predicts that the population, P, of a town t years from now can be modeled by thefunction P(t) = 9t³-t²+20t+10 000. What will the population of the town be five years from now?​


Answer:

The population will be 19,300.

Step-by-step explanation:

Mani lang 'to. Plug and play.

P(t) = 8t³ - 7t² + 200t + 10 000

The given is 10 so change all t's with 10.

P(10) = 8(10)³ - 7(10)² + 200(10) + 10 000

Eliminate the exponents.

P(10) = 8(1,000) - 7(100) + 200(10) + 10 000

Multiply.

P(10) = 8,000 - 700 + 2,000 + 10 000

Add/subtract from left to right.

P(10) = 8(1,000) - 7(100) + 200(10) + 10 000

The outcome is the answer to the question.

P(10) = 19,300

Ans: The population will be 19,300.

19. a demographer predicts that the population P of a town t years from now can be modelled by the function(t) = 6t4-5t3+200t +10000. what will the population of the town be three years from now?​


Answer:

10951

Step-by-step explanation:

Required Answer:

f(3)

Function:

f(t) = 6t⁴ - 5t³ + 200t + 10000

Solution:

f(t) = 6t⁴ - 5t³ + 200t + 10000

=> f(3) = 6(3)⁴ - 5(3)³ + 200(3) + 10000

=> f(3) = 6(81) - 5(27) + 600 + 10000

=> f(3) = 486 - 135 + 600 + 10000

=> f(3) = 351 + 600 + 10000

=> f(3) = 10951
