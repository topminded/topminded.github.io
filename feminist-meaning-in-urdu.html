15. what is the feminist??​


Feminism is a social, political, and cultural movement that seeks to achieve gender equality and dismantle patriarchal systems of power and oppression. At its core, feminism advocates for the rights and empowerment of women, as well as the dismantling of gender-based discrimination and stereotypes that limit people's potential and opportunities.

Feminists work to challenge and transform social norms and cultural practices that reinforce gender inequality and work towards creating a world where all genders are treated with respect, dignity, and equality. Feminism has evolved over time and encompasses a wide range of ideologies and perspectives, including liberal feminism, radical feminism, intersectional feminism, eco-feminism, and more.

In summary, feminists believe in promoting gender equality and challenging societal norms and practices that restrict people's potential and opportunities based on their gender identity. They advocate for the rights and empowerment of all genders and seek to create a more just and equitable world for everyone.

18. what is Urdu relations to the Mongol empire​


Answer:

Urdu & Mongol Empire are not related other than a few distant connections. What is today known as Urdu was known as Hindustani before the 20th century, which evolved due to the fusion of dialect of Shauraseni Prakrit spoken in Delhi, Haryana & Western Uttar Pradesh, with Persian (Turkic influence is low) as it was the lingua franca of the Indian Islamic diaspora during the Mediaeval & Early Modern Age. This language was patronised by the Mughals, who were of Turko-Mongolic descent & claimed descent from Genghis Khan. That is the only connection between the two. Mongols never invaded the major part of India. They had control in Kashmir (Lohana dynasty being a Vassal) & temporarily held sway parts in parts of Western Punjab. Thus the influence of Mongol Culture or even Steppe Culture through Turks is minimal, as all these people whilst ruling India associated themselves with Persian Culture, albeit with influences of Indic cultures as well.
