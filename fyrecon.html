5. where was freon used prior to the ban on CFCs


Answer:

Freon, which is a brand name for a type of chlorofluorocarbon (CFC), was used in a variety of applications prior to the international ban on CFCs due to their harmful effects on the ozone layer.

Explanation:

Some of the most common uses of Freon included:

Refrigeration and Air Conditioning: Freon was widely used as a refrigerant in air conditioning units and refrigerators.

Aerosol Sprays: Freon was also used as a propellant in aerosol sprays such as hairspray, deodorant, and insecticide.

Solvents: Freon was used as a solvent in the production of various consumer goods, such as cleaning products, paints, and adhesives.

Fire Suppression Systems: Freon was used in fire suppression systems to extinguish fires in enclosed spaces such as computer rooms, data centers, and museums.

However, after the scientific discovery that CFCs were depleting the ozone layer, which protects the Earth from harmful UV radiation, the international community agreed to phase out the production and consumption of CFCs. The Montreal Protocol, an international treaty signed in 1987, aimed to reduce the production and consumption of ozone-depleting substances, including CFCs. Today, alternative chemicals and technologies are used in place of CFCs in many applications.

25. Freon in an air-conditioning unit has a volume of 0.75 liter. It is allowed to function in a room where the pressure is about 850 mmHg, assuming that the temperature is in constant state. Find the final pressure of freon when its volume is increased to 1.50 liters.​


Given:

[tex]P_{1} = \text{850 mmHg}[/tex]

[tex]V_{1} = \text{0.75 L}[/tex]

[tex]V_{2} = \text{1.50 L}[/tex]

Unknown:

[tex]P_{2}[/tex]

Solution:

[tex]P_{1}V_{1} = P_{2}V_{2}[/tex]

[tex]P_{2} = P_{1} × \frac{V_{1}}{V_{2}}[/tex]

[tex]P_{2} = \text{850 mmHg} × \frac{\text{0.75 L}}{\text{1.50 L}}[/tex]

[tex]\boxed{P_{2} = \text{425 mmHg}}[/tex]
