What is ventricular fibrillation?

Ventricular fibrillation (V-fib) is a dangerous type of arrhythmia, or irregular heartbeat. It affects your heart’s ventricles.An arrhythmia that starts in your ventricle is called ventricular fibrillation. This occurs when the electrical signals that tell your heart muscle to pump cause your ventricles to quiver (fibrillate) instead.Because sustained V-fib can lead to cardiac arrest and death, it requires immediate medical attention.

What causes ventricular fibrillation?The cause of ventricular fibrillation is not always known but it can occur during certain medical conditions. V-fib most commonly occurs during an acute heart attack or shortly thereafter.

17. Why is it necessary to stimulate papillary muscles in the ventricle slightly earlier than the rest of the ventricular wall myocardium?


Answer: It is necessary to stimulate papillary muscles in the ventricles slightly earlier than the rest of the ventricular wall myocardium because contraction of the papillary muscles exerts tension on the AV valve flaps through the chordae tendineae, preventing valve prolapse and subsequent backflow of blood into the atria.
