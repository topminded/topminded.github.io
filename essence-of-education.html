1. what is the essence of education?


Well, the essence of education is to mold individuals to be skillful someday.

Education is generally understood as a form of learning whereby knowledge, skills and habits are etched out in the minds of an individual through instruction, training, sharing, and communicating. In a nut-shell education is usually understood as a transfer of information and knowledge from one individual to the other. In whatever way knowledge is transferred the ultimate end of education must necessarily be formative. At the end of the day it is these formative effects that will shape and help mould an individual in the way he/she thinks, believes, views, feels, acts and reacts in the society.

The Organisation for Economic Co-operation and Development (OECD), an international economic organisation founded in 1961, identifies three forms of learning: formal education, informal education and non-formal education. Formal education is institutionalized teaching and learning established according to a pre-determined and pre-defined set of curriculum. This means that courses and contents are provided to a school, a college or a university. Formal education is intentional from the learners’ perspective. Informal education is a form of education outside an institutionalised setting of education. It is neither structured nor usually certified. It could either be unschooling education or autodidactic education. Unschooling education means an educational method of learning that rejects compulsory school education as a source of education. This form of education adheres to an education derived from life experiences, domestic responsibilities, family, personal interests and curiosities and social interaction. Autodidactic education is self education or learning on your own and by yourself though it is usually complemented by formal classroom learning. Non-formal education is learning that takes place in a formal educational organisation but it is not recognised within a curriculum or syllabus framework, for example community based programmes, Scouts and Guides etc. The objective of the learner here could be to enhance one’s knowledge and passion for learning.

Whatever maybe the form of education, minimum basic education must be provided to every human being and the Indian Constitution has very eloquently framed the Right to Education as a means that will give compulsory free education to all children below the age of fourteen. Basic education acts as a radar showing and provides the right direction to people. An educational institution is not merely one that only focuses on academic subjects. Rather it is a place that has a lot more role to play. It helps in building social skills leading to healthy relationships with fellow students. This helps us in developing life-long networks. At school, children are also taught the right manners and behaviour, ethics and moral values that will help them become better human beings. This is a cricual period for character and personality development. Elementary school education is an acclaimed and accepted conventional perception and practice of education outside the purview of basic academic education. In truth education must go beyond traditional confines. Our actions in life are the impact of an acquisition of knowledge either through instructions, observations or even assimilation. Education does form the essence of our actions in life. Education is not only the subjects we get in our academic syllabus. It is much more. In reality it means many essential things in life that include ethics, morality, values, principles, etiquette, responsibility, manners, behaviour, relationship, respect and approbation. Education should be a means that will help each one of us answer queries and solve problems, help us set our goals in life, teach us how to deal with people, how to plan, how to handle pressure, how to achieve success handle failures and how to learn to accept things in life. Education is important because it helps shape our overall personality

5. TITLE "THE ESSENCE OF EDUCATIONAL RESEARCH"INSTRUCTION After reading the supplemental materials provided, compose a three-paragraph essay entitled "The Essence of Educational Research". ​


ESSENCE OF EDUCATIONAL RESEARCH INSTRUCTIONThe essence of educational research lies in its ability to contribute to the understanding and improvement of educational practices. Through research, educators and educational institutions can gather valuable information that can help them make informed decisions and take actions that will lead to positive outcomes for students. Educational research also plays a critical role in advancing knowledge and understanding of teaching and learning, helping to identify best practices and areas for improvement.One important aspect of educational research is its emphasis on empirical evidence. Rather than relying on anecdotal or subjective information, educational research is grounded in the scientific method and seeks to collect and analyze data in a systematic and objective manner. This rigorous approach helps to ensure that research findings are reliable and valid, and that they can be used to make informed decisions about educational policies and practices.Another key aspect of educational research is its focus on practical applications. While research can certainly contribute to theoretical knowledge, its primary aim is to identify practical strategies and interventions that can improve educational outcomes. As such, educational research often involves collaboration between researchers and educators, with the goal of translating research findings into effective teaching and learning practices.

In conclusion, educational research is a critical component of effective educational practice. By providing empirical evidence and practical recommendations, research helps educators and educational institutions make informed decisions and take actions that can improve outcomes for students. As such, a commitment to ongoing research and evidence-based practice is essential for promoting the success and well-being of students in educational settings.

23. What is the essence of a voters’ education program?


Answer:

Voter Education Project (VEP) raised and distributed foundation funds to civil rights organizations for voter education and registration work in the southern United States from 1962 to 1992. The project was federally endorsed by the Kennedy administration in hopes that the organizations of the ongoing Civil Rights Movement would shift their focus away from demonstrations and more towards the support of voter registration.

The VEP helped make great inroads in the registration of voters, especially in rural areas. Contrary to the initial hopes of the Kennedy administration, the VEP did not reduce the violent realities of resistance to integration found in the South and highlighted by news stations around the world. In the Deep South, white resistance to Black voting rights turned out to be even more violent than their opposition to integrating lunch counters and bus depots. Instead of diminishing, news stories of police repression, brutality, bombings, and murders increased as white political leaders, the Ku Klux Klan, and White Citizen Councils used arrests, terrorism, and economic retaliation to prevent Blacks from voting.

25. anatomy and physiology: their essence in the lives of the movement practitioners and educators?​ 100 words essay


ANATOMY AND PHYSIOLOGY

Anatomy comes from the Greek (anatomia) which means "to open a piece". Literally, anatomy comes from 2 words, namely "Ana" which means part / separate, and "tomi" which means cut. Therefore, Anatomy can be defined as a special science that studies the structure of human organs both as a whole and in parts and studies their physical relationship with other body parts.

The physiology also comes from Greek which means as a science that studies how an organism performs its main functions. Literally, Physiology comes from 2 words, namely "Physis" which means nature or how it works and "logos" (Logi) which means science. Therefore, physiology can be defined as the study of how the function of each body tissue in living things.

Anatomy and physiology are two interrelated disciplines, both theoretically and practically. Both have an important role in the development of the medical world.

What Is The Essence Of Anatomy And Physiology?

1. As a basic science in the field of health

Studying anatomy and physiology is very necessary in the development of the world of health. This relates to the following:

→Studying the physiological anatomy of the structure of the human body

→Useful for knowing the normal and abnormal conditions of a body part

→Useful in determining the diagnosis of disease and how to treat it

→Understand healthy and unhealthy body metabolic processes

→Useful in the interests of surgical and gynecological processes, e.g. knowing the location of nerves, blood vessels, etc.

→Increase understanding of the morphology of certain tissues and organs and their functions

2. As a basis for studying the structure and growth patterns of both plants and animals.

Studying the anatomy of plants and animals is also needed in the development of studies in the fields of biology, veterinary medicine, animal husbandry, agriculture, etc.

3. As a basic science in developing research and technological innovation in the medical field

Many medical experts have succeeded in studying anatomy and physiology for years to find many findings that can be used as references in the medical world. These findings are widely used by modern researchers to further develop technological innovations in the medical field.

4. As a basic science for artists, especially in terms of fine arts or visual art

Anatomy and physiology are not only useful in the field of health; they are also needed by artists. Much has been learned about the visual arts, including proportion, texture, gesture, color, and human expression. Therefore, anatomy and physiology are also important to be studied by artists in order to be able to describe or describe humans accurately and proportionally.
