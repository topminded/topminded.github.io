5. weather condition during typhoon (signal no. 1-4) ​


Answer:

Weather conditions during typhoons vary depending on the strength and signal level of the typhoon. Here are the general weather conditions for each signal level:

Signal No. 1: Winds of 30 to 60 kph are expected within the next 36 hours. The public is advised to stay updated on the latest weather reports and to take precautionary measures against potential flooding and landslides.

Signal No. 2: Winds of 61 to 120 kph are expected within the next 24 hours. The public is advised to stay indoors, avoid traveling, and prepare for possible power outages and damage to infrastructure.

Signal No. 3: Winds of 121 to 170 kph are expected within the next 18 hours. The public is advised to stay indoors and to be alert for potential storm surges, landslides, and flash floods.

Signal No. 4: Winds of over 171 kph are expected within the next 12 hours. The public is advised to take immediate action to protect themselves and their property. Evacuation may be necessary in certain areas, and damage to infrastructure and agriculture is likely.

22. 1. What is the meaning of PAGASA? 2. How many typhoon signal numbers are there in total? 3. What is the speed of the lowest signal number? Speed of the highest signal? 4. What is the category (name) of the storm if its: a. Signal #1 b. Signal #2 c. Signal #3 d. Signal #4 e. Signal #5


Answer:

PAGASA stands for "Philippine Atmospheric, Geophysical, and Astronomical Services Administration." It is the national meteorological and hydrological agency of the Philippines.

There are four typhoon signal numbers in total, which are issued by PAGASA to warn the public of incoming typhoons or tropical cyclones.

The speed of the lowest signal number, which is Signal No. 1, is 30-60 kilometers per hour (kph). The speed of the highest signal number, which is Signal No. 4, is 171-220 kph.

The category (name) of the storm depends on its maximum sustained winds, as follows:

a. Signal #1: Tropical depression (maximum sustained winds of up to 61 kph)

b. Signal #2: Tropical storm (maximum sustained winds of 62-88 kph)

c. Signal #3: Typhoon (maximum sustained winds of 89-117 kph)

d. Signal #4: Severe typhoon (maximum sustained winds of 118-220 kph)

e. Signal #5: Super typhoon (maximum sustained winds of more than 220 kph)

25. safety measures during typhoon (signal no. 1-4) ​


Answer:

Safety measures during typhoon vary depending on the signal number declared by the weather bureau. Here are some general safety measures to follow for each signal level:

Signal No. 1:

Listen to the radio, TV, or check online for updates and announcements

Secure your home, including doors, windows, and roof

Trim tree branches that may fall on power lines

Prepare emergency supplies, such as food, water, and flashlights

Stay indoors as much as possible and avoid unnecessary travel

Signal No. 2:

Follow the same measures for Signal No. 1

Prepare sandbags to prevent flooding in your home

Stay away from low-lying areas and riverbanks

Evacuate if advised by local authorities

Turn off all electrical appliances and unplug them

Signal No. 3:

Follow the same measures for Signal No. 2

Secure important documents, such as IDs and passports, in a waterproof container

Stay in the strongest part of your home, such as the basement or ground floor

Be ready to evacuate at any time

Signal No. 4:

Follow the same measures for Signal No. 3

Evacuate to a safe place if advised by local authorities

Do not go outside during the typhoon, as it may be extremely dangerous

Stay calm and listen to updates and instructions from authorities
