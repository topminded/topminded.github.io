Answer:

If Anita has 81 roses and 27 vases, then the ratio of roses to vases is 81/27, which simplifies to 3/1.

To find how many roses can be put in 9 vases, we can use the ratio 3/1 to set up a proportion:

3/1 = x/9

Where x represents the number of roses that can be put in 9 vases.

To solve for x, we can cross-multiply and simplify:

3(9) = 1(x)

27 = x

So Anita can put 27 roses in 9 vases.

To find how many roses can be put in 3 vases, we can set up another proportion:

3/1 = x/3

Where x represents the number of roses that can be put in 3 vases.

Cross-multiplying and simplifying:

3(3) = 1(x)

9 = x

So Anita can put 9 roses in 3 vases.

Finally, to find how many roses can be put in 1 vase, we can set up another proportion:

3/1 = x/1

Where x represents the number of roses that can be put in 1 vase.

Cross-multiplying and simplifying:

3(1) = 1(x)

3 = x

So Anita can put 3 roses in 1 vase.

23. 5 step in making origami vase design​


Answer:

Step 1: Cut Paper Into Pieces

- You need 12.5 cm x 5 cm size paper strips of 36 pieces in each color. (Total 72 pieces)

- Take A4 or any size papers of two different colors and cut required number of strips to size

Step 2: Fold the Paper Strips

- Take one strip of any color and fold it in half longitudinally

- Once again fold it in half, so that the width of fold is 12.5 mm, having 4 folds

- Now fold at middle and then at 1/4th portion to form like a square

- Finally fold edges inside and make a "V" shape as shown

Step 3: Start Connecting the Folded Strips

- Take one pink folded strip and one light green strip

- Insert light green strip through the pink one as shown. This will form a "L" shape with both the colored strips

- Continue connecting strips in a zigzag manner

Step 4: Make Rings

- Connect 9 pieces of pink and 9 pieces of light green to form one length of chain

- Fold the chain to a round shape and insert one end to another to close the ring

- Make 4 such rings with folded strips

Step 5: Close Bottom

- Place one ring on a piece of card stock and draw the circular outline

- Cut the card stock to size and attach the bottom with glue

Step 6: Glue All 4 Segments Together

- Glue on edges of each ring and join them together.

- Allow the glue to dry.

30. there are 3 flowers in vase A and 2 flowers in vase b 6 more flowers are added to vase a how many flowers must be added to vase b so that the ratio of the number of flowers in vase a to the number of flowers in vase b remains the same as before?


Answer:

6 flowers

GIVEN:

Vase A = 3 flowers

Vase B = 2 flowers

6 more flowers are added to vase a

Step-by-step explanation:

Ratio of Vase A to Vase B = 3:2

Vase A + 6 more flowers

3 flowers + 6 more flowers = 9 flowers

3 : 2 = 9 : x or [tex]\frac{3}{2}[/tex] : [tex]\frac{9}{x}[/tex]

3x = 18

x = 6

6 flowers must be added to vase b so that the ratio of the number of flowers in vase a to the number of flowers in vase b remains the same as before.
