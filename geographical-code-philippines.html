5. advantages and disadvantages of philippines geographical location


The Advantages and Disadvantages of Philippines Geographical Location

The Advantages of Philippines Geographical Location

It is strategically located which allows trade relations with many countries, both in Asia and the West. The Philippines is one of the gateway countries to Asia.Filipinos speak English as a second language, making the Philippines one of the major providers of BPO services in the world.The Philippines has rich natural resources such as coastal beaches and mountains that are used for Tourism. The Philippines has a rich diverse cultural.

The Disadvantages of Philippines Geographical Location

The Philippines is strategically located in the middle of the Pacific Ocean, which allows for close trade relations with many countries in Asia and the West. The Philippines also has vast oceans around the archipelago which can also cause smuggling problems of illegal imports and exports of goods to and from the country so that the government can lose a source of revenue as no appropriate taxes are levied.The Philippines has scattered islands that are a barrier to fast communication across the archipelago, making communication very slow.The Philippine government center is far from the regions, especially in the distant mountainous provinces, which cannot support the full management of the regions. They cannot provide direct support in terms of services related to livelihood, education, and overall community grievances.To protect and defend the Philippines with its scattered islands requires armed forces. This requires a sufficient budget to supply the logistics used for the armed forces such as weapons, ships, airplanes, etc. However, this problem is compounded because the government does not have enough budget to supply the necessary logistics.There are different cultures, dialects, and ways of life of different groups of people living on different islands making it difficult to unite these groups for support from other countries.There are inadequate infrastructure issues such as roads, bridges, docks, airports, and electricity in the remote areas. Therefore, it is difficult for the Philippine government to reach the communities to provide needs such as health, education, and livelihood programs
