12. why does the larva and adult stages in complete metamorphosis help an insect species to survive?


There are several reasons why the larval and adult stages in complete metamorphosis (also known as holometabolism) help insects to survive. One reason is that the larval stage is often adapted for feeding and growing, while the adult stage is adapted for reproduction. This separation of functions allows each stage to be optimized for its specific role, which can improve the overall fitness of the species.

Another reason is that the larval and adult stages often occupy different habitats or niches, which can help the species to avoid competition and reduce the risk of predation. For example, some insect larvae may live in the soil, where they can feed on plant roots, while the adult insects may live in the trees, where they can feed on leaves or nectar. This habitat switching can increase the chances of survival for both the larvae and the adults.

Additionally, the process of metamorphosis itself can provide some benefits for the insect. For instance, the transformation from larva to adult involves a radical change in body form and physiology, which can help the insect to escape from predators or parasites that may have targeted the larval stage. This can also enable the adult insect to move to a new habitat or food source, increasing its chances of survival and reproduction.

Overall, the larval and adult stages in complete metamorphosis provide a range of benefits that can help insect species to survive and thrive in a variety of environments.
