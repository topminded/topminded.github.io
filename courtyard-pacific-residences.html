1. The area of a square courtyard is equal to the area of a rectangular courtyard whose length is eight meters more than the side of the square courtyard and whose width is six meters LESS than the side of the square courtyard. Find the dimensions of the square and rectangular courtyards.


Answer:

The side of the square is 24 meters.

The length of the rectangle is 32 meters and the width is 18 meters.

Step-by-step explanation:

Area of a square and rectangle

The area of a square can be calculated by the formula:

A = [tex]s^{2}[/tex]

where s is the sides of the square.

The area of a rectangle can be calculated by the formula:

A = l x w

where l is the length and w is the width.

In this problem, we are given three mathematical statements. We need to know the dimensions of the square and rectangle.

Step 1: Translate the mathematical statements given into working mathematical equations.

Statement 1: The area of a square courtyard is equal to the area of a rectangular courtyard

Equation 1: [tex]Area_{square} = Area_{rectangle}[/tex]

                  [tex]s^{2}[/tex] =  l x w

Statement 2: length is eight meters more than the side of the square

Equation 2: l = s + 8

Statement 3: width is six meters less than the side of the square

Equation: w = s - 6

Step 2: Substitute equations 2 and 3 to equation 1 to get the whole mathematical equation of the problem. Simplify the equation.

  [tex]s^{2}[/tex] =  l x w

  [tex]s^{2}[/tex] =  ( s + 8 ) x ( s - 6)

  [tex]s^{2}[/tex] =  

 [tex]s^{2}[/tex] -  

0 = 2s - 48

2s = 48

s = 24

Step 3: Substitute the resulting value of s to equations 2 and 3 to get the value of the length and width.

l = s + 8

l = 24 + 8

l = 32

w = s - 6

w = 24 - 6

w = 18

Therefore, the side of the square is 24 meters while the length of the rectangle is 32 meters and the width is 18 meters.

12. The town of Brookfield sent out a survey to its residents to determine what new landmark they should build in the City Center. Of the residents who were surveyed 2/5, of the residents voted for a park, 1/4 of the residents voted for a garden, and the remaining residents voted for a zoo. If a total of 14,000 residents were surveyed, how many residents voted for a zoo?


The proportion of residents who voted for a park is 2/5, and the proportion who voted for a garden is 1/4. Therefore, the proportion of residents who voted for a zoo is:

1 - (2/5 + 1/4) = 1 - (8/20 + 5/20) = 1 - (13/20) = 7/20

If 7/20 of the residents voted for a zoo, and a total of 14,000 residents were surveyed, the number of residents who voted for a zoo is:

(7/20) x 14,000 = 4,900

Therefore, 4,900 residents voted for a zoo.

26. The length of a rectangular courtyard given by the expression 3x-1. If the area is given by the expression 3x²+2x-1. Find the width of a courtyard.


Answer:

The width is x + 1

Step-by-step explanation:

Formula for the Area

A = bh

The given area 3x-1 as the length and 3x²+2x-1 as the area

Let w represent the width

3x²+2x-1 = (3x-1)(w)

Divide both sides by 3x-1

[tex]\frac{3x^{2} +2x-1}{x-1}[/tex] = w

x+1 = w

The width is x + 1.

Checking:

A = bh

3x²+2x-1 = (3x-1)(x+1)

3x²+2x-1 = 3x²+2x-1

(number of residents who listen to radio) - (number of residents who watch both TV and listen to radio) = number of residents who listen to radio only

(total number of residents) = 300 + 270 - 120 = 450
