26. How do you understand these words of Jesus,"Blessed are those who have not seen and have believed"?​

Answer:

“Blessed are those who have not seen but believed,” was Jesus' message to Thomas and to others who doubted Jesus and his words. This are also his words to us today. Every time we doubt God’s love and mercy, we are like Thomas. Every time we are not sure of God’s loving presence in our lives, we are like Thomas and the other doubting disciples.

“My Lord and my God!” were Thomas’ only words. And Jesus told Thomas: “Have you come to believe because you have seen me? Blessed are those who have not seen and have believed.”

We may not have seen the Lord through our naked eyes, but we can see him through our hearts. Today, we continue to give the same declaration as Thomas, “My Lord and my God!” The disciples stood their ground and stood witness to Jesus as Lord and God throughout centuries.

My mom, she sacrifices everything just for us. And god didn't let her to fell down. Because she believes in god.

My classmates and I were practicing for our performance in Nutri Jingle (this one is a singing-dancing contest in which the lyrics are revolving around the theme for the school year’s nutrition month theme) when, all of the sudden, it rained heavily. Some of us panicked, asking me how are we supposed to go back to our homes since I am the leader of the group. At first, I panicked, too, but calm down after a while and said that I will wait for all of them to be safe and sound in their homes before I go to mine. Some protested, saying that it will cause me hunger and shorter time to get enough sleep but I insisted.
