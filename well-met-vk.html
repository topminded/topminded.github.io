4. Find the measure of arc VK and m<C if m<G = 60°.Notice: Offensive, incomplete, or irrelevant explanations will be reported. ​


✏️CIRCLE

[tex]\red{••••••••••••••••••••••••••••••••••••••••••••••••••}[/tex]

[tex]\underline{\mathbb{PROBLEM:}}[/tex]

Find the measure of arc VK and m∠C if m∠G = 60°.

[tex]\red{••••••••••••••••••••••••••••••••••••••••••••••••••}[/tex]

[tex]\underline{\mathbb{ANSWER:}}[/tex]

[tex]\qquad\LARGE\rm» \:\: \green{m\overset{\frown}{VK} = 120 \degree}[/tex]

[tex]\qquad\LARGE\rm» \:\: \green{m \angle C = 120 \degree}[/tex]

[tex]\red{••••••••••••••••••••••••••••••••••••••••••••••••••}[/tex]

[tex]\underline{\mathbb{SOLUTION:}}[/tex]

- The measure of an arc is twice the measure of inscribed angle that intercepts it.

[tex] \rm m\overset{\frown}{VK} = 2(m\angle G)[/tex]

[tex] \rm m\overset{\frown}{VK} = 2(60 \degree)[/tex]

[tex] \rm m\overset{\frown}{VK} = 120 \degree[/tex]

- The measure of arc VK is 120 degrees. Now solve for the inscribed angle C which intercepts arc VGK.

[tex] \rm m \angle C = \frac{1}{2} (m\overset{\frown}{VGK}) \\ [/tex]

- Solve arc VGK as it sum with arc VK is 360°.

[tex] \rm m\overset{\frown}{VGK} + m\overset{\frown}{VK} = 360 \degree[/tex]

[tex] \rm m\overset{\frown}{VGK} + 120 \degree = 360 \degree[/tex]

[tex] \rm m\overset{\frown}{VGK} = 360 \degree - 120 \degree[/tex]

[tex] \rm m\overset{\frown}{VGK} = 240\degree[/tex]

- Thus, the measure of arc VGK is 240 degrees. Now solve for the angle C that is half the measure of arc VGK.

[tex] \rm m \angle C = \frac{1}{2} (m\overset{\frown}{VGK}) \\ [/tex]

[tex] \rm m \angle C = \frac{1}{2}(240 \degree) \\ [/tex]

[tex] \rm m \angle C = 120 \degree[/tex]

[tex]\therefore[/tex] The measure of arc VK is 120 degrees while the measure of angle C is also 120 degrees.

6. 18. What value of k will make the equation V2k + 1 = Vk + 8 true?A. 6B. 7C. 8D.9​


[tex]\large\mathcal{ » \: QUESTION \: : }[/tex]

What value of k will make the equation V2k + 1 = Vk + 8 true?

[tex]\large\mathcal{» \: GIVEN \: :}[/tex]

[tex] \huge \tt \: V2k + 1 = Vk + 8 [/tex]

[tex]\large\mathcal{» \: SOLUTION\: :}[/tex]

Lets solve for k

[tex] \tt \: v2k + 1 = vk + 8[/tex]

Step 1: Add -kv to both sides.

[tex] \tt \: 2kv+1+−kv=kv+8+−kv[/tex]

[tex] \tt \: kv+1=8[/tex]

Step 2: Add -1 to both sides.

[tex] \tt \: kv+1+−1=8+−1[/tex]

[tex] \tt \: kv=7[/tex]

Step 3: Divide both sides by v.

[tex] \tt \: \frac{kv}{v} = \frac{7}{v} [/tex]

[tex] \tt \: k = \frac{7}{v} [/tex]

[tex]\large\mathcal{» \:ANSWER :}[/tex]

[tex]\huge \tt»\color{darkviolet} \: k = 7[/tex]

there are 8 ways

1.Get to Know Your Audience

The first step in meeting customer expectations is simply establishing a clear picture of who, exactly, your audience is.

2.Make Sure You’re Reaching the Right Buyers

Most companies launch their products and services with a clear idea of who their ideal buyers are.

3.Look for New Ways to Meet Customer Needs

Once you’ve identified who your most valuable audience is and what it is that they’re looking for, it should be your goal to continuously find new ways to better meet their needs.

4.Set Clear Standards for Your Support Team

As the team that interacts with your customers most often, your support team has a major impact on whether or not your company meets customer expectations.

5.Be as Transparent as Possible

One of the most important factors in whether you’re able to meet customer expectations is whether you take the time to help them set accurate expectations in the first place.

6.Develop a Customer-centric Culture

Many companies put the responsibility of managing customer happiness solely on their support teams.

7. Collect Feedback Regularly

As you work towards meeting customer expectations, the best way to gauge whether your efforts are successful is to collect feedback on a regular basis.

8.Scope Out Your Competitors

If a customer has worked with one of your competitors in the past, their expectations of your company will be largely based on that previous experience.
