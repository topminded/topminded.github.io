26. Ethics in gathering information through media and technology


Answer:

The communicator's Place in the System Ethics in gathering information ... thus ensuring the technological and media convergence, but cannot offer.

Answer:

Abstract

The communicator’s Place in the System Ethics in gathering information (newsgathering) intended for mass communication is facing challenges due to an increase in electronic sources and the development of social media. Documentation limited to the electronic environment is leading to a decreased quality of press products and an increased mistrust in information environments. The purpose of this study is to highlight the way in which interpersonal communication encourages the gathering of new information, necessary for impacting news. However, heavy news can seldom be generated without interpersonal communication. But, at the same time, the chances of creating a quality media product increase by associating the unique information directly obtained by the journalist with data obtained from the online sector and from interventions in social media. The process of journalistic communication consists of two distinct stages: 1) from the source to the journalist and the newsroom (editor); 2) from the newsroom (publishing company) to the public (Szabo 1999, 2011). Under these circumstances, the journalist’s activity classically unfolds on the unseen side of the information route. The journalist contacts his/her sources, employing various work techniques with these, then draws up a journalistic product. Except for some elements present in live broadcasts, the public only sees the final product, and not the actual elements of production. Currently, professional journalists are often under online pressure. Their readers, as well as their viewers or listeners, are eager to find out the latest and most interesting news directly from them. The public will access the units of content using the tools at hand, and should they not find what they are looking for, they will direct their search to different places. Thus, the journalist is in an information loop, collecting the information from the sources, and, in turn, instantaneously becoming a source for the public.
