29. 1. How concretes are constructed? 2. When doing a cement mixture upon constructing a concrete, what are the things you need to consider to create a strong and durable concrete?


Answer:

1. Concrete is typically constructed by mixing cement, water, and aggregates (such as sand, gravel, or crushed stone) together to form a paste that hardens over time. To construct concrete structures, the mixture is poured into a formwork or mold, where it is allowed to set and cure until it reaches its desired strength.

2. When creating a cement mixture for concrete, there are several things that need to be considered to ensure that the resulting concrete is strong and durable. These include:

- Water-to-cement ratio: The amount of water used in the mixture should be carefully controlled, as too much water can weaken the concrete's strength and durability. Generally, a lower water-to-cement ratio results in a stronger and more durable concrete.

- Type of cement: Different types of cement have different properties and strengths, so it's important to choose the right type of cement for the intended use of the concrete.

- Aggregates: The type and size of aggregates used can also affect the strength and durability of the concrete. Larger aggregates can result in a stronger concrete, but may also make it more difficult to work with and finish.

- Mixing: The mixture should be thoroughly mixed to ensure that all components are evenly distributed. Overmixing can cause the concrete to lose strength, while undermixing can result in a weaker concrete.

- Curing: After the concrete has been poured, it should be kept moist and at a consistent temperature to allow it to cure properly. This can help to improve its strength and durability over time.

By carefully considering these factors and taking steps to ensure that the concrete is mixed, poured, and cured properly, it is possible to create a strong and durable concrete that can withstand the test of time.
