9. how community and society connected to urbanization​


Urbanization refers to the process of increasing the proportion of a population living in urban areas. As cities and towns grow, they become centers of economic, social, and cultural activity, which can have a significant impact on both the community and society.

Communities are groups of people who live in the same area and have shared interests, values, and goals. Urbanization can bring people from different communities together, creating new and diverse communities in urban areas. These new communities may have different needs and priorities than the communities they came from, and they may also face new challenges such as housing affordability, access to services, and social inequality.

Society, on the other hand, refers to the larger social structure and systems that govern and organize human behavior. Urbanization can have a profound impact on society, as the growth of cities can create new economic opportunities, social networks, and cultural exchanges. However, it can also lead to social disparities and inequalities, as some groups may benefit more from urbanization than others.

Overall, community and society are closely connected to urbanization, as the growth of cities and towns can have a significant impact on both the local community and the larger social structure.
