Si Lucia ng Syracuse (283–304), na kilala rin bilang Saint Lucy o Saint Lucia (Latin: Sancta Lucia), ay isang martir na Kristiyano na namatay sa panahon ng Pag-uusig sa Diocletianic. 16. St Therese developed the theme, body of Christ


Answer:

If you were to really take a good look at St. Therese’s childhood, the last thing you would think is that she would be apt to accept any more suffering. In fact, the idea that she would accept suffering and also to invite suffering makes her rather extraordinary. I believe that her desire to be consoled as a child and even into her teen years caused her to have an intimate and familiar kinship with Jesus and his own suffering. Therese was amazing - anyone else in her situation would have run from suffering - she saw so much of it and took it on without hesitation.

When Therese was very little, her mother died and she was left to be raised by her father and older sisters. Her father doted on her, he doted on her so much that it was fairly evident that Therese was his favorite. Her father’s deep affection should have been enough to keep her close to home, but she was being propelled forward by a great cause. Even in her father’s love, there was sorrow, a sadness, a calling.

27. It was completed by fr. Diego Cera at st. Joseph parish church in Las Pinas and as declared as national cultural treasure of the Philippines in 2003. ​


Answer:

bamboo organ

Explanation:

The Las Piñas Bamboo Organ in St. Joseph Parish Church in Las Piñas, Philippines, is a 19th-century church organ. It is known for its unique organ pipes; of its 1031 pipes, 902 are made of bamboo. It was completed after 6 years of work in 1824 by Father Diego Cera, the builder of the town's stone church and its first resident Catholic parish priest.[1]
