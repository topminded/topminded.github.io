5. How can you prevent hacking?


Answer:

Preventing hacking can be a complex task, as hackers can use a variety of techniques to gain unauthorized access to systems and networks. However, there are several strategies that can be used to reduce the risk of hacking:

Use strong and unique passwords: Strong passwords that are unique to each account can make it more difficult for hackers to gain access.Keep software and systems up to date: Software and system updates often include security patches that can address known vulnerabilities.Use anti-virus and anti-malware software: Anti-virus and anti-malware software can help to detect and remove malicious software that may be used by hackers to gain access to systems.Implement a firewall: A firewall can be used to control access to a network, and can help to block unauthorized access attempts.Use network segmentation: Network segmentation can be used to limit the potential damage of a successful hack by isolating different parts of a network.Educate employees: Employees should be educated about the risks of hacking and how to recognize and prevent attacks.Use encryption: Encrypting data can help to protect it from unauthorized access, even if a hacker is able to gain access to the system.Regularly monitor and audit systems: Regularly monitoring and auditing systems can help to identify potential vulnerabilities and suspicious activity.Implement a incident response plan: Having a plan in place in case of a security incident can help minimize the damage and get the organization back to normal operations as soon as possible.

It's important to note that no system can be completely hack-proof, so it's important to have a comprehensive security strategy in place, and to continuously review, update and improve it as technology and threats change.

There are two main factors that determine what type of hacker an individual is: their motives and legality of their actions. Hackers are divided into three types—white, black, and grey hat, a naming system that was derived from old western films, where the protagonists would always wear white hats and vice versa for villain characters

1. Black Hat

Black hat hackers are normally responsible for creating malware, which is frequently used to infiltrate computerized networks and systems. They’re usually motivated by personal or financial gain, but can also participate in espionage, protests, or merely enjoy the thrill. Black hat hackers can be anyone from amateurs to highly experienced and knowledgeable individuals looking to spread malware, steal private data, like login credentials, along with financial and personal information. Upon accessing their targets and depending on their motives, black hat hackers can either steal, manipulate, or destroy system data.

2. White Hat

Also known as “ethical hackers,” they’re often employed or contracted by companies and governmental entities, working as security specialists looking for vulnerabilities. While they employ the same methods as black hat hackers, they always have permission from the system’s owner, making their actions completely legal. White hat hackers implement strategies like penetration tests, monitor in-place security systems, along with vulnerability assessments. Ethical hacking, the term used to describe the nature of a white hat hackers’ actions, can even be learned through independent sources, training, conferences, and certifications.

3. Grey Hat

As the name suggests, these individuals utilize aspects from black and white hat hackers, but will usually seek out vulnerabilities in a system without an owner’s permission or knowledge. While they’ll report any issues they encounter to the owner, they’ll also request some sort of compensation or incentive. Should the owner not respond or reject their proposition, a grey hat hacker might exploit the newfound flaws. Grey hat hackers aren’t malicious by nature, but do seek to have their efforts rewarded. Since grey hat hackers don’t have permission to access the system by its owner, their actions are ultimately considered illegal, despite any alarming findings they might reveal.

19. how do hackers hack your password?


How do hackers hack your password?

Hackers are people who are gaining unauthorized access to your computer or system. Their activity is called hacking. This is considered to be an illegal activity. These people could apply various activities to gain access to your system or computer without you knowing it. Some of their activities are the following:

Phising - this is a technique that hackers applied to their victims by means of showing you a link to a webpage or site that would make you input your account details (such as username, passwords, account number) to a banking website or any website. They could make it appear like it is a legit website. Then, once the victim has input his or her details in the form, the hacker could get this and used their obtained information to log in to the website. Phishing is considered an effective way of the hacker to get your details. To prevent this, do not click on links which you are not sure of and always ask first your bank or service provider to verify if they are really in need of your details.  

Brute Force Attack - this technique applies when the hacker would want to guess your password or credentials of your online account. Normally, they would have a software that could perform brute force guessing on letters of your password. The software can compare each letter of your password until they would guess the right key for it. Once that they have obtained all the correct letters, they can use it to log in your account. To prevent this attack, always use a strong password. Stronger passwords are harder and longer to guess using brute force attack.
