3. Which of the following medical procedure is an open-heart surgery that involves replacing the damaged artery in the heart by an artery from another part of the body? a. atherectomy b. bypass surgery c. angioplasty


Answer:

C

Explanation:

Coronary bypass surgery redirects blood around a section of a blocked or partially blocked artery in your heart. The procedure involves taking a healthy blood vessel from your leg, arm or chest and connecting it below and above the blocked arteries in your heart.

13. What is cataract surgery? How many or what are its risks when doing cataract surgery?


Answer:

Cataract Surgery is the removal of the natural lens of the eye that has developed an opacification and its replacement with an intraocular lens.

The risks of cataract surgery may worsen pre-existing eye conditions, such as glaucoma, uveitis (inflammatory eye disease) or corneal diseases. Rarely, the lens implant may dislocate into a wrong position, with time.
