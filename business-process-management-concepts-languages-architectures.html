1. IT Architecture concepts and What is Software Architecture. ​


Answer:

Software architecture is a set of concepts and principles that indicate a blueprint or plan for a software system. It refers to the fundamental elements of a software system, including its components, modules, and interfaces, as well as the relationships and connections between these elements. Software architecture provides an overall framework and high-level model for building software, helping to understand the system's workings and to make decisions about client and business needs.

IT architecture, on the other hand, refers to the concepts and principles that describe a blueprint or plan for aligning technology with business. It refers to the fundamental elements of an IT system, including hardware, software, networks, and data, as well as the relationships and connections between these elements. IT architecture provides an overall framework and high-level model for aligning technology with business, helping to understand the system's workings and to make decisions about business technology needs.
