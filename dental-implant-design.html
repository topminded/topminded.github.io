13. what is the meaning of implantation​


Answer:

In humans, implantation is the stage of pregnancy at which the embryo adheres to the wall of the uterus. At this stage of prenatal development, the conceptus is called a blastocyst. It is by this adhesion that the embryo receives oxygen and nutrients from the mother to be able to grow.

Explanation:

Implantation happens very early on in pregnancy, soon after the female egg cell has been fertilized by sperm in the fallopian tubes. The fertilized egg then travels down the fallopian tubes, transforming from a single cell to a ball of multiple cells called a zygote. This zygote then implants into the uterus lining. Usually, implantation occurs five to six days after ovulation (when the egg cell is released from an ovary).

21. what is implant and how to use it?


Answer:

In the medical context, an implant is a medical device that is placed inside or on the surface of the body as a replacement for a missing or damaged body part. Implants can be made from a variety of materials such as metal, plastic, or ceramic, and can be designed to perform various functions depending on the needs of the patient.

Some common types of implants include dental implants, breast implants, joint replacements, and pacemakers. Implants are typically implanted through surgical procedures and may require a period of recovery and rehabilitation.

It's important to note that the use of implants is a medical procedure that should only be performed by trained healthcare professionals. If you're interested in receiving an implant, you should consult with a healthcare provider to discuss the risks, benefits, and appropriate usage for your individual case.
