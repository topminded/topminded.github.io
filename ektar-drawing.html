1. what is ektar instrument​


Explanation:

The ektara is a drone lute consisting of a gourd resonator covered with skin, through which a bamboo neck is inserted. It is used in parts of India and Nepal today by Yogis and wandering holy men to accompany their singing and prayers. In Nepal, the instrument accompanies the singing of the Ramayana and Mahabharata.

15. Classify the following instruments1. Ghatam2. Dhol3. Shank4. Bansuri5. Rabab6. Banam7. Tabla8. Manjira9. Karta10. Ektar​


Answer:

1.) Ghatam: Percussion instrument, clay pot-shaped drum from South India

2.) Dhol: Percussion instrument, double-headed drum from North India

3.) Shank: Wind instrument, conch shell used in Hindu rituals

4.) Bansuri: Wind instrument, bamboo flute from North India

5.) Rabab: Stringed instrument, short-necked lute from Afghanistan

6.) Banam: Stringed instrument, bowed instrument from Assam, India

7.) Tabla: Percussion instrument, pair of hand drums from North India

8.) Manjira: Percussion instrument, small cymbals from India

9.) Karta: Wind instrument, horn made of conch shell from Rajasthan, India

10.) Ektar: Stringed instrument, one-stringed drone instrument from India
