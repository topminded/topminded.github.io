1. What is the initial task of curriculum designers?


The curriculum designer's duties include creating detailed and attractive quality curriculum and resources, liaising with subject matter experts, outlining objectives, and providing a possible timeframe.

In general, the steps of curriculum development consist of diagnosing needs, formulating goals, selecting and organizing materials, selecting and organizing learning experiences, and developing evaluation tools.

1. Needs Analysis and Diagnosis

The first step in curriculum development is analyzing and diagnosing needs. Needs analysis can be done by studying three things, namely the needs of students, the demands of society/the world of work, and the expectations of the government (educational policy).

2. Goal Formulation

After the needs are determined, the next step is to formulate goals. The goals in the curriculum are hierarchical, ranging from the most general (complex) goals to more specific and operational goals. The hierarchy of goals includes: national educational goals, institutional goals, curricular goals, as well as instructional goals.

3.Material Organizing

On a macro basis, curriculum materials are prepared based on certain procedures which are part of the overall curriculum development. This is related. with the activity of selecting, assessing, and determining what type of field of study should be taught at a particular type and level of schooling, then the main points and sub-topics of discussion as well as an outline description of the material, also including its scope and sequence. The benchmark for these activities is determined by the objectives of the type and level of the school concerned.

4.Organizing Learning Experiences

After the curriculum materials are selected and organized, the next step is to select and organize the learning experience. The way in which learning experiences are selected and organized can be done using a variety of approaches. strategies, methods and techniques that are tailored to the objectives and nature of the material to be provided.

5.Use of Evaluation Tools

The development of evaluation tools is intended to review whether the activities that have been carried out are in accordance with the objectives that have been set.
