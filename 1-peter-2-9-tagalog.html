Talambuhay ni Peter Abelard

Peter Abelard (/æb.ə.lɑːrd/; Latin: Petrus Abaelardus o Abailardus ; Pranses: Pierre Abélard , binibigkas [a.be.laːʁ]; 1079 - Abril 21, 1142) ay isang medyebal na pilosopo ng pilosopiyang Pranses, teologo, at pinakatanyag na lohika. Ang kanyang pag-ibig para sa, at kapakanan ng, Héloïse d'Argenteuil ay naging maalamat. Ang Chambers Biographical Dictionary ay naglalarawan sa kanya bilang "ang keenest thinker at boldest theologian ng ika-12 siglo".

Sinaunang iskolar ng Pranses, teologo. Latin pangalan Petrus Aberardus Petrus Abaelardus. Ang nagtatag ng teorya ng nominasyon sa pangkalahatang kontrobersiya. Matuto mula sa Roskellinus , Guillaume ng Champaign , Anselmus of Ran. Ang pag-iisip nito ay nagmula sa lohika (dyalektiko argumento) sa interpretasyon ng bibliya, teolohiya, etika, at masalimuot na talakayan ang pundasyon ng mamaya na iskolar na paraan. Ito ay kilala rin bilang isang love affair na may isang babaeng disipulo, si Eloise (1100 - 1164), na nagresulta sa kanyang kastrasyon, at isang roundtrip na sulat. Pangunahing artikulo "Logic" "Maayos at Negatibo (Chic Ethnon)" "Ang kwento ng aking malungkot" atbp.

26. explain each in a relationship to marriage 1. Corinthians 7:4-52. Ephesians 5:25-31 3. peter 3:1-2;7 4. mark 10:9​


1. Corinthians 7:4-5: This passage is often read at weddings to remind married couples of God's desire for them to remain faithful to each other and to his teachings. It calls for them to remain pure in body and mind, sharing a strong bond of unity, and loving each other deeply.

2. Ephesians 5:25-31: This passage prescribes roles for husbands and wives within marriage. It calls for husbands to love their wives as Christ loved the church, and for wives to submit to their husbands. It also instructs husbands to care for their wives as they would their own bodies.

3. Peter 3:1-2;7: This passage encourages married couples to respect each other in their relationship and to show empathy and understanding towards one another as they love and serve God. It also calls for husbands to treat their wives gently and considerately and not to be harsh.

4. Mark 10:9: This passage stresses the seriousness and importance of marriage, as it is described as a permanent and sacred union between a man and woman. It declares that what God has joined together, no human should try to separate.
