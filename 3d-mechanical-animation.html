24. how animators creates a 3D and 4D movies???​


There is a lot of work that goes into creating 3D and 4D movies, and it involves many different steps and processes. Here are some general steps that animators might follow when creating these types of movies:

1. Concept and story development: The first step in creating any movie is to come up with a concept or idea for the story. Once the basic story is in place, the animators will work on developing the characters, settings, and other elements of the movie.

2. Storyboarding: Once the concept is developed, the animators will create storyboards, which are a series of sketches that outline the basic action and movement of the movie.

3. Modeling: In the 3D and 4D movie-making process, the animators will create digital models of the characters, sets, and other objects that will be used in the movie.

4. Texturing: Once the models are created, the animators will add textures to them, which give them a more realistic look and feel. This involves adding color, patterns, and other details to the surfaces of the objects.

5. Rigging: After the models are textured, the animators will create a rig for each character. This is a system of controls that allows the animators to manipulate the character's movements and facial expressions.

6. Animation: With the models and rigs in place, the animators can begin to animate the movie. This involves creating keyframes and in-between frames to create the illusion of movement and action.

7. Lighting: Once the animation is complete, the animators will work on lighting the scenes in the movie. This involves adding light sources, shadows, and other effects to create a realistic look and feel.

8. Rendering: Finally, the animators will render the movie, which involves creating a series of images that make up the final movie. This can take a long time, as rendering can be a complex and time-consuming process.

In the case of 4D movies, additional elements such as special effects, motion seats, and other sensory experiences are added to the movie to create a more immersive experience for the audience.
